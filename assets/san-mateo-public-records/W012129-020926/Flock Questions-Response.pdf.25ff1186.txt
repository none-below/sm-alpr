1. Steps taken to ensure ALPR vendor compliance with Department policy 
ALPR audits are overseen by Lieutenant Casazza, the administrator and highest-ranking 
member of the San Mateo Police Department’s Flock Committee, assigned in July 2025. 
Prior to December 2025, audits were conducted informally. To enhance transparency and 
accountability, the Department formalized the audit process in late 2025 and has 
conducted documented monthly audits since November 2025. These audits review both 
Department member use of the system and Flock’s conﬁguration to ensure policy 
compliance. 
2. Department process for auditing ALPR cameras 
• 
Within the ﬁrst 15 days of each month, the auditor reviews Flock activity from the 
prior month. 
• 
All search activity—internal and external—is reviewed to conﬁrm compliance with 
Department policy and adequacy of search justiﬁcation. Searches lacking sufficient 
justiﬁcation result in notiﬁcation and retraining; continued noncompliance may 
result in progressive discipline. External agencies conducting improper searches are 
removed from access to the Department’s database. 
• 
The auditor reviews system conﬁguration settings, including conﬁrmation that out-
of-state searches are disabled. Authorized partner agencies are spot-checked 
monthly to identify any conﬁguration anomalies. 
3. Veriﬁcation that no Department cameras have statewide or national lookup 
enabled 
The Department conducts monthly reviews of Flock conﬁguration and sharing settings 
to conﬁrm that none of its 31 cameras have statewide or national lookup capabilities 
enabled. 
4. Review of Flock sharing conﬁguration logs 
Yes. The Department reviews Flock sharing conﬁguration logs in addition to search logs. 
5. Ongoing process to detect future conﬁguration changes 
The Department will continue conducting monthly audits. Future audits will include 
targeted conﬁguration spot checks following Flock software updates to ensure continued 
compliance with Department policy.
