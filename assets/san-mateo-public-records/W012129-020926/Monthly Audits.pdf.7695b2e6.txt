SUMMARY: 
The San Mateo Police Department obtained city council approval for the use of Flock Automated 
License Plate Readers in 2020.  After obtaining council approval, the police department entered 
into a contract with Flock Safety for fifty-four cameras.  Of those fifty-four cameras, forty-nine 
are fixed ALPR cameras and are placed in specific locations throughout the city limits.  The five 
remaining cameras are affixed to message boards/traffic trailers.  These message boards/traffic 
trailers are moved throughout the city depending on need. 
ANALYSIS: 
Only authorized users within our agency are granted access to the Flock system, ensuring its use 
is restricted, secure, and compliant with policy.  Our accounts are limited to trained personnel, 
including Officers, Crime Analysts, Dispatchers, and Community Service Officers.  All users are 
required to complete department-approved training to operate or access the Flock ALPR system 
pursuant to Lexipol policy 463.9 and Standard Operating Procedures (SOP’s) 205.3 and 205.4.  
A recent review of our organizational and shared network user accounts was conducted, and it 
was confirmed only our current employees trained and authorized to utilize the system have 
access to our Flock account and that our data is not shared or accessible by any agencies outside 
the State of California. 
Since the inception of our Flock ALPR System, our in-house administrators have conducted 
regular reviews of our internal organizational use to ensure compliance with law and policy.  
This process includes clarifying policy expectations and providing guidance when appropriate.   
The audits and follow-up process is intended to ensure accountability, protect the integrity of the 
ALPR system, safeguard privacy rights, and maintain public trust while ensuring the system is 
used strictly for legitimate law enforcement purposes.   
As the Flock ALPR system has developed, there are now drop-down options that require an 
offense type, reason, and an option for case number or CAD number when our users are 
conducting searches. This new system went in place on December 1, 2025 and should further 
enhance the integrity of our ALPR usage and provide clearer documentation during audits and 
reviews.
