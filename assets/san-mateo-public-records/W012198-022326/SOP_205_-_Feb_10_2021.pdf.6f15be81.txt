205.1   PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This standard operating procedure provides guidelines for the operation and accessing of SMPD’s
Automated License Plate Reader (ALPR) program and data. This document is intended to
supplement SMPD Lexipol Policy 462.
Our Department currently uses four independent ALPR hardware and/or software technology
platforms - Flock Safety Inc., PIPs Technology, Vigilant Solutions, and the Northern California
Regional Intelligence Center (NCRIC) Astrometrics.. Our Department only subscribes to Vigilant
Solutions for access to their ALPR data and does not utilize their hardware. We partner with
the NCRIC to store the ALPR data we collect with PIPs and Flock, and our end-users access
and review this data through PIPs, BOSS, Flock, Vigilant, and the NCRIC’s web-based internet
platform.
In accordance with the California Values Act, ALPR data may not be used for the purposes of
immigration enforcement or other similar duties.
This Operating Procedure, along with SMPD Lexipol Policy 462, shall be posted conspicuously
on our Department website.
205.2   DEFINITIONS
The following definitions for ‘Operator’ and ‘End-User,' are listed in our Policy 462, and come from
California Civil Code 1798.
“Automated license plate recognition operator” or “ALPR operator,” means a person that
operates an ALPR system. For our Department purposes, this will usually refer to our personnel or
authorized designee operating a vehicle or other item of equipment with ALPR cameras collecting
license plate data.
“Automated license plate recognition end-user” or “ALPR end-user” means a person that
accesses or uses an ALPR system. For our Department purposes, this will usually refer to our
personnel or authorized designee accessing historical data that is collected and stored in a
centralized database(s).

system shall complete Department approved training. The training will be coordinated by the
Department Training Division, and shall include the following:

205.4   ALPR END-USER TRAINING
Prior to accessing the ALPR platforms to review data as end-users, any person doing so shall
complete Department approved training. The training will be coordinated by the Department
Training Division, and shall include the following:
•
A general review of how the ALPR Platform works
•
The authorized purposes for accessing the ALPR platform
•
How to access the software system, by way of a login/password protected registry
•
How the ALPR platform maintains logs of operator access and use
•
How the ALPR platform retains and purges data from its systems
Any person(s) using the ALPR platform as end-user shall complete Department approved training.
205.5   ALPR SYSTEM MONITORING AND AUDITING
The Support Services Captain has administrative responsibility for the ALPR platforms. This
responsibility may be delegated to a designee as appropriate.
205.5.1   ALPR PLATFORM AUDITS
PIPs Technology
Data captured via the PIPs Technology PAGIS software program on our patrol mobile units are
transmitted via the mobile unit’s cellular connection to the BOSS search engine server operated
by NCRIC. This data is retained on the PIPs Technology processor in the mobile unit until the
operator exits the software program. At that point all data recorded during that session is purged
from the mobile unit as well as the PIPs Technology server.

Vigilant Solutions Platform
Data captured via Vigilant Solution's software platform is securely hosted by Verio, a subsidiary
of NTT Communications. This data, license plate, date, time, and location are retained and
automatically deleted from the server based on our own retention policy. A record of the end-user
access to the Vigilant Solutions platform is also maintained by Verio, and available for review and
audit by SMPD. This record includes the following:
•
End-User usage information, to include entry of system sign-on information
(username, password, etc)
•
Date and time of access
•
Entry of required search information by the End-User (case number, etc)
•
Connection between searched data and listed justification for the search
•
Any other relevant information as determined by the person conducting the audit
Flock Safety Hardware and Software Platform
Data captured via Flock Safety Inc. is hosted in the cloud using Amazon Web Services and stores
license plate data such as date, time, location, photo of plate and video footage. License plate
data is held in the server based on our own retention policy. Video footage is held for 30 days,
then it is automatically deleted and purged from their system. Access to the data requires VPN
and/or multi-factored authentication. Security, monitoring, and auditing of data maintained in the
Flock Safety Amazon Web database, as well as the end-user review of such data, is described
in the Flock Safety "Government Agency Customer Agreement." A record of the operator's use of
the Flock Safety platform is also maintained by Amazon Web Services and available for review
and audit by SMPD.
This record includes the following:
•
The device used to capture data
•
The date, time, and location of the data capture
•
General information on use of the system (how many license plates uploaded to the
server, etc).
•
Operator usage, to include entry of system sign-on information (username, password,
etc)
•
Date and times of access

NCRIC Astrometrics Platform
The security, monitoring, and auditing of data maintained in the NCRIC databases, as well as end-
user review of such data is described in the NCRIC’s “Automated License Plate Reader Policy.”
This policy is maintained by NCRIC.
The Support Services Captain, or their designee, shall conduct audits of end-users usage of the
NCRIC platform on a quarterly basis to ensure compliance with Lexipol policy 462, this procedure,
and Civil Code 1798. Our Department can obtain such data for the purposes of an audit directly
from the NCRIC. The results of these audits shall be submitted to the Support Services Captain
for their review.
Audits should review information such as:
•
End-user access, to include entry of system sign-on information (username, password,
etc)
•
Date and time of access
•
Entry of required search information by the End-User (case number, etc)
•
Connection between searched data and listed justification for the search
•
Any other relevant information as determined by the person conducting the audit
The Support Services Captain, or their designee, shall conduct audits of operators use of the PIPs
Technology, Flock Safety, Vigilant Solutions, and BOSS and NCRIC platforms on a quarterly basis
to ensure compliance with Lexipol policy 462 and Civil Code 1798. The results of these audits
shall be submitted to the Support Services Captain for their review.
Audits should review information such as:
•
Proper maintenance of system security
•
Date, times, and location of operation
•
Amount of license plate data collected and proper quality of data (photographs
of sufficient quality, license plate data uploaded matches information listed in
photographs, etc)
•
Any other relevant information as determined by the person conducting the audit
205.6   PROTOCOL REVIEW
This Standard Operating Protocol should be reviewed on an annual basis with the Support
Services Captain, or their designee, and any other person(s) as determined by the Support
Services Captain.

205.7   ALPR COMMITTEES
ALPR Steering Committee
An ALPR Steering Committee will include the Support Services Captain, Communications and
Records Manager, Traffic Lieutenant, and Investigations Lieutenant. The Steering Committee
will provide direction to the ALPR subcommittee as changes in policy, legislation, and/or best
practices are identified and work with Command Staff, City Attorney's Office, or other appropriate
stakeholders to ensure the ALPR program conforms with all relevant requirements and standards.
ALPR Subcommittee
The ALPR Subcommittee will consist of an Investigations Sergeant, a Traffic Sergeant, the Police
IT Senior Systems Analyst, and designated officers from Traffic, Investigations, and Patrol. They
will meet to discuss research and develop new policy and procedure updates, equipment issues,
consolidation of different platforms and other projects.
