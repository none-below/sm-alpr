On 4/22/2026 12:21:02 PM, Brian C wrote:
Captain Maravillas,
Thank you for the rolling release on 4/22. The files are interesting reads and informative in their own right -- 
but I want to flag that the batch produced to date does not appear to reach the records described in W012297-
030826, and use this message to focus the request on what I think is the core question.
The emails produced are Flock platform notifications about portal-mediated sharing between Flock customers -
- for example:
- "Home Depot - #0654 (CA) shared Flock cameras with you!" (an outside org sharing its cameras with SMPD)
- "Camera Access Request from US Postal Inspection Service" (an outside agency requesting access to SMPD's 
cameras, routed through the portal for SMPD to accept or reject)
- "Action Requested: Pending Flock Camera Share Requests" (digest reminders to approve pending portal 
requests)
In each of the responses produced, SMPD (or the other Flock customer) remains the footage owner and 
controls whether access is granted through the portal UI. That is normal portal activity, not a disclosure under 
Section 5.3 of San Mateo's contract with Flock.
Section 5.3 addresses a different situation: Flock Group Inc., as vendor, independently accessing, using, 
preserving, or disclosing SMPD Footage to law enforcement authorities, government officials, or third parties 
based on Flock's own "good faith belief" that such action is reasonably necessary -- without SMPD's approval 
or involvement. This concern is not hypothetical. Reporting over the past year has documented instances of 
Flock making customer ALPR data available to federal agencies, including ICE, outside of any explicit sharing 
agreement executed by the customer agency. Section 5.3 is the contractual provision that would authorize that 
kind of disclosure using SMPD-owned Footage, and such disclosures would not surface in the portal sharing 
workflow or in the monthly sharing-configuration audit memos.
With that in mind, I'd like to ask for a direct response to Item 6 of the original request:
"Any policy, procedure, or system documentation describing how SMPD monitors or tracks vendor-initiated 
disclosures under Section 5.3."
Specifically -- does any record exist, of any kind, that describes a mechanism by which the City would become 
aware of a Section 5.3 disclosure? That could include contract provisions, addenda, policies, procedures, 
system configurations, standing requests to Flock, transparency reporting cadences, or internal procedures for 
reviewing vendor-initiated disclosures. If the answer is "no responsive records," a direct statement to that effect 
-- in response to Item 6 specifically -- would resolve this item. If records do exist, I would ask that they be 
produced.
The remaining items (1, 2, 3, 5) likely turn on the answer to Item 6: if no mechanism exists by which the City 
learns of Section 5.3 disclosures, then the absence of notifications, tracking records, and accounting logs is the 
expected result.

Dear Brian C,
 
The City received the following records request from you on March 08, 2026.
 
Pursuant to the California Public Records Act (Government Code § 7920 et seq.), I am requesting the 
following records related to Section 5.3 of the Master Services Agreement between the City of San Mateo and 
Flock Group Inc., executed August 25, 2023.
Section 5.3 authorizes Flock Safety to “access, use, preserve and/or disclose the Footage to law enforcement 
authorities, government officials, and/or third parties” based on Flock’s “good faith belief” that such action is 
“reasonably necessary.” This provision survives contract termination under Section 7.3.
Records requested:
1. All notifications from Flock Safety to SMPD regarding any access, use, preservation, or disclosure of SMPD 
Footage (as defined in MSA § 1.10), including any ALPR information (as defined in Civil Code § 1798.90.5), to
law enforcement authorities, government officials, or third parties pursuant to Section 5.3.
2. All SMPD records documenting, reviewing, or tracking any disclosures by Flock Safety under Section 5.3.
3. Any communications between SMPD and Flock Safety regarding the scope, applicability, or exercise of 
Section 5.3 — including any review conducted following Attorney General Bulletin 2023-DLE-06 (October 27, 
2023), which directed law enforcement agencies to review vendor agreements for provisions allowing access 
beyond California public agencies.
4. Any analysis, memorandum, legal review, or correspondence assessing whether Section 5.3 — which applies 
to “Footage” as defined in the contract, including but not limited to ALPR information — is consistent with 
Civil Code § 1798.90.55(b), which prohibits sharing ALPR information except to California public agencies.
5. Any request from SMPD to Flock Safety for a log, report, or accounting of disclosures made under Section 
5.3, and any response received. Also, any disclosure log, report, or accounting provided by Flock Safety to 
SMPD, whether pursuant to a request or proactively.
6. Any policy, procedure, or system documentation describing how SMPD monitors or tracks vendor-initiated 
disclosures under Section 5.3.
I request responsive records in electronic format where available. If no records are located for any individual 
item, please respond to that item separately and continue processing the remaining items. Please contact me if 
clarification would help narrow or expedite this request.
