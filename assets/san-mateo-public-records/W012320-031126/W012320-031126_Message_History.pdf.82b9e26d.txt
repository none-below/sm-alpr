Thank you for the itemized response. I have follow-ups on several items.
Item 1(a) — End-user audit records: Denied as records of investigation 
(§ 7923.600) and ALPR information (§ 1798.90.55(b)). Access logs are 
administrative records documenting system usage — not "information or 
data collected through the use of an ALPR system" as defined in § 
1798.90.5(b). The California Supreme Court in ACLU v. Superior Court 
(2017) 3 Cal.5th 1032 held that ALPR data collected through mass 
scanning is not exempt as records of investigation because it "is not 
conducted as part of a targeted inquiry into any particular crime." 
Administrative logs of who accessed the system are further removed from 
any investigation than the scan data the court addressed. Government 
Code § 7922.525 requires disclosure of reasonably segregable non-exempt 
portions. I request the access logs the department confirmed on March 5, 
2026 it maintains through Flock, with the following fields redacted: 
license plate numbers or vehicle descriptions searched, search results 
returned, and any information identifying the subject of an 
investigation. The remaining fields — date/time, username, agency 
affiliation, and whether a case number was entered — cannot identify any 
vehicle, person, or investigation.
Item 1(b)–(d) — Operator audit records and audit results: Response 
states all audit records were provided via W012129-020926. Those records 
are three sharing configuration memos reviewing user counts and sharing 
settings. My request specifically noted that sharing configuration memos 
would not be responsive to a request for search activity audit records. 
These memos also do not appear to satisfy the department's own Policy 
463.10 audit requirements, which call for monthly internal audits and 
quarterly external audits — not solely a review of sharing toggles. Can 
you confirm that no audit records exist beyond the three Casazza memos 
previously produced?
Item 2(a)–(c) — Aggregate search counts: Denied as not an existing 
record. Flock's platform maintains search audit logs with fields 
including date, time, username, case number, and offense type. An export 
of these logs is an existing record — not a new compilation. Aggregate 
counts derived from these logs are not "information or data collected 
through the use of an ALPR system" under § 1798.90.5(b). They are 
administrative metadata about system usage that cannot identify any 
vehicle, person, or investigation. The California State Auditor
