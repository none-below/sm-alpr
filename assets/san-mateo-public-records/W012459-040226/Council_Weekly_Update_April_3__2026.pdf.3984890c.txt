Transportation Safety Improvements at Delaware and State
Streets
The City has received a number of emails and media inquiries about the recent pedestrian-
involved collision at Delaware Avenue and State Street.
The City continues to actively seek ways to improve roadway safety. As part of this effort, we will
be making near-term safety improvements at the intersection of North Delaware Street and State
Street, which will include rectangular rapid flashing beacons (RRFBs), and shortened
crossings with striping and delineators, designed and installed by City staff. This work is
anticipated to be completed within 3-4 months.
As part of a follow up capital improvement project, the City is also planning to add concrete
curb extensions, yellow high-visibility crosswalks, new curb ramps and AB 413
daylighting improvements, which prevent cars from parking or stopping within 20 feet of a
crosswalk to help with pedestrian visibility. This work will either be packaged with another
project, or will be completed as part of a Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) project.
Long-range safety improvements, including Class II bike lanes, are also already planned for the
section of Delaware Street spanning from State Street to Peninsula Avenue, as identified in the
Bicycle Master Plan. The City is also advancing planning efforts to improve safety nearby along
the Peninsula Avenue corridor, in collaboration with the City of Burlingame, as part of the grant-
funded Peninsula Corridor Multi-Modal Complete Streets Study.
The City recently completed a major overhaul of its Transportation Safety webpage to better
highlight the City’s commitment to safety, ongoing investments in transportation, recent planning
efforts and capital projects which improve safety for all modes of travel. The City also added a
Transportation Safety email list to its Email Alerts & News | City of San Mateo sign up form.
Public Works staff will keep this page current and send out periodic updates to those who
subscribe for this information.
In the meantime, we are responding to every email that comes in, providing information about
the City's transportation safety efforts, directing folks to the new webpage and our planning
documents and encouraging everyone to sign up for updates.

Coordinated Outreach and Enforcement Efforts Reduce RV
Impacts
The police department continues to dedicate significant resources to addressing recreational
vehicle impacts through a coordinated approach involving the Homeless outreach team,
downtown team unit and vehicle abatement officers.
This strategy balances persistent outreach—focused on providing resources and encouraging
voluntary compliance—with a gradual enforcement process that moves from initial warnings and
citations to towing only when necessary.

--- page 4 ---
In early March, 27 vehicles, including one
non-RV, were identified citywide. By month’s
end, approximately 13 of these vehicles had
left the area. This reduction was the direct
result of staff conducting daily monitoring
and persistent follow-up, which included:
Regular patrols to identify and track
RV locations.
Repeated, person-centered contacts
with occupants to build rapport and
offer services.
Continuous notification, including
posting and re-posting notices to
ensure occupants have multiple
opportunities to comply.
Escalated enforcement, issuing
citations only after multiple attempts
at voluntary compliance were
unsuccessful.
Vehicle abatement officers play a critical
role by marking RVs and returning to
locations several times to verify movement.
As a result, four RVs were towed in March;
however, these tows were only conducted
after staff exhausted numerous outreach efforts and provided repeated notices over an extended
period.
By the end of March, the total count was reduced to nine RVs and one additional vehicle. This
measurable reduction reflects the effectiveness of sustained, proactive engagement. However,
maintaining these results requires a substantial, daily investment of staff time. History shows that
when resources are redirected, RV presence increases quickly. The current success
underscores the necessity of dedicated staffing to manage RV-related impacts through a cycle of
daily fieldwork, compassionate outreach and consistent follow-up.
Extensive Police Work Leads to Conviction in 2020 Homicide
Case
On March 26, 2026, a jury returned guilty verdicts against two defendants for the Oct. 30, 2020,
murder of a father and the attempted murder of his 2-year-old son on Eleanor Drive. The
investigation revealed the shooting was a retaliatory act for a 2019 arson homicide; however,
detectives determined the victim in this case had no connection to that prior crime.
The conviction followed a 24-day trial where both defendants were found guilty of first-degree
murder and assault with a semiautomatic firearm. The jury also found true the special
circumstance of lying in wait. Both men now face life in prison without the possibility of parole,
with sentencing expected in June.
The successful prosecution was the result of an exhaustive investigation that combined
traditional police work with technology, including video surveillance and automated license plate
reader (ALPR) data, which played a critical role in identifying the suspect vehicle:
Video surveillance: Detectives reviewed more than 300 hours of footage to identify a
Nissan Maxima that arrived in the neighborhood five hours before the shooting. This
evidence was critical in securing the "lying in wait" charge.
Vehicle identification: Because license plate reader (ALPR) records were not shared
between agencies at the time, detectives manually coordinated across jurisdictions—

cross-referencing 1,380 DMV records with thousands of toll and traffic records to identify
the suspect vehicle.
Case-breaking discovery: Records showed a suspect purchased the vehicle three days
before the murder and transferred the title out of his name the day of the crime.
Digital forensics: The team analyzed tens of thousands of pages of records from more
than 30 social media accounts to link the suspects to the homicide.
While technology was a vital tool, the case ultimately required significant manpower to analyze
and connect the data—highlighting the importance of both investigative work and the tools that
support it. The police department is proud of the detectives' dedication in seeking justice for the
family and the community.

Finance
Update on January Security Incident
In January 2026, a security incident occurred in which three employees fell victim to a phishing
attack and the attackers changed their payroll payment elections within the Workday system
without their knowledge.
Although staff responded quickly to the security breach, payroll deposits for three employees
were sent to incorrect bank accounts. Staff worked with our bank on the issue and was able to
restore two of the payroll deposits in full and one partially. As a result of this incident, the City will
reflect a loss of $5,658.
Following this security incident, IT Department staff developed plans to improve Workday
security including the deployment of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Workday access.
Employees citywide will be trained on utilizing this tool for Workday with the goal of going live
with this new functionality in May.

San Mateo Recognized as Tree City
USA for 44th Consecutive Year
The Arbor Day Foundation has officially approved San
Mateo’s application for the 2025 Tree City USA
designation. This marks the 44th year the city has
received this recognition, reflecting a long-term
commitment to planting and maintaining trees that
enhance the community’s quality of life.
Established in 1970, the Tree City USA program
encourages municipalities to prioritize expanding their
urban forest while protecting existing trees. To earn
this designation, cities must meet four core standards:
Maintaining a tree board or department.
Implementing a community tree ordinance.
Spending at least $2 per capita on urban forestry.
Participating in an annual Arbor Day celebration.
As one of approximately 3,500 Tree City USA communities nationwide, San Mateo remains
steadfast in its efforts to preserve and expand the local urban forest.
