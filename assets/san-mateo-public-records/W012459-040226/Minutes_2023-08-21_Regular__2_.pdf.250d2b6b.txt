Zoning Code Amendments for City Projects, Modifications to Planning Applications, and Height Limitations and
Exemptions – Ordinance Adoption
Adopt an Ordinance to amend the San Mateo Municipal Code Sections 27.02.060 “ Height Limitations and
Exemptions”, 27.02.140 “ Exceptions”, 27.06.020 “ Zoning Administrator – Powers and Duties”, 27.06.050 “ Council
Consideration and Determination”, and 27.08.080 “ Planning Application Modifications” to streamline the review
process for city projects, clarify the modification process for approved planning applications, and clarify height

review pursuant to California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Section 15061(b)(3).

Municipal Code Amendments for New Development Parking Garage Security Requirements – Ordinance Adoption

with less than five dwelling units, and standardize video surveillance and lighting requirements; and determine the
amendments qualify for a Categorical Exemption from further environmental review pursuant to California
Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Section 15061(b)(3).
Enactment:  Ordinance No. 2023- 04

New Year' s Eve Storm - Status Report and Staff Direction
Matt Fabry, Public Works Deputy Director, and Chuck Anderson, PE President Schaaf & Wheeler provided a
presentation regarding an overview of the stormwater draining system, impacts from the 2022 New Year' s Eve
storm, the Marina Lagoon, Schaaf & Wheeler analysis of what went wrong on New Year’ s Eve, future wet weather
preparation and next steps.
Council asked questions of staff.
Public Comment – Daniel Cwirko- Godycki requested to please release rainy day funds immediately, support
stormwater protection initiative. Russ Willsher concerned with weather conditions; release rainy day funds. Amy
Jussel Bedard urged to release rainy day funds and the stormwater fee and is concerned that voters don’ t see it as
a city wide issue. Mantosh Kumar stated take action now. Rich Kranz commented about a lagoon level meter with
a camera on it and to release rainy day funds. Lawrence Ives commented on infrastructure issues. Michelle Cwirko-
Godcki said make everyone safe - use rainy day funds. 
Nikki Huth advised to work on the east and west side simultaneously. Annette Bianco commented on severe
flooding and urged taking urgent action to make everyone safer this winter. Andrea Shov voiced that neighbors
were in a panic, release rainy day funds. Keith Carlson remarked to release rainy day fund and go over and above. 
Heidi Moser concerned with pumps. Lila Kanda concerned with upcoming heavy rains and asked for money. 
Valerie Oblath stated cannot control mother nature; prevent and prepare for coming winter. Mareva Godfrey
declared the storm was terrifying. 
Charlie Schirripa said use the rainy day fund. Andy Middione expressed urgent action needed; use rainy day. Katia
Terentyera said no time to waste; release rainy day fund. Anna Cwirko- Godycki stated to support flood free San
Mateo; release rainy day fund. Alex Cwirko- Godycki needs critical down payment to start. Jeff Lamont commented
on impacts from flooding on lives and requested $ 8.5 million from rainy day fund. Jay Kuhre declared water is the
biggest threat to the city of San Mateo. Susan Manheimer urged to please use the $8.5 million, don’t kick the can
down the road. Kevin Simpson concerned with dam and the earthquake impact. Rick Sakuda explained dredging
