Thank you for the responsive records on W012461-040226. I'd like to

The four audit memoranda produced appear to be Policy 463.10 audits 
responsive to Items 1 and 2. Item 3 requested records produced under SOP 
205.5.1, which establishes requirements distinct from Policy 463.10 in 
reporting chain, scope, platforms, and audit criteria.
Reporting chain:
SOP 205.5.1 requires quarterly audit results submitted to the Support 
Services Captain. All four memoranda are addressed to the Chief of 
Police. No submission to the Support Services Captain was produced.
Platform scope:
SOP 205.5.1 requires operator audits of all ALPR platforms. All four 
memoranda review Flock only. SOP 205 names four additional platforms 
(Vigilant, BOSS, PIPS, NCRIC). The department has stated it no longer 
uses Vigilant, BOSS, or PIPS [W012129-020926]. However, an operator 
audit of these platforms should confirm they remain decommissioned and 
that no SMPD data persists in them. Additionally, SMPD maintains a 
Memorandum of Understanding with NCRIC [W012376-032026] and actively 
shares ALPR data with NCRIC via the Flock platform — NCRIC is not a 
decommissioned platform. SOP 205 has not been amended to reflect changes 
in platform usage — the requirement as written covers all five.
Operator audit criteria:
SOP 205.5.1 operator audits are quarterly. The four memoranda are 
monthly. The memoranda review sharing configuration (data sharing 
limited to California agencies, "Revoke Out-of-State Sharing" setting 
selected). SOP 205.5.1 does not specify sharing configuration review as 
an operator audit criterion.
End-user audit criteria:
SOP 205.5.1 requires quarterly end-user audits including review of 
search justifications and case number entries. The March 2026 memorandum 
references "search reasons" and "entries not in compliance with Lexipol 
Policy 463" but does not describe: (a) how search justifications were 
evaluated, (b) whether case number entries were reviewed, (c) what 
compliance criteria were applied, (d) how many searches were reviewed, 
(e) whether any non-compliant entries were identified, or (f) what 
corrective actions, if any, resulted. The earlier memoranda (December 
2025, January 2026, February 2026) do not reference search activity at 
all.
NCRIC end-user audits:
Item 4 returned "No responsive records." SMPD maintains a Memorandum of 
Understanding with NCRIC [W012376-032026] and shares ALPR data with 
NCRIC via the Flock platform. NCRIC appears on SMPD's Flock sharing list

and itself shares with 318 organizations — including private 
institutions that do not qualify as public agencies under § 
1798.90.5(f). SOP 205.5.1 requires quarterly end-user audits of NCRIC 
specifically, including review of search justifications and case number 
entries. No audit of NCRIC's use of SMPD ALPR data — whether accessed 
directly or via the Flock network — has been produced.
Could you confirm that no records exist matching the SOP 205.5.1 
requirements — quarterly audits submitted to the Support Services 
Captain, covering all ALPR platforms, with end-user audits of NCRIC 
including review of search justifications and case number entries — 
separate from the Policy 463.10 memoranda already produced?
