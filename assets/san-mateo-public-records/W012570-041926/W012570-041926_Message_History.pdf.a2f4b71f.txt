Record(s) Requested: Pursuant to the California Public Records Act (Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq.), I 
request the following records from the San Mateo Police Department.
This request seeks existing records only (Gov. Code § 7922.530; Sierra Club v. Superior Court (2013) 57 
Cal.4th 157).
Context: SMPD's Flock transparency portal listed University of the Pacific ("UOP") among 
organizations with access to SMPD ALPR data between at least February 18, 2026 and March 11, 2026. 
UOP is a private institution not subject to CPRA (confirmed by UOP's Chief of Police in writing).
Scope of records. This request reaches records held by Flock Safety as SMPD's agent or contractor, or 
otherwise accessible to SMPD through its Flock account. Such records are "writings ... prepared, owned, 
used, or retained" by SMPD within the meaning of Gov. Code § 7920.530 and are subject to CPRA 
regardless of which server they physically reside on. Three SMPD-sourced instruments confirm this 
directly:
1. SMPD's Flock Master Services Agreement, § 4.1 — provides that SMPD retains ownership of the data 
processed by Flock on SMPD's behalf.
2. SMPD Policy 463 (published Dec 12, 2025) — § 463.2 assigns "ALPR data retention and access" to the 
Field Operations Captain; § 463.5 makes the Field Operations Captain "responsible for ensuring 
systems and processes are in place for the proper collection and retention of ALPR data" and refers to 
"ALPR information gathered and retained by this Department."
3. SMPD SOP 205 (published Nov 10, 2025), § 205.5.1 — Flock ALPR data "is held in the server based

on our own retention policy," and end-user and operator audit records "maintained by Amazon Web 
Services" (for Flock), Verio (for Vigilant), PIPs, or NCRIC are "available for review and audit by

Together, these establish that the requested records are SMPD records within the meaning of Gov. Code

Records requested — each item is limited to records or information exchanged between SMPD and UOP 
during the sharing period, whether disclosed by SMPD to UOP (a non-public institution), provided to 
UOP by Flock Safety on SMPD's behalf, or provided by UOP to SMPD:
1. ALPR data SMPD disclosed to UOP — plate reads, detections, hotlist hit notifications, query results, 
or any other ALPR information (as defined by Civil Code § 1798.90.5(c)) that SMPD transmitted to 
UOP or returned to UOP through the Flock platform, including any bulk exports, canned reports, data 
feeds, or files provided to UOP personnel.
2. Flock platform audit-log entries memorializing UOP's access to SMPD data and SMPD's 
corresponding disclosures — whether held by SMPD directly or by Flock Safety on SMPD's behalf — 
including:
a. search queries UOP user accounts executed against SMPD data;
b. plate reads or results returned to UOP users in response to those queries;
c. timestamps for each query and response; and
d. the UOP user account identifiers associated with each query.
3. Any documentation SMPD or Flock provided to UOP describing the scope of UOP's access to SMPD 
ALPR data — for example, camera lists, coverage maps, retention-window notices, or hotlist/alert-rule 
descriptions made available to UOP.
4. The memorandum of understanding, sharing agreement, data-use agreement, or equivalent executed 
instrument between SMPD and UOP governing UOP's access to SMPD ALPR data. In response to prior 
PRA W012174-021826, SMPD stated that agency access authorization is "completed within the Flock 
platform" and that no MOU or sharing-configuration records were located in agency files. Per the Scope 
of records paragraph above, any such instrument held by Flock Safety on SMPD's behalf is an SMPD 
record under Gov. Code § 7920.530 and is within the scope of this request.
5. Communications between SMPD personnel and UOP personnel concerning UOP's access to SMPD 
ALPR data, including the establishment, continuation, or termination of that access.
Anticipated exemption issues:
* Civil Code § 1798.90.55(b) (via Gov. Code § 7927.705). The statute's sharing restriction operates 
against a public agency's disclosure of ALPR information to non-public entities. The SMPD-to-UOP 
component of the records requested here is information SMPD already disclosed to such an entity. 
Information UOP provided to SMPD does not implicate that provision's sharing restriction.

* Gov. Code § 7923.600 (investigation records). § 7923.600 exempts records of complaints to, or 
investigations conducted by, a state or local police agency, and investigatory files compiled by such an 
agency. Two features of the present situation sit outside that scope: (a) the records requested are not 
investigative files compiled for a specific SMPD investigation, but bulk ALPR data SMPD made broadly 
accessible to UOP for undefined use; and (b) any investigative use of that data by UOP is an 
investigation by a private institution — UOP is not a state or local police agency within § 7923.600's text.
* Cal. Const. Art. I § 1 / third-party privacy. If plate data implicating third-party privacy appears in the 
produced records, the appropriate redaction baseline is the one SMPD applied in disclosing that data to 
UOP. Whatever redactions SMPD applied (or did not apply) in that disclosure reflect SMPD's operative 
privacy judgment on that data.
* Segregability (§ 7922.525(b)). Items 3 through 5 (scope documentation, agreements, communications) 
concern the fact and terms of the sharing arrangement rather than underlying plate data, and should be 
producible with minimal or no redaction regardless of how Items 1 and 2 are handled.
If any portion is denied, please:
1. provide specific exemption citations per Gov. Code § 7922.540;
2. identify the withheld record type and volume;
3. indicate whether the denial rests on a claim that the records are held by Flock Safety rather than 
SMPD; and
4. if the denial invokes Civil Code § 1798.90.55(b) or any other rationale treating the records as 
protected "ALPR information" restricted from disclosure to non-public entities, state whether SMPD 
considers its prior disclosure of the same records to UOP — a non-public institution — to have been 
lawful under that provision, and identify the legal authority supporting that position.
