As discussed at our Command Staff meeting, pursuant to Penal Code 13650, which requires POST and each local law enforcement agency to conspicuously post on their websites all current standards, policies, practices, operating procedures, and education and training materials that would otherwise be available under the California Public Records Act, we need to move forward with publishing as many of our Standard Operating Procedures as appropriate alongside our policies. As a reminder, the only areas we will not publish are confidential investigative processes that, if released, would pose a safety issue for our department personnel. 

This will be a team effort. I have completed a review of all 38 SOPs and assigned them for review based on current assignments. Please see the attached list. In some cases, you were assigned an SOP because you supervise the Sergeant responsible for that area. Please feel free to collaborate with the appropriate members of your team as needed.

Please complete the following by 3/3/25:
	•	Carefully review your assigned SOP’s.
	•	Advise whether each document is appropriate for public posting.
	•	Identify any necessary updates to operational practices and coordinate those revisions with Paul so we can ensure our SOP’s are current, clear, and aligned with how we are doing business.
	•	Once you have completed the above, please send an “all clear” or “do not post” to Paul for your assigned SOP’s 

Additionally, if you are currently working on any new SOP’s, please send them to Paul as soon as possible to ensure timely review and release.

Our goal is to remain compliant, transparent, and operationally sound. I appreciate everyone taking the time to give this thoughtful attention.
