Record(s) Requested: Pursuant to the California Public Records Act (Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq.), I 
request the following records from the San Mateo Police Department.
This request seeks existing records only (Gov. Code § 7922.530; Sierra Club v. Superior Court (2013) 57 
Cal.4th 157).
Context: In response to PRA W012198-022326 (closed March 3, 2026), the Department stated: "the 
Department does not currently have the associated SOPs posted on its website. We acknowledge this 
oversight and are actively working to ensure the SOPs are published as soon as possible." SOP 205.1 
requires that "This Operating Procedure, along with SMPD Lexipol Policy 462, shall be posted 
conspicuously on our Department website." Civil Code § 1798.90.51(b) imposes a parallel conspicuous-
posting obligation on the agency's usage and privacy policy. As of April 20, 2026, the Department's 
License Plate Readers page at cityofsanmateo.org/3211/License-Plate-Readers links only to the full 
policy manual; SOP 205 is not linked from that page, nor is it returned by a site-wide search of 
cityofsanmateo.org.
Records requested:
1. The current URL at which SOP 205 is posted on the San Mateo Police Department or City of San 
Mateo website. If SOP 205 is still not posted as of the date this request is processed, please confirm that.

2. If SOP 205 has been posted since March 3, 2026: the date it was posted, and any web publishing log, 
content management system record, or internal communication reflecting the posting event (for

4. Any records identifying other SMPD Standard Operating Procedures that the Department considers 
subject to conspicuous posting under SOP 205.1, Civil Code § 1798.90.51(b), or Department practice. 
The SOP index, table of contents, or equivalent listing is responsive.
5. If SOP 205 or any other SOP has been posted since March 3, 2026, the URL(s) at which those 
documents are posted.
6. Any legal memorandum, risk assessment, compliance analysis, or written position — prepared by or 
for the Department, the City Attorney's office, or City administration — addressing the Department's 
obligations under SOP 205.1 and Civil Code § 1798.90.51(b) regarding conspicuous posting of SOP 205 
and Policy 463, or addressing the Department's compliance status with those obligations. Drafts, memos, 
email analyses, and briefing materials are responsive.
Anticipated exemption issues:
* Gov. Code § 7923.600 (investigation records). A URL, a posting date, and routine web-publishing 
workflow records are not records of investigation.
* Civil Code § 1798.90.55(b). The statute restricts sharing of ALPR information — license plate data, 
reads, hit notifications, and associated access records. A public URL where an ALPR policy document is 
posted, and administrative records about posting that document, are not ALPR information as defined 
by § 1798.90.5(c). The same statutory scheme affirmatively requires the policy itself to be posted 
conspicuously on the agency website (§ 1798.90.51(b)(1)); § 1798.90.55(b) cannot coherently be invoked 
to withhold the URL at which a document the statute mandates be public has been published.
* Deliberative process. Items 2 and 3 concern the execution of a publication decision the Department has 
already publicly announced (March 3, 2026 response to W012198-022326), not pre-decisional 
deliberation about whether to post.
If any portion is denied, please provide specific exemption citations per Gov. Code § 7922.540. If volume 
is burdensome, please contact me to narrow scope rather than denying categorically.
Thank you.
