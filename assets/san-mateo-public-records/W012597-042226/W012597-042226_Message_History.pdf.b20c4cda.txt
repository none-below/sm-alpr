Subsequent production under W012297-030826 included a December 3, 2025 email from 
hello=flocksafety.com@mail.flocksafety.com to SValencia@cityofsanmateo.org titled "Action

date 09-10-2025) among ten pending share requests to SMPD.
That email pre-dates the W012328 Item 1 window (1/1/26 forward) and may not have been within the 
original scope. Two observations follow:
(a) The 12/3/25 email establishes that Flock sends SMPD named-agency add-event notifications via 
email. It follows that removal events — if Flock sends equivalent notifications — would also surface in 
SMPD inboxes. That contradicts the "no corresponding records" framing for the UOP removal.
(b) The "I did not locate a log of this information in the Flock platform" answer addresses only the Flock 
platform and does not address whether removal notifications arrived via email.
Accordingly, I request:
1. All emails sent from or received by any SMPD email address, from September 1, 2025 to the present,

The records requested in categories 1 and 2 concern administrative configuration of SMPD's external-
agency sharing network — specifically UOP's addition to the sharing list in September 2025, its publicly 
visible presence on the Flock transparency portal, and its removal between February 18 and March 11, 
2026. Records of this kind are not records of a law-enforcement investigation within the meaning of 
Government Code § 7923.600; the subsequent production under W012297-030826 of Flock-originated 
access-request and sharing-confirmation emails without exemption confirms that categorization. Civil 
Code § 1798.90.55(b) restricts the sharing of ALPR information (plate reads, search results); it does not 
restrict disclosure of agency names, access-request dates, removal dates, permission types, or internal 
discussion about which agencies should appear on a public sharing list.
To the extent Item 1 surfaces emails that contain a mix of administrative content and material the city 
believes is exempt, Government Code § 7922.525(b) requires segregable production: the non-exempt 
portions — including at minimum the From, To, Date, and Subject fields — must be produced with the 
exempt portions redacted. Please do not apply a blanket exemption where segregation is available. For 
any record withheld in whole or in part, please identify the record individually by sender, recipient, date, 
and subject; cite the specific exemption asserted; and — where § 7923.600 is asserted — identify the 
investigation to which the record relates and whether that investigation is active or closed.
