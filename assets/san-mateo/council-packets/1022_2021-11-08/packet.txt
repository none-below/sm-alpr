SUBJECT:
Age Friendly Action Plan  – Update and Review 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation with updates regarding the Age Friendly Action Plan including formatting and provide feedback. 
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo has been designated as an Age-Friendly City by the World Health Organization for its commitment 
to being a welcoming community for people of all ages.
The World Health Organization established the Global Network of Age-Friendly Cities and Communities to connect
organizations worldwide with the common vision of making their community a great place to grow old in as a response to 
global population aging. It focuses on action at the local level that fosters the full participation of older people in 
community life and promotes healthy and active aging.
San Mateo received this distinction with the help of an Age-Friendly Community Task Force comprised of primarily older 
adults in the community. The task force, in collaboration with the Senior Commission, is committed to conducting a 
baseline assessment on the needs of older adults, establishing a 3-year action plan to address the identified needs, and 
developing tools to measure progress.

SUBJECT:
Letter of Support for the re-opening of the Mills-Peninsula Medical Center Aquatics Program (Mickelson Pool)
RECOMMENDATION:
Discuss the possibility of crafting a letter of support for the re-opening of the Mills-Peninsula Medical Center Aquatics 
Program (Mickelson Pool).
BACKGROUND:
During previous commission meetings members of the public expressed a request for the Commission to offer support to 
their cause of keeping the Mickelson Pool open at Mills-Peninsula Medical Center. Although it is understood that this 
facility and service falls out of the jurisdiction of the department, commissioners agreed to discuss the possibility of 
drafting a letter of support.  At this meeting, commissioners will discuss whether to move forward with crafting a letter of 
support when resources and safety conditions allow. An example draft letter will be used to help guide the conversation.

Dear Ms. Wagner,
The City of San Mateo’s Senior Citizens Commission is sending you this letter of support in the 
hopes that the Mickelson Aquatic Facility will re-open when conditions caused by the Covid-19 pandemic 
improve.
We are a commission appointed by the San Mateo City Council with a focus on improving the lives 
of older adults in the San Mateo community. As such, we recognize the positive impact that the Mickelson 
Aquatics Facility has had on the lives of our senior and disabled populations. We respect and understand 
that you have communicated that the decision to permanently close the pool was made primarily because of 
the impact of Covid-19 on resources and safety. 
We respectfully ask that if conditions improve, and resources become available, that the Mickelson 
Pool re-open and therapeutic aquatic programs be restored.
Respectfully
Ellen Wang
City of San Mateo Senior Commission Chair
