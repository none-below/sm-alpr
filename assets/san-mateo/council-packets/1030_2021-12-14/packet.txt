SUBJECT:
Pre-Application Review for a Proposed Three-Story Self-Storage Building at 1050 S. Amphlett Blvd. (PA-2020-071)
RECOMMENDATION:
Review the proposed use and project design on a preliminary basis, receive public comments, and provide input to the

BACKGROUND:
The applicant, Endeavor Assets, LLC., has submitted a Preliminary Planning Application (PA-2020-071) to construct a new 
three-story self-storage building on the vacant lot at 1050 S. Amphlett Boulevard. The proposed building consists of 182 
storage units spread among the three floors with six off-street parking spaces. As proposed, the project would require a 
Special Use Permit (SUP) for the proposed commercial self-storage use and a Site Plan and Architectural Review (SPAR) for 
the new commercial building, grading and associated site improvements, and relief from the on-site loading requirement.
As a Pre-Application Study Session, the Planning Commission should focus its review on the project’s proposed use and 
compatibility with the adjacent residential uses, building design and intensity, and other aspects necessary for the 
Commission to make positive findings and approve the SUP and SPAR after the formal planning application is submitted 
and reviewed by staff. This report will provide an overview of the project’s location and development history, building 
program, applicable codes and policies, and introduce two specific elements of the project that would benefit from 
Planning Commission input. Additionally, staff requests that the Commission provide input and direction on other project 
aspects, if any, that should be considered as part of the formal planning application.
PROJECT DESCRIPTION:
Project Site
The project site is located at 1050 S. Amphlett Boulevard, on the northwest corner of the intersection of Sunnybrae Blvd. 
and S. Amphlett Blvd. The site has a narrow rectangular shape, with a frontage of 472 feet along S. Amphlett Blvd and a 
narrower 31-foot frontage along Sunnybrae Blvd, and a total lot area of 14,647 square feet (0.34-acres). The site’s General 
Plan Land Use Designation is Community/Regional Commercial, and it is zoned C3-2 (Community/ Regional Commercial). 
Adjacent uses include U.S. Highway 101 to the east and the Arbor Rose Condominiums to the west. The existing site is 
substantially undeveloped and consists of two existing trash enclosures that service the adjacent Arbor Rose 
Condominiums. The project site and the Arbor Rose Condominiums, which share the C3-2 zoning designation and

Community/ Regional Commercial General Plan Land Use Designation, comprise the former San Mateo Times site. The 
parcels adjacent to the north and south along S. Amphlett Blvd. have a zoning designation of C4-1 (Service Commercial)

Project History
The City Council originally approved the redevelopment of the project site into residential uses and a three-story self-
storage building under PA-2009-052 on June 21, 2010. The project was known as the “San Mateo Times Redevelopment” 
and included approvals for the following entitlements:

Site Plan and Architectural Review (SPAR) and Special Use Permit (SUP) for a 3.51-acre Planned Unit Development 
(PUD) to allow for the construction of 14 single-family detached dwellings, 60 townhomes, and a three-story self-
storage building;

A Zoning Reclassification of the development area to C3-2 (Community/Regional Commercial); and, 

A General Plan Amendment to amend the Land Use designation to Community/Regional Commercial.
At the time of application, the development area consisted of two zoning designations: C4-1 (Service Commercial) and R2 
(Two-Family Dwellings). While a self-storage building would have been permitted by-right in the site’s prior C4-1 zoning 
designation, residential uses in the C4 zone are explicitly prohibited. To allow residential uses through a Special Use 
Permit, the 2009 planning application requested a Zoning Reclassification to C3-2 and a General Plan Amendment. 
Because a building permit for the originally-approved self-storage building was not filed within the three-year vesting 
period and additional one-year extension provided by Municipal Code Section 27.62.230, the entitlements for the 
originally-approved self-storage building expired; however, the City Council’s adoption of the site’s Zoning Reclassification 
from C4-1 to C3-2 remained valid. Building permits for the residential portion of the 2009 planning application were issued 
and the units have since been constructed, which are now known as the Arbor Rose Condominiums. 
The Zoning & General Plan map changes approved under PA-2009-052 are included in Attachment 2. A Current Zoning 
Map is included in Attachment 3.
At this time, the applicant is proposing a self-storage facility, which is the same use that was previously approved on the 
site, but with a different architectural design.  
Building Program and Site Plan
The proposed project is a three-story self-storage building that is 23,480 square feet in size and includes 182 public storage 
units ranging in size from 25 square-feet to 200 square-feet. There are six off-street parking spaces (four of which are 
covered) that are accessed via two driveways on S. Amphlett Blvd. There is no on-site loading, however, undedicated 
loading/parking spaces are proposed within the public right-of-way along S. Amphlett Blvd. The area between the back of 
sidewalk and the face of the building (approximately eight feet in depth) along the property’s S. Amphlett frontage will 
include landscaping and trees. Additionally, the project proposes to enlarge the two trash enclosures to service both the 
proposed building and the existing residential condominiums. 
The proposed architectural style is best characterized as industrial based on the rectangular massing of the building, 
uniform building height, and its use of smooth, textured, and ribbed fiber cement panels to express various building 
volumes. A discussion of the building design is continued in the Items for Planning Commission Discussion section of this 
report. Project plans showing the proposed site plan, floor plans, and elevations are included in Attachment 4.

APPLICABLE CODE AND POLICY REVIEW:
General Plan
The General Plan Land Use Map designates the project site as Community/ Regional Commercial, which is intended to 
provide goods and services that rely on large trade areas and are usually not available in neighborhood shopping centers. 
A preliminary list of General Plan policies applicable to the proposed project is included in Attachment 5.
As a commercial building located on the former San Mateo Times site, the project presently conforms to Land Use Element 
Specific Area Policy PA 5.5 Former San Mateo Times Site, in that the project would be located along the S. Amphlett Blvd 
frontage for continuity of existing commercial uses and would provide a buffer from the freeway. The preliminary list of 
General Plan policies in Attachment 5 includes other policies for the Commission’s consideration in this study session.
Zoning Code
The project site is zoned C3-2 (Community/ Regional Commercial), which implements the goals of the General Plan in 
encouraging the continuation of commercial uses along S. Amphlett Blvd and accommodating service uses of community-
wide and regional significance. The project is also subject to the development standards of the C3-2 zoning district, found 
in Chapter 27.34 of the Zoning Code, including floor area, building height, off-street parking and buffers. 
The project conforms to the floor area ratio, off-street parking, and building height development standards of the C3-2 
zoning district. The project proposes a FAR of 1.6 where a maximum of 2.0 is allowed. The proposed building height of 34 
feet also conforms with the maximum height limit of 35 feet. The project will provide six off-street parking spaces, which is 
the minimum number allowed under the Zoning Code. The Zoning Code requires one on-site loading space, which may be 
omitted upon approval of a variance (Municipal Code Section 27.78.040) or a Site Plan and Architectural Review (SPAR) 
(Municipal Code Section 27.64.390).
While the Municipal Code does not require setbacks for properties located in commercial zoning district, Section 27.30.060 
of the Municipal Code requires all commercial zoning districts to provide buffers, or transitional yards, when adjoining a 
residential zoning district.  The project site, which shares its street-side (Sunnybrae Blvd.) and rear property lines with the 
Arbor Rose Condominiums, has a five-foot public utility easement along S. Amphlett Blvd, which effectively acts as a five-
foot front setback. Additionally, the project proposes a 10’-6” street-side setback along Sunnybrae Blvd, a 15-foot side 
setback along the northern property line, and no rear setback. Because the Municipal Code only requires buffers between 
commercial and residential zoning districts (not residential uses), the proposed project is not required to provide a buffer 
from the adjacent residential use and, therefore, conforms with the C3-2 buffer development standards. However, this 
interface between the commercial building and the adjacent residential use may still be something that the Commission 
provides input on to ensure that the intent of the Zoning Code is being met.
A project data sheet listing the applicable Zoning Code standards and the pre-application’s conformance to them are 
included in Attachment 6. 
Pedestrian Master Plan
The 2012 Citywide Pedestrian Master Plan contains recommendations to improve the pedestrian realm throughout the 
City. Upon filing of the formal planning application, staff will evaluate the project for consistency with the Pedestrian 
Master Plan, particularly for sidewalk standards. Based on the type of development, the applicable sidewalk standard is 
A.8 Retail/Commerical Type C New Development along the S. Amphlett Blvd frontage. This standard recommends a 
minimum overall sidewalk width of 14 feet along the S. Amphlett Blvd frontage.
As presently designed, the project proposes an overall sidewalk width of 8’-7”, consisting of a 5’7” through zone and a 3’0” 
frontage zone, along S. Amphlett Blvd, which is less than the recommended overall width of 14’-0” in the Pedestrian 
Master Plan.  It should be noted, however, that the overall width of A.8 Retail/Commerical Type C New Development 
sidewalk standard includes a planter/furniture zone, a through zone, and a frontage zone, which is necessary for 
commercial areas with a mix of uses and high pedestrian traffic.  As the project site is located on a frontage road for U.S. 
Highway 101 and surrounded by service commercial uses, a reduced overall sidewalk width may be appropriate at this

The 2020 Bicycle Master Plan provides policies and recommendations to implement citywide infrastructure improvements

ITEMS FOR PLANNING COMMISSION DISCUSSION:
Proposed Use
As previously indicated, the City Council approved a Special Use Permit (SUP), Zoning Reclassification, and General Plan 
Amendment in June of 2010 (PA-2009-052) for a mixed-use Planned Unit Development (PUD) to allow residential uses and 
a three-story self-storage building. The entitlements for the self-storage portion of the 2009 application expired because 
building permits for the self-storage building were not submitted prior to the PUD’s expiration. It should be noted, 
however, that despite the expiration of the self-storage facility’s entitlements, the City Council’s adoption of the site’s 
Zoning Reclassification from C4-1 to C3-2 remains valid notwithstanding the expiration of the self-storage facility’s 
entitlements.
The Planning Commission should provide input on the proposed self-storage facility use, which will require the approval of 
a new Special Use Permit (SUP) as part of the formal application review.  When granting an SUP, findings must be made 
that the use will not have an adverse effect on the general health and/or welfare of the community, and that it will not 
cause disturbance to adjacent properties by traffic or by excessive noise, smoke, odor, noxious gas, dust, glare, heat, 
fumes or industrial waste. The Commission, when considering the proposed use, should consider the impact to the 
surrounding properties from the intensity of use and the building’s massing, and its impact to waste management, parking 
and trip generation.  The Commission should also consider concerns raised by community members at the project’s 
November 4, 2021 Neighborhood Meeting over the project’s anticipated effect on waste management, traffic, and natural 
light onto adjacent residential uses and comment on the project’s intensity as a way to reduce its effects on the adjacent 
residential uses to the southwest.
As indicated above in the Project Site section, the project site is located along S. Amphlett Blvd., which consists 
substantially of parcels zoned C4-1 from E. 5th Ave. southward to Folkston Ave. Other warehouse and storage uses exist 
along S. Amphlett Blvd and are generally up to two stories in height, in compliance with the maximum height limit of 30 
feet in the C4-1 zoning district. Additionally, the C4-1 zoned parcels along this portion of S. Amphlett Blvd. abut residential 
uses to the west, as does as the subject site. As most of the buildings along this C4-1 zoned section of S. Amphlett Blvd. 
were constructed prior to 1961, when buffers between commercial and residential district were first required, they are 
currently nonconforming in regard to required buffers.  If any of the commercial buildings were to be demolished or 
structurally altered so that their nonconforming status is lost, all new construction would have to provide a minimum 
buffer of ten feet along the rear, or western, property line as required by Section 27.30.060(a)(3) of the Municipal Code.
Because the existing townhomes adjacent to the project site are not in a residential district, the Municipal Code does not 
require a buffer between the townhomes and proposed storage facility and would permit a 35-foot-tall, three-story 
building to be constructed on the project site’s shared property line with residential uses, subject to applicable Zoning 
Code requirements. The proposed building, if approved in its current state, would be the tallest building with the least 
required separation from the adjacent residential uses permitted along this section of S. Amphlett Blvd.
As indicated previously, the project proposes to enlarge the two trash enclosures on the site to service both the storage 
building and the condominiums. At the Neighborhood Meeting, community members stated that the dumping of trash and 
furniture frequently occurs on the project site, despite the presence of the trash enclosures, and raised concerns over the 
potential increase in dumping from the customers of the proposed storage facility. It should be noted that Municipal Code 
Section 7.32.060 prohibits dumping any waste on a lot unless deemed a dumping ground by the City. Submittal of a solid

An additional concern raised at the Neighborhood Meeting are potential traffic impacts related to the proposed use, which 
are typically associated with commercial vehicles and moving vans. Due to the project’s location between the Sunnybrae 
neighborhood and U.S. Highway 101, site access is limited and vehicles accessing the project would be using residential 
streets (most likely 10th, Birch and Sunnybrae). As part of the project’s formal planning application review, the City will 
conduct a transportation analysis to assess the project’s traffic impacts and determine if mitigations are needed. 
Furthermore, the building’s intensity also has a direct impact on the size and shape of the design. Community feedback 
raised at the Neighborhood Meeting included lowering the height of the building and/or reduce the number of stories to 
allow more movement of light and air into the adjacent residential use. Lowering the height or reducing the number of 
stories could also reduce the potential amount of trash and unwanted furniture accumulated on site and reduce project 
generated traffic by reducing the number of storage units, and therefore the number of people utilizing the site. Additional 
discussion on the project’s building design and massing are included in the Building Design section below.
Based on the current proposal and the discussion in this section, staff anticipates the project may be able to make the 
positive findings that the use will not cause disturbance to adjacent properties by excessive noise, smoke, odor, noxious 
gas, dust, glare, heat, fumes or industrial waste. As part of the project’s formal planning application review, the City will 
conduct noise and vibration and air quality analyses, in addition to an environmental site assessment, to evaluate the 
project’s impacts on adjacent properties by these environmental factors and determine if mitigation measures are needed. 
Staff also anticipates that the project, as proposed, may make the negative findings that the use will have an adverse 
effect on the general health and/or welfare of the community and that it will cause disturbance to adjacent properties by 
traffic.
Building Design
As previously indicated, the overall architectural style is best categorized as industrial based on the rectangular massing of 
the building, uniform building height, and its use of smooth, textured, and ribbed fiber cement panels to express various 
building volumes. The site’s approximate 472-foot frontage along S. Amphlett Blvd and narrower 31-foot frontage along 
Sunnybrae Blvd. limit the elements that can be incorporated into the building design to reduce the apparent mass. Instead 
of incorporating physical wall articulation, the illusion of wall articulation is created by the strategic placement of ribbed 
and slightly raised smooth panels to imitate the appearance of smaller buildings along the building’s S. Amphlett Blvd. 
frontage. Panels portraying smaller buildings are also utilized on the rear façade, but rather than using raised smooth 
panels, only ribbed panels are used. 
Under the original approval (PA-2009-052), the City Council adopted Condition of Approval 15 Commercial Rear Elevation/ 
Wall based on the recommendation by the Planning Commission. This Condition requires that the building provide 
screening and a transition between the residential and commercial uses. The building’s rear façade consisted of a solid 
split-face block wall; however, this Condition of Approval required a “a dense landscape screening of the rear commercial 
wall that would be both quickly established and encouraged to thrive based on necessary conditions such as adequate 
footprints, depth, and/or irrigation requirements.”  
The original approval also required an appropriate transition between the commercial and residential uses at the corner of 
S. Amphlett Blvd. and Sunnybrae Blvd in accordance with Condition of Approval 15c. The transition was created at both 
the north and south ends of the building by reducing the height and utilizing spandrel glass as the primary exterior 
material. Additionally, the originally approved building was setback five feet from its rear property line, which was shared 
with the residential uses of the development. As currently proposed, the project does not provide any screening of the 
rear wall of the storage building, does not provide any additional measures to soften the transition between the uses, and 
does not provide a rear setback. 
Elevation drawings of the originally approved self-storage building are included in Attachment 7. The adopted Condition of 
Approval 15 Commercial Rear Elevation/ Wall from the original application is included in Attachment 8.

PUBLIC COMMENTS:
The applicant held a formal pre-application neighborhood meeting on November 4, 2021, in which 10 members of the 
public attended. Questions and comments including the following:

The project should be lowered to two-stories.

The design of the rear of the building creates a three-story blank wall for Arbor Rose residents.

The three-story building will block out light and cast shadows on adjacent residential units.

Concerns about sharing the trash enclosures with the storage building:
o
Can the storage building add a compost component to the trash enclosures?
o
Will the storage building cause additional dumping or odor?

Who will be responsible for managing the property and ensuring that it is maintained free of debris?

Limit the parking along S. Amphlett Blvd. to a maximum of two hours so commercial vehicles and moving vans 
cannot park overnight. 
In addition to requesting two-hour parking along S. Amphlett Blvd, community members raised concerns that the 
anticipated number of commercial vehicles and moving vans utilizing the proposed storage facility will result in an increase 
of these vehicles parking overnight along S. Amphlett Blvd.  Currently, oversized trucks are frequently left along this 
section of S. Amphlett Blvd for extended periods of time. Residents report that the abandoned vehicles make them feel 
unsafe and they worry that abandoned vehicles will negatively impact property values. 
The applicant’s summary of the neighborhood meeting is included in Attachment 9. Public comments submitted to City 
staff during the pre-application review are included in Attachment 10. Any additional public comments received following 
the publishing of this report will be provided separately to the Planning Commission on the date of the Study Session.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
In accordance with Public Resources Code Section 21065, the review of a pre-application is not a project under the 
California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) because the Planning Commission is not taking action at this time. Based on 
the size and scope of the proposed project, staff anticipates that the preparation of an Initial Study and Mitigated Negative 
Declaration will be necessary. However, a final determination on CEQA compliance will not be made until the formal 
planning application has been submitted and reviewed by staff.

Special Use Permit (SMMC 27.44.030) to permit self-storage as a compatible use on a parcel zoned C3-2;

SELF-STORAGE FACILITY
1050 S. Amphlett Blvd., CA, 94402
DESIGN CONCEPT
The Self-Storage Facility serves the community's need for clean, safe, and efficient self-storage for household and
light commercial items and furniture.   With housing prices and office leasing at a premium in the San Francisco
Peninsula, there is a need for reasonably priced storage space for accumulated personal property and surplus
commercial furniture, inventory and supplies. It is meant to accommodate items not requiring forklifts, but
handtrucks and dollies for the safe transport and positioning of stored goods within the building.
PLANNING SIZE CONSTRAINTS
By definition, a self-storage facility provides storage space for rent, and its viability as a commercial venture depends
on the amount of space it can make available.  In the case of our facility at 1050 S. Amphlett Blvd., the most limiting
size constraints are the lot configuration, the parking space requirements and the planning code height limits.
1050 S. Amphlett Blvd is 472.50 ft. wide by 31 ft. deep.  Given the 5 ft. utility easement along the frontage, the site
only allows for a 25 ft. deep by 340 ft long structure when parking to building area ratios are complied with.  The
height limit of 35 ft. from finish grade to the top of roof beam only permits us to have a three-story structure.
Meetings with Darcy Forsell former Zoning Administrator in 2018  revealed that the Planning Division will no longer
allow parking space layouts that will result in vehicles necessarily backing out into South Amphlett Blvd, traffic.
This meant that the parking scheme for the former approved design submitted by the previous owners would not
work anymore.  After redesigning the parking layout and more consultation with Ms. Forsell, it was determined that
the only feasible parking configuration would be hammerhead type parking arrangements, given the limiting
dimensions of the site.  An enclosed 1st floor garage on the northern end and a carport/parking lot on the southern end
of are designed to provide a total of 6 standard parking spaces and 1 handicap accessible parking space for the staff &
tenants.
In a phone call on Sept. 13, 2018 to this Architect.  Ms. Forsell stated that she presented our preliminary schematic
design to her superior and the planning staff during a then recent, regularly scheduled meeting.  It was thought that
the hammerhead parking scheme would work and her boss decided that we would not be required to undergo the
Large Project Pre-Application Process, because this is just a revival of a previously approved project.
However it was later determined from consultation with Planning Manager Aaron through Senior Planner Rendell
Bustos in July, 2020 that the project will have to go through the Pre-Application Process after all, because we have to
apply for a Special Use Permit to address a zoning issue created by the previously approved application made by the
former property owner, and it has been more than 10 years since the previous application approval.
DESIGN FEATURES
Consultations with Extra Space Storage and Superior Storage yielded information about the storage unit marketing
mix responsive to the vicinity of the proposed facility.  These companies also suggested that heavier and large
volume items should be stored on the ground floor in larger units while smaller items should be located on the upper
floors in smaller units.
Recology was consulted about trash enclosure configurations.  As a condition of the sale of the lot to the present
owner, easements have been provided to accommodate trash enclosures at both ends of the lot that will serve both the
storage facility and the existing residential townhouses behind it
Consultation with Element Structural Engineering Inc. determined that a steel frame with floor slabs on steel decking
would be most efficient to handle the structural and seismic loads anticipated. Meanwhile, Lea and Braze Civil
Engineering came up with minimal grading and an efficient stormwater drainage system scheme featuring bubbler
areas at both ends of the site to avoid overloading street drainage systems.
The architectural layout of the building provides double-loaded 5 ft. wide corridors for all three floors.  These are
positioned to accomodate the suggested storage unit mix.  A central stairway and service elevators at each end of the
facility will handle vertical transport of stored goods.  Fire exit stairways at both ends of the building are located to
comply with the exit travel distances allowed for the proposed sprinkled, Type VB construction building, with direct
access to the public right of way.
In order to avoid projecting the image of a huge industrial warehouse, the steel and concrete structure will be
sheathed in a colored, textured, fiber cement rainscreen panel system by CERACLAD.  Because the lot and space
program only allows for minimal articulation of the building facades, these rainscreen panels will configured to as a
series of shallow framed, colored and textured areas to simulate the image of a series of smaller structures, breaking
up the lenght  and massing of the building along Amphlett Blvd.   This will be mirrored at the rear wall facing the
townhouses by a simplified rainscreen scheme that will also serve to break up the bulk of the structure.  The textured
and ribbed surfaces of the panels will also serve to diffuse sound and light reflection along the length of the walls.
The Self-Storage Facility will feature landscaping compatible to the local environment and will engineered to address
stormwater drainage issues to avoid overloading the existing drainage utilities.  It will also have an exterior lighting
designed for safe site illumination while avoiding excessive glare. The facility will also feature camera equipped
security and alarm systems for the public's safety.
The steel structural frame and many other materials suggested for the storage facility are recyclable.  A solar power
panel system is proposed for the roof.  Building construction will follow applicable green building codes. And finally,
the self-storage building is designed to comply with current handicap accessibility codes.

Applicable General Plan Elements and Policies are listed to facilitate further discussion and

be required to provide further clarification and/or documentation to ensure conformance with 
all applicable polices and guidelines.     
 
Land Use Element 
 
LU 1.5:  
Building Height. Maintain maximum building height limits contained in Appendix 
C, and as specified in Policy LU 6A.2, closely matched with the Land Use 
categories and Building Intensity standards. 
 
LU 1.10: 
Commercial Development. Encourage industrial, service, retail, and office 
development which is compatible with the desired character of the area and 
with adjacent residential areas in terms of intensity of use, height, bulk and 
design as delineated on the Land Use Plan, Building Height Plan and Building 
Intensity Plan. Commercial development adjacent to residential areas shall 
address concerns pertaining to traffic, truck loading, trash/recycling activities, 
noise, visual impacts, and public safety including hazardous material storage, fire 
safety, air pollutant emissions and odors.

--- page 23 ---
LU 4.32: 
Recycling and Composting. Support programs to recycle solid waste in compliance 
with State requirements. Require provisions for onsite recycling for 
 
all new development and expand composting of green waste and food scraps, as 
 
directed by the City’s Climate Action Plan which is an appendix of the General 
 
Plan. 
 
PA 2.2:  
Amphlett Boulevard. Retain the residential and commercial service uses. 
 
 
Require new buildings and additions to be compatible with adjoining residential 
 
 
areas including adequate screening and landscaping. 
 
PA 5.5:  
Former San Mateo Times Site. Mixed use development may be considered on 
 
 
this site located off Amphlett Boulevard in the Sunnybrae neighborhood. 
Commercial uses should be concentrated along the Amphlett Boulevard frontage 
for continuity of existing commercial uses and to provide a buffer from the 
freeway. Any development on the former San Mateo Times site should be 
compatible with the existing residential scale, height, density and development 
pattern of the Sunnybrae neighborhood. Any redevelopment of the San Mateo 
Times property shall minimize potential nuisances on the surrounding residential 
neighborhood related to traffic, parking, trucks, garbage, recycling, noise, air 
pollutant emissions and odors. 
 
Circulation Element 
 
C 2.4: 
Transportation Fee Ordinance. Require new developments to pay their 
proportionate share of the costs for planned on and off site roadway 
improvements. Utilize a Transportation Fee Ordinance to finance necessary 
improvement equitably.

SUBJECT:
Updated Standards and Specifications for Mechanical and Automated Parking Facilities
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of a Resolution to update the City’s “Standard Drawings and Specifications” to 
include standards and specifications for mechanical and automated off-street parking facilities. 
BACKGROUND:
As a way to provide required parking within constrained urban areas, the use and adoption of automated and/or 
mechanical parking facilities has been on the rise throughout California and the Bay Area.  As development costs for 
projects continue to increase, implementation of these alternative parking systems can offer a way to reduce development 
costs that constrain a project’s feasibility.  Currently, automated and/or mechanical parking facilities are not explicitly 
addressed by the Zoning Code but have, on occasion, been permitted within the City of San Mateo.  The most recent 
example of a project with a mechanical parking facility is the multi-family residential building at 1919 O’Farrell Street that 
was approved by the Planning Commission on October 26, 2021.  In addition, staff has had discussions with multiple other 
developers and applicants over the past few years who have expressed interest in utilizing mechanical and/or automated 
parking systems to meet off-street parking requirements.  
Existing Parking Standards
The City of San Mateo’s current off-street parking standards are contained in Article 1 of Chapter 27.64 of the Zoning Code 
and establish parking requirements for various uses, minimum parking stall dimensional requirements and access 
standards, among other things.  Section 27.64.120 (Stall Dimensions) stipulates that “all required off-street parking stalls 
shall have a vertical clearance, length and width in conformity with the requirements of the City “Standard Drawings and 
Specifications,” in addition to the requirements contained in the Zoning Code.  The current “Standard Drawings and 
Specifications” for off-site parking, which were previously adopted via resolution, are included as Attachment 2. These 
were developed for parking stalls on paved streets, surface parking lots, or in garages, and did not anticipate new parking 
system technologies.  Currently, automated and/or mechanical parking systems would have to meet the requirements 
specified in the attachment or request a concession or waiver.  Such requirements preclude a majority of alternative 
parking systems, which contain vehicle storage compartments and function differently than traditional parking stalls,  
and/or require costly customizations which can significantly reduce the cost reduction benefits of these systems.  
2018-2019 Code Amendment Initiative
Prompted by the increasing use and adoption of mechanical and automated parking facilities to provide required parking, 
the City initiated a Zoning Code amendment in 2018 to establish standards for these alternative parking systems.  The City 
retained the services of Walker Consultants, a parking design and consulting firm, to assist staff with the development of 
the mechanical and automated parking standards.  On November 13, 2018, the Planning Commission held a study session, 
to review draft development standards for mechanical and automated parking facilities and receive public input.  Based on

--- page 63 ---
CITY OF SAN MATEO
the feedback received at the study session, revised development standards were brought back to the Planning Commission 
on March 12, 2019 for review and a City Council recommendation.  Following public comments and discussion, the 
Commission voted to continue the proposed code amendments to a date uncertain with a request for more information 
on system requirements for residential developments.  The minutes from both Commission meetings are included as 
Attachment 3.  This initiative has been on hold since the March 2019 Planning Commission meeting due to staffing 
limitations and a need to focus on other Planning initiatives and priorities. 
DISCUSSION: 
Under Municipal Code Section 27.64.010, City “Standard Drawings and Specifications” are adopted by resolution of the 
City Council.  Consistent with the Code, and based on the immediate need to establish objective standards for mechanical 
and automated parking facilities in multi-family residential projects, staff is proposing that the “Standard Drawings and 
Specifications” referenced in Section 12.76.120 be updated via resolution.  The proposed mechanical and automated 
parking facilities standards, which are included in Attachment 1, are based on the draft code language that was reviewed 
by the Commission in 2018-2019.  These standards are intended to be interim and establish basic dimensional and 
performance requirements that can be applied to multi-family residential projects.  It is anticipated that comprehensive 
development standards for mechanical and automated parking facilities will be drafted and brought back to the Planning 
Commission for consideration in mid-2022. 
For reference, automated and mechanical parking facilities are defined as follows:

An automated parking facility is a parking facility where vehicular storage and retrieval is accomplished entirely 
through a mechanical conveyance system that does not require an attendant to park a vehicle. 

A mechanical parking facility is a parking facility that uses mechanical devices or systems to retrieve or store one or 
more vehicle(s) by means of a lifting mechanism that vertically and/or horizontally stacks and stores the vehicle(s).  
Interim Development Standards
As further detailed in Attachment 1, it is recommended that all mechanical and/or automated parking facilities conform 
with the following: 
Stall Dimensions

Width.  Platform widths shall have a minimum unobstructed width of 6-feet 9-inches.

Length.  Platform lengths shall have a minimum unobstructed length of 17-feet 5-inches.

Vertical Clearance.  A minimum vertical clearance height of 7-feet for any vehicle loading bay or area 
where persons exit and/or enter a vehicle, unless otherwise approved by the Building Official.  All other 
stalls shall have a minimum clearance height of 5-feet.

Backup Space.  A minimum of 24-feet of backup space for all stalls that backout into a drive aisle.
Performance Standards 

Mechanical and/or automated parking facilities may satisfy off-street parking requirements for residential 
uses in multi-family or mixed-use-developments located outside of R1 (single-family) zoned areas and 
must be fully enclosed within a building or undergrounded.

Onsite signage to identifying maximum vehicle heights permitted within the mechanical and/or automated 
parking facility. 

On-call maintenance service agreement for the life of the use on the property. 

Annual maintenance inspection reports for the lifetime of the facility.

Submittal of an operations plan to include information on retrieval efficiency, operational details, 
emergency backup power, and schematic/technical drawings upon application.  
BUDGET IMPACT:
Updates to the City’s parking “Standard Drawings and Specifications” are fully funded by the Community Development 
Department’s annual budget with no impact to the General Fund.

EXHIBIT A 
 
PARKING STANDARDS 
MECHANICAL AND AUTOMATED PARKING SYSTEMS 
All mechanical and automated parking systems must comply with the specific standards and 
requirements set forth below, in addition to all applicable provisions of the City of San Mateo Municipal 
Code.  
 
DEFINITIONS 
1. MECHANICAL PARKING is a parking facility that uses mechanical devices or systems to retrieve 
or store one or more vehicle(s) by means of a lifting mechanism that vertically and/or 
horizontally stacks and stores the vehicle(s).   
 
2. AUTOMATED PARKING is a parking facility where vehicular storage and retrieval is accomplished 
entirely through a mechanical conveyance system that does not require an attendant to park a 
vehicle(s). 
APPLICABLITY  
 
1. LOCATIONS PERMITTED.  A mechanical and/or automated parking facility(ies) may be used to 
satisfy off-street parking requirements for residential uses in multi-family or mixed-use 
developments located outside of R1 (single-family) zoned areas.  Such facilities are not 
permitted on surface lots and must be fully enclosed within a building or undergrounded. 
 
2. COMPACT PARKING STALLS.  Required parking provided in mechanical and/or automated 
parking facility(ies) are not considered compact stalls and are not subject to compact stall 
limitations outlined in Municipal Code Section 27.64.265. 
STALL DIMENSIONS 
All required off-street parking stalls provided in a mechanical and/or automated parking facility(ies) shall 
be designed to conform with the following: 
1. Width.  Platform widths shall have a minimum unobstructed width of 6-feet 9-inches. 
2. Length.  Platform lengths shall have a minimum unobstructed length of 17-feet 5-inches. 
3. Vertical Clearance.  A minimum vertical clearance height of 7-feet for any vehicle loading bay or 
area where persons exit and/or enter a vehicle.  All other stalls shall have a minimum clearance 
height of 5-feet.   
4. Backup Space.  A minimum of 24-feet of backup space shall be provided for all stalls that 
backout into a drive aisle.  
PERFORMANCE STANDARDS  
Mechanical and/or automated parking systems shall be subject to the following:  
1. Mechanical and/or automated parking facilities may not be used to provide off-street visitor, 
loading, or accessible parking spaces.

2. Electric vehicle charging stations must comply with Municipal Code Sections 23.70.030 and 
23.70.040.  Electric vehicle charging stations can be located within mechanical or automated 
parking facility(ies) and/or provided in standard parking stalls.   
3. All lifts and associated mechanical equipment shall be screened from public view.   
4. All mechanical and/or automated parking facilities shall include an on-site alternative power 
source (e.g., a battery backup system) with sufficient capacity to store and retrieve cars in the 
event of an electrical power outage.  
5. The facility shall be designed to contain vehicle liquids and/or debris from spilling onto other 
vehicles.  
6. Signage shall be provided clarifying operations of the spaces to users and maximum vehicle 
heights permitted within the mechanical and/or automated parking system.   
7. For off-street mechanical and/or automated parking facilities where entering vehicles are 
required to stop before a mechanically operated barrier before entering the parking facility, 
such barrier shall be placed a minimum of 18-feet from the parcel line where the entrance is 
accessed. 
REQUIREMENTS  
All mechanical and/or automated parking facilities shall be subject to the following requirements:  
1. Compliance with all applicable local, state, and federal standards, including, but not limited to, 
Fire and Building Code requirements. 
2. Recordation of an “Agreement Guaranteeing Maintenance of Mechanical Parking Facility” prior 
to building permit issuance. 
3. The property owner shall enter into an agreement with the mechanical or automated facility 
manufacturer or other qualified service professional to provide on-call maintenance services to 
ensure that mechanical and/or automated parking facilities to be fully operational and usable 
with limited downtime.  The agreement shall be valid immediately after installation and 
operation of the facility and shall be valid for as long as the use is on the property. 
4. A maintenance inspection report for any automated, semi-automated or mechanical parking 
facility prepared at the sole expense of the property owner shall be submitted six (6) months 
after installation, and annually thereafter, to the Planning Division for review.  At minimum, said 
report shall provide information on vehicle retrieval times and failure rates.  The property owner 
shall make any necessary repairs to provide uninterrupted operation of the mechanical or 
automated parking facility. 
5. An operations plan that at minimum includes details on how the facility will be accessed, 
retrieval efficiency, and operational details, schematic or technical drawings, and the location 
and specifications of an emergency back-up power source shall be required upon application 
submittal.

Question:  Ministerial type of permit?
Staff:  In order for an applicant to go through a ministerial process they must meet all of the criteria and 
provide proof that they are still below the threshold. Part of the application process for ministerial permits 
is to provide an updated radio frequency report that is done by a license engineer. We will review the 
report and if it is under the maximum exposure level, we can proceed with going through with it as an 
ministerial permit.
Question:  Is the applicant giving that information or the consultant? 
Staff:  The licensed engineer, if applying for a building permit. A structural analysis is the type of technical 
report. 
Question:  Are you proposing permitted uses in any zoning district?
Staff:  The federal law provides we can't discriminate between carriers and we can't effectively prohibit 
the provision of telecommunication service. The advice is to allow it in all zoning districts but have a 
preference for certain zoning districts over others. 
Question:  Can a telecom company come in and say we want to put a dish on the side of somebody's 
house? 
Staff:  No. But a property owner can apply to have additional on their property. 
Question:  Can people sell space in their backyard to a telecom to allow them to put an antenna in their 
backyard? 
Staff:  That would be treated as a SPAR application. 
Question:  Does the law prohibit us from requiring a SPAR? 
Staff:  No.
Question:  Could we say all applications have to go through a SPAR review? 
Staff:  Yes. Unless it’s an eligible facilities request which is when there is an existing facility and the 
company wants to make it a little bit taller or bigger. The Federal Communications Commission and the 
General Government say you can't make them do a SPAR for an eligible facilities request, you can only 
require a ministerial permit which covers beefing up an existing installation, it does not cover new 
installation. 
Question:  Is it possible that somebody can throw a bunch more antennas up on private property with 
more frequency without you knowing?  
Staff:  That is possible. The eligible facilities requests will more often come in for towers, which is 
different than the antennas that are sitting on top of the PG&E poles.
