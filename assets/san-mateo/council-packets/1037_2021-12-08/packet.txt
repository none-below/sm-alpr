1. Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission Minutes - Approval  
 
Motion to approve the minutes of September 8, 2021.  Motion passed 3-0 
 
Moved:    Robbins, Seconded:  Narita 
Ayes:       3 - Robbins, Narita, and Loraine 
Noes:       None 
 
PUBLIC COMMENT 
 
Richard Hedges thanked Public Works for repaving the Fashion Island bridge, also encouraging Public Works to 
look at paving the Mariners Island bridge going over Highway 92 on both approaches.  With saltwater leaching 
into the rebar it could cause crumbling concrete, creating a danger to vehicles.  Mr. Hedges also asked for status 
on raising the wall at San Mateo Creek, per FEMA recommendation and make sure the work is completed within 
the timeframe of Measure S.  A future agenda item could be an update on the progress of the raised wall at San 
Mateo Creek.  (Chair Loraine encouraged the speaker to reach out to Public Works at 
publicworks@cityofsanmateo.org) 
 
Dave Clark spoke on the passing of Governor Newsom’s bill limiting the use of gas leaf blowers, reiterating the 
need to abolish gas leaf blowers. 
 
Persons wishing to comment on any item not appearing on the agenda may submit to the Recording Secretary a “Request to Speak” 
form.  State law prevents the commission from taking action on any matter not on this agenda.  Your comments may be referred to staff 
for further review.

2. Proposed Crown Castle Master License Agreement for Use of City Owned Poles. 
Matthew Trieu, Engineering Technician, and Marc Myers, Permit Utility Manager with Crown Castle, provided 
a presentation to the Commission to review the proposed Master License Agreement with Crown Castle Fiber 
LLC, provide comments to staff, and make a recommendation to the City Council.  Tracy Scramaglia, Senior 
Engineer, was also available to answer questions.

Noes:       None 
 
 
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENT FROM COMMISSIONER AND STAFF 
 
Director Mitch announced that November’s meeting will be cancelled.  The December meeting will include an 
annual update and overview on Public Works and would like to make this an annual item each December.  The 
bicycle and pedestrian project update item will be moved to January so staff can include the update on the North 
Central project. 
 
Chair Loraine suggested the possibility for commissioners to chime in on future agenda items.  Mr. Loraine also 
asked that staff copy the commissioners on the response to Mr. Hedges’ question. 
 
Announcements from Chair Loraine included:  
• The ongoing campaign San Mateo Walk Transit deadline has been extended to October 31. 
• Loraine encouraged folks that use one of the three Caltrain stations or SamTrans on El Camino, to 
provide input on the survey at www.cityofsanmateo.org/transitwalk. 
• The districting process in San Mateo has had public meetings with draft maps but there is still an 
opportunity to weigh in during these later stages.  Input is encouraged on the map alternatives and 
comments can be made at www.cityofsanmateo.org/districtelections.  The next public hearing is 
tomorrow at 6:00 pm on Zoom. 
• Several emails were received on abolishing gas-powered leaf blowers and reminded the public that the 
electric leaf blower rebate program is still available with up to $500 back on the purchase of electric leaf 
blowers.  More information can be found on the sustainability page by going to www.cityofsanmateo.org; 
clicking community, community resources, and sustainability page which shows resources and programs.
