A.  Report on Art Donation Committee Analysis of Proposed Statue Donation to Library:  Ms. De Winter 
and Mr. Linhares summarized their review, as members of the Board’s Art Policy Committee, of the 
proposal by Vince and Jackie Siminitus to donate a bronze sculpture depicting a grandfather and 
granddaughter reading together on a bench.  Under the Library’s Artwork Donation Policy & Guidelines, 
the criteria to be considered for acceptance of donated works of art include inherent artistic quality, 
context, permanence, and public safety.  Per the policy, the artwork should also be an original.  After 
analysis, the committee agreed that the artwork does not meet the criteria of being an original piece 
because it is not custom made.  As for context, the scale of the artwork is small if placed in front of the 
3-story Main Library.  
Since the proposal is that the artwork is placed in front of the Main Library, the City of San Mateo Civic 
Arts Committee: Guidelines for The Art in Public Places also needed to be considered.  Per this policy, 
the criteria to be considered for acceptance of donated works of art included context of artwork with 
site, diversity, and site criteria.  After analysis, the committee agreed that 1) for context of artwork with 
site, the artwork does not meet the social/cultural context of the site since the figures are both

Caucasian and not reflective of the variety of patrons that visit the Library; 2) for diversity, the piece is 
not reflective of our diverse cultural community; and 3) for site criteria, the Library has a statue of a 
little girl with books on the Children’s Patio so there is a similar piece of art on site.
In addition to considering the above policies and guidelines, the committee also solicited input from 
over a dozen San Mateo residents to get their thoughts on the proposed art.  Some of the residents 
commented that the sculpture does not fully depict the diversity of our community.
In summary, the committee recommended to not accept the artwork due to the possibility that 
displaying art that does not adequately represent the diversity of our community may do more harm 
than good.  Another recommendation the committee made was to consider moving the statue 
depicting the little girl with books from the Children’s Patio to a more prominent location.  This could 
address the Siminitus’ belief that the building does not adequately give the feel of a library.  It is 
possible, however, that the statue cannot be moved without risk of damage.
The Board of Trustees agreed to postpone the vote on whether to accept the artwork to a future 
meeting.  This will give Ms. De Winter and Mr. Linhares an opportunity to contact Vince and Jackie 
Siminitus and invite them to a meeting to provide their feedback on the recommendation of the Art 
Policy Committee. 
6.
NEW BUSINESS
A. Presentation of San Mateo Public Library’s FY 21/22 Departmental Workplan:  City Librarian James 
Moore highlighted the key initiatives of the San Mateo Public Library’s FY 21-22 Departmental Work 
Plan and some key achievements that have been made.  The major policy initiatives to be implemented 
by the department during the fiscal year are to 1) Improve Library User Experience; 2) Build Sustainable 
Outreach Program; and 3) Promote Equity, Diversity, Inclusion, and Social Justice Engagement.  A 
sampling of achievements that were highlighted included adult librarians thoroughly weeding the 
Friends of the Library bookstore and rearranging furniture to improve selection and customer 
experience, the appointment of Aisha Washington as the Supervising Librarian overseeing the branches 
and the outreach program, and all staff attending the virtual conference “Focus on Equity, Anti-racism, 
and Work in the New Normal” for Staff Development Day.  
Work that is currently in progress toward these key initiatives include a survey that was circulated to 
the public and staff regarding the possibility of adjusting the operating hours to better suit the 
community.  Once the results of the survey are compiled, Mr. Moore will share the results with the 
Board of Trustees and obtain their feedback.  Work on the branch enhancements are in progress, and 
Mr. Moore will be exploring the possibility of applying to the California State Library for a matching 
grant “Building Forward Library Infrastructure Program” to help with these costs.  Other work being 
done toward the completion of work plan goals are to explore the possibility of an internship program, 
continuing professional growth for employees, better marketing to young people of color for job 
opportunities with the Library, and expanding the outreach program with attendance at regular 
recurring events such as farmers markets.
The Board of Trustees offered their feedback and support for the work already completed and the work 
currently in progress toward these goals.  Lynne Esselstein, Executive Director of the Foundation, 
advised Mr. Moore that there may be interest from the community around a Fund-a-Need for a pilot 
internship program.  There may also be donor interest in the Marina Library enhancement project as 
well.  Mr. Moore and Ms. Esselstein agreed to meet within the week to discuss this in more detail.

Offer of a Bronze Sculpture from Vince & Jackie Siminitus
I.
Overview
San Mateo residents Vince and Jackie Siminitus have graciously offered to purchase a bronze sculpture 
depicting a grandfather reading to his granddaughter on a bench.  They propose that it would be placed in 
front of the Main Library.  Their intent is to provide art signifying that San Mateo is an age-friendly city, and 
that the piece would be a "tangible symbol of the library's age-friendly programming."
The Library's Artwork Donation Policy & Guidelines require our Board’s Art Policy Committee to review 
the donation proposal.  We are to use the Criteria for Acceptance of Donated Works of Art and make a 
recommendation to the Library Board of Trustees to accept or not accept the artwork. 
Since it proposed that the artwork is placed in front of the Main Library, the City of San Mateo Civic Arts 
Committee: Guidelines for The Art in Public Places should also be considered.  
The artwork has not yet been made, so we are not able to view the actual piece.  Mr. and Mrs. Siminitus 
have provided a website for the manufacturer of the piece they are proposing.  It is entitled “Talking Times” 
(https://randolphrose.com/products/talking-times-rg261).  
II. Criteria for Acceptance of Donated Works of Art
Under the Library's Policy, the criteria to be considered include:

Inherent Artistic Quality - the policy describes this as "Originality of concept and style, 
craftsmanship of the proposed artwork or of the artist's prior artwork, artist's use of materials and 
fabrication process."  

Context - works of art should be compatible in scale, material, form, and content with their 
surroundings and should not require unusual or extraordinary installation techniques. 

Permanence -  due consideration must be given to the proposed artwork’s structural and surface 
soundness, and its inherent resistance to theft, vandalism, weathering, and excessive cleaning, 
maintenance or repair costs. 

Public Safety - proposed works of art should be evaluated to ensure that they do not present a 
hazard to public safety.
In addition to the above criteria, the Committee also is to consider whether duplication is an issue.  The 
policy states that “To assure that the artwork will not be duplicated and is an original, the artist or owner 
will be asked to warrant that the work is unique and an edition of one unless stated to the contrary in a 
written agreement."  Mr. And Mrs. Siminitus readily acknowledge the piece is not original.
The City of San Mateo Civic Arts Committee: Guidelines for The Art in Public Places includes the following criteria: 

Context of Artwork with Site - "...Consideration should be given to the architectural, historical, 
geographical and social/cultural context of the site."

In addition to considering the above policies and guidelines, it was suggested at our August 23 Board

reached out to over a dozen people and have included a representative cross-section of their comments 
below.
III.Analysis of the Library’s Artwork Donation Policy
1.
Inherent Artistic Quality and Duplication - this includes originality of concept and style. While 
the craftsmanship, use of materials, etc., are high quality, the piece of proposed art is not custom 
made.  Thus, it is inherently not an original piece.  Other libraries and organizations may have this 
exact piece.  Regarding duplication, another piece representing a girl with books sits on the 
Children's Patio (see attached photo).  
2.
Context - this piece could be appropriate in certain locations, though the scale is small if placed in 
front of our 3-story Main Library.  We recommend the Board consult an architect if a 
recommendation is made to move forward with the piece. 
3. Permanence – the proposed piece would be generated via a process designed to ensure that it is 
sound and would not weather.  It would require limited maintenance, excessive cleaning, etc.
4. Public Safety – it would likely not be a hazard to public safety.  
IV.
Analysis of City of San Mateo Civic Arts Committee Guidelines for Art in Public Places 
1.
Context of Artwork with Site - the artwork does not meet the "social/cultural context of the 
site".  The grandfather and granddaughter are both Caucasian, and the level of customization 
required to add more figures and differing ethnicities is cost-prohibitive for the donors (they 
mentioned this in their presentation).  
2.
Diversity - the piece is not reflective of our diverse cultural community.  While well-intentioned, it 
may result in some residents not feeling included and/or welcome in the library if they do not see 
themselves reflected in the statue.  
3.
Site Criteria - as noted in item #1 of Section III above, the library has a statue of a little girl with 
books on the Children's Patio (see attached photo), so there is a similar piece of art on site.

SAN MATEO PUBLIC LIBRARY 
ARTWORK DONATION POLICY & GUIDELINES 
[Revised by the Library Board of Trustees, 2-28-11, renewed 9-23-19] 
 
 
The San Mateo Public Library appreciates the desire of individuals and organizations to 
donate works of art for display in the new Main Library or in either the Marina or 
Hillsdale branch libraries. It is the intent of the Library Board of Trustees to accept only 
artwork that will be part of a permanent art collection at the Library to be on display. 
 
The following procedure clarifies the process of donating, consideration by the Art Policy 
Committee, and formal acceptance of the work by the Library Board of Trustees. 
 
1. Persons who wish to donate artwork to the Library are asked to consult with the 
City Librarian who will outline the process. 
 
2. The prospective donor(s) shall submit a completed Artwork Donation Proposal 
form which will be forwarded to the Art Policy Committee. 
 
3. The Art Policy Committee shall review the proposal and later schedule an 
opportunity for the prospective donor to make a presentation of the proposal in 
person before the Committee. The Art Policy Committee may choose to 
physically view the piece of art during their evaluation process.  
 
4. The Art Policy Committee evaluates the proposed donation using the Criteria for 
Acceptance of Donated Works of Art listed below and makes a recommendation 
to the Library Board of Trustees to accept or not accept the artwork.  
 
5. The Library Board of Trustees will consider the Art Policy Committee’s 
recommendation and formally act when a decision has been made. If accepted, the 
date of acceptance constitutes the date of the accession of the artwork into the 
Library’s collection.  
 
6. A formal agreement is signed by the donor transferring ownership of the artwork 
to the San Mateo Public Library.  The agreement shall specify that the Library 
cannot accept a donation having conditions and that all donated artwork will 
become the property of the Library without any obligation to the donor.

City of San Mateo Civic Arts Committee 
Guidelines for The Art In Public Places 
Implementing Chapter 23.60 of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code 
 
 
 
In accordance with Chapter 23.60 of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code, the City of San Mateo Civic 
Arts Committee is charged with implementing the City’s Art in Public Places program.  This includes 
approving individual requests to install art as part of private development projects, as required by Chapter 
23.60.   
 
The Committee shall also make recommendations to the City Council regarding use of funds which are 
paid in-lieu of providing art work as part of private development projects.   
 
The following guidelines shall be used by the Civic Arts Committee in evaluating public arts as part of 
private development projects, and in the use of in-lieu funds.  However, from time to time the Guidelines 
may be subject to amendment by a vote of the Civic Arts Committee. 
 
1. Public Art Criteria: 
 
a. Criteria for Artwork: Criteria to be used when considering approval of artwork, either as part 
of a private development project or acquisition of artwork through the use of in-lieu funds, 
shall include but not be limited to the following: 
 
• 
Inherent Artistic Quality:  The assessed aesthetic merit of the piece as a work of art, 
independent of other considerations. 
 
• 
Context of Artwork with Site: Works of art must be compatible in scale, material, form, 
and content with their surroundings. Consideration should be given to the architectural, 
historical, geographical and social/cultural context of the site. 
 
• 
Media: All forms of visual art may be considered. Works may either be portable or 
permanently attached. 
 
• 
Permanence: Due consideration shall be given to the structural and surface soundness, 
and to inherent resistance to theft, vandalism and weathering. 
 
• 
Ability to Maintain: Significant consideration shall be given to the cost and amount of 
ongoing maintenance and/or repair anticipated, and the ability to provide adequate 
maintenance. 
 
• 
Public Safety and Accessibility: Each work shall be evaluated to ensure that it does not 
present a hazard to public safety and complies with all applicable building codes and 
accessibility requirements.  
 
• 
Diversity: The Arts Commission is committed to acquiring art works that are reflective of 
diverse cultural communities and perspectives. To that end, the Commission shall 
actively seek artwork from artists of diverse racial, sexual, and cultural identities. The 
program shall also strive for diversity in style, scale, media and numbers of artists 
represented 
 
• 
Feasibility: Proposed objects shall be evaluated relative to their feasibility and convincing 
evidence of the artist’s ability to successfully complete the work as proposed. Factors to 
be considered include, but are not limited to: project budget, timeline, artist’s experience, 
soundness of materials, city approval requirements, and level of community support.

ARTICLE VI BOARDS AND COMMISSIONS 
6.01 Library Board of Trustees. Powers and Duties. 
There shall be a library board of trustees consisting of five members which shall have 
the power and duty to: 
(a) Exercise the sole responsibility for establishment from time to time of library 
department policy in selection of books, reading, visual, auditory and like material to 
be acquired or purchased and the classification thereof, subject, however, to 
limitations of the budget for such purposes. 
(b) Make and enforce such bylaws, rules and regulations as may be necessary or 
appropriate in the maintenance and operation of public libraries free to the permanent 
inhabitants and non-resident taxpayers of the city, including fines, penalties, and 
provision for collection of obligations to the library. 
(c) Accept in the name of the city, money, personal property, or real property donated 
to the city for library purposes, subject to approval of the council, except that unless 
the council by ordinance provides otherwise, the board shall have the power to accept 
gifts other than real property in an amount or value of Five Hundred Dollars or less 
for library purposes or use without specific consent of the council where no obligation 
attaches to the city other than use for benefit of the libraries, and to hold, convert to 
cash, invest in insured savings, or expend same for the use and benefit of the libraries. 
Funds received by way of gift or bequest for the benefit of the city libraries shall not 
be subject to appropriation for other purposes, nor other than as provided by the 
donors except as may be authorized by a court of competent jurisdiction. 
(d) Hold in trust and provide for the proper application and use to the library of any 
gift, devise, or bequest in accordance with the terms and conditions thereof where 
payment into the city treasury is inconsistent with such terms and conditions, provided 
the council shall first have approved acceptance of such terms and conditions. 
(e) Approve or disapprove the appointment of the librarian, and disapprove of the 
suspension or removal of the librarian by the affirmative vote of a majority plus one 
of the board. 
(f) Sell or otherwise dispose of surplus books and other excess materials peculiar to a 
public library under such procedures and upon such conditions as the council shall by 
ordinance prescribe.
