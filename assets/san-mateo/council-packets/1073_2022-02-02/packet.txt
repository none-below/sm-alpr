SUBJECT:
Facility Naming Policy for Parks, Open Spaces and Public Facilities - Adoption
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt the proposed Naming Policy for Parks, Open Spaces and Public Facilities. 
BACKGROUND:
At the December 1,2021 meeting, Commissioners reviewed and provided feedback on the first draft of the proposed 
Naming Policy for Parks, Open Spaces and Public Facilities. Subsequent to the Commission review, the proposed policy was 
reviewed by the City Attorney’s office.  A red-line version with both Commission and City Attorney recommended changes 
is included as Attachment 1.  The changes recommended by the City Attorney’s office were relatively minor; however, 
there is an additional section, Section VI-Miscellaneous that has been added.  
There were two broader policy questions raised by the Commission that staff posed to the City Attorney’s office for 
direction.  One was related to whether the City could include privately-owned parks and open spaces within the policy and 
the second was whether Section IV: Naming Rights as Part of a Fundraising Campaign should be included in the policy. 
Regarding the first issue, the response by Assistant City Attorney Gabrielle Whelan, is as follows:
“Re Section 2(B), a privately-owned open space would be approved in conjunction with a planning application.  While the 
City’s land use approval power does not extend to naming rights on private property, it is possible that the City could 
impose a condition of approval on a private development to require a specific name and the developer could agree to that 
condition of approval.  If a developer did not agree, in my opinion, the City could not force it unless there were unique 
circumstances that warranted the City’s requirement (e.g., it was a historical site and the City needed to preserve a 
reference to a historical site at that location – perhaps as a CEQA mitigation measure).  Examples of this would be the 
name “Bay Meadows” or perhaps a reference to Native American history if Native American artifacts had been located at 
that site.  
Regarding Section IV: Naming Rights as Part of a Fundraising Campaign, the City Attorney’s office has provided three 
options for Commission consideration: 
1)
The City currently has an internal policy, Business Sponsorship, Recognition and Endorsement Policy that is 
intended to provide guidance to all city departments relative to seeking sponsorships and monetary or in-kind  
support for programs and activities. There is one reference in the policy relative to naming rights: 
   
Procedures:   Any sponsorship valued at over $25,000 or that involves naming rights to any City event, 
program, or facility shall be brought forward to the City Council for review or approval.

As this is a broad policy that applies to all departments, the Commission could recommend that additional 
language be added to the policy that provides more detail and guidance relative to facility naming. 
2)

3)
The section can remain as part of the current proposed policy. 
Staff is looking for Commission consensus on choosing one of the three options.

PARKS, OPEN SPACE LANDS AND PUBLIC FACILITY NAMING POLICY
Section I:  Purpose and Application 
A. Purpose: To provide guidelines and procedures for the naming of City Parks, Open 
Space lands and public facilities and amenities within parks. 
B. Application: This policy has been created to guide staff, the Park and Recreation 
Commission and the City Council in taking action concerning requests to name City 
parks, open space lands and public facilities and amenities within parks.  This 
process does not apply to the naming of streets or other non-park civic facilities. 
C. The City Council shall have the final authority to approve names of parks, open space 
lands and public facilities and amenities within parks after review and 
recommendation by the Park and Recreation Commission and Department of Parks 
and Recreation staff.  
Section II: Definitions 
A. Parks: Real property owned and managed by the City used for public recreation 
purposes, whether developed or undeveloped, including lands identified as open 
space. 
B. Public Facilities and Amenities: City owned facilities/amenities that are located 
within a park or within part of a facility.  Examples include, but are not limited to, 
athletic/ball fields, recreation buildings, picnic areas, trails, sports courts, 
playgrounds, gardens and meeting rooms within a facility.  
Section III: Policy
A. Parks:
1)  Each park and open space shall be named after its geographical location or 
by its significant or unusual natural, cultural, geographical, historical or 
environmental feature.  Names shall not be duplicated and shall be 
compatible with the area in which the park and open space is located.  y are 
located. 
2) Naming parks after individuals or organizations may be considered under 
special circumstances such as a significant donation of land or funding to the

Section III: Process for Naming Parks and Public Facilities and Amenities

Public Facility or Amenity facility to the Director of Parks and Recreation for

one or more of the criteria outlined above in Section B.1. The Director will conduct 
any research necessary to make an informed recommendation to the Park and 
Recreation Commission. The Commission will review the proposed naming 
recommendation, conduct a public hearing and make a recommendation to the City 
Council to either support or deny the request
The City Council will review the proposed naming recommendation, conduct a public

initiating a naming request on their own.

B. Upon approval by the City Council, the site shall officially be designated as such on 
all maps and records. 
C. A dedication plaque consistent with the existing setting and landscape shall be 
placed at each named facility and where possible, include information on the 
contributions of the individual.  Responsibility for the purchase and installation of 
any dedication plaque shall be borne by the requesting party unless otherwise 
negotiated with the Department of Parks and Recreation.  On delivery to the City, 
the dedication plaque shall become the property of the City. The location, size and 
type of recognition objects shall be approved by the Department of Parks and 
Recreation. 
The City will make every reasonable effort to preserve any dedication plaque or 
other object placed at the site.  If required, necessary, due to repair of the 
surrounding area, construction or re-design of a facility or any other reason, the 
plaque may be relocated, removed, stored or otherwise disposed in the sole 
discretion of the City.  In the case where a facility is decommissioned or demolished, 
the City will make every effort to preserve the plaque somewhere on the site.  
Section IV: Naming Rights as Part of a Fundraising Campaign 
A. When undertaking major park or facility renovations and/or new construction, the 
Department of Parks and Recreation may choose to engage in fundraising campaigns  
in order to raise community awareness and provide financial assistance to the City.  
This policy allows for Naming Rights to fund amenities within a park, such as 
buildings, playgrounds, athletic/ballfields, picnic areas, etc.  Naming Right 
opportunities and levels of sponsorship shall be identified in advance and publicized 
as part of the fundraising campaign.  Recognition should be commensurate with said 
donations or funding.  
An agreement between the City and major donors shall be negotiated which 
outlines the terms of the donation, including the length of time the Naming Right 
shall be in effect. 
 
B. Naming Rights shall be aligned with the mission and values of the Department of 
Parks and Recreation and the City of San Mateo and shall avoid products, services or 
affiliations that promote political or religious organizations, religious affiliations and 
alcohol, tobacco and cannabis products, or are otherwise considered derogatory or 
offensive by community standards.  will not be considered.  Names must be tasteful 
and non-controversial. The City Council shall have the right to approve or deny any 
proposed facility name.

Section V: Non-Facility Naming Opportunities
A. As an alternative to the facility naming opportunities described above, the City 
may choose to recognize individuals, either living or deceased or organizations by 
the placement of objects such as public benches, flagpoles, rocks, trees, or other 
features etc. within a park or facility.  The approval, placement and identification of 
these dedications will be at the discretion of the Parks and Recreation Department 
staff.  A dedication plaque, appropriate to the setting and landscape will be installed.  
When such recognitions are initiated by the City, the City shall pay for the purchase 
and installation costs.  
    
Section IV: Miscellaneous
A. No park, open space or facility shall be named after a seated, elected or appointed 
official. 
B. This policy encourages naming of parks and facilities in a manner which reflects the 
City’s ethnic and cultural diversity and elicit a positive image for all residents.
