A. Presentation on the Biotechnology Learning Center:  Supervising Librarian Rita Morin provided a 
comprehensive presentation on the Biotechnology Learning Center at the Main Library that included 
historical information. The Biotechnology Learning Center started with the Library Foundation 
envisioning a biotech center in 2004.  A procurement of two million dollars was obtained from the 
Genentech Foundation as a one-time donation to the capital campaign, and an advisory committee 
made of community members, educators, local doctors, and two Genentech librarians developed the 
original vision for the center.  When the new San Mateo Main Library opened on August 27, 2006, it 
included the nation’s first biotechnology center in a public library.  In the first few years, the trust fund 
paid for collections, programs, and part of the salary for the center’s librarian.
The current librarian overseeing the biotech center is Michelle Baute.  The advisory committee now 
consists of Ms. Baute and a local doctor.  Since there is not any more money in the trust fund, the

operating budget for programs is zero.  For programs, Ms. Baute looks for topics that she thinks will be 
of interest and relevance to the community and then conducts research to find scientists who are doing 
groundbreaking or interesting work.  The speakers that have been willing to volunteer their time to 
present information are passionate about their work and often have a soft spot for libraries.  The 
normal schedule for biotech programs is March through May, and October through December.  
Upcoming topics for fall programming includes oceanography, intermittent fasting, and the link 
between the gut and immunity.  
The Library Board of Trustees thanked Ms. Morin for her presentation and provided feedback and 
offered their assistance regarding the opportunity to go to biotech and science companies, including 
Genentech, to interest them in helping to fund more biotech programs in the future.  City Librarian 
James Moore shared that he will contact Genentech regarding the possibility of a future donation.  
Before contacting Genentech, Mr. Moore will prepare a draft message that he will present to the 
trustees for their feedback.
B. Introduce Review of Artwork Donation:  City Librarian James Moore provided information to the 
Library Board of Trustees on an artwork donation that was donated to the San Mateo Public Library 
Foundation, who in turn gifted it to the Library (SMPL).  It is a bronze replica of the Frederic Remington 
“Coming Through The Rye” sculpture.  There is no firm way to value the sculpture and it has not been 
officially accepted as a gift to the Library yet.  The donor expressed the hope that the sculpture can be 
displayed at the Library at least temporarily, but he agreed in an MOU that it will be solely up to SMPL 
to decide how to use it.  Mr. Moore shared that the imagery of the sculpture struck a couple of 
employees as potentially controversial or offensive in a time of reckoning with racial prejudice, and that 
the artist Frederic Remington was known to write in disparaging terms about Native Americans and 
other racial or ethnic groups.
Mr. Moore asked the Board of Trustees for assistance in deciding what to do with the sculpture 
including displaying it permanently or temporarily, or selling it to raise money for the collection.  Mr. 
Moore said he is also willing to return the sculpture to the donor if it is decided to not display it at all.  
The Trustees offered feedback and suggestions including that it may be a good educational opportunity 
to display the sculpture along with information about Fredric Remington, and coupling the display date 
of the sculpture with an event that features a Native American speaker.  
6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Report by City Librarian James Moore:  

The new pilot hours began on April 11.  The Main Library is now open until 8:00 p.m. Mondays 
through Thursdays, and the Hillsdale and Marina branches each gained two additional service 
hours per week.  Additional service hours have not been added to the Sunday schedule yet.  
Library management are still working with the bargaining units and scheduling has not been 
resolved.  Mr. Moore hopes to have a decision on the Sunday service hours within the next few 
weeks.

National Library Week was a huge success.  Several events were held during the week including 
the Technology Lending Petting Zoo and the Youth and Adult Poetry Contests.

Mr. Moore attended the Day in the District and spoke with Congresswoman Jackie Speier about 
the structural problems at the Marina Library.  A representative from Congresswoman Speier’s 
office contacted City Manager Drew Corbett later that day to speak about the Marina Library, 
and Mr. Corbett confirmed that the Marina Library structural repairs are a top priority.


Mr. Moore was interviewed by a disc jockey from the radio station KLOVE.  The disc jockey 
expressed amazement at how many people are not aware of all the great things that the 
Library is doing.  

During the month of April, SMPL is holding a new library card design contest.  There will be 
winners in three categories: children, teenagers, and adults.  The winners will be announced by 
May 26, and there will be an award reception at the Main Library in the Oak Room on June 9.

In partnership with the First Partner’s Office and the California State Library, State Parks is 
providing free vehicle day-use entry to over 200 participating state parks to California public 
library card holders.  San Mateo Public Library currently has three state park passes available 
for check out, and will soon have eleven.  Patrons are able to place them on hold and check 
them out for three weeks, but cannot renew them.
Reports by Board Members:

Ms. De Winter attended the first in-person Foundation Board meeting since the onset of the 
pandemic, the Foundation Legacy event during National Library Week, and both days of the 
Day in the District event.  Ms. De Winter expressed her appreciation to Mr. Moore and Ms. Diaz 
Nash for speaking about the structural problems at the Marina Library during the Day in the 
District event.

Mr. Linhares attended the Technology Petting Zoo and the donor reception during National 
Library Week, and the first day of the Day in the District event.  Mr. Linhares expressed his 
appreciation to Mr. Moore for agreeing to take the lead with the Library Board of Trustees in 
speaking at future Day in the District events.

Ms. Diaz Nash attended the Day in the District and shared how great Mr. Moore was at 
motivating Congresswomen Jackie Speier to advocate for structural repairs at the Marina 
Library.  Ms. Diaz Nash also attended Executive Director Lynne Esselstein’s going away 
reception, and shared that she is still spreading the word about the vacant Executive Director 
position with the Library Foundation.

Mr. Volz attended the reception and the poetry readings during National Library Week.  Mr. 
Volz shared how helpful staff member David was when he was checking out some books at the 
Main Library.  David was very informative about the Technology Lending program and the 
California State Park Pass program.  David did not know that Mr. Volz was a Library Board 
Trustee until after he had provided all this information to Mr. Volz.
