SUBJECT:

RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend appropriation of Measure S funding for capital improvement projects in 2022-23.
BACKGROUND:
The table below reflects the latest projections for Measure S revenue, operating expenditures, debt service, and existing 
capital improvement plan (CIP) projects from the current five year period: Based on the above projections, the Measure S 
Fund has approximately $0.8 million in available capacity. 
The Police Department (PD) has identified $0.4 million in radio infrastructure replacement need for 2022-23 as a part of 
the need to replace $1.7 million over multiple fiscal years for city-wide radio infrastructure. Of the $0.4 million requested 
from PD for 2022-23, $0.2 million is requested from Measure S. 
The Parks & Recreation Department and Public Works Department have jointly requested $0.6 million to repair the 
Gateway Park Pedestrian Bridge.

The Measure S Proposed 2022-23 Budget projects $6.1 million in revenues, $1.9 million in operating expenditures, $2.7 
million in transfers out for debt service payments related to the lease revenue bonds that were issued in 2020 to finance 
the cost of flood control and street rehabilitation, $0.3 million in transfers out to the General Fund for amortized
