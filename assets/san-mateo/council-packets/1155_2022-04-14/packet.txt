SUBJECT:
Wastewater Treatment Plant Costs - State Revolving Loan Agreement Approval
RECOMMENDATION:
Staff recommends that the Governing Board of Directors approve a resolution approving a State Revolving Fund (SRF) loan 
to provide financing for capital improvements to wastewater facilities, approving financing agreements with the City of 
San Mateo and the Estero Municipal Improvement District, and approving related documents and actions.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo (City) and the City of Foster City/Estero Municipal Improvement District (District) are co-owners of a 
wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) operated by the City of San Mateo. Ownership and operation are governed pursuant 
to an operating joint exercise of powers agreement. Under that agreement, as amended, the City pays 75.88% and the 
District pays 24.12% of the capital costs associated with WWTP improvements. 
In 2009, the Regional Water Quality Control Board issued a Cease and Desist Order requiring the City, the Town of 
Hillsborough and the Crystal Springs County Sanitation District to take specific actions to eliminate the occurrence of 
sanitary sewer overflows. In order to comply with the 2009 Cease and Desist Order, the City developed a preliminary 10-
year Integrated Wastewater Master Plan (Clean Water Program) calling for a major upgrade to the WWTP and 
improvements to the City’s collection system. Current estimated costs for all improvements are approximately $988.6 
million (all subsequent costs estimates are approximations). The City’s share is $836 million, of which $343.7 million is for 
collection and $493 million is for the WWTP. The $493 million includes the shares attributable to Hillsborough ($29.6 
million), the Crystal Springs County Sanitation District ($13 million) and the County of San Mateo ($728,000) (collectively, 
the Regional Customers). The Regional Customers will repay the City over time for their respective shares via an annual 
capacity charge. The District’s share is $151.8 million.
The City and the District will require financing to complete these improvements. The City and the District established the 
Authority in 2016 to facilitate the financing of both agencies’ capital contributions to the WWTP upgrade project. The 
Authority may be used by either member to facilitate any capital financing.

Estimated Sources and Uses of Funds
SRF Loan
The District and the City of San Mateo applied to the State of California for a low-interest loan under the State Revolving 
Fund Loan program and were approved for a maximum (project) loan amount of $137,000,000. The District’s share of that 
loan is $33,044,400. The City’s share is $103,955,600. Interest on the loan will be capitalized during the construction 
period and will be added to the approved project cost authorization. $8 million has been allocated for capitalized interest, 
the maximum loan authorization is $145,000,000. Funding from that loan will be used mostly in fiscal year (FY) 2022-23 
with some draws in FY 2023-24. The interest cost on the SRF loan will be half of the State’s general obligation bond rate, 
and is significantly lower than what the Authority can obtain in the municipal bond market. The expected rate on the SRF 
loan will be 1.60%. 
REQUIRED APPROVALS 
The resolution before the Board today consents to the Authority, the City, and the District entering a loan agreement with 
the State of California that documents a loan to the Authority in the maximum amount of $145,000,000, and approves two 
financing agreements, with the City and the District, respectively, that document the obligation of the City and the District 
to pay their respective shares of the debt service and related costs of the Authority under the loan agreement. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
The Authority is a conduit financing authority. The loan from the State is secured by financing agreements between the 
Authority and the City and District, respectively. Debt service on each financing agreement reflects each agency’s share of 
debt service on the SRF loan. The City’s share is 75.88% and the District’s share is 24.12%. The Authority will incur 
expenses to secure this source of financing. Each Authority member will pay its pro rata share of these expenses. The 
Authority will not incur unreimbursed expenses or debt service in connection with these loans.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This loan authorization is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that will 
not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)
