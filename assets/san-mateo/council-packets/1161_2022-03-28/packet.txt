Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:32 p.m. by President Elizabeth De Winter. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Elizabeth De Winter led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call.  Board Members Elizabeth De Winter, Rod Linhares, and Lisa Diaz Nash were 
present.  Board Member Austin Volz joined the meeting after the consent calendar.  Board Member 
Shaylona Wheeler was absent.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
A motion was made to adopt the consent calendar, and the motion passed 3-0.



Ayes:  Ms. Diaz Nash, Mr. Linhares, and Ms. De Winter

Noes:  None
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   NEW BUSINESS
A.  Demonstration of New Lending Items in Library’s Collection:  Supervising Library Assistant Jon Jung 
shared information with the Library Board of Trustees on the history of technology lending programs in 
libraries and the various reasons that libraries offer these types of technology and alternative lending 
programs. Examples of some of the items that some libraries offer include laptops, mobile Wi-Fi units, 
tools, cooking equipment, musical instruments and sound production equipment.  Thanks to funding 
from the San Mateo Public Library Foundation, the Technology Lending Program at San Mateo Public 
Library will be adding cutting-edge robotics kits, musical instruments, digital recording devices, coding 
education tools, and more to the Technology Lending Program that already offers Chromebooks and 
Mi-Fi units.  During National Library Week April 3 – 9, there will be a Technology Device Lending 
Collection petting zoo to introduce these new items to the community.
B.  San Mateo Public Library Impact Report 2020-2021: Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca 
shared the San Mateo Public Library Impact Report 2020-2021 with the Library Board of Trustees.  This 
annual report highlights some of the exciting programs, diverse resources and services, and community 
connections at the Library during Fiscal Year 2020-2021.  Ms. Mendonca expressed her appreciation for 
the employees that served on the task force to create the report.  She added that the graphics were 
produced in-house using Canva.  The Library Board of Trustees offered their congratulations for a 
wonderful report and offered a suggestion for a possible use of trends in next year’s report.  Library 
Foundation Executive Director expressed her appreciation that the Foundation is highlighted so 
prominently in the report.  City Librarian James Moore expressed his appreciation to all the employees 
that worked on the report.

C.  Updated Public Service Hours Pilot Proposal:  City Librarian James Moore presented his proposal to 
pilot updated service hours.  Mr. Moore shared that his goal is to do what is best for the largest 
percentage of patrons.  To develop the proposed service hours, he combined several years of 
observation of patron usage patterns along with the feedback that he received from patron and staff 
surveys conducted in the latter part of 2021.  The proposed pilot hours would increase public access by 
31 hours over the current reduced schedule due to the Covid-19 pandemic.  Overall, it will result in an 
additional two hours of service over the schedule that was in place before the Covid-19 pandemic.  The 
Main Library would close one hour earlier at 8:00 p.m. on Mondays through Thursdays, in exchange for 
providing two additional hours of public access on Sundays at the Main Library, and four additional 
hours of service at the Hillsdale and Marina branches.  The Board of Trustees asked questions and 
offered their feedback.  Mr. Moore said that managers are currently working on the difficult task of 
revamping work schedules with the tight staffing that currently exists, and employees will need to be 
given reasonable notice of their new work schedules.  Mr. Moore advised that the pilot schedule will 
probably not begin until late April or early May.  
6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Report by Lynne Esselstein, Executive Director of the Foundation:  The Foundation is coordinating with 
the Library to conduct spring fundraising during National Library Week, April 3 -9.  There will be a 
weeklong celebration of the Library including a youth poetry contest, a technology lending petting zoo, 
a reception for Leadership Circle and Legacy Fund donors, and Library Giving Day on April 6.  The 
Foundation’s theme for fundraising will be to raise money to improve the user’s experience.  Examples 
of potential improvements for the present time include purchasing dividers for public work spaces.  For 
future improvements, an example is to enclose the teen area.

Report by City Librarian James Moore:

The State Library grant application is now available and the due date to apply is March 21, 
2022.  This is a 1.25 million dollar matching grant, with the City of San Mateo also contributing 
1.25 million dollars.  The funds will be used to make structural repairs to the Marina Library 
that will stabilize the foundation for an additional ten years.  A decision will still need to be 
made for the long-term future of the Marina Library.  Mr. Moore thanked Ms. De Winter again 
for contacting City Council Member Diane Papan about the structural problems at the Marina 
Library.  There has been tremendous progress made since Ms. Papan became aware of the 
problem.
