CHABADNP PROPOSAL FOR ART IN PUBLIC PLACES 
July 11, 2022 
 
 
On August 28, 2018, ChabadNP received Planning Commission Approval for the construction of a new facility at the corner of San 
Mateo Drive and Monte Diablo Avenue.  The approved 2-story over garage building included metal panels on its east elevation.  The 
panels had suggested perforations and Hebrew lettering.  Given that these panels were part of the Planning Commission’s approval, 
ChabadNP was told that they could not be considered as public art.   
 
PANELS INCLUDED IN THE PLANNING COMISSION APPROVAL

One of the many goals of this project was to incorporate a design element representative of the Jewish culture into these panels.  
Security concerns regarding the use of Hebrew letters, coupled with the size and location of the panels prompted ChabadNP to 
rethink the imagery.  After considering several possibilities, they opted for an artistic representation of a pomegranate tree branch 
that repeats throughout the panels.  The Jewish culture recognizes the pomegranate as an emblem of righteousness.  It can be seen 
throughout its history in different forms and mediums.  In recent discussions with Christina Horrisberger and Heather Stewart 
regarding the panels, it was determined that the evolution of their design warranted submission of a proposal for their consideration 
as Public Art. 
 
 
 
PROPOSED PUBLIC ART PANEL SYSTEM

The system is composed of 18 panels grouped together to form an approximate 25 feet wide by 19 feet tall rectangle.  It is attached 
to the building’s east elevation starting at the second floor, 14 feet, 11 inches above the sidewalk along San Mateo Drive.  Placement 
at this height puts the bottom just above the property’s fence line so the panels should be visible in in their entirety from any nearby 
sidewalk or when traveling by vehicle.   
The art piece is intended as a visual focal point and place marker on the heavily trafficked San Mateo Drive. The size and placement 
of the screen give it clear near and far visibility from both San Mateo Drive and Monte Diablo Avenue. Its branch and pomegranate 
pattern and warm color is symbolic of the Congregation’s relationship to the environment and to the verdant quality of the 
surrounding neighborhood. The appearance of the screen and the building face will vary throughout the day as everchanging 
shadow patterns are cast on the façade. In the evening, when the building is occupied, the metal work will transform into a glowing 
lantern. The intricate pattern of the panels will both diffuse the light entering the sanctuary and add a beautiful element to the 
building for the enjoyment of the Congregation, the pre-school children, and the greater San Mateo community.

The artist commissioned by ChabadNP to design this piece is Greg Zall, a Bay Area artist and craftsperson with over 30 years of 
experience in design, woodworking, marquetry, and art in architecture. Greg attended the College of the Redwoods’ prestigious Fine 
Woodworking Program, under the mentoring of master furniture maker James Kreno. His portfolio (http://www.gregzall.com) 
includes a wide range of accomplishments from gallery work, private commissions, public works, and fine furniture.  Greg also 
teaches at the California College of the Art and at the College of the Redwoods. 
Greg’s aesthetic, focusing on clean lines and attention to detail, has gained him commissions from many San Francisco Bay Area 
clients including Gap Headquarters, Kidsoft and MacDougal Creative (events coordinator to President Clinton.) His resume includes 
custom work for bay area designers and architects such as the Ark of the Torah at Stanford University, Tehiyah Day School and 
Kehillah High School.  The design and fabrication of the window screen and Ark for the Gideon Hausner Jewish Day School won him 
the Faith and Form Magazine 2016 Award for Religious Art and Furnishings. 
Greg’s work can be seen in many national and local galleries including the Sonoma County Museum of Art, Highlight Gallery in 
Mendocino, Dovetail Gallery in Healdsburg, and Pritam and Eames in New York, as well as in the pages of Metropolitan Home and 
Fine Woodworking Magazine. His work is in private collections throughout the Bay Area and displayed at the following locations: 
 
Gap Headquarters, San Francisco, CA 
Stanford University, Hillel Student Center 
Kidsoft, Silicon Valley, CA 
MacDougal Creative (events coordinator to President Clinton) San Francisco, CA 
Shomrai Torah Synagogue, Santa Rosa, CA 
Tehiyah Day School, Richmond, CA 
Gideon Hausner Jewish Day School, Palo Alto, CA 
Kehillah High School, Palo Alto, CA 
For his design of the screen for Chabad in San Mateo, Greg responded to the clients’ wish for arboreal imagery with a branch and 
pomegranate graphic that borrows from both traditional American quilt patterns and Jewish iconography. The environmental nature 
of the art also speaks to the functionality of the screen which is to both filter sunlight and temper solar gain entering the sanctuary. 
The work will be a beautiful addition to the building that can be enjoyed by the congregants, the children attending the pre-school, 
and the members of the community.
