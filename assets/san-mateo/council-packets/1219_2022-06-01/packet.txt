SUBJECT:
2022-24 Parks and Recreation Business Plan 
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on the proposed 2022-24 Parks and Recreation Department Business Plan. 
BACKGROUND:
The City operates on a two-year Business Plan model with the first year recommended for adoption and the second year 
remaining as proposed. The budget will be delivered to the City Council and made available to the public prior to the 
Council taking final action on June 20, 2022. The guidelines for preparing the 2022-2024 operating budget were similar to 
those prior to the pandemic, that being for each department to develop a baseline budget to include projected funds 
needed to maintain existing levels of services and operations and then to identify new resource requests to enhance 
programs and operations as hiring freezes were lifted. The proposed 2022-24 Business Plan for the Parks and Recreation 
Department provides additional resources to meet projected service demands while also providing for some new 
programs and services. The major budget highlights of the department are as follows: 
Horticulture: There has been a 5% increase in the Right of Way (ROW) contract for city medians a $11,180.45 increase, a 
66% increase in the Custodial contract for Shoreline restroom after a completive bid process resulting to an increase of 
$10,000, and a 4% increase in compensation for staff an increase of approximately $90,500. 
Recreation/Community Services: The proposed budget sees an increase of $1.2 million dollars, The expenditure is largely 
for the increase of proposed services as patrons return to programming. Approximately $700k in spending made up of 
merit positions being filled and an increase of per diem hours as we have returned to almost pre pandemic levels of 
Summer Camp enrollments. Approximately $600k in contract instructors to be fully offset as their classes are revenue 
generating. The increase in expenditure is counterbalanced by a projected revenue increase of $1.4M.
Arbor:  The Contractor Services budget was increased from $665k to $835k after a year-to-year comparison of like services 
showed a significant increase in the cost of work. Additionally, we are now contracting out the watering of newly planted 
trees. Funds ($42k) previously allocated to per diem employees have been re-assigned to Contractor Services reflecting 
outsourcing of new tree watering. 
Golf:  Golf revenue is projected to increase by approximate $650k. This is due to the restaurant returning to normal 
operations for dining and banquets. Further the VIP membership program is forecast to contribute $125k and the balance 
is from projected rounds of golf.
