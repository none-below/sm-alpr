CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
May 23, 2022
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:30 p.m. by President Elizabeth De Winter. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Elizabeth De Winter led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call.  Board Members Elizabeth De Winter, Austin Volz and Shaylona Wheeler 
were present.  Board Member Rod Linhares joined the meeting after the Consent Calendar at 5:37 p.m.  
Board Member Lisa Diaz Nash was absent.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
A motion was made by Mr. Volz to adopt the consent calendar, seconded by Ms. De Winter, and the 
motion passed 3-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   OLD BUSINESS
      Continued Review of Sculpture Gift to Library:  At the Library Board meeting on April 25, 2022, City
      Librarian James Moore provided information to the Trustees on a bronze replica of the Frederic
      Remington “Coming Through The Rye” sculpture that was donated to the San Mateo Public Library
      Foundation, who in turn gifted it to the Library.  Mr. Moore asked the Trustees for assistance in deciding 
      if the Library should display the sculpture.  The trustees offered feedback and suggestions including that 
      information about Fredric Remington should be included if the sculpture is displayed, and coupling the 
      display date of sculpture with an event that features a Native American speaker.
      At this meeting, the Trustees viewed the sculpture.  The Trustees also viewed the area on the third floor 
      of the Main Library where Western books are displayed as a possible location to display the sculpture.
      Discussion ensued and the Trustees agreed that displaying the sculpture could be an educational 
      opportunity for patrons to learn about Frederic Remington, including the fact that he was known to
      write in disparaging terms about Native Americans and other racial or ethnic groups.  Next, an appraisal 
      needs to be obtained.  Mr. Moore has already contacted an appraiser in San Francisco and the appraisal 
      will be scheduled soon.  In addition, the donor will be contacted to see if he is willing to supply a display
      case for the sculpture.  This topic will be discussed further at a future Board meeting.
6.   NEW BUSINESS
Annual Rotation of Library Board Officers:  A motion was made by Ms. De Winter for Mr. Linhares to 
rotate into the President position and for Mr. Volz to rotate into the Vice-President position, the motion 
was seconded by Ms. Wheeler, and the motion passed 4-0.

7.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Report by City Librarian James Moore:  

There is a new web page on the Library’s website devoted to the Technology Lending Service.  
The web page lists all the items in the collection and how many are currently available to 
borrow. 

The Teen Zone on the 3rd floor of the Main Library is popular with teens during after-school 
hours.  Other age groups also use this area because the Library cannot limit where patrons can 
sit.  There are plans to enclose this area in the future.

The Teen Zone Art Contest winners were announced and they received their prizes.  The 
winner, Issabella Bianco, will have her artwork displayed in the Teen Zone.  The 1st runner up is 
Hailey Lanet and the 2nd runner up is Keira McLintock.

On May 19th, Kate Adams, Congresswoman Jackie Speier’s Deputy Chief of Staff, formally 
notified City Manager Drew Corbett that the Library’s Community Funding Request for funds to 
repair the Marina Library has been submitted to the Appropriations Committee for 
consideration.  Ms. Adams said that Congresswoman Speier will continue to advocate for this 
project, but advised that it is a long process and it will not be known which projects are funded 
until the Fiscal Year 2023 appropriations are signed into law, which might not be until the end 
of this year or in 2023. 

Also on May 19th, confirmation was received from Senator Feinstein’s office that the Library’s 
Fiscal Year 2023 Congressionally Directed Spending Request was received.  The Senate 
Appropriations Transportation, Housing, and Urban Development Subcommittee requested 
additional information about the submission, which was sent immediately.

The Library has received nine additional State Parks day passes from the California State 
Library, bringing our total to twelve circulating passes.  We may receive more depending on 
demand levels.

There is another beautiful display on the 3rd floor of the Main Library created by employee 
Noreen Chwee in honor of Asian American and Pacific Islander Heritage Month.  Normally, 
there would be several great programs during the month including music, calligraphy and 
martial arts demonstrations, but those programs have been halted due to COVID-19.  We hope 
to resume them next year.

The balance on the unrestricted San Mateo Public Library Foundation grants at the end of the 
second quarter of FY 21-22 is $247,320.72.  Spending from this grant funding has been reduced 
this year due to the possibility that fundraising by the Foundation could be reduced temporarily 
due to the vacancy of the Executive Director position.    
Reports by Board Members:

Ms. De Winter attended the last Foundation Board meeting.  Also, the Library Board meeting 
on June 27, 2022 will need to be cancelled due to the unavailability of a few Trustees.

Mr. Linhares did walk-throughs at all three Library locations.

Ms. Wheeler reported that she is glad to be back and expressed her appreciation to everyone.

Mr. Volz did walk-throughs at all three Library locations, and attended a Young Chambers 
Musicians Concert in the Oak Room at the Main Library.
