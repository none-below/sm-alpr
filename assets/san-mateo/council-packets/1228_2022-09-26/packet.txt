Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:33 p.m. by President Rod Linhares. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Rod Linhares led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena Gomez 
conducted roll call.  Board Members Rod Linhares, Austin Volz, Shaylona Wheeler, Lisa Diaz Nash and 
Elizabeth De Winter were present.  
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
Mr. Volz requested that the statistics be pulled for further discussion.  A motion was made by Ms. De 
Winter to adopt the minutes on the consent calendar, seconded by Ms. Diaz Nash, and the motion 
passed 5-0.
After discussion, a motion was made by Ms. Diaz Nash to include statistics from 2019 in future reports, 
seconded by Ms. De Winter, and the motion passed 5-0.
A motion was made by Ms. De Winter to adopt the statistics on the consent calendar, seconded by Ms. 
Diaz Nash, and the motion passed 5-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   OLD BUSINESS
      Art Gift Value Appraisal Update:  City Librarian James Moore provided an update to the Library Board of 
      Trustees on the formal assessment he received from Dr. Sven Bruntjen, National Gallery of Art, Hobart 
      Associates Appraisals, on the value of a Remington sculpture that the San Mateo Public Library 
      Foundation gifted to the Library.  The sculpture is the most popular replica work of the original work 
      first cast in 1903.  Forty of these replicas have sold at auction over the last twenty years, and
      Dr. Bruntjen found some that sold for $5,000, but many sold for much less.  For insurance purposes, Dr. 
      Bruntien estimated the 2022 fair market value of the sculpture is $10,000; however he estimated it 
      would sell for approximately $2,000 at auction.  Dr. Bruntien clarified that since the selling price of these 
      replicas have varied widely over the years, the sculpture could sell for considerably higher or lower 
      than this estimate.  
      Mr. Moore advised the Board that he no longer wants to display the sculpture in the Library.  The
      sculpture depicts four men on horses holding guns with their arms raised to the sky.  After the 
      recent school shooting in Uvalde, Texas, Mr. Moore would like to consider selling it or even offering
      it back to the donor.  The Board offered their support of selling the sculpture and possibly using the
      proceeds for a worthwhile addition to the Library’s collection. 
      A firm selling value still needs to be assigned to the sculpture, and then the sculpture needs to be
      accepted by the Board as a gift to the Library.  It will go on a future Gifts List with the 2022 fair market
      value of $10,000 for insurance purposes.

6.   NEW BUSINESS
Update on Funding of Repairs to Marina Library:  Mr. Moore advised the Board that the Library received 
a nice surprise.  Without being directly asked to do so, Assemblyman Kevin Mullin advocated for 
funding in the State’s budget to provide critical structural repairs at the Marina Library, and the state 
awarded $1.3 million for these repairs.
This award is in addition to ongoing efforts to raise funds for Marina Library repairs.  Mr. Moore is 
hopeful that enough money can be raised for a complete rebuild or renovation.  In addition, the area 
where the Fish Market restaurant is next to the Marina Library is going to be developed, and the 
developer may be interested in a new library.  The Board suggested joining future Zoom meetings with 
the developer and members of the neighborhood to advocate for the Library.  
7.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Report by City Librarian James Moore:  
•
Mr. Moore shared some background information with the Board on what led him to the library 
profession.  More San Mateo Public Library staff will speak at future Board meetings to share 
their journeys to the library profession.
•
The pilot hours for the Main Library will begin on Sunday, August 7th.  The Main Library will 
open at 11:00 a.m. instead of 1:00 p.m. on Sundays for the duration of the pilot.  
•
3 Bees Café will be returning to the Main Library after the renewal of their lease has been 
submitted for approval to the City Council.  Mr. Moore is hopeful that the café will reopen 
sometime in late August.
•
The Library Foundation started a Legacy Fund in 2021 meant to insure stable financial support 
for the Library even during times when annual fundraising efforts are challenging.  Individuals 
and businesses who donate $500 or more will have their names listed on a special plaque.  The 
plaque will be installed above the automatic book return in the lobby of the Main Library 
sometime in August.
•
The schedule to replace all the public computers (PCs) is every five years, and the Library is now 
due for this service.  The usage of PCs has been trending downward for over a decade, but 
usage has been substantially down since the pandemic began.  It seems that more patrons are 
coming to the Library to access our Wi-Fi than to use the PCs.  Since it is not known if the 
substantial decrease in PC usage is due mainly to the pandemic, and if usage will increase as we 
move out of the pandemic, Mr. Moore concluded that it is best to have more computers than 
we need right now rather than have too few with no way to increase our supply for another 
five years.  It is a possibility that if PC usage does not increase over the next five years, the 
space currently dedicated to the PCs could be utilized for something else.  More time is needed 
to plan and consider the implications of having more space and how it can best be utilized, as 
well as how to finance space enhancements.
•
The African American Library Advisory Committee (AALAC) will host a San Mateo Public Library 
author and lecture event fundraiser, with administrative support from the San Mateo Public 
Library Foundation (SMPLF), on October 1, 2022 at the College of San Mateo.  Peabody Award-
winning journalist Dr. Jelani Cobb will be delivering a lecture that addresses The Half-Life of 
Freedom: Race and Justice in America Today.  Each of the Library’s cultural advisory 
committees receive approximately $2,500 per year from SMPLF funds to do programming for 
the community.  The AALAC is ambitious and seeks to do more high profile programming than 
that stipend can support.  During the pandemic, the AALAC was the only Library cultural 
advisory committee that continued programming throughout the pandemic, while the others
