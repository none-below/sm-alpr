September 26, 2022
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:32 p.m. by President Rod Linhares. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Rod Linhares led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena Gomez 
conducted roll call.  Board Members Rod Linhares, Austin Volz, Shaylona Wheeler, Lisa Diaz Nash and 
Elizabeth De Winter were present.  
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
Ms. Diaz Nash requested that the statistics be pulled for further discussion.  A motion was made by Ms. 
Diaz Nash to adopt the minutes on the consent calendar, seconded by Ms. De Winter, and the motion 
passed 5-0.
After discussion, a motion was made by Ms. Diaz Nash to adopt the statistics on the consent calendar, 
seconded by Ms. De Winter, and the motion passed 5-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   NEW BUSINESS
Outreach Update Presentation:  Before sharing a progress report on the Library’s outreach services, 
Supervising Librarian Aisha Washington shared a little about her professional background and how her 
love of libraries led her to a career in library services.
Ms. Washington highlighted the Library’s outreach events during the 2021-2022 Fiscal Year including:
•
Movies in the Park held at different parks in San Mateo on Fridays in September 2021.  Free 
books were distributed.
•
Dia de los Muertos event at Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Community Center (MLK Center) in 
October 2021.  Free books were distributed, and Project Read staff promoted literacy 
programs.
•
Fall Fest event in October 2021 was a partnership with MLK Center.  Free books were 
distributed.
•
EGGStravaganza at Central Park on April 16, 2022 was a partnership with Parks and Recreation.  
Despite the rainy weather, 100 people attended.
•
Health & Wellness event at MLK Park on May 22, 2022.  This is an annual event hosted by San 
Mateo Police Activities League (SM PAL) to promote healthy lifestyle choices to local families.  
Summer Learning Challenge books and reading logs were given out and special events were 
promoted.
•
Junior Giants Opening Day on June 18, 2022 was a partnership with SM PAL to promote 
Summer Learning Challenge.  Free books and reading logs were distributed.

•
Montara Bridge Housing outreach event on June 30, 2022 was a partnership with Adobe 
Services.  This was a community event for tenants at Montara Bridge apartments, an affordable 
housing community.
•
Home Delivery Service currently has 15 patrons who have books regularly delivered to them.  
Deliveries have increased from once to twice monthly.
•
Other outreach services include staff that go to the Senior Center to give instruction and 
assistance for navigating online services such as Libby, Hoopla and Kanopy; and school visits to 
do presentations to classes and students during assemblies.
•
Since April 2022, the Library’s outreach vehicle (Mateo) has been experiencing mechanical 
problems.  The parts are currently unavailable, and City Librarian James Moore shared that the 
company that sold Mateo to the Library went out of business during the pandemic.  Mr. Moore 
said it may be a good time to start considering what type of vehicle would be a good 
replacement vehicle.  Mr. Moore recently did a presentation to the Emerging Leaders class of 
the Leadership Council of San Mateo County and this will be a project they will take on to assist 
Mr. Moore and others in brainstorming ideas for a replacement outreach vehicle.
Lastly, Ms. Washington is working to build more community partnerships and expand the number of 
regular outreach events and destinations.  Other goals for the future include finalizing an outreach 
webpage on LibHub (staff website), and to create a new procedure for compiling statistics.
6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Report by City Librarian James Moore:  
•
Summer Learning Program wrapped up on August 31st.  Over 5,000 youth of various ages 
participated and over 4,000 books were given out.
•
New library cards from our recent Library Card Design Contest are available.  September is 
Library Card Sign-up Month.
•
The recruitment for the new Supervising Librarian for Children’s Services is in the final stages.  
The recruitment for the two Children’s Services Librarians will begin soon.
•
The grant from the California State Library to work on improvements to the Marina Branch was 
received.  The amount was $458,600, which will be matched by the City of San Mateo.  We are 
still waiting to hear if we have received funds from Congresswoman Speier’s office and Senator 
Feinstein’s office.
•
The new information desks at the Marina and Hillsdale branch libraries have been installed.  
This was the last major piece of the recent project to improve the branches.  The desks will 
provide much more modern, attractive, and ergonomic workspaces.
•
AbilityPath is a local nonprofit whose mission it is to empower people with special needs to 
achieve their full potential.  We partnered with them to do an art show in our 3rd floor café 
space.  The closing reception will be held next Thursday, September 29th from 1-2pm in the Oak 
Room.
•
The Friends of the Library will host a $5.00-a-bag sale in the Main Library’s Oak Room patio on 
Saturday, October 15th.  Details will follow in next month’s newsletter.
•
Our fall biotech speaker series will kick off on October 11th with Dr. Cassandra Quave, scientist 
and author of The Plant Hunter, who will discuss her work studying and identifying plants with 
medicinal potential to help combat infectious disease and antibiotic resistance.  The November 
and December programs will feature speakers on intermittent fasting and keto, and the gut 
microbiome and fermented foods.

Hi Everyone,
SMPL currently has no specific language in our behavioral policy regarding what 
cannot be brought into the library by patrons, except to say that bicycles aren’t 
allowed. Although we have at least one patron who has for years brought in a large 
amount of neatly organized personal belongings without issue, (he did cause issues 
previously, such as shaving and bathing in the bathrooms, being aggressive with 
staff, and daily leaving a trash bag unattended until I personally spoke with him in 
2017), there remain periodic instances where patrons bringing large amounts of 
personal belongings that lead to conflict or compromise staff’s and other patrons’ 
ability to comfortable enjoy the library. Often bringing suitcases and other large 
bundles of personal belongings strongly correlates with staff having to repeatedly 
ask the person to refrain from other unacceptable behavior (bathing, sleeping, etc.) 
in the library which sometimes leads to them being banned for a period of time. 
I am proposing a review of our current policy to determine if it can be updated in 
such a way to provide clearer guidance to staff when we must address situations 
with patrons who bring large items into the library that most reasonable people 
would find inappropriate for utilizing a shared in-door community space. 
Specifically, I recently had to call SMPD for assistance in removing a man who had 
brought in a dolly carrying a suitcase, as well as unclean plastic bags containing 
cans, bottles, and foodstuffs. Just a week before that I noticed a man at the 
computers with a large garbage bag full of cans and bottles. I said nothing because 
I was uncertain if it was technically violating our policy, but I imagine some patrons  
found it off-putting to see this in the library because it seemed unclean and

person’s specific needs. When creating policies, I think it is best to balance kindness 
with pragmatic and practical thinking to create reasonable, clear, and effective
