LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
October 24, 2022
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:34 p.m. by President Rod Linhares. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Rod Linhares led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call.  Board Members Rod Linhares, Austin Volz, Shaylona Wheeler, Lisa Diaz 
Nash and Elizabeth De Winter were present.  
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
Ms. Diaz Nash requested that the minutes be pulled to correct a typographical error on the last 
page.  The error was that Ms. De Winter was typed as Mr. De Winter.  A motion was made by Ms. 
Diaz Nash to adopt the minutes as corrected, seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion passed 5-0.
City Librarian James Moore requested that the statistics be pulled for further discussion.  After 
discussion, a motion was made by Mr. Volz to adopt the statistics on the consent calendar, seconded 
by Ms. De Winter, and the motion passed 5-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       A community member named Drew reported that there is an area on the P1 level of the
       parking garage that is very dark and needs more lighting.  This is the area along the back wall facing 
       west before the turn to go to the P2 level.  Mr. Moore will contact Public Works about this.
5.   NEW BUSINESS
Review of Library’s Personal Belongings Policy:  The Library Board of Trustees began the review of 
the policies regarding allowable personal items of some other libraries in the Bay Area and in 
Southern California to help determine if an update should be made to the policy for San Mateo 
Public Library (SMPL).  Some of the policies of the other libraries are strict and specific, while others 
are broader and more flexible.  Bicycles are currently the only personal item prohibited from being 
brought into the Library per San Mateo Public Library Rules of Conduct Policy.  Mr. Moore gave the 
Board information on some of the challenges that have occurred with some patrons bringing 
personal items into the Library, including but not limited to a wet sleeping bag and a bag of cans and 
bottles.  In the case of the wet sleeping bag, staff reported seeing bed bugs or body lice on a piece of 
furniture where the wet sleeping bag had been placed.  Bedbug-sniffing dogs were called in and did 
not detect any bedbugs.  Board members had questions and offered feedback, including that the 
Library is one of the best places for people to get back on track.  Drew, a member of the public, 
offered feedback as well.  He said that it would not bother him if someone was in the Library with 
their personal belongings.  Drew shared that he lives by San Mateo County Hospital and has been 
approached by patients who may be mentally ill or unhoused who have asked him for rides.  He 
thought that patrons who have had little or no experience with unhoused or mentally ill people may 
be more negatively impacted by people who come into the Library with suitcases, sleeping bags, or 
other similar personal items.  Mr. Moore will invite a couple of staff members to talk at the next

6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
       Report by City Librarian James Moore:  
•
Mr. Moore enjoyed attending the Atkins Golf Tournament fundraiser for literacy programs 
along with members of the Foundation.
•
Mr. Moore was one of the city officials who volunteered to be dunked in water at the 
Central Park Centennial Celebration.  Proceeds from the dunk tank activity raised funds for 
the Fallen Heroes Memorial.
•
Mr. Moore has met with the director of Public Works, the project manager, and the project 
consultant about the structural testing at the Marina Library that was done on October 22nd. 
•
Electrical problems at the Main Library are being assessed.
•
The Sunday pilot hours have been in effect for several months.  Mr. Moore will provide an 
update at the next meeting on November 28, 2022.
•
Mateo, the Library’s community outreach van, is irreparable.  This is an opportunity to think 
of ways to best use the Library’s next community outreach vehicle.  Mr. Moore is striving to 
have dedicated staff for outreach in the future.
•
Once the Café returns to the Main Library, Mr. Moore is seeking to make a subtle change to 
the policy about consuming food and beverages inside the Library.  Library management 
staff have been discussing the possibility of allowing food and beverages that are purchased 
outside of the Library, but only allowing eating in designated areas.  It is challenging for staff 
to enforce the current policy that prohibits the consumption of food and beverages that are 
purchased outside of the Library.
Reports by Board Members:
•
Ms. De Winter attended the Main Library Biotechnology Center’s virtual event The Plant 
Hunter: The Potential of Medicinal Plants to Fight Superbugs, and the Mayor’s thank you 
event for Boards and Commissions.
•
Ms. Diaz Nash attended the Jelani Cobb, the Mayor’s thank you event for Boards and 
Commissions, and the reception at the Main Library for the 101st Airborne.
•
Ms. Wheeler attended the Mayor’s thank you event for Boards and Commissions, and will 
soon be attending a Foundation Board meeting.
•
Mr. Volz attended the Mayor’s thank you event for Boards and Commissions.
•
Mr. Linhares did walk-throughs at all three SMPL locations.
Report by Library Foundation Representative Diane Ervin:
•
Over 200 people attended the Jelani Cobb event.  The Foundation hopes to partner with the 
African American Library Advisory Committee to do more fundraising events in the future.
•
The Foundation is looking into the formation of a committee who would do advocacy 
outreach with the City Council on behalf of the San Mateo Public Library.
•
Sarah Meier-Heredia has been hired as Development Officer, a newly created position.  
Sarah will be working to identify best practices for event planning, including to plan an 
alternative spring fundraiser event in place of the labor-intensive annual Authors Gala.
•
The Fall Appeal begins on December 1st.
•
Gives Day will be in December.  More information will be forthcoming.

Background Information on Proposed Food Policy Change
•
Currently prohibited behaviors in the Library under behavioral rules include: 
“Consuming foods and beverages that are purchased outside the Library,” as a prohibited behavior.
 
•
Proposing to change policy of prohibited behaviors to:
 “Consuming foods and beverages (other than liquid in a container with lid) outside of designated 
eating zones at the Library.”
•
Proposed eating zones Main Library: 
Staff have recommended the third-floor café tables, third-floor terrace, Children’s Makerspace and patio 
currently the best spaces to allow eating. Any eventual space enhancement considerations will include 
incorporating more easily cleanable floors and furniture in some other (to-be-determined) spaces that 
could be designated as eating zones.
•
Reasoning for suggested policy change: 
Prior to the arrival of current café vendor, 3 Bees Coffee, SMPL struggled to find and keep a long-term 
café partner at the Main Library. One of the main challenges for the vendors was achieving profitability. 
This is a key motivation for seeking to eventually move the café to the first floor. Prior to the pandemic 3 
Bees was on its way to achieving sustained profitability. The Library sees having a café onsite as a public 
service and, thus, provides the café tenant with very favorable terms that do not require the café to pay 
rent unless they earn $10,000 in a month.  
The initial food policy was intended to support the Library café vendor’s profitability and maintain a 
comfortable, hygienic setting by, in theory, prohibiting outside food (which was sometimes strong 
smelling and messy). In practice, staff found it unfeasible to prevent patrons from bringing such items to 
the library and it became widespread. 
The pandemic offered an opportunity to reset patron behavior by banning food in the library as a safety 
measure. Since restoring library hours, SMPL patrons have adapted to not eating (other than reasonable 
snacks) in the library. As we excitedly prepare for the reopening on the café we would like to remove 
the ban on outside food as it was never realistically enforceable. Instead, we think a better policy is to 
simply say that food, wherever it was purchased can only be eaten in designated areas. We believe that 
allowing patrons to eat outside food in specified areas will not hurt the profitability of the café, as they 
were achieving profitability prior to the pandemic, despite the inability to enforce the rule. Also, most 
patrons seem to opt for convenience when hungry, and went to the Library café to get coffee or filling 
snack because the 3 Bees products were high quality and shopping there allowed patrons to stay onsite. 
The amended policy provides our patrons with clear guidance on how to considerately enjoy light food 
items while enjoying their visit at the Main Library.
