CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
November 28, 2022
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
The meeting was called to order at 5:43 p.m. by Vice President Austin Volz.  President Rod Linhares  
was not present.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Austin Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena Gomez 
conducted roll call.  Board Members Austin Volz, Lisa Diaz Nash and Elizabeth De Winter were 
present.  Board Members Rod Linhares and Shaylona Wheeler were not present.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
A motion was made by Ms. De Winter to adopt the minutes on the consent calendar, seconded by 
Ms. Diaz Nash, and the motion passed 3-0.  City Librarian James Moore requested that the statistics 
be pulled for further discussion on the Sunday pilot hours.  Mr. Moore and the Library Board 
reviewed the statistical information that pertained to the number of visitors the Main Library had 
during the pilot hours of 11 a.m. to 1 p.m.  The data reflects that there were more visitors during 
this time period than during the pre-pandemic hours of 8 p.m. to 9 p.m., Mondays through 
Thursdays.  A decision will be made at a future date on the permanent hours for the Main Library.  A 
motion was made by Ms. Diaz Nash to adopt the statistics on the consent calendar, seconded by Ms. 
De Winter, and the motion passed 3-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   OLD BUSINESS
Impact of Homelessness, Mental Health and Drug Related Issues on Overall Library Service: City 
Librarian James Moore introduced Librarian Paul Vaughn to the Library Board of Trustees.  Mr. 
Vaughn is an experienced librarian who has worked for San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) for many 
years.  There was discussion about whether there should be an update to the Library Rules of 
Conduct Policy regarding items that are prohibited from being brought inside the Library.  Mr. 
Vaughn informed the Board that most patrons do not bring items into the Library that pose a 
problem, but there have been some instances that have been troublesome.  Agreement was 
reached that the policy should be updated to prohibit patrons from bringing any personal items 
inside the Library that pose a health and safety risk, or impede upon another patron’s personal 
space, or block walkways.  In addition, the policy should be written in a broad way to allow staff the 
flexibility to assess individual situations.  For example, suitcases should not be specifically prohibited 
because there are patrons that bring in suitcases that do not pose a health and safety risk, impede 
upon another patron’s personal space, or block walkways.  Mr. Moore will draft an updated policy 
and present it to the Board for their review and approval at the January 23, 2023 meeting.

6.   LIBRARY’S FOOD POLICY
      Mr. Moore presented an amendment to the section of the Library Rules of Conduct that pertains to 
      the consumption of food and beverages to the Library Board of Trustees for their approval.  The
      amendment updates the policy to read under the list of prohibited behaviors “consuming foods and 
      beverages (other than liquid in a container with lid) outside of designated eating zones at the 
      Library.”  A motion was made by Ms. Diaz Nash to approve the policy as amended, seconded by
      Ms. De Winter, and the motion passed 3-0.
7.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
       Report by City Librarian James Moore:  
•
The Main Library was a 4-Day Vote Center, and Marina Library was a ballet drop-off 
location.  We were pleased to make the Marina Library a drop-off location because it is the 
only one in that area of San Mateo.  We are still awaiting data from the County Elections 
Office on the number of people who voted at those locations.  
•
San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) is among the California public libraries who signed up to 
offer CAreer Pathways Services.  CAreer Pathways Services offered at SMPL are Coursera, 
GetSetUp, Job & Career Accelerator, LearningExpress Library Complete, LinkedIn Learning, 
Northstar, Skillshare, and VetNow.  Free access to these digital platforms for workforce 
support are available to libraries thanks to funding provided by the State of California and 
administered by the California State Library.  Board Members offered ideas on how to 
market these services.
•
Mr. Moore met with the members of the Library Outreach CAP (Community Acceleration 
Project) regarding possible alternatives to the Library’s community outreach vehicle 
“Mateo” that is irreparable.  The three e-charging stations in the Main Library’s parking 
garage appear too narrow for anything larger than a medium-sized sedan.  Members of CAP 
will interview key staff persons on their outreach work to determine if a van is actually 
needed, as opposed to a smaller electric hatchback.
•
Mr. Moore, along with the Deputy City Manager, began serving as liaison at the beginning of 
the fiscal year to the City’s external Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) taskforce.  The 
members of this taskforce will serve for one year and consist of community leaders and 
professionals that serve diverse communities.  The taskforce was formed to advise the City 
of San Mateo on how to best reach and better serve historically underrepresented 
communities.  The City Manager also appointed Mr. Moore to work with staff and a 
consultant on creating a DEI strategic plan for the City.  These projects have challenged Mr. 
Moore to stretch, and he has found them very rewarding.
Reports by Board Members:
•
Mr. Volz attended the Baywood Coffee Social and did walk-throughs at all SMPL locations. 
•
Ms. Diaz Nash shared a request from some of the election workers that worked at the 4-Day 
Vote Center.  They would like more sheltered bike racks in front of the Main Library.  Ms. 
Diaz Nash said she enjoyed her time on the Library Board and that her last day would be 
December 4, 2022.
•
Ms. De Winter thanked Ms. Diaz Nash for her time on the Library Board.

Library Rules of Conduct Policy
Prohibited behaviors on Library premise may include but are not limited to the following:
• Vandalizing or misusing furniture, equipment, or materials
• Harassment of any kind toward other library users and library staff
• Causing a public disturbance, which includes, but is not limited to, use of abusive or obscene
language; talking loudly; or making excessive noise
• Viewing obscene content on library computers or personal devices; or other violations of the
Computer Use Policy
• Disobeying the direction of a library staff member
• Leaving children unattended [Library employees are not responsible for unattended children]
• Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways
• Monopolizing Library space or resources including moving furniture
• Bringing bicycles (or any wheeled transport that cannot be folded and/or safely stored away) inside 
the Library
• Bringing large, oversized, or bulky sets of personal belongings that cannot be safely stored away
• Bringing personal items judged to be unsanitary, pose a health or safety risk, or disruptive to library 
usage and operations  
• Using rollerblades, scooters, or skateboards on Library property**
• Using the restroom inappropriately, such as bathing or laundering
• Strong pervasive odors
• Sleeping [as a safety precaution, staff will awaken persons sleeping on Library property*]
• Soliciting or panhandling
• Smoking on Library property*
• Bringing animals other than service animals inside the Library
• Not setting cell phones or devices on silent mode; and speaking on cell phones or devices in
spaces other than designated “phone zones”
• Consuming food and beverages (other than liquid in a container with lid) outside of
designated eating zones at the Library.
• Leaving personal items unattended [the Library is not responsible for personal belongings
