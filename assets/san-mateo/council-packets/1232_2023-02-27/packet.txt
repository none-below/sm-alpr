CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
January 23, 2023
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:30 p.m. by Vice President Austin Volz.  The President role is 
currently vacant.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Austin Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena Gomez 
conducted roll call.  Board Members Austin Volz, Shaylona Wheeler and Elizabeth De Winter were 
present.  There are currently two vacancies on the Library Board of Trustees.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. De Winter, seconded by Ms. Wheeler, and 
the motion passed 3-0.
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   OLD BUSINESS
Amendment to the Library Rules of Conduct Policy: The Library Board of Trustees reviewed the 
suggested updates to the Library Rules of Conduct Policy as it pertains to personal belongings that are 
allowed inside the Library.  In addition, the Board reviewed the request to remove the temporary 
COVID-19 policies that are no longer applicable. A motion to approve the suggested updates and 
remove the temporary COVID-19 policies was made by Ms. De Winter, seconded by Ms. Wheeler, and 
the motion passed 3-0. 
6.   NEW BUSINESS
      A.  Consider Potential Library Priority Recommendation for Blue Sky Council Session:  The Library
      Board of Trustees discussed some ideas for recommendation for the Library to put forth as priorities 
      for the City Council to consider at the upcoming annual Blue Sky session.  Suggestions included 
      resuming work on the Space Modification Project, a replacement community outreach vehicle, 
      continued funding for the Genentech Biotechnology Learning Center programs to increase
      understanding of biotechnology to the general public, a dedicated building technician, and funding
      for costly maintenance issues including new paint for the Main Library.
      B.  Rotation of Library Board Officers:  Due to the recent departure of two Board members, the 
      Library Board of Trustees rotated members into the roles of President and Vice President.  A motion
      was made by Ms. De Winter for Mr. Volz to rotate into the role of President and for Ms. Wheeler to 
      rotate into the role of Vice President, seconded by Ms. Wheeler, and the motion passed 3-0.

7.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
       Report by Suzanne Sims, Library Foundation Board of Directors:  
•
The Foundation Board just had their monthly meeting.  Ms. Sims invited all the Library Trustees 
to attend future Foundation Board meetings, which are held on the 3rd Tuesday of each month.
•
The Foundation will be providing a check to the Library soon for approximately $85,000.
•
National Library Week is April 23 through April 29.  Co-Presidents Alexandra Gillen and Jeanne 
Back have been preparing for National Library Week activities.  
       
       Report by Deputy City Librarian Rukshana Singh:  
•
San Mateo Public Library Foundation’s Take a Break Program on December 6th was a big 
success.  The Main Library hosted free performances by local performance artists, including an 
ukulele performance, youth choir and tango dancers.
•
On December 8th, San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) signed the contract to bring the San Mateo 
County Genealogical Society (SMCGS) collection to the Main Library.  SMCGS will also provide 
experienced long-time volunteers to assist patrons with genealogical resources and will help 
SMPL to offer more genealogical focused programs.
•
City Librarian James Moore attended a press conference in December at the Foster City Hall 
chambers where Congresswoman Jackie Speier announced that SMPL will receive federal 
funding for the Marina Library.  Lisa Diaz Nash, former Library Board President and now San 
Mateo Deputy Mayor, accepted the check on SMPL’s behalf.  The $1.5 million in funds brings 
the total funds for the Marina Library structural repairs to just under $4 million.
•
The annual Los Posadas holiday program held on December 12th drew 118 participants.  The 
program had been halted since the pandemic began.  The event was sponsored by SMPLF and 
planned by the Hispanic Cultural Advisory Committee in collaboration with liaison Librarian 
Joey Squillacioti.
•
There will be a program coming in March and an upcoming display to honor San Mateo’s 
“Walking Doctor”, Dr. James Hutchinson, who was San Mateo’s first African American doctor 
and Hillsborough’s first African American resident.  Dr. Hutchinson passed away in March 2021.  
There is a corner named after him at San Mateo Drive and Tilton.  Hopefully the upcoming 
display can be a meaningful step toward making SMPL a destination for those seeking insight 
on local history.
•
On January 14th, over 75 community members attended the first Fixit Clinic.  Fixit Clinics are 
free community-based educational workshops where neighbors, friends, and families 
collectively learn about and repair their broken items.  Fixit Clinics rely on volunteer coaches to 
help disassemble, troubleshoot, and repair items.  These quarterly Fixit Clinics will be held at 
the Main Library.  The next one is scheduled for April 1, 2023.  This program is thanks to grant 
applicant Librarian Carla Avitable.  SMPL is just one of 20 California libraries to win a 
Sustainable Libraries Grant.
7.   ADJOURNMENT
Mr. Volz adjourned the meeting at 6:00 p.m. 
Next Meeting:  Monday, February 27, 2023, at 5:30 p.m., Cedar Room, Main Library
