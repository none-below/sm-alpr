CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
February 27, 2023
Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 5:30 p.m. by President Austin Volz.  
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Austin Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena Gomez 
conducted roll call.  Board Members Austin Volz, Shaylona Wheeler and Elizabeth De Winter were 
present.  There are currently two vacancies on the Library Board of Trustees.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR
A motion to approve the minutes was made by Mr. Volz, seconded by Ms. De Winter, and the motion 
passed 3-0.  
City Librarian James Moore informed the Board that the number of in-person visitors have increased 
since last year but are still down approximately 57% compared to pre-pandemic.  After discussion, a 
motion to approve the statistics was made by Ms. De Winter, seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion 
passed 3-0.
Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca highlighted a correction that was made on the financial 
report for May.  Sub-account m910 (Fund-A-Need) was added, and $71,495.00 was subtracted from 
sub-account m100 (Unrestricted SMPLF Grants) and added to sub-account m910.  After discussion, a 
motion to approve the financial reports and accept the gifts was made by Ms. De Winter, seconded by 
Ms. Wheeler, and the motion passed 3-0.  
4.   PUBLIC COMMENT
       None.
5.   NEW BUSINESS
      A.  Library’s 2023 Departmental Work Plan Priorities:  Mr. Moore shared information on the Library’s 
      three major Work Plan priorities for 2023.  First, since the Library’s current 5-year Strategic Plan will 
      expire this year, a new Strategic Plan will need to be developed and completed by the end of this
      calendar year.  The planning process will include goal setting that is in alignment with the City Council’s
      strategic goal setting, assembling a diverse planning team from various perspectives at the Library, 
      and selecting a consultant.  The Strategic Plan will be used to communicate the Library’s vision
      for San Mateo, and the mission, values and goals of the Library in support of that vision to the
      community.
Second, work will continue to be done on improving the Library’s facilities.  New adjustable height desks 
were recently installed at the branches, and new bottle-refill fountains for the branches have been 
ordered by the Public Works project manager.  For Hillsdale, Public Works has identified a new vendor 
for the trash enclosure project.  Work will continue on the project to organize all the storage areas at all 
three Library locations and to discard items that are no longer needed.  Work will begin on the 
4 of 33

--- page 5 ---
remaining Space Modification Project renovations that still need to completed as funds become 
available. 
Lastly, the Library will continue its work on service improvement in the areas of diversity, equity and 
inclusion.  Staff will continue to seek more inclusive programming opportunities, including senior-
friendly activities.  The Library Rules of Conduct Policy was recently updated to thoughtfully 
accommodate patrons that come to the Library with suitcases.  To support United Against Hate Week, 
the Library made displays and presented films.  Mr. Moore is also looking into the possibility of a local 
history center or a rotating history display at the Main Library in the future.     
      B.  Agenda Building for Future Meetings:  The Library Board of Trustees discussed potential agenda
      items for upcoming meetings.  Two suggestions were made for standing topics: bi-monthly updates on
      structural repairs at Marina, and updates on the Strategic Plan.  Mr. Moore said he will be able to 
      include updates on Marina in his City Librarian Report.  For the updates on the Strategic Plan, Mr.
      Moore said it is a little early for regular updates because a consultant still needs to be hired.  Mr. Moore
      asked the Board if they can each find three examples of strategic plans as homework.  He clarified that it 
      does not have to be from a library.
      C.  Library Board of Trustees Meeting Schedule: Mr. Moore advised the Board that it would be
      possible to schedule bi-monthly meetings if they desired to do so.  After discussion, it was decided to
      wait to make any decisions until the two vacancies on the Board are filled.  
6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS             
       Reports by Board Members:  
•
Mr. Volz and Ms. De Winter have been visiting the Library regularly to check out books.
•
Ms. Wheeler attended the African American Library Advisory Committee event at the Main 
Library on Saturday, February 18th.  San Mateo Public Library is showcasing items from the 
Domini Hoskins Black History Museum collection through the month of February for Black 
History Month.  The reception honored the curators and historians and attendees were able to 
view the works of local artist Ella Haysbert.  Mr. Moore said that San Mateo Police Chief 
Barberini and twenty officers toured the Domini Hoskins Black History Museum.
        Report by City Librarian James Moore:  
•
The State and Federal funds received so far for the structural repairs at the Marina Library total 
approximately 3.5 million dollars.  The City of San Mateo will match the State funds for an 
additional $500,000.  Public Works has taken over the role of project manager for this project.  
These funds will go to the structural repairs only.  Additional funds would need to be raised for 
any facility renovations, such as adding community meeting room space.  Mr. Moore advised 
the Board that there is an opportunity to advocate for funds for additional improvements at the 
branches.  One possible opportunity would be to reach out to local businesses.
•
The Library is still very short-staffed and the Children’s Division is especially short-staffed.  A 
recruitment was recently done for librarians and the applicants that were offered positions 
decided not to accept the job offers due to the wages not being high enough for them.  Mr. 
Moore explained that this is a complex issue.  If an applicant is looking strictly at wages, it 
appears that San Mateo Public Library has lower wages than peer libraries in the area.  
However the total compensation that includes health and retirement benefits is comparable.  
Contract negotiations are done every two years and compensation will be looked at closely.
•
Denmark’s Trio con Brio Copenhagen, recognized as one of the world’s finest piano trios, 
performed at the San Mateo Main Library on February 4th.  This program was presented in

partnership with Music at Kohl Mansion, with funding support from the San Mateo Library 
Foundation.
•
Mr. Moore asked the Board if there are opportunities to hold more community events.  He said 
the cultural advisory committees, African American, Asian, Iranian American, and Latino, have 
been instrumental in coordinating community events.  The African American Library Advisory 
Committee (AALAC) has been the most ambitious of the cultural committees.  The Iranian 
American Library Advisory Committee also hosts the biggest annual Iranian American events in 
the Bay Area at the Main Library.  Ms. Mendonca added that a patron recently commented that 
libraries are now community centers. 
7.   ADJOURNMENT
Mr. Volz adjourned the meeting at 6:45 p.m.

DIRECTOR'S MESSAGE
Over the past year the San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) continued
seeking a balance between restoring many of the services that
defined us before COVID hit the world and thoughtfully adapting
to a changed world. After reducing services during the height of
COVID, in 2022 we not only fully restored pre-pandemic hours at
the beginning of fiscal year 21-22, but expanded them along with
increasing public computer access from 2 hours to 3 hours per day
and made our popular small conference study rooms fully available
for use again.
We are still emerging from the pandemic into a world where we
must continue reimagining our practices, services, and identity as a
resilient, impactful, and trusted cultural institution. Public libraries
remain integral to healthy communities across America today
because we have intelligently, creatively, and enthusiastically
evolved to meet the changing needs of our communities, while
maintaining our core values. Here at SMPL, fostering a love of
literacy, exploration of our world, and self-directed learning will
remain our guiding principles as we move forward into a new era.
