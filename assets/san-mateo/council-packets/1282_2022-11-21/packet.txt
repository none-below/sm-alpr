SUBJECT:
Process for Filling a City Council Vacancy
RECOMMENDATION:
Review the information provided in this report, consider public input, and direct staff on the process to select candidates 
and appoint a successor to fill the Council vacancy resulting from Deputy Mayor Papan's upcoming departure from Council.
BACKGROUND:
Deputy Mayor Diane Papan ran for office in the California State Assembly and was elected to the District 21 Assembly seat 
at the November 8, 2022 election.  On November 17, 2022 Deputy Mayor Papan submitted her resignation letter to the 
City Clerk with an effective date of December 4, 2022. Her last meeting as a City Council Member will be December 1, 
2022.
This study session is for Council to consider its options for filling this upcoming vacancy, and to provide direction to staff on 
how to proceed. This report outlines the law on the filling of Council vacancies and describes the different processes the 
Council has used to fill the five vacancies that have occurred since 1998.
The Law on Filling Council Vacancies
City of San Mateo voters established the rules for filling Council vacancies in Section 2.08 of the City’s charter. Section 
2.08 provides that a City Council vacancy shall be filled by appointment by the Council. This section also establishes a 30-
day time limit after the vacancy occurs for appointing the successor and provides that if the appointment is not made 
within this period, the appointment shall be made by the Mayor. 
Papan was elected in 2020 for an at-large Council term that is set to end in December 2024; therefore, the appointed 
successor shall serve a two-year at-large term through December 2, 2024. The City recently transitioned to by-district 
City Council elections, with voters in Districts 1, 3, and 5 electing their first Council members by district in 2022. In 2024, 
voters in Districts 2 and 4 will elect their first Council members by district and there will no longer be at-large seats on 
the San Mateo City Council. The next election for Districts 1, 3, and 5 is November 2026.
The Processes used to Fill Previous Council Vacancies
While the charter provides that the City Council shall appoint a successor to fill a Council vacancy, it does not prescribe a 
procedure for selecting a successor. Since 1998, there have been five City Council vacancies. 
Resignation of Council Member Jerry Hill
In 1998, a Council vacancy was created when Council Member Jerry Hill was elected to the Board of Supervisors. After 
discussing a number of options for filling the vacancy, the Council decided to appoint the next highest vote getter at

the November 1997 Council election. This decision was made at a study session convened by the City Council in

Death of Council Member Gary Yates
On May 20, 2000, Council Member Gary Yates passed away while in office. The Council conducted a study session at 
which it decided to solicit letters of interest from members of the public interested in serving on the Council. At its next 
meeting, the Council considered two motions to appoint a successor both of which failed by a 2-2 vote. The following 
day, the Mayor appointed the successor.
Resignation of Council Member Carole Groom
In 2008, a Council vacancy was created when Council Member Carole Groom was elected to the Board of Supervisors. To 
fill this vacancy, the Council adopted a three-step process for making the appointment. First, staff was directed to 
advertise and solicit interest in the appointment, and an application deadline was established. Then, a special meeting was 
set to allow those interested in the appointment to make a public presentation to the City Council. Finally, the day after 
the presentations, the Council held a second special meeting at which it selected the successor.
Resignation of Council Member Robert Ross
On December 31, 2014, Council Member Robert Ross resigned from City Council effective January 6, 2015. At a study 
session, Council decided on the procedure for appointment that included direction on advertising and involved the 
submitting of applications and presentations to the Council, as well as a formal Council vote by ballot. 
Resignation of Council Member Maureen Freschet
Council Member Maureen Freschet stepped down from the City Council effective November 4, 2019. At a study 
session, Council decided to replicate the same process as was used for Ross’ resignation in 2014 that included 
advertising, application submittal, and a special meeting with presentations to the Council, and a formal vote by ballot.
Direction Needed
Attached to this staff report is a summary of the possible methods suggested to fill the vacancy as well as some concerns, 
a calendar timing matrix to assist in designing the process based on a 30-day timeframe, a sample notice announcing the 
vacancy and seeking applications, and a sample application that could be used. These pieces were updated based on the 
last Council recruitment in 2019.
To publicize the recruitment and seek applicants in the community, staff is proposing a widespread outreach campaign. 
This would involve using the City’s digital channels including a high-visibility announcement on the website, social 
media platforms and numerous email listservs such as those receiving agenda notices for all Council, commission and 
board meetings. Noticing will be shared through community partners by placing several display advertisements in the 
San Mateo Daily Journal, running the announcement on the San Mateo TV channel, and sharing with the list of City 
Services Academy graduates as well as the City’s neighborhood associations.
Staff is seeking direction from Council with regard to:
1.
The preferred recruitment method
2.
Verification of proposed timing for the recruitment
3.
Scheduling additional meeting(s) as may be required
BUDGET IMPACT:
Depending on Council’s direction, advertising through the Daily Journal and on the City’s social media platforms is 
recommended. An additional $2,500 to $5,000 may be needed in the City Clerk's advertising budget. If it is found 
additional funds are needed, a budget adjustment may be submitted after the costs are known.

APPLICATIONS FOR VACANCY
ON THE SAN MATEO CITY COUNCIL
AVAILABLE
The San Mateo City Council is seeking applications to fill a vacancy for the 
City Council position to be vacated by Diane Papan on December 4, 2022 
for an interim at-large term from December 12, 2022 through December 2, 
2024. Effective December 2024 the City’s Candidate election system 
transitions completely from an at-large system to a by-district system. In 
2024 the voters will have an opportunity to first elect representatives from 
District 2 and 4 for a full four-year term in the November 5, 2024 general 
election. 
Eligible applicants must be at least 18 years of age and a qualified elector 
and resident of the City of San Mateo, continuously, for at least 30 days by 
the time of appointment. The application also requires the filing of a 
Statement of Economic Interest (Form 700) for calendar year 2022.
The Council will be looking for an individual who has a thorough 
understanding of the issues facing the City and who can demonstrate a 
history of active civic leadership, community service and engagement in 
San Mateo. In addition to regular Council business, the individual selected 
must be available to actively participate as a Council liaison to numerous 
external agencies and organizations.
Applications are available at the City Clerk’s office, City Hall, 330 W. 20th 
Avenue, San Mateo, or on the City’s website, www.cityofsanmateo.org, 
and must be submitted no later than 5:00 p.m. on December 7, 2022.  
Any endorsements on behalf of candidates, via email, regular mail, or in 
writing are to be sent to the City Clerk at 330 W. 20th Avenue, San Mateo, 
CA  94403.
<Optional>Applicants will make a 10-minute presentation at a Special City 
Council meeting on December 12, 2022, in the City Council Chambers 
located at City Hall. The City Council will consider all applications and make 
an appointment by ballot at this Special Meeting. 
The new City Council member will be sworn into office at the December 
12, 2022 City Council meeting following appointment proceedings. For 
additional information, please visit www.cityofsanmateo.org or contact 
City Clerk Patrice Olds at polds@cityofsanmateo.org or by calling (650) 
522-7042.

Eligibility
Eligible applicants must be at least 18 years of age and a qualified elector and resident of the City of San Mateo, 
continuously, for at least 30 days by the time of appointment. The Council will be looking for an individual who has a 
thorough understanding of the issues facing the City and who can demonstrate a history of active civic leadership, 
community service and engagement in San Mateo. In addition to regular Council business, the individual selected must 
be available to actively participate as a Council liaison to numerous external agencies and organizations as well as San 
Mateo Commissions.
Instructions
Completed applications, with supplemental, and FPPC Form 700 Statement of Economic Interests (per Government 
Code 87200) must be received by the City Clerk’s Office by December 7, 2022. Deliver to: City Hall, 330 W. 20th Avenue, 
San Mateo, CA  94403.
A PDF version of the completed application and form may be emailed to the City Clerk at polds@cityofsanmateo.org by 
the deadline with a hard copy mailed or delivered within 24 hours. The email date will suffice to establish meeting the 
deadline.
Presentation
Qualified applicants will make a 10-minute presentation at a Special City Council Meeting on December 12, 2022. The 
topic of the 10-minute presentation is up to the applicant.
Endorsements
Any endorsements on behalf of candidates are asked to do so in writing: via email or regular mail and should be sent to 
the City Clerk at the above street or email address. All endorsements are public documents and will be included in the 
packet for Council consideration. Any endorsement not sent via the City Clerk and distributed to all of the City Council 
will be excluded.
