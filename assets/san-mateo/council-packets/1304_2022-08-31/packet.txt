SUBJECT:
End of Year Review for Fiscal Year 2021-2022 for the Parks and Recreation Department
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the End of Year Review for Fiscal Year 2021-2022.
BACKGROUND:
The effect of the pandemic has impacted our business. At the onset of the Shelter in Place order in March 2020, the Parks 
and Recreation Department went through a number of cancelations for all of our programming, resulting in refunds and 
loss of revenue. As we all now know this period of uncertainty and ongoing public concern resulted in the department 
needing to pivot to online classes and virtual offerings with mixed results as people resorted to more outdoor activities for 
recreational purposes. From a financial point of view, the Parks and Recreation Department traditionally managed 
approximately $6.6 Million of revenue annually and understandably this was impacted by the inability to offer paid 
programming. For the past 2 and a half years this loss of revenue has been a great concern as our department employs 
approximately 55 full time staff and a large number of seasonal per diems. Over the past 12 months Covid restrictions in 
California have relaxed and as patrons return the department would like to present a financial update for the 
Commissioners.
When reviewing expenditures, it is interesting to note that our overall expenditures did not decrease greatly with the 
primary reduction coming from seasonal per diems’ costs. When reviewing revenues, there was a substantial increase for 
golf during the pandemic, while other recreation revenue was as low as 25% of pre-pandemic levels. What is evident is 
that revenue is bouncing back as at the end of 2021-2022 we had approximately $5.6 Million in revenue. For the year 
ahead we have just experienced one of our strongest summer quarters even compared to pre-pandemic levels and fall 
registration has been favorable as well. We hope that the golf revenue remains at a high level and if this is the case at year 
end it estimated to be close to pre-pandemic levels. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact as this is only an informational update.
