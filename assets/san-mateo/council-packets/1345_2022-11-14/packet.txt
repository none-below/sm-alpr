200 S. Fremont St. Art Proposal – Approval
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve the Art in Public Places art entitled ACHILLES for the project at 200 S Fremont St. - Fremont Terrace.
BACKGROUND:
The approved development project on 200 S. Fremont St., Fremont Terrace, is a four-story, 15-unit residential 
condominium building from Wall Street Properties. The proposed art location is on the southwest corner of S. Fremont St. 
and 2nd Ave. The Art in Public Places obligation amount for this project is $51,160.
Project Manager Regan Catanzaro and artist J. Manuel Carmona have visited the Civic Arts Committee (CAC) twice prior in 
a study session, first in January 2020 and last on November 2021, to discuss various concepts for the art styles proposed. 
The proposed art design responsive to the feedback from the CAC is entitled “ACHILLES”. The design is by Mr. Carmona 
and he has collaborated with Artworks Foundry in Berkeley to complete the sculpture foundry fabrication work. 
Upon project approval and completion of installation, the applicants will be required to complete the following criteria 
prior to issuance of the Certificate of Occupancy:
•
Review by City staff to confirm project is installed as approved.
•
Complete the submittal of copies of invoices and the related proofs of payment that meet or exceed the obligation 
amount required.
•
Prepare and sign the Maintenance Covenant for the art and submit the document for recordation to the County of 
San Mateo.
A Notice of Completion will be issued to the project manager and copied to the Building and Planning Divisions informing 
that the Art in Public Places Condition of Approval is complete.
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact, this project is a private installation within the noted property.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This Art in Public Places sculpture is not a project subject to CEQA, because it can be seen with certainty that it will not 
cause a physical change in the environment. (Public Resources Code Section 21065.)

Release Request for Qualifications for Equity Art Project – Approval
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve the Request for Qualifications (RFQ) document and its release to the public.
BACKGROUND:
On July 20, 2020, the San Mateo City Council adopted a resolution reaffirming the City’s commitment to racial equity and 
requested an art project that would promote racial equity. The Council identified the Civic Arts Committee (CAC) as the 
appropriate body to lead this effort in recommending an artist(s) and their proposed art project.  The CAC has expressed 
its strong desire to collaborate with the community to help create a unique art project that explores San Mateo’s 
commitment to racial equity with the goal to facilitate communication and to improve equity for all in our community. 
An RFQ has been developed to seek professional and/or emerging artists to complete a Racial Equity Art Project in 
Downtown San Mateo on the street of the Pedestrian Mall.  The priority consideration is that the artists are from City of 
San Mateo, the County of San Mateo, or within and/or affiliated within the Bay Area. Artists of color and BIPOC, women, 
gay/lesbian/transgender/non-binary/queer, and members of historically marginalized groups are strongly encouraged to 
apply.
The new Pedestrian Mall on South B Street includes the blocks between 1st and 2nd Aves. and 2nd and 3rd Aves. The defined 
street art areas are within the center Emergency Vehicle (EV) lane that is a total of 15 feet wide and 300 feet long. With 
two streets, there is a total of approximately 9,000 square feet of space available. That allows a variety of possibilities with 
regards to the use of the space and the number of artists.
Racial Equity is the core theme for the street art project. The RFQ offers some idea and inspirational subjects that artists 
may choose to use for singular or blended into proposal designs.
Process
The CAC previously discussed and agreed upon the project process. One key element in the process is the community 
engagement activities, which is to collect feedback regarding the proposals and designs for CAC to review. Briefly, key 
elements of the process include the following:
1.
Application: Applicants submit their qualifications per the listed criteria.
2.
CAC Selection: The CAC will review the RFQ applications and select the artist(s) to submit proposals. Anticipating 5 
applicants, but the selection numbers may be flexible. 
3.
Proposal: The selected artists will receive a $1,000 stipend to develop a proposal including the design and all 
project elements of the proposal as listed. An important element is to identify and define the space the artist(s)

Community Feedback: There will be multiple activities to solicit feedback from the community as listed. This 
feedback will be provided to the CAC to assess the community’s feeling and support about the design(s) and the

Guidelines for Art in Public Places. 
5.

The RFQ is tentatively scheduled to be released on November 16, 2022, with a target to begin the installation in May 2023

standing meeting schedule, it will require the CAC to schedule two Special Meetings, one in February and one in April for 
the two selection processes. The remaining steps are to confirm dates for the community engagement activities for the 
design feedback. 
Budget
There is no comprehensive budget currently defined; the proposals will best inform the final budget. The Art in Public 
Places (AiPP) Fund has received in-lieu payments that have strengthened the funds available to a balance of approximately 
$900,000; other AiPP activities have reserved approximately $500,000. Allowing for flexibility in a finalizing a budget is 
more responsive to this substantial public art project and support the recommended project selections to the City Council.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The Art in Public Places Fund has dedicated and sufficient funds, both budgeted and allocated, that will be available for 
this project. As part of the approval of the Art in Public Places 2022 Report & Plan, the Council has approved $10,000 for 
the artist selection process.

facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of use. (CEQA Guidelines 
Section 15301.)

City of San Mateo – Equity Art Project RFQ 
Page 3 
Background 
The City of San Mateo is seeking professional and/or emerging artists to complete a Racial Equity Art Project in 
Downtown San Mateo on the street of Pedestrian Mall strip.  
 
On July 20, 2020, the San Mateo City Council adopted a resolution reaffirming the City’s commitment to racial 
equity and requested an art project that would promote racial equity. The Council identified the Civic Arts 
Committee as the appropriate body to lead this effort in recommending an artist(s) and their proposed art 
project.   
 
The City adopted the Art in Public Places (AiPP) ordinance in 2005. The ordinance requires new development 
projects either to install publicly visible art as part of the project or contribute a percentage of the cost of the 
project for the AiPP fund. All City public art activities are funded through the AiPP fund. 
Call for Artists 
This RFQ is open to all artists, with priority consideration given to those living in or having a relation to the City of 
San Mateo or San Mateo County. There is a strong interest to find the most appropriate local artist(s)/group of 
artist(s), however those who identify with Bay Area are also welcomed to submit. Artists of color and BIPOC, 
women, gay/lesbian/transgender/non‐binary/queer, and members of historically marginalized groups are strongly 
encouraged to apply.  
 
Artists should be comfortable creating works that center on racial equity and social justice.  
Project Overview 
Public Art is a way to engage and celebrate community. In this tradition, the Civic Arts Commission, in 
collaboration with the community, is facilitating an opportunity to create a unique art project that explores San 
Mateo’s commitment to racial equity with the goal to facilitate communication and to improve equity for all in 
our community. 
 
Location 
This work will be located at the new Pedestrian Mall in Downtown San Mateo. The Mall includes South B Street 
between 1st and 2nd Avenue and 2nd and 3rd Avenue, each block is approximately 300 feet long. This location is the 
heart of the City and it concentrates public spaces, dining, and shopping for an enjoyable experience for all 
visitors. This Downtown location has a high visibility by pedestrians, making it an ideal location for a community 
art project.  
 
Having two blocks available, there are multiple options artist(s) may seek that include, but are not limited to: 
 
One artist that uses both blocks for the project that offers a larger area for a more content  
 
Each block has a different artist that allows for different styles 
 
Content of each block may be singular in style or utilize both blocks for a broader design that is consistent 
in styles 
 
The art space is limited to the Emergency Vehicle (EV) lane in the center of the street, but artist(s) can 
decide how much of the space they would like to use within that allotted space. 
 
Ideas for Inspiration 
As currently envisioned, this project will be painted on the street and will be designed to enhance the public use 
and aesthetics of the Pedestrian Mall while highlighting racial equity. The art will have a lifespan of approximately 
5 years and will be required to use durable materials and paint to ensure the longevity of the artwork.

Guidelines for Art In Public Places 
Implementing Chapter 23.60 of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code 
January 20, 2009 
 
 
 
 
In accordance with Chapter 23.60 of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code, the City of San Mateo Civic Arts Committee is 
charged with implementing the City’s Art in Public Places program.  This includes approving individual requests to install art 
as part of private development projects, as required by Chapter 23.60.   
 
The Committee shall also make recommendations to the City Council regarding use of funds which are paid in‐lieu of 
providing art work as part of private development projects.   
 
The following guidelines shall be used by the Civic Arts Committee in evaluating public arts as part of private development 
projects, and in the use of in‐lieu funds.  However, from time to time the Guidelines may be subject to amendment by a vote 
of the Civic Arts Committee. 
 
1. Public Art Criteria: 
 
a. Criteria for Artwork: Criteria to be used when considering approval of artwork, either as part of a private 
development project or acquisition of artwork through the use of in‐lieu funds, shall include but not be limited 
to the following: 
 
 
Inherent Artistic Quality:  The assessed aesthetic merit of the piece as a work of art, independent of other 
considerations. 
 
 
Context of Artwork with Site: Works of art must be compatible in scale, material, form, and content with 
their surroundings. Consideration should be given to the architectural, historical, geographical and 
social/cultural context of the site. 
 
 
Media: All forms of visual art may be considered. Works may either be portable or permanently attached. 
 
 
Permanence: Due consideration shall be given to the structural and surface soundness, and to inherent 
resistance to theft, vandalism and weathering. 
 
 
Ability to Maintain: Significant consideration shall be given to the cost and amount of ongoing maintenance 
and/or repair anticipated, and the ability to provide adequate maintenance. 
 
 
Public Safety and Accessibility: Each work shall be evaluated to ensure that it does not present a hazard to 
public safety and complies with all applicable building codes and accessibility requirements.  
 
 
Diversity: The Arts Committee is committed to acquiring art works that are reflective of diverse cultural 
communities and perspectives. To that end, the Committee shall actively seek artwork from artists of 
diverse racial, sexual, and cultural identities. The program shall also strive for diversity in style, scale, media 
and numbers of artists represented 
 
 
Feasibility: Proposed objects shall be evaluated relative to their feasibility and convincing evidence of the 
artist’s ability to successfully complete the work as proposed. Factors to be considered include, but are not 
limited to: project budget, timeline, artist’s experience, soundness of materials, city approval requirements, 
and level of community support.
