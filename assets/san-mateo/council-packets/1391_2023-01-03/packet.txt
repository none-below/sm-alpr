SUBJECT:
Police Records Supervisor – Job Specification Update 
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to approve revisions to the job specification for the classification of Police Records Supervisor, and 
approve the change in position title for the incumbent Police Records Supervisor.
BACKGROUND:
The Police Department operates a Records Unit responsible for the processing and management of police reports and 
related documents and a Property Unit responsible for the receipt, storage, safekeeping, release and disposal of all 
property and evidence that comes under the control of the San Mateo Police Department. 
The Records and Property Units are both staffed by civilian Police Records Specialists, with oversight provided by two 
Police Records Supervisor positions, one for each unit.  The supervisor position assigned to the Property Unit is currently 
vacant.  Staff wishes to update the job specification for the Police Records Supervisor Position to more accurately reflect 
the duties required of the position assigned to the Property Unit, and also to modify the title of the classification to be 
reflective of the Property Unit functions.  
The job specification revisions have been discussed with the impacted bargaining group, and they are supportive of the 
changes.  Per San Mateo Municipal Code Section 2.57.030(d), job specification revisions become effective upon approval 
by the City Council. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact associated with revising the Police Records Supervisor job specification, as there is no change in 
compensation due to the revisions.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This action is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that will not result in 
direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)

All meeting noticing requirements were met.

DEFINITION 
 
To plan, organize and supervise the activities of the Records or Property Bureaus of the Police 
Department; to provided administrative and technical staff assistance; and to do related work as 
required. 
 
SUPERVISION RECEIVED AND EXERCISED 
 
Receives direction from the Police Technical Services Administrator.

Recommend and assist in the implementation of goals and objectives; establish schedules and

procedures to improve operations and ensure compliance with new and existing laws. 
 
When assigned to Records Unit, develop, implement and modify clerical and informational systems 
and procedures to improve operations and ensure compliance with new and existing laws. 
 
When assigned to Records Unit, apply laws, court decisions, rules and regulations on use and 
confidentiality of police records and implement training and procedures to ensure compliance; 
approve release or inspection of police reports.     
 
Personally perform the most difficult tasks related to police records or property work. 
 
Research, collect data, and prepare a variety of comprehensive statistical and written reports as 
required.

SUBJECT:
Beresford Park Batting Cage, Ball Fields, and Concession Building – Use Agreement  
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve a five-year use agreement with San Mateo Youth Softball Association for the batting cage, ball fields, and 
concessions building at Chanteloup Field in Beresford Park for the period of January 4, 2023 through December 31, 2027, 
and authorize the City Manager to execute the use agreement in substantially the form presented. 
BACKGROUND:
San Mateo Youth Softball Association (SMYSA) has long used Chanteloup Field as its primary field and operated a 
concession to help offset the cost of operating the league. In addition, SMYSA installed and maintains the batting cage 
located at that field. This use agreement continues the current operating relationship with SMYSA. The terms and 
conditions of the use agreement are consistent with those of other youth sports organizations that operate in local parks, 
such as San Mateo National Little League at Martens Field in Lakeshore Park. 
Key provisions: 
•
SMYSA is responsible for repair and maintenance of the batting cage. 
•
SMYSA must make the batting cage reasonably available for use by other San Mateo youth sports organizations. 
Allowed operating times are Monday-Saturday: 9am-7pm; Sunday: 10am-7pm. Only rubber balls may be used. 
•
SMYSA must provide sufficient netting to reduce noise of balls hitting fence. 
Concession: 
•
Concession prices shall be those generally prevailing for similar concessions. 
•
A portion of the proceeds must be used to fund field improvement projects. 
Other: 
•
Use of amplified sound must comply with City sound ordinance and good neighbor practices and is authorized for 
events such as opening day ceremonies, announcing upcoming batters, scores, and other game related 
information. No play-by-play, on-going commentary, or music (except National Anthem). 
•
Use and storage of vehicles must be approved in advance and may only be operated under adult supervision. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Standard youth field fees shall be applied to SMYSA per the Master Fee Schedule. No fees shall be charged for use of 
batting cage, which was originally funded by SMYSA. SMYSA shall be responsible for the maintenance and upkeep of the 
batting cage. No established rental or fees shall be paid to City for the concession operation; however, SMYSA shall work 
cooperatively with City to identify and fund needed field improvement projects using a portion of the concession 
proceeds. City shall provide water service. All other utilities associated with concession operation shall be paid by SMYSA.

Pruning Objectives and Specifications  
 
The objective for pruning street trees is to achieve a structurally sound; low risk condition including sound 
structure, live branches, removal of chronic disease, and clearance for access and traffic control visibility; 
uniformly shaped tree crown; while retaining as large a foliar crown size and leaf area as possible.  
 
Trees shall be pruned to achieve the objective based on the anticipated mature tree size, site conditions, 
available space, and current tree conditions.  
 
Branches to be removed shall be selected to promote or retain a primary leader, acceptable branch aspect 
ratios, and consideration of the branch origination point as related to the portion of the crown the leaves 
occupy. The type of pruning cut to be performed shall be considered:  
 
Branch removal cut – removal of a whole branch back to the parent branch or trunk leaving an intact branch 
collar;  
 
Reduction cut – a reduction of the length of a branch back to a lateral branch of equal or smaller diameter and 
large enough to assume the apical dominance;  
 
Heading cut – cutting a small twig back to a bud, cutting a branch back to a node without a lateral branch, or 
cutting a branch back to a lateral branch too small in diameter to assume apical dominance (less than 1/3 the 
diameter of the parent branch).  
 
Whenever reasonable, branch removal cuts and reduction cuts shall be preferred over heading cuts. Unless 
directed by the City to avert a high-risk situation, trees shall not receive a majority of heading cuts.  
 
Defects that will remain after pruning operations shall be reported to the Managing Arborist or Tree 
Maintenance Specialist. Any structural weakness, decayed trunk or branches, split crotches or branches, shall be 
reported as soon as they are observed. If the detection of severe decay or split trunk or branches may change 
the assignment of pruning the tree, the pruning shall be stopped until the Managing Arborist/Tree Maintenance 
Specialist can review the findings and make a decision about the fate of the tree.

INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS 
 
 
MINIMUM SCOPE OF INSURANCE  
 
Coverage shall be at least as broad as: 
1. Commercial General Liability (CGL):  Insurance Services Office (ISO) Form CG 00 01 12 07 
covering CGL on an “occurrence” basis, including products-completed operations, personal & 
advertising injury, with limits no less than $2,000,000 per occurrence. If a general aggregate limit 
applies, either the general aggregate limit shall apply separately to this project/location or the 
general aggregate limit shall be twice the required occurrence limit. 
2. Automobile Liability:  ISO Form Number CA 00 01 covering any auto (Code 1), or if Contractor 
has no owned autos, hired, (Code 8) and non-owned autos (Code 9), with limit no less than 
$1,000,000 per accident for bodily injury and property damage. 
3. Workers’ Compensation: as required by the State of California, with Statutory Limits, and 
Employer’s Liability Insurance with limit of no less than $1,000,000 per accident for bodily injury 
or disease.  
If the contractor maintains higher limits than the minimums shown above, the City requires and shall be 
entitled to coverage for the higher limits maintained by the contractor. 
Other Insurance Provisions 
The insurance policies are to contain, or be endorsed to contain, the following provisions: 
Additional Insured Status 
The City, its elected and appointed officials, employees, and agents are to be covered as insureds on 
the auto policy for liability arising out of automobiles owned, leased, hired or borrowed by or on behalf 
of the Contractor; and on the CGL policy with respect to liability arising out of work or operations 
performed by or on behalf of the Contractor including materials, parts or equipment furnished in 
connection with such work or operations. General liability coverage can be provided in the form of an 
endorsement to the Contractor’s insurance (at least as broad as ISO Form CG 20 10, 11 85 or both CG 
20 10 and CG 20 37 forms if later revisions used). 
Primary Coverage 
For any claims related to this contract, the Contractor’s insurance coverage shall be primary insurance 
as respects the City, its elected and appointed officials, employees, and agents. Any insurance or self-
insurance maintained by the City, its elected and appointed officials, employees, or agents shall be 
excess of the Contractor’s insurance and shall not contribute with it. 
Notice of Cancellation 
Each insurance policy required above shall provide that coverage shall not be canceled, except after 
thirty (30) days’ prior written notice (10 days for non-payment) has been given to the City.

SUBJECT:
Utility Trucks Purchase - Purchase Contract
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to approve an alternative purchasing procedure and award a contract to National Auto Fleet Group for 
the purchase of three (3) new 2023 Ford F350 Utility trucks in the amount of $310,263.47, and authorize the Public Works 
Director to execute the purchase contract in substantially the form presented.
BACKGROUND:
The Public Works Department and Parks and Recreation Department utilize various specialty utility trucks to perform 
essential services. Replacement of these utility trucks is imperative to provide services to the community. The proposed 
purchase of three (3) new 2023 Ford F350 utility trucks is intended to replace existing City trucks that are between 14-15 
years old and at the end of their useful life based on years in service, mileage, condition, and cost of maintenance. Staff 
recommends replacing these vehicles to ensure reliability. One (1) of the three (3) new Ford F350 Utility trucks is a new 
one-time addition to the fleet with funding previously approved in the fiscal year 2021-22 budget. 
San Mateo Municipal Code Section 3.60.070(h)(3) authorizes the City Council to approve an alternative purchasing 
procedure when it finds doing so is in the best interest of the City.  Sourcewell (formerly NJPA) is a government 
organization that solicits, receives, and reviews competitive bids for various specialty equipment, including the Ford utility 
trucks.  Sourcewell has conducted competitive, public bids to select vehicle vendors to achieve competitive pricing and has 
selected National Auto Fleet Group.  Therefore, the purchase of three (3) new 2023 Ford F350 trucks from National Auto 
Fleet Group is in the best interest of the City because the Ford utility truck is the recommended replacement for existing 
Public Works and Parks and Recreation trucks, and the City may rely on bids obtained from Sourcewell because it has 
solicited, received, and reviewed competitive bids for the desired utility trucks through its cooperative purchasing 
program. Based on the above, staff recommends the purchase of these three (3) new 2023 Ford F350 Utility trucks from 
National Auto Fleet Group. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Funding for two (2) replacement trucks will be funded from the Vehicle and Equipment Replacement Fund. Funding for 
one (1) new addition to the fleet will be funded from the Sewer Fund. Sufficient funding is available in both programs.
The total amount of $310,263.47 includes the chassis, utility body and a 10% mark-up.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
The approval of this purchase contract is not a project subject to CEQA because the replacement of the City’s supply of 
service vehicles is a continuing administrative or maintenance activity. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(2).)

including flooding and storms; and

confirmation of the proclamation at the City Council meeting of January 3, 2023.

item ratifying and confirming the Proclamation of the Existence of a Local Emergency, as issued by the

CONFIRMING THE EXISTENCE OF A LOCAL EMERGENCY
WHEREAS, Chapter 2.78 of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code empowers the City Manager 
as the Director of Emergency Services to proclaim the existence or threatened existence of a local 
emergency when the City is affected or likely to be affected by a public calamity and the City Council is 
not in session, subject to ratification by the City Council at the earliest practicable time; and
WHEREAS, conditions of extreme peril to the safety of persons and property have arisen within 
the City of San Mateo, caused by recent significant wet weather events, including flooding and storms, 
commencing on or about 3:00 a.m. on the 31st day of December 2022, at which time the City Council of 
the City of San Mateo was not in session; and
WHEREAS, the City Council of the City of San Mateo does hereby find that the aforesaid 
conditions of extreme peril did warrant and necessitate the proclamation of the existence of a local 
emergency; and
WHEREAS, the Director of Emergency Services did proclaim the existence of a local emergency 
within the City of San Mateo on the 2nd day of January 2023 at 2:40 p.m., and such proclamation is 
attached hereto as Exhibit A and incorporated by reference.  
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the City Council of the City of San Mateo that the 
Proclamation of Existence of a Local Emergency, as issued by the Director of Emergency Services, is 
hereby ratified and confirmed.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that during the existence of this local emergency, the powers, 
functions, and duties of the Director of Emergency Services and the Emergency Organization of this 
City shall be those prescribed by state law, ordinances, and resolutions of this City and by the City of 
San Mateo Emergency Plan.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that that the Director of Emergency Services of the City of San Mateo 
acting as the executive officer for the San Mateo Consolidated Fire Department, a Joint Powers 
Authority providing fire and emergency services within the City of San Mateo is hereby authorized to 
execute for and on behalf of the City of San Mateo, and on behalf of the San Mateo Consolidated Fire, 
any agreement or application for, and to file the agreement with, the California Governor's Office of 
Emergency Services for the purposes of obtaining federal financial assistance.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the local emergency shall be deemed to continue to exist until 
its termination is proclaimed by the City Council of the City of San Mateo.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that this action is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an 
organizational or administrative activity that will not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the 
environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)

PROCLAMATION OF THE EXISTENCE

WHEREAS, Section 2.78.030(a) of the City of San Mateo Municipal Code empowers the Director of 
Emergency Services to proclaim the existence or threatened existence of a local emergency when the City is 
affected or likely to be affected by a public calamity and the City Council is not in session; and

hereby find that:

Mateo caused by the recent significant wet weather events, including flooding and storms, commencing on or 
about 3:00 a.m. on the 31st of December 2022.

session. 
 
NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY PROCLAIMED, that a local emergency now exists throughout the City of 
San Mateo.

powers, functions, and duties of the emergency organization of the City of San Mateo shall be those prescribed 
by state law, Ordinances, and Resolutions of the City of San Mateo and by the City of San Mateo Emergency 
Plan.
