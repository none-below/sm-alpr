SUBJECT:
Clean Water Program Update and Sewer Rate Increase
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the status of the Clean Water Program and provide feedback on a proposed five-year sewer rate 
structure and draft Proposition 218 Notice to increase sewer rates.
BACKGROUND:
Clean Water Program
The City is currently under a Regional Water Quality Control Board Cease and Desist Order and National Pollutant 
Discharge Elimination System permit requiring improvements to its sanitary sewer collection system and the wastewater 
treatment plant (WWTP) to prevent sanitary sewer overflows and discharges of untreated or partially treated wastewater. 
The Clean Water Program (CWP) consists of a series of projects to upgrade and replace aging infrastructure, increase 
system capacity to prevent sanitary sewer overflows, and meet current and future regulatory requirements for 
wastewater treatment. The CWP is a capital intensive 10-year effort that began in 2015 and is estimated to cost nearly $1 
billion through fiscal year 2025-26. Significant sewer rate increases were required to fund the CWP. 
Sewer Rates
On March 5, 2018, Council adopted a multi-year sewer rate increase and rate restructure for fiscal years 2018-19 through 
2022-23. As part of the 2018 rate restructuring, the City moved from charging solely based on volumetric usage to a hybrid 
volumetric/fixed-fee structure, which helped stabilize revenue. The rate restructuring, in addition to approving a multiyear 
rate increase, allowed the City to finance the CWP through bonds and obtain low-interest federal Water Infrastructure 
Finance and Innovation Act (WIFIA) and State Revolving Fund loans. 
As part of a study session held in April 2022, Council supported a future multi-year rate sewer rate schedule with 
inflationary-level increases to ensure sufficient revenue to fund ongoing operating and maintenance costs and capital 
improvement projects. Staff worked with the City’s sewer rate consultant, Bartle Wells, to determine recommended sewer 
rates for meeting the City’s ongoing needs. The proposed sewer rate schedule for fiscal years 2023-24 through 2027-28 is 
shown in Attachment 1, which includes a maximum 3% increase in rates for each of the five fiscal years. Staff is seeking 
feedback from Council on the proposed rate structure. A comparison of the City’s fiscal year 2022-23 sewer rates with 
other sewer agency rates in San Mateo County is included in Attachment 2.
Compliance with Proposition 218 
Proposition 218 requires the City to mail a written notice of any proposed sewer rate increases to affected property 
owners at least 45 days prior to a scheduled public hearing, during which time, property owners may submit written 
protests opposing proposed rate increases. A draft of this notice is shown in Attachment 3. At the public hearing, currently 
scheduled for June 5, 2023, Council will consider all protests related to the proposed rates.  If a majority of parcel owners

Next Steps
After receiving feedback from Council, the Proposition 218 Notice will be finalized and distributed, initiating the rate

ordinance will also occur at the June 5, 2023 meeting. Council will then consider adopting the ordinance after a second 
reading on June 20, 2023, establishing the new sewer service rates that will be effective thirty days later. As such, the 
proposed new rate structure for fiscal year 2023-24, if approved, will be effective August 1, 2023, with each subsequent 
fiscal year’s rates starting on July 1.
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budgetary impact associated with this recommendation. Proposed rate structures and rate increases will be 
presented to Council during the study session as preliminary values. Input received from Council at this study session will 
be used to refine various parameters in preparation for the formal sewer rate setting process.

Why the Rate Increases Are Needed 
Specifically, the proposed rate increases will enable the City 
to generate the funding needed to: 
• Continue construction and long-term financing of the City’s 
aging wastewater treatment plant 
The City’s existing wastewater treatment plant includes 
several facilities which have reached the end of useful life. 
The treatment plant is being rebuilt to ensure the City can 
continue to provide high-quality service and meet new 
regulatory requirements to protect San Francisco Bay. 
• Construct critical improvements to the City’s sewer 
collection system to meet regulatory requirements, prevent 
sanitary sewer overflows, and replace aging pipelines 
The City needs to address increased regulatory 
requirements and issues associated with sanitary sewer 
overflow prevention, including construction of relief sewer 
lines and facilities necessary to prevent overflows during 
heavy rains, as well as increased cleaning, inspection, and 
rehabilitation of the existing collection system. 
• Keep rates aligned with the cost of providing service 
Pay for the increases in annual operating and maintenance 
expenses required to meet the City’s regulatory permit 
requirements, fund increasing annual debt service 
requirements, and keep revenues aligned with the costs of 
providing wastewater service.
 
Methodology Used to Calculate the Proposed Rates 
The proposed sewer rates are based on a cost-of-service 
analysis that includes a) financial projections to identify future 
wastewater system funding needs, and b) apportionment 
of costs and development of rates for each customer class 
based on factors including the volume and strength of 
wastewater discharge. 
The proposed rates are designed to recover the costs of 
providing sewer service to each parcel. 
Proposed Sewer Rates 
The following table shows current and proposed sewer 
rates.  Residential rates include two components: 
1) A Fixed Monthly Charge per residential dwelling unit that is 
billed independent of usage, and 2) Sewer Usage Charges 
billed based on metered water usage. Commercial and non-
residential customers will continue to be billed Sewer Usage 
Charges based on customer class and metered usage, 
subject to a minimum charge. Residential and commercial 
Sewer Usage Charges are based on metered water usage 
from the preceding November through March time period 
(when there is minimal outdoor irrigation) and levied per 
hundred cubic feet (CCF). One CCF equals approximately 
748 gallons. 
The City’s current sewer rates for a typical residential 
customer are in the middle range compared to other 
regional agencies in San Mateo County. The rates for fiscal 
years 2023-24 through 2027-28 reflect the maximum annual 
rate increase proposed under this Proposition 218 notice. 
The City will review the rates each year and determine the 
actual annual increase up to these maximums. 
Approved sewer rates for 2023-24 would be effective August 
1 and effective July 1 for each subsequent fiscal year under 
the proposed rate structure. Please note sewer charges for 
most customers are billed annually on the property tax bill.
