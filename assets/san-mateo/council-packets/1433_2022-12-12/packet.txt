From:
Eldridge, Karyl
To:
City Council (San Mateo)
Subject:
In support of an orderly process
Date:
Wednesday, December 7, 2022 4:01:08 PM
Dear Members of the San Mateo City Council,
I am writing to express my support for an orderly process in the mayoral transition for the City of San Mateo.
It was with shock and dismay that I witnessed the council proceedings on Monday, December 5.  I had expected a
straightforward transition to Amourence Lee as the new mayor in the rotational system that has been in effect in San
Mateo for many years.  Instead I witnessed a disturbing effort to rewrite the rules to serve the political ends of new
council members who had been sworn into office not hours before.
The rotational system is designed to assure a peaceful and orderly succession in the role of mayor for the City of San
Mateo.  Also, by virtue of the fact that it allows all council members an opportunity to serve in their turn, it helps
assure the equitable distribution of power over a span of time.  It is now Amo’s turn to serve as mayor, and the
attempt to deprive her of this is an affront to the underlying rationales for such a system in the first place, which are
both powerful and sound.
The first order of business for a new council is to elect the most senior council member as mayor.  In the current
situation, this is Amourence Lee.  I sincerely hope the council will forego further efforts at disruption and fulfill the
expectation that it will perform the task assigned to it by the city charter.  We do not want the disregard of rules in
an effort to corral power to become the new San Mateo Way.
Sincerely,
Karyl Eldridge
Vice Chair of One San Mateo

From:
Ted McKinnon
To:
Clerk; Adam Loraine; Lisa Diaz Nash; Rob Newsom; Amourence Lee
Subject:
Mayoral Succession and the current crisis
Date:
Wednesday, December 7, 2022 5:13:16 PM
Dear Councilmembers,
The fact this is the first year of District elections has nothing to do with the mayoral rotation. What it
instead indicates is that the move to District elections enabled a power shift, and if that power shift
did not fully meet the expectations of a particular group expecting to benefit from it, they were
willing to undertake extraordinary measures to make up the gap they were unable to achieve at the
ballot box. There is no means available for the selection of the fifth council seat in a way that
engages the entire city. That would take a special election the current process does not provide for.
To indicate otherwise is merely a disingenuous and cynical way of attempting to disguise what
otherwise can only be characterized as a blatant act of political self-interest. The charter clearly
provides for a means of both succession for mayor and a tie-breaking vote for a deadlocked vote on
the appointment of a vacant city council seat. That should be followed and, in the future,
strengthened. The current experience calls for, if anything, the formalization of mayoral succession
so that in the event of deadlocked City Council vote, the incumbent candidate assumes the position
automatically, thus obviating the circus we are currently witnessing.
Sincerely.
Ted McKinnon

ETHICS SAN MATEO

DEDICATED TO TRUTH, TRANSPARENCY AND ETHICAL CONDUCT IN CITY GOVERNMENT

December 12, 2022
Statement of Position

Regarding Allegations of Unethical and/or Unlawful Appointment Influence

During the continuation of the December 5, 2022 regular City of San Mateo City Council
meeting held on December 7, Council Member Lee stated she had received at least two
separate communications, “Backdoor” offers, regarding her support for specific candidates for
the now vacant seat on the city council. Paraphrased, she reported the offers were that if she
supported a certain candidate, she would become Mayor. No further details were provided.
Ethics San Mateo believes, if proven, these offers would be a serious breach of ethical
standards on the part of the person or persons making the offer, and possibly be a violation of
the Brown Act (as alleged by Lee) and of California Penal Code Section 58, a felony.

Ethics San Mateo’s purpose is to promote ethical conduct by our city government and
leadership, those seeking leadership roles, and by businesses and organizations conducting
transactions with or seeking to influence city government.

As our mission is to lead and support efforts by the residents of San Mateo to ensure our city is
served according to the highest level of ethical standards and behavior, we are calling for the
below actions to take place:

e The City Attorney immediately open an investigation into the allegations, with the
purpose of ascertaining if there was a breach of codified ethical standards.

e The City Manager make a request to the San Mateo Police Department to opena
criminal case investigation, supporting the need for such action with a transcript of
Council Member Lee’s statements during the meeting on December 7".

e Council Member Lee to provide full details, documentation, evidence, etc., of the
incidents to both the Police Department and City Attorney.

Ethics San Mateo will attempt to follow the progress of the investigations, as allowed by
the City Attorney, City Manager and Police Department. At this time, we will not conduct
any independent research into this; because with the possibility that a felony has
occurred in addition to a breach of ethical standards, we wish to avoid interfering with
the formal Law Enforcement and City Attorney’s investigations.
