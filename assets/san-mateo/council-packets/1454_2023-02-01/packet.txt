SUBJECT:
Parks and Recreation Picnic Rentals – Pilot Program for Prioritization 
RECOMMENDATION:
Provide feedback on a recommended one-year pilot program for implementation of prioritization for picnic rentals.
BACKGROUND:
There are currently 28 reservable picnic sites and 19 drop-in picnic sites within the City of San Mateo Parks system. The 
Parks and Recreation Department’s current practice is to allow residents of San Mateo to reserve the 28 reservable picnic 
sites. The City defines “residents” as those who live within the boundaries of the City of San Mateo and within the 
jurisdiction of its municipal corporation. For purposes of picnic reservations, residents of areas in unincorporated San 
Mateo are considered County residents, not City residents. 
Staff has recently revisited this issue and in an effort to streamline processes recommend updating fees for non-residents 
and non-profits to reserve park facilities, with priority being given to City residents. Staff recommends implementing a 
one-year pilot program to examine the prioritization process, starting July 1, 2023. The pilot program will provide City 
residents priority; City residents will be able to reserve sites 9 months in advance and non-residents and non-profit 
organizations will be able to reserve 3 months in advance. 
City residents will most likely not be adversely impacted because staff has identified underutilized picnic sites throughout 
the year. Staff believes that implementation of the pilot program will assist in higher utilization of facilities, while providing 
residents with priority access. This change will be consistent with practices at neighboring cities and a survey of local 
jurisdictions’ policies regarding picnic site reservation (Attachment 1). 
Once the commission provides guidance on the implementation of the program, the next steps include updating the 
Master Fee Schedule to include updated rates for non-resident and non-profit reservations and providing a 
recommendation to the City Council. At the end of the one-year pilot program, staff will update the commission and 
consider making any adjustments to the prioritization process.
BUDGET IMPACT:
It is anticipated that to streamline this process and make it more transparent it could result in an increase in patronage 
and revenue.
