SUBJECT:
Proposed Parks and Recreation Fee Schedule for FY 2023-24
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of the proposed FY 2023-24 fee schedule for Parks and Recreation facilities and 
miscellaneous fees as provided in Attachment 1 of the Administrative Report.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City reviews and adjusts fees. Generally, fees are adjusted based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for the 
previous year. The Finance Department has identified the official CPI for this year to be 6.0%. As the CPI increase is higher 
than previous years, the Department is only changing fees to adjust for inflation and align with other agencies. Thus this 
year’s fee increases recommendations are mainly based on information gathered from market surveys and other industry 
standards, instead of tracking the CPI. The recommended fee changes for the Parks and Recreation Department are 
detailed in Attachment 1. 
The most notable changes to the proposed Parks and Recreation Fee Schedule for FY 2023-24 are:
-
A simplification of the fee structure for facility rentals by three user classifications: (1) non-profits; (2), not-for-
profits; and (3) business organizations and all other users.  An updated facilities fee table is being proposed 
(Attachment 11) that clearly illustrates the new fee structure. It is anticipated that the proposed new format for 
the fees table will improve readability and increase ease of access for users. 
-
Picnics areas have been consolidated on the fee schedule (Attachment 4). 
-
City Council appeal fees will now be listed in the Clerk’s Fee Schedule along with all other departments.
Additionally, the Development Impact Fee calculations have been added for clarity. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Most recommended changes would not have a significant impact on the Department’s budgeted revenue. 
Revenue generated from the payment of Park in-Lieu or Park Impact fees are difficult to predict, as the amount collected 
depends upon economic factors and the number of projects subject to these categories of fees. It is unlikely that the 
facility fee structure streamline will impact the Department’s budget.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This recommendation is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is a government fiscal activity which does not involve any 
commitment to any specific project which may result in a potentially significant physical impact on the environment. 
(CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(4).)

As part of the budgetary process, the Park and Recreation Commission shall review the Cost Recovery Plan and the performance of each activity compared to 
the plan and make any recommendations for changes to the plan.
The thresholds shall include both minimum and target levels of cost recovery.
Strategies for achieving the minimum and target thresholds shall be established by staff for each activity area. These strategies may include immediate or 
gradual action depending upon the best interests of maintaining a strong program, achieving the target within a reasonable time period and current fiscal 
conditions.
Activity areas unable to meet their minimum threshold after these strategies have been applied shall be reviewed by the Park and Recreation Commission for 
consideration of recommending appropriate action: reduce costs; reduce cost recovery target; eliminate activity.
Activities that have achieved or exceeded their target threshold shall be evaluated to determine if the target can/should be increased.
 The Cost Recovery Plan shall, where appropriate, also include specific other fee considerations (e.g. benchmarking to a specific competitor) that may 
supersede cost recovery target considerations.
Once a Cost Recovery Plan is established, and within that context, staff shall have the authority to set and adjust fees, except that no fee increase shall exceed 
20% within a 1-year period without the review of the Park and Recreation Commission and approval of the City Council.

Facility Indirect Overhead (Fac IOH): Calculated charges for the Public Works Building Maintenance for on-going maintenance and 
repairs (e.g. plumbing, electrical, mechanical, carpentry, painting).
Recreation Cost Tier Definitions
Direct: The specific “go-away” costs at the course or activity level. These include leaders, instructors and materials or services (e.g. 
transportation) specific to the course or activity.
Program Direct Overhead (DOH): Direct supervision of the program, and minor miscellaneous items such as supervisor mileage.
Facility Overhead (FOH): The combination of facility direct costs (the day-to-day costs to keep the building open- receptionist, custodial 
services, custodial products, utilities) and facility direct overhead (primarily facility supervision).
Operating Indirect Overhead (Op IOH): Division level and shared costs, such as departmental management, brochure, printing, 
photocopying, training, accounting support, technology, credit card discount costs, administrative office clerical support, computer, and 
recreation equipment sinking fund charges
