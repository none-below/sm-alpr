Necessary Steps
STEP ONE:  Staff will conduct research to determine what other agencies’ name their 
Senior Citizens Commission.  Staff will also reach out to the City’s DEI (Diversity, Equity 
and Inclusion) committee to see if they have any feedback / thoughts.
STEP TWO:  Staff will bring information from research to Senior Citizens Commission for 
discussion and determination of “top picks” for potential name change. Staff will also 
receive recommendations for any other potential changes / updates to the Senior 
Citizens Commission municipal code at this time.
https://law.cityofsanmateo.org/us/ca/cities/san-mateo/code/2.29

Necessary Steps
STEP THREE:  Staff will outreach to Senior Community to receive feedback regarding 
their preference for potential top names as selected by Senior Citizens Commission.  
STEP FOUR:  Staff will return to Senior Citizens Commission with results of outreach and 
request final recommendations from commission on name change and municipal code 
updates.  
STEP FIVE:  Staff meets with City Attorney to review proposed language changes as 
recommended by the Senior Citizens Commission.  If any substantial changes 
recommended from attorney, will bring back to Senior Citizens Commissions for further 
review.  If no significant changes, will proceed to STEP SIX.

Necessary Steps
STEP SIX:  Staff prepares a report for City Council.  Report is reviewed by Department 
Head and City Attorney again.
STEP SEVEN: The City Council decides if they wish to take up the introduction of the. 
ordinance
STEP EIGHT: There must be a second reading (or adoption) of the ordinance.
STEP NINE: The ordinance takes effect 30 days after its adoption.

Proposed Timeline
April-May 2023:  Staff will research other agencies’ Senior Citizens Commission names and 
work with DEI for name recommendations.  Staff will also review any other potential changes 
/ updates to the Senior Citizens Commission municipal code.
May 2023:  Staff presents results of research and receives feedback from commission 
regarding top name choices and any additional changes to municipal code.
June – July 2023:  Staff conducts outreach to Senior Community for top name choices. 
September 2023: Staff reports out on results of outreach and receives final 
recommendations from Senior Citizens Commission to present to Council.
