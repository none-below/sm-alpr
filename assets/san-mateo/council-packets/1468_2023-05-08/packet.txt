Project Guardian – Presentation
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive informational presentation regarding Project Guardian, presented by Ed Barberini, Police Chief, City of San Mateo.
BACKGROUND:
The San Mateo Police Department (SMPD) acknowledges and understands that some members of our community may be 
vulnerable, have special needs, or react differently when contacted by emergency personnel or law enforcement officers. 
With those considerations in mind, SMPD created a vulnerable persons registry, titled Project Guardian. We believe this 
program will be of special interest to older adults within the City of San Mateo and therefore staff would like to share 
additional information regarding this program to Senior Citizens Commissioners.

Highlights of 
Project 
Guardian
Free and Voluntary
Database is not limited to one specific group.  Project Guardian is for people 
with autism, development or intellectual disabilities, physical disabilities, 
dementia, or anyone with a condition that might make the person more 
vulnerable or react differently when contacted by law enforcement personnel 
Seeks to reduce the stress and fear that can be experienced by persons with 
special needs during an interaction with law enforcement by providing 
information to the officers prior to the contact (actions to avoid, favorite 
topics to discuss, etc.)
Assists officers by providing information that can be used to calm, locate, and 
engage with an individual who may not be able to communicate effectively 
during an emergency

SUBJECT:
Senior Citizens Commission Name Change - Feedback
RECOMMENDATION:
Provide feedback on potential name change options for the Senior Citizens Commission. 
BACKGROUND:
At the January 23, 2023, special meeting of the Senior Citizens Commission, commissioners inquired about potentially 
changing the name of the commission. At the March 13, 2023, regular meeting of the Senior Citizens Commission, staff 
provided information regarding the steps and procedures for changing the commission's name.  Staff have surveyed 
neighboring cities regarding their senior commission’s names.  If the commission chooses to proceed with the name 
change process, please provide feedback of potential name options (no more than three) for consideration for community 
outreach.
