Page 2
Public Comment – There were no speakers.
3.
Senior Citizens Commission Name Change - Feedback
Commissioners were asked to provide feedback on potential name change options for the Senior Citizens 
Commission. Commissioners asked questions and provided comments. Commissioners were in consensus 
regarding the following names: Senior Advisory Commission, Senior Commission, Older Adults Commission or 
for the name to stay the same.
Public Comment – There were no speakers.
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
•
Stephanie Douglas, Community Services Section Manager, announced that the Senior Center and Recreation 
Department were gearing up for Summer. Additionally, it was announced that the Recreation Department was in 
need of Summer staff. Commissioners were encouraged to refer anyone they may know.
•
Vice Chair Lee shared information about the following items:
1.
She and Commissioner Wang attended an event in Half Moon Bay hosted by the Center for Age-Friendly 
Excellence. They learned about what Age Friendly Task Forces in other cities were doing and shared what the 
City of San Mateo Age Friendly Task Force was doing.
2.
She had a meeting with Supervisor Noelia Corzo, San Mateo County District 2 Supervisor, where she 
requested that the needs and concerns of older adults are taken into consideration when decisions are 
made.
•
Commissioner Wang motioned, seconded by Vice Chair Lee to request a future agenda item regarding funding 
for future events/activities for the Senior Citizens Commission.
•
Commissioner Lee motioned, seconded by Commissioner Wang to request a future agenda item regarding 
information and feedback about Senior Center programs and activities.

“Research shows that spending time in the outdoors is associated with better mental 
and physical health, and that many Californians face barriers to accessing their parks 
including proximity, not feeling welcome due to language barriers, economic barriers, 
and not being exposed to the outdoors. California has a wealth of state parks, and many 
public libraries are likely within driving distance of one. By reducing barriers to park access 
and partnering with entities such as public libraries, the state can help more Californians 
explore the outdoors and gain the benefits of our parks.”
