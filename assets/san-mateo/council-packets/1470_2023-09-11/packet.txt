SUBJECT:
Senior Citizens Commission Name Change- Survey Results and Recommendation
RECOMMENDATION:
Review results received from the Senior Citizens Commission Name Change Survey and determine if commission would 
like to proceed with making a recommendation to City Council for a municipal code update.
BACKGROUND:
At the January 23, 2023 Special Meeting of the Senior Citizens Commission, commissioners inquired about potentially 
changing the name of the commission. At the March 13, 2023 Regular Meeting, staff provided information regarding the 
steps and procedures for changing the name of the commission. At the July 10, 2023 Regular Meeting, staff provided 
information on neighboring agencies' commission names. Commissioners selected four possible options to be included in a 
survey to San Mateo senior residents to receive feedback on community's preference. 
 
The survey was distributed to 5,000+ residents ages 50+ via Senior Center Newsletter, RecTrac Registration Software, and 
hardcopies were available at the Senior Center. We received 160 responses to the survey.  Respondents were asked to 
rank the following four choices in order of preference with 1 being most preferred and 4 being least preferred. 
 

Senior Advisory Commission 

Senior Citizens Commission

Older Adults Commission

Senior Commission
 
Staff are presenting the data from the survey so that the commission can decide whether to make a recommendation to 
City Council for a municipal code ordinance update.

Thank you for the opportunity to provide feedback. 
I served on the Senior Citizens Advisory Commission approximately 12 years ago while I
was still working and commuting to San Francisco. I resigned after approximately one year
because I could not justify taking 1/2-1 day off for meetings that seemed to have no real
direction nor practical focus.   The day that the previously forwarded staff report and
supporting materials were basically read aloud to us for the entire meeting was the day I
decided to resign. There was no discussion, no advising, nor purpose for that meeting— and
it cost me one PTO day to attend that meeting as well as others that had been similarly of
minimal purpose.
I have served on other commissions and City committees in the past and currently serve on
the Measure S Committee. Based on those experiences, I would recommend  that the work
of the Senior Commission be reimagined and that thought be given to what type of
programming, activities, planning input, etc. would be truly useful to City staff, the Council,
and to the residents of this community. If some thought is put into this, the Senior
Committee/Commission could become one whose focus would be to make this City one
where those ages 55-90+ can live safe, rich and fulfilling lives. However, if there is no
practical, useful purpose for the Senior Committee/Commission, I recommended that it be
discontinued or its work folded into that of some other committee/commission. 
While it takes staff support, the Commission could sponsor community workshops with
police regarding personal safety, with exercise specialists, sponsor a monthly seniors
walking group, etc. Such a Commission/Committee could also provide planning
recommendations regarding traffic flow, crosswalk recommendations, services, development
needs etc. That would not only benefit seniors but families with young children as well as
the community as a whole. 
I hope you are able to think of ways to prioritize the service goals of this
Commission/Committee. Seniors (post 55+) are a significant demographic in this
community and are regular voters and are economic contributors to the community. It is
important they have a meaningful voice in City government. 
Thank you again for the opportunity to provide feedback. 
Karen Moen,
