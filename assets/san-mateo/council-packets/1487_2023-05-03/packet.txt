SUBJECT:
Proposed Five Year (2023-2028) Parks and Recreation Capital Improvement Plan
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on the Proposed Five Year Capital Improvement Plan for FY 2023-2028. 
BACKGROUND:
The Parks and Recreation Department develops an updated five-year capital improvement plan (CIP) every year. Every 
other year, alternating with the development of the two-year operating budget, the update of the CIP plan is more 
extensive. This is one of those years. Staff reviews the current list of identified park improvements as well as estimates of 
expected revenues over the same period and recommends changes based on priorities and anticipated income. The 
Proposed FY 2023-2028 CIP plan is included as Attachment 1. Budget estimates for new and existing projects are provided 
for each of the next five years. The largest source of revenue for park and recreation capital improvements are park in-lieu 
and park impact fees, which are derived from residential development activity.  The proposed expenditure plan is subject 
to change as projections of fees have not been in alignment with residential development activity. Parks and Recreation 
staff perform due diligence by speaking with Planning Division staff as well as making direct contact with developers in 
order to ascertain the likelihood of a project becoming “shovel ready” within the time frame. Numerous projects have not 
eventuated as expected and as a result neither have the park in-lieu and park impact fees. 
Currently the Department has approximately $12 million in reserves which will meet our projected requirements for the 
construction of the Central Park Playground and East Hillsdale Park. Further plans are a concern at this stage until reserves 
are replenished. Below are the highlights for the proposed five-year plan: 
•
The primary focus is the completion of Central Park Playground inclusive of Fallen Heroes Memorial Grove and 
East Hillsdale Park. 
•
Additional funding is being recommended for project #610023 Sports Field Conversion - Synthetic Turf which will 
fund the renovation of existing synthetic turf fields as artificial turf has a useful life of around 8-10 years. 
•
Additional funding is being recommended for project #610027 Central Park to complete Phase 1 of the Master 
Plan, which includes playground renovation, including a new bathroom facility and picnic area. 
•
Further out and as funding allows, staff has tentatively programmed funds for upgrades to playgrounds and 
surrounding areas given their age and current condition. Priorities continue to be Sunnybrae, Shoreview, 
Washington, Los Prados, West Hillsdale and King. All of these projects have exceeded (or will shortly exceed) their 
20-year life cycle. 
The projects that we will be funding with In Lieu Fees are designated as Funding Source “ILF”. In addition, there are 
numerous projects that we do not have funds for however the City acknowledges that they should be addressed if any

the summer of 2013 at the Poplar Creek Golf Course and protect

roughs from two water wells on the course.  One of these wells 
collapsed in 2013, leaving no back up if production from the 
second well is interrupted. An inability to irrigate for even a short 
period of time can jeopardize the economic health of the golf 
course fund. This project will drill a new well to replace the one 
that collapsed.
