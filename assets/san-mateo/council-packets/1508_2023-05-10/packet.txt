2. Climate Action Plan – Annual Progress Report 
Andrea Chow, Sustainability Analyst, provided a presentation to the Commission to receive a progress report on 
the implementation of the Climate Action Plan. 
 
Commissioners asked questions of staff.  
 
Public Comment.  Kay Funk and Brian Thurston spoke to the item. 
 
There was no motion for this item, but commissioners asked for updates about the electric vehicles in the city 
fleet; updates on the use of methane being compressed into renewable gas; continued outreach on reducing on-
road transportation emissions; clarification on why solid waste emissions had a 22% increase; coordination with 
other cities/counties on outreach and education to elicit behavioral change; suggestion for Public Works to be 
present at next annual Climate Action Plan update. (Ms. Kleinbaum will reach out to Public Works)

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENT FROM COMMISSIONER AND STAFF 
• Andrea Chow shared activities scheduled for Earth Day, which kicked off on April 1 with a Fixit Clinic 
at San Mateo Library; on April 8 there were gardening workshops at the San Mateo Arboretum; on April 
22 at 10:30 AM the Zero Waste Chef will Make Soup Not Waste on an induction cooktop followed by a 
resource fair with sustainability partners; at 2:00 PM there will be a Visible Mending Workshop for 
Youth and at 3:30 PM a Visible Mending Workshop for Adults; on May 9 there will be a workshop for 
homeowners to learn how to Electrify Your Home and Save Money; on May 13 there will be a Shred and 
E-Scrap Event at Beresford Park from 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM and also on May 13 there will be a Compost, 
Plant, and Seed Swap from 10:00 AM – 1:00 PM at the Corporation Yard; on May 20 at 10:30 AM the 
library will host a Vegetable Gardening seminar and the Grand Opening for the Seed Library. More 
information is available on the city’s website.
