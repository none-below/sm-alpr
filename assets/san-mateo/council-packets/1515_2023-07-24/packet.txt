CITY OF SAN MATEO 
                      LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
                                 June 26, 2023 
 
 Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Austin Volz, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:30 
p.m. 
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Mr. Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board members Austin Volz, Sheila 
Canzian, Wendy McCall and Richard Wysong.  Vice President Shaylona Wheeler was not 
able to attend. 
 
3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION  
City Librarian James Moore requested to pull the statistics for further discussion.  Mr. 
Moore said the visitor count is still down compared to pre-pandemic.  He will bring pre-
pandemic program statistics to the next meeting on July 24, 2023.  Motion to approve the 
consent calendar was made by Mr. Volz, seconded by Ms. Canzian, and the motion 
passed 4-0. 
 
4. PUBLIC COMMENT 
Paul Vaughn, Librarian II with the San Mateo Public Library (SMPL), stated that contract 
negotiations are currently in progress with the City of San Mateo.  He stated that the pay 
rate for SMPL librarians are the lowest on the Bay Area Peninsula, and some 
administration or entry level positions have a higher starting pay rate than a librarian at 
the top pay step.  Mr. Vaughn said the morale is low due to the low pay rate.  Mary Kwan, 
Senior Library Assistant with SMPL, asked the Library Board of Trustees for help in 
speaking to the City Council.  She stated her concerns for SMPL to be able to attract or 
retain quality staff due to the low pay.  Ms. Kwan shared that she grew up in San Mateo 
and has worked for SMPL for 30 years, starting as a library page.  Ms. Kwan stated the 
staff at SMPL are dedicated and continued to serve the community throughout the 
pandemic.  Eric Groth, Librarian II with SMPL, stated that he has worked for SMPL for 17 
years and that the morale is currently the lowest that he has ever seen it.  He said the 
City of San Mateo is offering the librarians a 3% raise over three years, and SMPL is not 
attracting the best candidates due to the low pay. 
 
5. NEW BUSINESS 
A. Welcome to New Library Trustee: Mr. Moore and the Library Board of Trustees 
welcomed new Trustee Richard Wysong.  Mr. Wysong shared that his first job as a 
teenager was at a library.  He also previously served on the SMPL Library Board of 
Trustees from 2007 to 2011, and his daughters have volunteered for SMPL. 
B. Summary of San Mateo Celebrates! Activities During National Library Week: Sarah 
Meier-Heredia, Development Officer of the San Mateo Public Library Foundation 
(SMPLF), presented a summary of the San Mateo Celebrates! activities held during 
National Library Week, April 23-29.  Previously, the big annual SMPLF Spring 
fundraiser was the Author’s Gala.  This new citywide culture and community festive 
San Mateo Celebrates! was launched this year as a 5-year plan to bring culture and 
community to the library and to bring the library out into the community.

Throughout the week, nine events were held at the Main Library, the Hillsdale & 
Marina branches, Central Park, College of San Mateo (CSM) theater, CSM Farmers’ 
Market, and on B Street in downtown San Mateo.  San Mateo Celebrates! will be 
celebrating SMPLF’s 30th anniversary in 2024 and the Main Library’s 20th anniversary 
in 2026.  By 2028, the Foundation’s potential fundraising goals for the Main Library 
include a museum-like lobby experience with a coffee shop, merchandise, and 
exhibits, an improved first floor art gallery experience, and professional grade sound, 
stage, and screens.  For the Marina and Hillsdale branches, the goal is to add 
community meeting rooms. 
C. San Mateo Public Library Strategic Planning Process: City Librarian James Moore and 
the Library Board of Trustees reviewed and discussed ideas from strategic plans of 
successful organizations to glean useful insights in preparation for the library’s 
impending strategic planning process.  Mr. Moore shared that the in-person visitor 
count has been down since the pandemic and he was concerned that the library could 
be at an inflection point.  He thinks the strategic planning process should focus on 
identifying good reasons for patrons to visit the library while still taking advantage of 
our remote offerings.  Some ideas that were suggested included more cultural 
offerings, serving as a center for democratic engagement, computer literacy programs, 
hands-on learning opportunities possibly with volunteer teachers, civic discourse 
programs including a civics class, and sustainability events.  Discussion ensued 
regarding effective methods to gather community and staff input regarding strategic 
planning, including connecting with community members in areas of San Mateo that 
are not close to the three SMPL locations.  When consulting applicants are considered, 
Mr. Moore would like to include one applicant that is not a library specialist.  The 
strategic planning process will officially begin toward the end of this year or at the 
beginning of next year. 
 
6. REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS 
 
A.  City Librarian James Moore: 
• 
SMPL’s new seed library offers free vegetable, herb, and California native flower 
seeds to San Mateo and Hillsborough residents.  There is a self-serve seed cabinet 
on the 2nd floor at the Main Library.  Mini-seed libraries are available at the Marina 
and Hillsdale branches.  Also, there is information on how to garden on SMPL’s 
website. 
• 
Trivia Night was held this month and it was a huge success!  The sign-up list was 
full within 24 hours after it opened, and 54 people attended. 
• 
The San Mateo County Genealogical Society (SMCGS) completed their move to the 
2nd floor at the Main Library.  There will be an open house event in August and 
Library Board and Foundation members are invited.  In addition, the SMCGS 
president would like to speak at the next Library Board meeting.  SMCGS 
volunteers will be available to provide 1:1 assistance to patrons searching their 
families’ histories.  They will also work with staff on creating more genealogy 
related programs at the library.  Lastly, they have committed to learning more to 
improve their assistance to Black Americans whose genealogical information can 
be challenging to trace due to slavery. 
B. Board Members: 
• 
Mr. Volz attended the Young Chambers Musicians concert in the Oak Room at the 
Main Library. 
• 
Ms. Canzian applied for a new library card with a great design.

recently moved all their genealogical research materials from the Canada College Library to the San Mateo Public Library.

provide enriching programs of researching personal family histories.
