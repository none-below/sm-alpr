Finance Director Rich Lee gave a presentation outlining the independent accountant's report on agreed-upon 
procedures applied to schedule of Measure S revenues and expenditures for the year ended June 30, 2022. Lee 
reviewed Measure S revenues in the adopted 2021-22 budget and summarized the amended totals, actuals, and 
budget variance. The 2021-22 ending fund balance equates to $8.8 with $3.7 designated for Capital Improvement 
Projects and $5.1 million in unrestricted funding that can be appropriate to purposes specific to Measure S.

approved to be extended by San Mateo voters in 2015. The adopted 2022-23 budget assumed $7.2 million in total 
revenues, of which $7.0 million is for the Measure S transactions and use tax. Based on April year-to-date total, revenues 
are on track to end the fiscal year in alignment with the adopted budget.

Based on April 2023 year-to-date trends, all budgeted items except for transfers out to CIP are expected to be within the 
budget. Transfers out to CIP are projected to be $3.0 million less than budgeted. The City’s practice is to commit 
unexpended transfers out to CIP at year end, as these represent funds committed for capital improvement projects that 
either a) are associated with a signed agreement with a contractor and/or b) represents funds that have been committed 
to deliver capital improvements or infrastructure. Commitment of fund balance prevents over-appropriation of funds in 
the subsequent fiscal year, as committed fund balance is restricted for specific purposes, and any new budget 
appropriations come from unrestricted fund balance or current resources (i.e. new revenues). 
As reflected in Table 3, the projected unexpended transfers out of $3.0 million will be committed at the end of the fiscal 
year, and the budget appropriation will be rolled over to fiscal year 2023-24 so that the funds can be expended and the 
Measure S-funded CIP projects can be completed.

BUDGET IMPACT:
Based on April 2023 year-to-date trends, Measure S is expected to end FY 2022-23 with $7.2 million in revenues and $5.1 
million in expenditures. Unexpended CIP funding of $3.0 million will be committed at the end of the fiscal year, and the 
budget appropriations will be rolled over to FY 2023-24.
The proposed 2023-24 budget for Measure projects $7.2 million in revenues, and $8.9 million in expenditures, which are 
comprised of $1.8 million in personnel expenditures, $0.2 million in operating expenditures, $0.1 million in indirect and 
overhead charges, and $6.8 million in transfers out. 
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This Measure S 2022-23 mid-year budget update and proposed 2023-24 budget is not a project subject to CEQA, because it 
is a government fiscal activity which does not involve any commitment to any specific project which may result in a 
potentially significant physical impact on the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(4).)
