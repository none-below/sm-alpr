California State Parks Pass Grant Presentation - Informational
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive presentation regarding the California State Parks Pass Grant. Presented by Johnathan Jung, Supervising Library 
Assistant and Rita Morin, Supervising Librarian.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo Library Services recently received grant funds from the State of California Park Pass program. A 
library card holder may borrow a California State Libraries Park Pass by placing a hold with their library card through the 
catalog or dropping by any San Mateo Public Library and asking the Accounts Desk if there is a Quick Pick pass available to 
borrow. The pass entitles library card holders to a free vehicle day-use entry for one passenger vehicle with capacity of 
nine people or less or one highway licensed motorcycle at over 200 participating state parks. Checkout is for 3 weeks with 
no renewals. The California State Library Parks Pass is a partnership between California State Parks and the California State 
Library.

SUBJECT:
Memorial Plaques for Deceased Dogs – Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on Memorial Plaques for Deceased Dogs and provide direction to staff as to the interest in moving 
forward with this previously approved program.
BACKGROUND:
On December 7, 2016, the Dog Park Memorial Plaque Program Policy was approved by the Park and Recreation 
Commission. Carol Ann Sherman, a local resident, brought the idea forward to create a memorial for owners of deceased 
dogs at Seal Point Dog Park, John Lee Dog Park at Bayside/Joinville Park, Los Prados Dog Park and Laurie Meadows Dog 
Park. Ms. Sherman started the Wolf Plaque Memorial Company to provide this service.  The plaques would consist of the 
name of the dog, a photo of the dog, and the birth and death date of the dog.  The cost of each plaque would be $300, and 
the City of San Mateo would receive 15% of each memorial plaque sold.  The contractor would be responsible for sale, 
installation, and maintenance of the plaques.  
 
At the March 1, 2023, Park and Recreation Commission meeting, the commissioners requested an update be brought back 
at a future meeting on the status of the program.  Although this program was approved in 2016, no plaques were ever 
installed, and staff have not received any inquires in the past year from members of the community regarding this 
program.  Additionally, the contractor that was approved to provide this service is no longer in business.  
 
Staff is seeking direction from the Commission as to the interest in moving forward with this program and pursuing 
requests for proposals since the contractor that was approved to provide this service is no longer in business.

There is no budgetary impact as this is an informational update only.
