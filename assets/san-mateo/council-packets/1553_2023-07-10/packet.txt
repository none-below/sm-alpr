SUBJECT:
Discussion ideas with artist regarding Racial Equity Art Project design revision.
RECOMMENDATION:
Civic Arts Committee to provide direction to artist regarding the expansion and additional design revisions for the Racial 
Equity Art Project final design.
BACKGROUND:
On June 20, 2023, the City Council approved selection of artist Rafael Blanco and his design “Cultural Pattern” for the 
Racial Equity Art Project. The Council directed the Civic Arts Committee (CAC) to work with the artist to revise the design 
and approve the final design. 
Mr. Blanco has received additional feedback from the City Council together with the CAC and community. He has agreed to 
expand the image up to 240 feet in each block of the Pedestrian Mall on B Street. The expansion of the design will allow 
for inclusion of other culture communities within San Mateo, a common request from all sources of feedback. The 
installation is scheduled for October 9th -28th:
•
Block A, 2nd to 3rd Aves – October 9th  – 18th  (10 days of implementation)
•
Block B, 1st to 2nd Aves – October 19th – 28th (10 days of implementation)
Attachment 1 is a list with a wide variety of larger communities that make up San Mateo. This list is informational 
inspiration for both Mr. Blanco and the CAC in discussion of the design ideas, it is not the intention that every culture will 
be represented through the art project.
The next CAC meeting is scheduled for September 11, 2023 and is tentatively planned for approval of the final design. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
This is a discussion for direction, there is no budget impact.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This Racial Equity Art Project discussion is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative 
activity that will not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 
15378(b)(5).)
