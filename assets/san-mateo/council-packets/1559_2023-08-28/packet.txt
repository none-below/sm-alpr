CITY OF SAN MATEO
                      LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
                                 July 24, 2023
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Austin Volz, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:31 p.m.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Mr. Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Austin Volz, Sheila 
Canzian, and Richard Wysong.  Trustee Wendy McCall appeared remotely by conference call.  
Vice President Shaylona Wheeler was not present.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
City Librarian James Moore requested to pull the program statistics for further discussion.  Mr. 
Moore said the number of programs and attendees are still down compared to pre-pandemic, 
especially programs for children.  Mr. Moore pointed out that the Children’s Division has been 
extremely short-staffed with only one full-time librarian until very recently when three new full-
time librarians were hired.  Mr. Moore expects that the Children’s Division will now be able to 
offer more programming. Trustees had questions and offered feedback on ideas to boost 
attendance.  Motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. Canzian, seconded by 
Mr. Wysong, and the motion passed 4-0.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
NEW BUSINESS
A. Calendar of Library Board Meetings FY 2023-2024: The Library Board discussed the 
calendar of meetings in fiscal year 2023-2024.  It was agreed to not have a meeting in 
December and reschedule the meeting in May to the 4th Tuesday of the month because the 
4th Monday falls on Memorial Day.  All other meeting dates will remain on the 4th Monday of 
each month.  A motion was made by Ms. Canzian to adopt this calendar, it was seconded 
by Ms. McCall, and the motion passed 4-0.
B. Presentation on the Partnership between the Library and the San Mateo County 
Genealogical Society (SMCGS): SMCGS President Kara Rosenberg presented information 
on the history of SMCGS, which was founded and incorporated as a non-profit organization 
in 1982.  The goals of SMCGS are to support a community of people interested in 
genealogy, provide educational opportunities, preserve and index San Mateo County 
records, and provide a variety of genealogical resources covering San Mateo County, the 
U.S., and the world.  SMCGS is an all-volunteer organization with over 200 members.  The 
SMCGS library has recently moved from Cãnada College to the San Mateo Public Library 
(SMPL), and Ms. Rosenberg hopes to bring more people to SMPL.  Future goals include 
expanding the collection to include resources that mirror the diversity of San Mateo County, 
work with SMPL to provide educational programs, and staff the genealogy section three 
days per week for three hours each day with volunteers who will offer free assistance to 
patrons.  Supervising Librarian Rita Morin informed the Board that SMPL has a subscription 
to Ancestry that patrons can access when using a computer at SMPL.  In addition, Ms. 
Morin informed the Board that an official invitation will be sent to them for the grand opening

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A.  City Librarian James Moore:
•
Recently, Supervising Librarian Rita Morin and Supervising Library Assistant Jon Jung 
did a presentation to the Senior Citizens Commission at the San Mateo Senior Center 
regarding the Parks Pass Program.  SMPL currently has thirteen state parks passes.  
Recently, Supervising Library Assistant Jon Jung applied for a California State Library 
Parks Pass program grant and it was granted for $44,634.  The California State Library 
Parks Pass program is a partnership between the California State Parks and the 
California State Library, supported with funds from the State of California.  SMPL will 
create Parks Kits which will help increase awareness of local parks and aspects of 
preparation and safety when visiting.  Educational programs will be planned to 
strengthen our community’s knowledge of our beautiful native flora and fauna, and to 
build a stronger connection between our community and local resources.  Talks are in 
progress with the City of San Mateo Parks & Recreation Department and San Mateo 
County Parks for possible collaboration on programming.
•
There was a proposal from an artist who does large-scale dreamscape photos but it is 
on hold for now because SMPL’s Art Exhibit Policy has been temporarily suspended 
due to concerns raised about how the program is run and how the art work was 
selected.  It is anticipated that the program will be back after the procedures are 
reviewed.
•
SMPL’s outreach van “Mateo” is irreparable.  A Ford E-Transit van has been identified 
as a suitable replacement vehicle and the funds are available to purchase it, but Ford 
has advised that it could take 14 months to receive it.
•
Mr. Moore met with design consultants from two companies per Ms. McCall’s referral, 
and they will both submit bids.  Two other bids from other companies were previously 
received.  Mr. Moore would like to choose one from these four companies.
B. Board Members:
•
Mr. Volz did not have any updates but asked if there is a request for any future agenda 
items.  Mr. Moore said a future presentation can be scheduled on the Parks Pass 
Program.
•
Ms. McCall will reach out to Peninsula Open Space Trust to inquire about a possible 
collaboration with the Library for programming.  In addition, Ms. McCall shared that she 
recently visited airports in Detroit and Cincinnati that had library areas that allowed 
visitors to select and read books while waiting.
•
Mr. Wysong toured many libraries during his recent trip to Europe.  He will try and 
prepare a report next month for the Library’s strategic planning process.
ADJOURNMENT
Mr. Volz adjourned the meeting at 7:01 p.m.
Next Meeting:  Monday, August 28, 2023 at 5:30 p.m., Cedar Room, Main Library

Santa Monica Public Library is a
learning, cultural, and community
hub. The Library supports literacy,
life-long learning, civic
engagement, equity, diversity,
inclusivity, and cultural
awareness. 
 
With approval from the Library
Board, and City Council, the
Library embarked on a strategic
planning process. Four strategic
focus areas were distilled from
community and staff feedback,
best practices, emerging trend
information, and City data. Over
the next two fiscal years, the
Library will accomplish a set of
goals in each of the four focus
areas; Vibrant Learning Center,
Wellbeing Cultivator, Dynamic
Third Place, and Community &
Cultural Connector.

Santa Monica Public Library maintains quality core services; collections,
research support, local history resources, and literacy. The Library offers the

success. The Library provides a wide array of free programs, classes, and
services, and embraces Santa Monica’s commitment to building a community
of readers. The Library's collection includes materials in Amharic, Chinese,
English, Farsi, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Russian, and Spanish.

The new Strategic Plan for the Santa Cruz Public Libraries is the culmination of a yearlong 
examination into the following three critical areas: 
1. Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)
2. The Santa Cruz County Community
3. Library Services
The Plan aspires to ensure that the Library remains adaptive and continues to provide 
outstanding service that meets the community’s needs. The goals evolved from community 
engagement and included surveys, organized community conversations, and workshops with 
the Library’s partners, staff, and senior management. 
Methodology 
The Santa Cruz Public Libraries began the Strategic Planning process in August 2021. The 
Library convened a team of eleven staff to lead the effort. The group reviewed the existing 
strategic plan for the Santa Cruz Public Libraries and those of other organizations. The Library 
collected in-person and online survey questionnaires, then developed a list of seventeen 
prioritized groups and surveyed and interviewed members of those groups using the Harwood 
approach. Applied Survey Research coded and analyzed the community data. 
The prioritized groups included: 
City Staff from Capitola, Santa Cruz, and Scotts Valley 
County Government Staff 
Educators 
Families with young children 
Frequent library users and Friends of the Santa Cruz Public Libraries 
Infrequent or nonusers of the Library 
Library Advisory Commission, 
Library partners and their clients 
Library staff 
Members and partners of the BIPOC community 
Members and partners of the disabled community 
Members and partners of the Spanish-speaking community

At the Library’s All Staff Training Day in October 2021, the Library conducted a strengths, 
opportunities, aspirations, and results (SOAR) exercise with staff. Former San Jose Public 
Library Director Jane Light led the exercise. Staff also engaged in activities that helped define 
the organization’s values. In addition to the staff exercises, the Library’s internal Equity Team 
began developing a Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Plan in 2020 with the goal of incorporating 
its work into the Strategic Plan.  Following that work, a Community Partner Dialogue took place 
in February 2022 to gather additional input from the following groups: The Friends of the Library, 
the Library Advisory Commission, the County Office of Education, Cabrillo College, Santa Cruz 
City Schools, the Chamber of Commerce, Community Foundation/Rise Together, the Diversity 
Center, Monterey Bay Economic Partnership, NAACP, United Way, and the Santa Cruz County 
Sheriff’s Office. 
 
After completing the Community Partner Dialogue event, Library Director Yolande Wilburn led 
the Library’s senior management team through a workshop to analyze the information gathered 
from the staff and community input and the work achieved by the Library’s Equity Team. Those 
workshops resulted in the final goals presented in this report. The Library then sought further 
input on the Plan from the Library Advisory Commission (LAC) and the Library Joint Powers 
Authority (LJPA) before seeking final approval from the LJPA.

A New Strategic Plan

Chief Librarian Tom Fay is leading the development of a new strategic plan for The Seattle Public Library in 2023 that will reflect the
current and anticipated needs and interests of the community. The plan is expected to identify near-term goals and objectives for the next
1-3 years and longer-term goals and objectives for the next 4-10 years.

The development of a new strategic plan began in 2022 with Fay conducting listening sessions to hear directly from patrons. The Library
then began working with a strategic foresight consultant to identify the plausible future scenarios our system and our community may
face, and the future scenarios we prefer to work toward.

Because community engagement is at the heart of our work, the Library's new strategic plan will be informed by patrons, community
partners, Library staff and other stakeholders.

About Our Strategic Direction

The Library's Strategic Direction is designed to help us achieve positive outcomes and lasting impacts in individual lives and throughout
our community, and to ensure we have the right tools, resources and organizational structure to support that work.

SUBJECT:
Presentation on Library's Latest Parks Pass Grant Developments
RECOMMENDATION:
There will be a presentation on the latest Parks Pass grant developments.
BACKGROUND:
Several months ago the San Mateo Public Library began circulating free passes to all of the California State Parks. Since 
then, the program has expanded to make more passes available to library patrons. A library supervisor wrote a grant 
proposal for additional funds to support more inclusive access to California State Parks by making items available that are 
needed for enjoying the outdoors to patrons who might otherwise not possess these items. In addition, the grant funds 
will be used to help educate residents on how best to safely access their California State Parks.
