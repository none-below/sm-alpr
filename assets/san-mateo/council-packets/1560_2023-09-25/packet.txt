CITY OF SAN MATEO
                      LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
                                 August 28, 2023
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Austin Volz, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:30 p.m.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Mr. Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Austin Volz, Shaylona 
Wheeler, Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall and Richard Wysong.  
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. Canzian, it was seconded by Ms. 
McCall, and the motion passed 5-0.  Deputy City Librarian Rukshana Singh said that the 
statistics are trending upward but are not yet at the pre-pandemic levels, with the exception of 
the circulation of e-books which has gone up quite a bit.  Ms. McCall pointed out that the 
number of library cards that have been issued has gone up.  Supervising Library Assistant 
Jonathan Jung speculated that this could be the result of more people moving to the area.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
OLD BUSINESS
Continued Review of Library Five-Year Strategic Plan Best-Practice Examples:  Ms. Wheeler 
shared five strategic plan examples from other organizations including Santa Clara Public 
Library, Santa Monica Public Library, Santa Cruz Public Library, The Carey School, and Seattle 
Public Library.  The Board discussed these examples and provided feedback.  Ms. Singh said 
that City Librarian James Moore has been meeting with consultants and a consultant should be 
chosen before the end of this year.  
6.
NEW BUSINESS
Presentation on Library’s Latest Parks Pass Grant Developments: Supervising Library 
Assistant Jonathan Jung and Supervising Librarian Rita Morin provided information on the 
California State Parks Pass and the California State Parks Pass Grant.  Mr. Jung provided an 
overview of the California State Parks Pass which became available to every public library in 
California in February 2022.  Every public library-card holder can check out a free non-
renewable vehicle day-use pass for 3 weeks to over 200 participating state park units.  In 
January 2023, Mr. Jung applied for a California State Parks Pass Grant for San Mateo Public 
Library (SMPL), and a grant for $40,000 was awarded in April 2023.  The goals of this grant 
program are to generate positive impacts in the areas of health, natural resource stewardship, 
and historical and cultural connections with an equity lens.  These funds will be used for 
supplies and programming.  Staff have been tagging and labeling backpack kits that contain 
first aid supplies, water bottles, and other items.  Patrons will also be able to check out 
individual items and tutorials will be provided. 
Ms. Morin said there is a program planned for seniors to take a trip to the Pacifica State Beach 
and the Pacifica Senior Center on October 13, 2023.  The Library is partnering with the City of 
San Mateo Senior Center for this event, and Pacifica State Beach park rangers will be of 
assistance as well.  If all goes well with this senior trip, Ms. Morin said another senior trip may

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A.  Deputy City Librarian Rukshana Singh:
•
City Librarian James Moore has been searching for a Ford E-Transit van to purchase to 
replace our current outreach vehicle “Mateo” that is irreparable.  We are looking for a 
van that is slightly smaller than Mateo so it will fit in our parking garage and be easier to 
access.  It appears there will be a long wait to receive the vehicle once it is ordered.
•
The Marina Library foundation repair project is in the pre-design phase until late fall, the 
design phase will run through spring 2024, the bid and award phase will take place in 
summer 2024, and the construction phase will begin in fall of 2024.  The library will 
have to close for the construction phase of the project.  Ms. Singh met with staff last 
week to start brainstorming ideas we might pursue if we can get additional funding for 
branch improvements.  Their ideas included additional programming space, creating 
space for teens, improved seating, and moving some collections around.
•
The Genealogy Open House was held on Saturday, August 26.  Many of the volunteers 
were present.  Thank you to Trustees Ms. Canzian and Ms. McCall and Foundation Co-
President Alexandra Gillen for attending.  We are the only public library on the 
peninsula with a genealogy collection and services.  The 1-on-1 appointments have 
started and are held on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays.  The Library Board of 
Trustees are invited to two upcoming genealogy programs in October:
o
October 7 from 2-4pm: Digitize and Document Your Family History with Ron 
Chan, Founder and Executive Director of the Bay Area Chinese Genealogy 
Group
o
October 28: Genealogy Resource Fair
•
We have been busy with the end of summer activities.  Summer learning wraps up on 
August 31, and the new Children’s Services librarians are busy planning for the end of 
fall.  One of the librarians recently created several 3D print models that children can 
choose from and print out from Friday-Sunday.  In honor of National Dog Month, the 
current models are different dog breeds.  The last Storytime in the Park was today and 
over 100 people attended.  Some of our staff with musical skills have been attending 
and singing songs along with the Children’s librarians.  
B. Board Members:
•
After helping a friend move, Mr. Volz was able to donate a large number of books to 
SMPL.
•
Ms. Canzian attended the Genealogy Open House and intends to use the services that 
will be offered.
•
Ms. McCall attended the Genealogy Open House, and chatted with new Children’s 
Librarian Erica about some upcoming projects she has planned.  Ms. McCall also 
shared that her husband obtained a new library card.
•
Ms. Wheeler said that the school year is starting on August 29 and she anticipates that 
she will be a frequent visitor to the Children’s Library at SMPL.
