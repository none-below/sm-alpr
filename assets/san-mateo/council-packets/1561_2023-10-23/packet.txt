Strategic Planning for Library: As part of the strategic planning discussion for San Mateo 
Public Library (SMPL), Mr. Wysong shared pictures of Scandinavian libraries from a recent 
trip.  He found that a common theme among their libraries was to use multiple methods to 
draw community members into the libraries.  Some of these methods included active cafés 
at the front of each library, periodicals and casual reading spaces placed close to the 
entrances, mixed use spaces for performances, engaging museum-like spaces with rotating 
exhibits, and spaces and activities for tweens and teens.  City Librarian James Moore and 
the Board of Trustees discussed ideas on what offerings would draw more people to SMPL.  
Mr. Moore said that he thinks in the foreseeable future there will be more space available 
due to less community demand for public computer use.  He estimates SMPL may only 
need to keep about 30% of the current computer inventory.  Mr. Moore would like to make 
the lobby more of a destination as well.  
Mr. Moore informed the Board that a consultant has not been selected yet.  Mr. Moore has 
met with a couple of consulting companies that are reputable but very expensive.  He spoke 
with the Redwood City Public Library’s director and learned that they created their strategic 
plan in-house while contracting out the arrangement of the community input gathering 
meetings.  The Board provided feedback and recommendations including contacting the 
Berkeley School of Business, Stanford Business School and Stanford Design School.  In 
addition, San Mateo Public Library Foundation (SMPLF) Secretary Martha Nora 
recommended the Stanford Business School.  Mr. Moore said that SMPL’s current strategic 
plan will last until the end of 2023 and he is comfortable exploring options and taking more 
time to make a well-considered decision.

Introduction of New City Manager Alex Khojikian: City Librarian James Moore introduced 
City of San Mateo’s new City Manager Alex Khojikian to the Library Board of Trustees.  Mr. 
Khojikian shared some background information about himself with the Board.  Prior to this, 
he was the Assistant City Manager for the City of Redwood City, and he has a total of 21 
years of experience managing city services and community relations for five different cities.  
Mr. Khojikian believes that libraries build communities.  He worked extensively on the new 
Half Moon Bay Library project when he was Deputy City Manager for the City of Half Moon 
Bay.  Mr. Khojikian said that the City of San Mateo has a great reputation with dedicated 
staff.  He sees the 11% staff vacancy rate as the biggest challenge that needs to be 
addressed.  Trustees asked questions and provided feedback, including that SMPL 
employees have sent emails and have spoken at a previous Library Board meeting about 
what they view as a discrepancy in pay among San Mateo City employees and their 
concerns that library employees are receiving lower salaries by comparison.  Mr. Khojikian 
said the employee contract negotiations should resume in mid-October and employees can 
contact him if they have any concerns or questions.

Art Exhibit Policy 
[Revised by the Library Board of Trustees, Feb. 28, 2011] 
 
Introduction 
The Art Exhibit policy is intended to provide guidelines for potential exhibitors and Library staff 
and outlines which exhibit space is available at the San Mateo Public Library. 
 
Space 
The primary public exhibit space for art within the San Mateo Public Library system is the Art 
Gallery at the Main Library at 55 West Third Avenue. The gallery space, located on the first 
floor.  Gallery hours will be the same as the Library’s hours.  
 
Requests display to 3-dimensional materials and to display materials in spaces other than the 
designated gallery space will be reviewed and approved by the City Librarian or his/her 
designee. 
 
Selection Process 
Twice a year the Library convenes the Art Selection Committee that includes representatives 
from the community and library staff.  The Art Selection Committee then extends an invitation to 
artists to bring their work in for consideration for gallery bookings. On the selected review dates, 
artists bring in their artwork, photos, or slides to be evaluated by the committee.  Review dates 
are scheduled in the fall for exhibits to be scheduled January through June, and in the spring for 
exhibits to be scheduled July through December. 
 
The Committee seeks art that represents the diversity of our community and varied artistic styles 
and mediums. Potential exhibitors must have a minimum of twelve (12) pieces available to hang 
for the duration of the one-month exhibit period. Art pieces may be available for sale, but that is 
not a requirement for exhibiting.  
 
Exhibit space is available to groups and individuals on an equitable basis. The Committee will 
consider a wide range of artistic expression in deciding on potential exhibitors. However, in 
deciding the suitability of any work, the Committee is particularly mindful that the display area 
is used by all segments of the community and all age groups. Exhibits may not include defaming 
or obscene materials as defined by the U.S. Supreme Court, or material which would lead to 
breach of peace or which advocates the violation of State criminal laws. It is not the intent that 
the exhibit space be used for advertising for commercial enterprises or political candidates. The 
views in the works exhibited are those of the artists and are not necessarily those of the Selection 
Committee or the Library. The City Librarian reserves final approval over any recommendation 
of the Selection Committee 
 
Artists who are interested in participating may submit an “Art Gallery Exhibition Request” to be 
notified when the next review will be scheduled.  Applications will be processed on a first 
come/first served basis, with scheduling priority given to local exhibitors and exhibitors who 
have not previously presented an exhibit.

Exhibition Procedures 
Within two weeks of being notified that a submission was accepted for exhibition, the artist must 
contact the Exhibit Coordinator to arrange the following: (a) possible exhibit dates (b) final 
selection of works to be exhibited and (c) other deadlines, especially for promotional purposes.  
 
All art must be suitably framed, with hanging apparatus (screw eyes and wire).  Work that is 
fragile in nature or whose framing or display arrangement is of questionable durability may be 
rejected. Framed art work must be installed on the gallery’s hanging system, no nails or hooks or 
mounting tape may be used.    
 
Interested artists may arrange for a reception to be held at the library, at their own expense, 
provided appropriate space is available and the event is open to the public. The Meeting Room 
Rental Fee will be waived for public receptions. If a private reception is desired the Meeting 
Room Rental Fee will apply. 
 
Participating artists are encouraged to promote their exhibit through invitations and 
announcements. The Library will arrange for a press release to inform the general community 
about the exhibit. Permission to photograph and reproduce any work accepted in the exhibition 
for publicity purposes is considered granted unless otherwise stated in writing. For each work, 
the artist is to provide a display card or title label indicating the name of the work, artist’s name 
and medium. Prices will not be displayed on individual works, but will be available as part of the 
exhibit information available to the public at the library’s service desks. All other promotional 
materials must be approved by the Exhibit Coordinator. 
 
At least two weeks prior to installation, artists are responsible for submitting a completed “Art 
Gallery Exhibitor Agreement Form” and the “Exhibitor’s Inventory” listing all works that are to 
be shown, together with the selling price (or, “NFS”, if not for sale). The listing must include the 
artist’s name, telephone number and instructions for contacting the artist to arrange for the 
purchase of any artwork. Artists are also requested to include a brief biography and artist’s 
statement.  
 
The exhibitor is responsible for the installation and dismantling of the exhibit as scheduled with 
the Exhibit coordinator. The exhibitor assumes total responsibility for the transportation of all 
work to and from the Library. If the Library must dismantle an exhibit because it is not removed 
as scheduled, the Library is not responsible for any damages and the exhibitor will be billed for 
staff time. The Library does not provide staff to serve as attendant’s during an exhibit. The 
provision of an attendant (if desired) is the responsibility of the exhibitor.  
  
Liability 
The exhibitor must complete and submit the Art Gallery Exhibitor Agreement Form that 
unconditionally releases the City of San Mateo, its elected and appointed officials and 
employees, and the Library Art Selection Committee from any liability in connection with the 
exhibit. The City does not accept liability for damages, losses or theft to the materials exhibited. 
The exhibit space must be left in satisfactory condition. Exhibitors using the space assume 
liability for damages to City property resulting from said usage, as assessed by the City. The 
Library will dispose of abandoned exhibit materials after reasonable effort has been made to 
contact the exhibitor.

Sales and Commissions 
Works of art may be offered for sale, with prices established by the artist. The City will not serve 
as sales agent, but will provide interested persons with a list of prices and contact information. 
The artist is responsible for conducting the sale of any work directly with the purchaser. Works 
that are not available for purchase must be designated “NFS” (Not for Sale). Works sold must 
remain on exhibit throughout the designated period.

Sales and Commissions

Works of art may be offered for sale, with prices established by the artist. The City will not serve
as sales agent; but will provide interested persons with a list of prices and contact information.
The artist is responsible for conducting the sale of any work directly with the purchaser. Works
that are not available for purchase must be designated “NFS” (Not for Sale). Works sold must
remain on exhibit throughout the designated period.
