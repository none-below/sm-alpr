May 28, 2024
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Prior to the start of this meeting, President Austin Volz notified City Librarian James Moore 
that he would be approximately 30 minutes late.  In the absence of Mr. Volz, Trustee Sheila 
Canzian called the meeting to order at 5:33 p.m. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant 
Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Sheila Canzian, 
Wendy McCall, and Richard Wysong.  Trustee Austin Volz was not present at roll call.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
Ms. Canzian requested that the word “Council” be corrected to “Board” in the paragraph 
under the consent calendar section on future agendas.  Mr. Moore advised the Board that 
the decrease in Wi-Fi usage listed on the statistics started impacting all Peninsula Library 
System libraries starting in February 2024.  Peninsula Libraries Automated Network is being 
consulted and Mr. Moore will let the Board know if there was any change that may have 
caused this decrease. A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. McCall, 
seconded by Mr. Wysong, and the motion passed 3-0.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
OLD BUSINESS
Recommended Updates to the Collection Development Policy:  Supervising Librarian Rita 
Morin presented an updated draft of the Collection Development Policy to the Board.  This 
draft included the recommendations the Board provided to Ms. Morin during the March 
Board meeting.  A motion was made to adopt the policy as presented by Mr. Wysong, 
seconded by Ms. McCall, and the motion passed 3-0.
6.
NEW BUSINESS
Recommended Updates to the San Mateo Main Library Meeting Room & Facilities Rental 
Policy: Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca presented a draft of the San Mateo 
Main Library Meeting Room & Facilities Rental Policy with some recommended updates.  
These recommended updates included adding that the necessary disclaimer is required on 
electronic promotional material in addition to printed promotional material, updated wording 
to make it clear that reservation requests will be considered in the order that they are 
received and according to priority use, a correction that the capacity of the Oak Room is 124 
people, and removing American Express as a form of payment that is accepted.
The Trustees asked questions and provided feedback.  Suggestions included adding that 
the necessary disclaimer should also be required on social media marketing, adding that the 
disclaimer is only required on events open to the general public, changing the word 
“preference” to “priority use” in the second paragraph, adding that in the event of multiple 
requests for the same date and time, reservations requests will be considered in the order

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A.  City Librarian James Moore:
•
There are not any new updates for the Marina Library repair project.  The 
construction work to repair the foundation was supposed to start in July 2024, but it 
has been delayed for one year.  Fortunately, the City of San Mateo has now

(SMPLF) planned in partnership with the Library during National Library Week.  
Deputy City Librarian Rukshana Singh was able to fill in for Mr. Moore as a speaker.

of Veterans from different branches and backgrounds who discussed their military 
service.  This panel included three officers from the San Mateo Police Department.  
Mr. Moore served as moderator for this event.  Lastly, Mr. Moore thanked Mr. 
Wysong for attending.

Library’s strategic planning process and meetings will be scheduled soon to gather 
feedback from community members and stakeholders.

the San Mateo Area Chamber of Commerce: Best Retail Book Store with the Friends 
of the Library Book Store at the Main Library, and Best Event Space with the Oak 
Community Meeting Room at the Main Library.

Meeting Room & Facilities Rental Policy 
[Policy revised by the Library Board – August 25, 2008 
Format revisions February 2014] 
Please print document & initial where indicated as acknowledgement of policy, whether or not it is directly applicable to your specific request.  Provide 
written signatures, digital signatures are not accepted. 
POLICY 
The San Mateo Public Library has a complement of meeting rooms that are available as a public service and may be rented 
at rates established in the fee schedule of the City of San Mateo. Preference Priority use of the Library’s meeting room calendar will 
be given to Library programs, use by other City departments, the Library Foundation and education based partners of the Library. 
The fact that a group is permitted use of a meeting room does not constitute an endorsement of the group’s policies or beliefs by 
the Library nor the City of San Mateo. 
A completed application form must be submitted to the Library Administration Office in person, by U.S. mail, or email to  
libraryreservations@cityofsanmateo.org or by fax (650-522-7801). The application must be signed by an authorized representative 
of the group who shall attend the meeting and be personally responsible for the conduct of the meeting and any damage to the 
Library. 
Each application will be reviewed and the contact person will receive confirmation either by email or U.S. mail. Applications 
must be submitted at least ten calendar days prior to the meeting date but not more than three months (90 days to the date of the 
request) in advance. Information about the room’s availability may be provided by phone (650-522-7802); however, applications  
will not be considered as confirmed until a signed application is submitted with full payment (at the corresponding fee rate) and 
confirmation is sent or provided by the Library staff. 
In order to ensure accessibility to as many community members as possible, the following restrictions will apply.  Use of 
meeting rooms will be limited to one meeting per month by an organization, unless otherwise authorized by the City Librarian. The 
Library advises that announcements or publicity for the meeting not be printed or distributed until written confirmation from the 
Library is received. 
  
(Initial) All promotional materials must be reviewed by the Library in advance of publication and distribution. Printed 
Printed and electronic materials, including social media, must include the following disclaimer: "The San Mateo Public Library 
facilities are being rented for this event. The viewpoints and materials presented at this event are not necessarily endorsed by 
the City of San Mateo or the San Mateo Public Library." Failure to comply with this requirement may result in cancellation of 
booking and forfeiture of all fees paid.  
 
TERMS and CONDITIONS 
 
1. Applicants must adhere to all of the Library’s meeting room regulations. Noncompliance will result in the loss of meeting room 
privileges. 
2. Reservations will be considered on a first come, first served basis. Simultaneous reservation requests submitted in person will 
receive priority over phone calls, faxes, email or mail. If there are multiple reservation requests for the same time, reservation 
requests will be considered in the order they are received during Administration Office business hours and processed 
according to priority use. 
3. Meetings may be open to the public or requested for private use. Private use functions will require that the group using the 
room declare such use at the time of submitting the application. 
4. Users of the meeting rooms must observe all of the Library’s regulations related to the facilities and accept financial 
responsibility for all damages to the Library, furniture and equipment. 
5. Meetings held during the Library’s hours of service must end at least 15 minutes before the Library closes. After hours use must 
be requested specifically on the application; and payment made at the time application is submitted. Fees for after-hours use 
are itemized on the Fees Schedule of the City of San Mateo. 
6. Refreshments may be served at the event if it is specified on the application form. The Library reserves the right to approve all 
caterers to be used by applicant. 
7. 
  
(Initial) The Library’s technology equipment and services may be requested by the applicant at the time of the 
application. In the event equipment malfunctions during use, it should be brought to the attention of Library staff. Applicants 
cannot attempt to repair malfunctioning equipment. All equipment must be turned off at the end of use. 
8. Attendance must not exceed the posted capacity of the meeting room being used: Oak Meeting Room (142 124); Laurel 
Meeting Room (36); Cedar Meeting Room (24); Computer Training Lab (26). Capacity levels for other areas of the Library 
can be confirmed at the time application is submitted. 
9. Room set up configuration should be requested at the time of the application, but not later than 5 business days before the

3. Overtime charges of twice the hourly rental rate will be assessed for events when the room is not completely vacated by the

4. The Library will attempt to accommodate requests to change the date and hours in the rental contract; however, the Library

approved at least 5 business days in advance of the event date. 
 
SET UP and CLEAN UP RESPONSIBILITIES 
1. Library staff will make an effort to set up tables and chairs to your specifications, but cannot always guarantee that this can be 
done. 
2. Setup requests for tables and chairs must be submitted at least 5 business days prior to the event. The Library must approve all 
set up requests. 
3.   
(Initial) Applicant will complete the Reservation Closing checklist and return to Library staff. 
4.   
(Initial) The applicant is responsible for clearing all tables and for placing all garbage in refuse bags/cans. The Library 
will supply plastic trash bags. 
5. All additional setups, signs or decorations are the responsibility of the applicant. The removal of these setups, signs or 
decorations is also the responsibility of the applicant. 
6. All decorations must be approved by the Library in advance and set up under Library supervision. Decorations must be attached 
with blue masking tape only, NOT cellophane tape, tacks, brads, or nails. The Oak Room is the only exception; it does have a 
bulletin board wall that can accommodate push pins. All tape must be carefully removed by the applicant. 
7. Equipment needs beyond tables and chairs must be requested in advance on the contract application. 
8. Heat, spark or flame producing devices, including coffee pots and candles, may NOT be used. Chafing dishes are acceptable if 
Sterno is used for heat AND table linens have been treated with a flame retardant OR Sterno is placed on a flame retardant 
surface.

arrangement.

(Initial) The applicant is responsible for cleaning the kitchen after using it.

SUSTAINABILITY GRANT
• The San Mateo Public Library was one of just 20 California local library systems selected to receive first-ever funding from 
the State Library to support projects focused on sustainability and climate resilience. 
• These funds allowed our San Mateo Library to offer more educational programming focused on climate education, 
environment-focused civic engagement, water conservation, air quality, energy efficiency, reducing waste and supporting 
local climate action and resilience plans. 
• Programs included mending workshops and repair clinics for bicycles, furniture and electronics, as well as family 
gardening workshops and a seed library where community members can get free seeds for gardening.
