SUBJECT:
Racial Equity Art Project - Final Design
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve the final design of Rafael Blanco's "Cultural Pattern" piece for the Racial Equity Art Project. 
BACKGROUND:
On June 20, 2023, the City Council approved the selection of the artist, Rafael Blanco, and his design, "Cultural Pattern" for 
the Racial Equity Art Project. The Council directed the Civic Arts Committee (CAC) to work with the artist to revise the 
design and approve the final design. 
On July 10, 2023, the CAC met with Rafael Blanco via Zoom to discuss and agree on the direction of the final design draft. 
The CAC and Rafael Blanco reconvened during a special meeting on August 14, 2023, to review the drafts of Mr. Blanco's 
final design. The CAC provided positive comments to Mr. Blanco's design including the expansion of cultures represented, 
the removal of the cultural labels, and the way Mr. Blanco wove the cultural patterns together. 
With no additional revisions requested, Mr. Blanco will present the final design to the CAC during this regularly scheduled 
CAC meeting. Staff recommends approving the final design. 
Once approved, implementation is set to begin on October 2nd and will conclude at the end of the month. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
The Arts in Public Places (AiPP) Fund balance has $500,000 allocated to the program, which will be used to fund the Racial 
Equity Art Project's $200,000 budget.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This project is categorically exempt from CEQA as an “existing facility,” because it consists of the repair, maintenance, 
and/or minor alteration of existing public facility (B Street Pedestrian Mall) with no expansion of use. (CEQA Guidelines 
Section 15301.)
