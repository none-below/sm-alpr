General Plan and Housing Element Implementation Plans – Overview and Council Direction
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation from staff on the General Plan and Housing Element Implementation Plans and provide staff with

BACKGROUND:
General Plan 2040
The City Council adopted General Plan 2040 on March 18, 2024, and it includes a Vision Statement and Guiding Principles, 
text describing key issues of community interest, and goals, policies and actions that outline how the City will address 
those issues. General Plan 2040 addresses all the topics required by State law and other topics that the community 
identified as being fundamental to the quality of life in the City. There are eight core chapters (elements) in General Plan 
2040, which include: Land Use; Circulation; Housing (adopted separately); Community Design and Historic Resources; 
Conservation, Open Space, and Recreation; Public Services and Facilities; Safety; and Noise.
Each element in General Plan 2040 includes a concise set of background narratives that summarizes current conditions in 
the City related to the topics in the element. The background information provides context about the issues the City plans 
to address, explaining why there is policy direction so that the policies and actions can focus on how the City can achieve a 
desired outcome. The narratives also provide information on other regulations and/or agencies that influence the 
decisionmaking process. Each element in General Plan 2040 addresses a specific set of topics and includes goals, polices 
and actions that provide a blueprint for how the City will address these issues. These goals, policies, and actions provide 
guidance to the City on how to direct change and manage resources over the next 20 years. 
Overall, General Plan 2040 includes 56 goals with 363 policies and 240 actions (excluding the Housing Element). The 
General Plan Implementation Plan (Attachment 1) identifies the lead department for each action, and assigns a priority 
level (when work will begin) and anticipated resources (staff and monetary) to implement the action.
2023-2031 Housing Element
The Housing Element was adopted on May 20, 2024, and submitted to HCD for certification on May 24, 2024. The Housing 
Element, which is a chapter of the General Plan, is administered separately because it covers a different period of time 
than the General Plan (eight years instead of 20 years) and because of specific legal obligations that the City must follow 
when implementing its policies and actions.  The Housing Element includes five overall goals to support compliance with 
state law and to achieve the City’s Regional Housing Needs Allocation (RHNA) – Supporting Production, Preservation, 
Protection, Promotion and Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing.

DISCUSSION:
The purpose of this study session is to share the implementaton plans for the City’s recently adopted General Plan 2040 
and 2031-2023 Housing Element, provide an overview of the actions that are underway or will be started in 2024/2025, 
and receive City Council direction on any changes to prioritization.  To understand what a goal, policy, and action is, and 
the relationship among them, the definitions are as follows:
•
Goal - Description of a general desired result that the City seeks to create through the implementation of its 
General Plan.
•
Policy - Specific statement that regulates activities in the City, guides decisionmaking and directs ongoing efforts as 
the City works to achieve a goal. General Plan policies establish standards that will be used by City staff, Planning 
Commission, and City Council when prioritizing initiatives and expenditures, reviewing and approving new 
development and in relation City decisionmaking.  
•
Action - Measure, procedure or technique intended to help reach a specified goal or implement a policy. The 
actions in the General Plan will serve as a to-do list for City staff and officials to implement the goals and policies of 
the General Plan.

identified budgets; however, the City Council will need to identify resources during future budget cycles to implement

City Council’s annual goal setting.

Housing Element Implementation Plan
The Housing Element Implementation Plan (Attachment 2) outlines the list of implementation actions that the City is 
committed to completing and the year in which that action must be started and/or completed.  As required by the State 
Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD), the City is obligated to meet the timeline associated with 
each implementation action.  To help understand the workload by year associated with implementing the Housing 
Element, a Gantt Chart has been prepared (Attachment 3). The anticipated workload to implement the Housing Element 
through the first three years of the Housing Cycle (2023-2025) is as follows:
•
Routine/Ongoing Programs (24 Actions) – These are ongoing programs that require work/activities annually to 
maintain compliance (such as the Housing Element Annual Progress Report and/or annual outreach to specified 
groups or organizations).  These actions require additional staff resources in the first 1-2 years to establish the 
process/framework to implement and then smaller amounts of work moving forward to maintain the program 
annually.
•
First Year (2023) (20 Actions) – The first year of the Housing Cycle was primarily focused on updating the Housing 
Element to achieve certification.  Nonetheless, 16 of the 20 actions were completed, with notable examples 
including adoption of Objective Design Standards (ODS) and increasing the administrative approval threshold for 
housing projects.  The remaining four actions will be incorporated into the 2024 work plan.
•
Second Year (2024) (34 Actions) – With the Housing Element adopted in the first half of this year, the staff focus

City Council Input and Direction 
Staff seeks input on the following question: 
Based on the implementation plans and their current priorities, does the City Council have any questions on the list of 
implementation actions or direction on changes to the prioritizations in either plan? 
If there is interest in elevating the priority of one or more actions, there may be a need to deprioritize the implementation 
of other actions to ensure that there are adequate staff resources to complete the necessary work and community 
outreach/engagement related to the action. With the significant workload associated with implementation of the Housing 
Element over the next two years, as well as the Downtown Area Plan update and comprehensive zoning code update to 
implement the General Plan, there are limited staff resources to prioritize additional actions without deprioritizing other 
actions.

STRIVE SAN MATEO GENERAL PLAN 2040 IMPLEMENTATION PLAN 
San Mateo’s General Plan will be implemented both through the day-to-day decisions that rely on its vision, goals and policies, as well as the 
actions summarized in this chapter. All substantive decisions about development projects, capital improvements, zoning changes and other plans 
and policies affecting land use, transportation and the physical environment will be reviewed for conformance with the policies in this General 
Plan, thus advancing the Plan’s overall vision and policy framework. To guide the implementation of this plan, the General Plan identifies a list of 
actions intended to provide an overall sense of the future priorities to support accomplishing the vision and goals of the General Plan. 
In addition to these actions, the policies in the General Plan establish standards that will be used by City staff in all departments, City boards and 
commissions, the Planning Commission, and the City Council when prioritizing initiatives and expenditures, reviewing and approving new 
development, and in related City decision making, to ensure that City decisions are clear, consistent, and advance San Mateo toward attaining 
the goals in General Plan 2040.

actions are already budgeted and ongoing, while the City Council will need to identify resources during future budget cycles to implement other 
actions. City staff may recommend changing priorities or adding or removing actions during the course of the annual budget process. The City 
Council may change the prioritization of actions through the annual City Council goal setting meeting. For each action, the Implementation Plan 
identifies the following:

IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE AND RESOURCES 
Although the City would like to implement all General Plan 2040 actions as expeditiously as possible, there are capital resource and staffing 
limitations, as well as limitations to the amount of work that the City and the City Council can effectively focus on during any one year or period 
of time. The completion of actions is contingent upon the availability of funding resources and City Council prioritization. It should also be noted 
that issues that cannot be anticipated may arise in the future that may act to divert resources from the programs and priorities of the General 
Plan and change the implementation timeline for the listed actions. 
The General Plan is a living document and the City’s priorities will evolve through the life of this Plan, and therefore changes and updates will 
need to be made to the Implementation Plan. Annually, as required by State Law and the Municipal Code, the Community Development 
Department will submit a report to the City Council on the status of the General Plan and its implementation. The Implementation Plan will also 
be a tool to assist with annual City Council goal setting and will be updated to align with Council goals and priorities each year.

Commission, and City Council when prioritizing initiatives and expenditures, 
reviewing and approving new development and in relation City decision making. 
» Action - Measure, procedure or technique intended to help reach a specified goal

City staff and officials to implement the goals and policies of the General Plan.
