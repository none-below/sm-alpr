SUBJECT:
Economic Development Plan Framework — Review
RECOMMENDATION:
Review the proposed framework for the Economic Development Work Plan and provide feedback to staff.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo is facing a $10.5 million budget deficit, driven by factors at the state level as well as local issues, 
such as lower hotel occupancy taxes following business closures, like the Marriott hotel. The local economic landscape is 
shifting rapidly due to changes like the transition to remote work and higher operating expenses affecting businesses of all 
sizes. These factors pose challenges for the City’s fiscal health while also presenting opportunities for growth in certain 
areas. To address local drivers related to the City economic conditions, staff is proposing the development of a 
comprehensive Economic Development (ED) Work Plan to help diversify the tax base, attract, and retain businesses and 
support sustainable economic growth in San Mateo. 
Upon review, if the Council approves the proposed framework, the resulting ED Work Plan will focus on supporting small 
businesses, building relationships with new and existing companies, and enhancing vibrancy in key areas like Downtown 
and neighborhood business districts across the city. The ED Work Plan will also prioritize regular check-ins with commercial 
brokers for real estate insights and staying abreast of local, regional, and national economic trends.
DISCUSSION 
The proposed framework for the ED Work Plan would include focus areas to be used to guide the City’s efforts to achieve 
fiscal sustainability and economic vitality. The primary components of the framework are: 
1.
Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats (SWOT) Analysis 
o
Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis to assess the City's current economic landscape and identify 
areas for improvement 
2.
Economic Development Goals   
o
Align the ED Work Plan with General Plan goals related to economic development, with emphasis on 
diversifying the economic base and identifying new revenue opportunities 
o
Develop a strategic plan for sustainable economic growth, setting measurable objectives and deliverables 
to monitor progress
3.
Revenue Generation and Tax Base Expansion 
o
Identify new revenue opportunities, such as analyzing underutilized sites within the 10 General Plan focus 
areas for possible mixed use or hotel development

o
Assess City real estate to ensure appropriate levels of revenue is being received
o
Advance new Downtown Plan
o
Engage stakeholders and identify opportunities for increased sales tax generation 
4.
New and Existing Commercial Office Users
o
Supporting Community Development efforts to improve planning application process and timeline
o
Coordinate interdepartmental resources to support small businesses, commercial, and office tenant 
improvements
5.
Business Attraction and Retention
o
Implement strategies to attract and retain businesses 
o
Establish small business support programs, with a focus on minority owned businesses
o
Explore wayfinding signage for business districts and associated parking
o
Retain a retail consultant to support development of retail in mixed use projects
o
Continue business engagement program 
6.
Economic Development Tools and Resources 
o
Update the City’s economic development website to better showcase available resources, development 
opportunities, and business incentives 
o
Evaluate ways to advertise and promote of City initiatives 
o
Quarterly City e-newsletters update for businesses
7.
Workforce Housing and Transportation Initiatives 
o
Collaborate with the Housing Manager and community stakeholders to address workforce housing 
o
Work with Transportation staff to explore solutions that support the needs of businesses and employees 
8.
Workforce Development
o
Complete a landscape analysis of workforce development programs and resources in San Mateo
o
Collect further community input and input from youth to develop a workforce development program
Expand and leverage workforce development efforts led by County’s Shared Prosperity Coordinating 
Council, County Human Services, and NovaWorks
9.
Supporting Safe, Healthy and Vibrant Business Districts through Public Safety
o
Police Department dedicates four full time police officers to the downtown area and maintains a police 
substation in the downtown corridor
o
Establish a “Business Watch” program downtown that encourages local businesses to work together 
to prevent and reduce crime
o
Continue to administer an Organized Retail Theft Prevention Program
o
Police officers’ regular attendance in all business districts including Hillsdale, Bridgepointe and 
Downtown districts
Staff seeks Council’s input on the general themes, strategic focus areas, and the high-level framework presented today. 
The goal is to establish a clear direction to finalize the ED Plan by late summer 2025. 
Should Council direct staff to proceed, next steps would be:
o
Late 2024/Early 2025: Engagement with business stakeholders for feedback on Work Plan Focus Areas
o
Spring 2025: Present a draft Economic Development Work Plan to the City Council for feedback and 
further direction.
o
Summer 2025: Finalize and adopt the Economic Development Work Plan. 
o
2025-2027: Implement the two-year Economic Development Work Plan and track progress on goals and 
deliverables.
