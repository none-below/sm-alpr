SUBJECT:
Fallen Heroes Memorial Artwork Fabrication -Budget Recommendation
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council a budget transfer of $103,400 from the Art in Public Places Fund to the Parks and 
Recreation Capital Improvement Budget for the fabrication of the Fallen Heroes Memorial artwork.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo Municipal Code (23.60.030) states there is a fund known as the "Art in Public Places Fund" and that 
this fund shall be maintained by the City treasurer, and shall be used for the acquisition, installation, improvement, and 
maintenance of artwork to be displayed in the City, and the administration of the Art in Public Places Program. 
The Parks and Recreation Department is constructing a Fallen Heroes Memorial at the direction of the City Council as part 
of the implementation of the Central Park Master Plan. Throughout the years, residents had been inquiring about where 
San Mateo has formal recognition for our fallen heroes and veterans. While there are six separate plaques scattered in 
Central Park recognizing veterans and service members, the City has no formal memorial. In response to community 
interest in 2015, Mayor Maureen Freschet recommended the creation of a memorial in Central Park to recognize all the 
City's Fallen Heroes, including public safety personnel. The San Mateo City Council unanimously agreed with the 
recommendation and directed that a Fallen Heroes Memorial be added to the Central Park Master Plan. An eleven-
member steering committee, comprised of City Councilmembers, veterans, retired police and fire representatives, 
community members, and city staff was assembled to develop the memorial's concept. 
The city contracted with RRM Design Group to develop a design concept for committee consideration. RRM returned with 
two concepts, including the Remembrance Poppy inspired by the national emblem of remembrance that was worn 
following World War I (Attachment 1). The Committee overwhelmingly chose the Remembrance Poppy which is the 
preferred design recommended to the Park and Recreation Commission. The City Council expressed unanimous support 
and approval for the design at its November 20, 2020, City Council Meeting, and later, at its May 17, 2021, meeting the 
Council appropriated $50,000 for construction of the memorial and approved the Fallen Heroes Recognition Policy. The 
design consists of seven vertical stainless-steel columns, each containing a segment of the poppy that, when viewed from 
a certain angle, will unite to form a single image. Each column will represent a branch of the military and the San Mateo 
Police and Fire Departments with engraved names of those who died in service. A concrete band will be embedded into 
the linear pavers with the specific unit’s motto etched in the concrete. Flush mounted uplighting will illuminate the names. 
Site furnishings will include items such as benches, monument signage and a donor recognition plaque. Brick pavers have 
been integrated into the design to serve as an opportunity for families to honor the service of a relative (who may not 
have died in the line of duty) as well as provide a means of fundraising.

CITY OF SAN MATEO
The committee has raised $303,836.81 in donations including $50,000 from the City of San Mateo to launch this project.  A 
pledge of $25,000 will be secured from the County of San Mateo upon the construction. While the committee exceeded its 
fundraising goal, construction costs have risen over the past several years. The current projected cost of the project is 
$408,927.00, resulting in a funding shortfall of $105,090.19. The cost to fabricate the memorial artwork is $94,000. The 
Parks and Recreation Department identified the City “Art in Public Places Fund” as a viable option to fund the balance of 
the project.
Per the Municipal Code Section 23.60.030, ART IN PUBLIC PLACES FUND, the department seeks the Civic Arts Committee to 
approve $94,000 plus a 10% contingency in the amount of $9,400 for a total of $103,400 for the fabrication of the Fallen 
Heroes Memorial Artwork. This is a City Council directed initiative and the Art in Public Places Fund is a fund that is to be 
used for the acquisition, installation, improvement, and maintenance of artwork to be displayed within the City. 
Should this action be approved by this commission, it will be brought before the City Council for consideration and final 
approval.  
BUDGET IMPACT:
The current fund balance of the Art in Public Places Fund stands at $999,946.84, with $294,567 appropriated (and 
available) by the Civic Arts Committee.  This action will result in a reduction of this balance by $103,400.
