CITY OF SAN MATEO 
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
March 25, 2024 
 
 Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Austin Volz, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order 
at 5:30 p.m. 
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Mr. Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant 
Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Austin 
Volz, Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, and Richard Wysong.  Trustee Shaylona 
Wheeler submitted her resignation prior to the meeting and was not present.   
 
3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION  
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. McCall, seconded by 
Mr. Wysong, and the motion passed 4-0. 
 
4. PUBLIC COMMENT 
There was not any public comment. 
 
5. NEW BUSINESS 
Recommended Updates to the Collection Development Policy:  Supervising 
Librarian Rita Morin shared that as part of her work on preparing a draft San 
Mateo Public Library (SMPL) Collection Development Policy for the Library Board’s 
review and approval, she has reviewed collection development policies for other 
libraries throughout the state and consulted with other library managers.  The 
current internal SMPL Collection Development Policy is quite lengthy and serves as 
an internal administrative guidance document.  The current public facing SMPL 
Policy for Selection, Disposal & Gifts of Library Materials is a one-page document 
that lists The Library Bill of Rights and the Right to Read Statement as guiding 
principles for the selection and disposal of library materials at SMPL.  Ms. Morin 
included important information from these two policies in preparing the draft SMPL 
Collection Development Policy.  The Board asked questions and provided feedback.  
Suggestions from Board members included listing that you need to be a library 
card holder to submit a reconsideration form, changing “patron” to “requestor”, 
including information on all the special collections for all ages in their own area in 
the policy and that these collections are only at the Main Library, and expanding 
on the foreign language and e-resources sections.  Ms. Morin will make the 
recommended changes and provide the Board with a new draft at the next Library 
Board meeting. 
 
6. REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS 
 
A.  City Librarian James Moore: 
• 
Shaylona Wheeler has resigned from the Library Board.  The Board can 
select a new Vice President at the next Library Board meeting.

Minutes of March 25, 2024 
Page 2 of 3 
 
 
 
• 
The San Mateo County Genealogical Society (SMCGS) will be moving into the 
office formerly used for passport services at the Main Library.  The services 
provided by SMCGS have been very popular. 
• 
Myra Jolivet from Center Four Consulting has begun work on SMPL’s next 
strategic plan.  It is estimated that a final strategic plan report will be 
completed in June. 
• 
A patron has questioned whether SMPL should circulate the book by Dr. 
Seuss “And to Think That I Saw It on Mulberry Street”.  The patron 
expressed the opinion that the book contained caricatures that could be 
considered racist.  As they apply to library collections, Mr. Moore said SMPL 
follows the Right to Read Statement adopted by the American Library 
Association Council and the Association of American Publishers.  The Board 
provided feedback on potential responses should this issue arise again in 
the future. 
• 
There is nothing new to report on the timeline for the Marina Library repair 
project.  When construction work begins next year, the library will need to 
be set up in a temporary location until construction is completed.  
Construction work could take up to one year. 
• 
Mr. Moore attended the Nowruz festival at the Main Library on March 17.  
SMPL has hosted this celebration of the Persian New Year for over 10 years.  
It is the largest Nowruz celebration in the Bay Area and possibly in 
California. 
• 
Library staff provided a tour of the Main Library for some seniors from Self 
Help for the Elderly.  The group was happily surprised by the breadth of 
services that are offered including the 30-minute appointments where they 
can receive one-on-one instruction to help set up a new tech device, open an 
email account, connect to a hotspot, use a laptop, find and enroll in library 
classes, reserve materials, navigate the library’s website, and more. 
• 
Mr. Moore is unable to attend the next Library Board meeting on April 22.  
The Board agreed to cancel this meeting and meet next on Tuesday, May 28. 
   
B. Board Members: 
• 
Mr. Volz visited the Main Library on March 24 and saw Deputy City 
Librarian Rukshana Singh while he was there.  He observed a fun video 
game event taking place for teens.   
• 
Ms. Canzian asked if the Board can help at the Downtown Festival at 
Central Park on April 13.  This event is part of the Library Foundation’s San 
Mateo Celebrates! festivities taking place during National Library Week April 
7 through April 13.  Mr. Moore said he will ask Sarah Meier-Heredia from 
the Foundation to contact the trustees to advise them how they can assist 
with this event. 
• 
Ms. McCall visited three libraries while in Ohio recently.  The offerings at the 
makerspace areas at these libraries were impressive.  As part of SMPL’s next 
strategic plan, Ms. McCall recommended that local tech companies are 
contacted to see if they can partner with SMPL to increase our makerspace 
options.

Collection Development Policy  
[Revision Adopted by the Library Board of Trustees, mm/dd/yyyy] 
Purpose of the Collection Development Policy 
The collection development policy is intended to provide the community and library staff 
documentation which informs and supports the development of the library collection. This 
document will provide guidance to San Mateo Public Library staff and inform the public of the 
principles upon which the library makes decisions regarding the development and maintenance of 
the collection. While this policy will not replace the judgment of librarians, it serves to assist them in 
making decisions when choosing from available materials. 
 
Library Mission Statement  
San Mateo Public Library builds community by inspiring imagination, discovery and reading. San 
Mateo is a resilient, innovative, and inclusive community that is knowledgeable and empowered, 
embracing the future while honoring its history. 
Scope of the Main Library 
The role of the main library is to serve as an information and resource center where the public has 
access to an in-depth collection of up-to-date and accurate information.  The Library promotes on-
site and remote reference, and information services to assist users in locating information on 
subjects ranging from practical questions to in-depth research.  The Library’s popular materials 
collection features current, high-interest materials, both fiction and non-fiction, in a variety of 
formats for all ages.  
 
Scope of the Branches 
The Branch Libraries provide high quality, personalized library services, programs, and books and 
materials to the neighborhoods adjacent to the Hillsdale and Marina Libraries. The interests and needs 
of the actual and potential users of the branch are continually evaluated so that each library has a 
collection reflecting the community that it serves. While each branch serves the basic information 
needs of its neighborhood, it does not duplicate the in-depth sources or special collections of the Main 
Library. Budget and space limit the branch collection to materials of high interest to their 
communities. 
Materials Selection 
Library materials are chosen to serve the information, education, cultural, and recreational needs of 
the community. The Library selects materials on a large variety of subjects and in different formats, 
responding to the wide range of ages, ethnicities, languages, educational backgrounds, interests, and 
reading skills of library patrons. Materials are available in a variety of formats, quantities, and titles.

Collection development decisions are based on staff judgement and expertise, knowledge of what is 
already in the collection, and reviews in professional journals. Selection criteria includes but is not 
limited to past and anticipated public demand; contemporary significance and timeliness; literary or 
artistic merit; the need for and availability of information or materials in the subject area; 
authoritativeness of the author or publisher; favorable reviews in standard selection sources; cost and 
budgetary constraints; and shelving space available in the library. Selections are made to build and 
maintain a balanced collection and to include a variety of viewpoints and opinions to meet community 
needs.  
 
Responsibility for Selection 
Ultimate responsibility for materials selection rests with the City Librarian who operates within the 
framework of policies determined by the Library Board of Trustees. Actual selection and collection 
management activities are shared among professional staff that perform this obligation consistent 
with this policy and established procedures. 
Suggestions for Purchase 
Library patrons are encouraged to suggest materials for the library to purchase. To do so, patrons 
should complete a ‘Suggest a Purchase’ form via the library website. Suggestions from the public will 
be evaluated by selectors based on the Library’s criteria for selection. All suggestions will be 
considered although not all suggestions may be added to the collection. This includes self-published 
works by local authors or otherwise. 
Donations & Gifts 
The Library welcomes the donation of library materials for its collection. The Library reserves the 
right to determine whether or not donated titles will be added to the collection. Materials that meet 
the Library’s selection criteria may be added to the collection. Donated titles that are not added are 
routed to the Friends of the Library who raise funds through their book sales. The revenue from the 
book sales is administered by the Library Foundation which awards grants to the Library for new 
materials. The Library Board also accepts monetary donations for the Library’s collection and to the 
Memorial Fund for the purchase of library materials in memory of or in honor of designated 
persons.  
The Collections  
The Library provides print collections in the form of circulating fiction and nonfiction books, large-
print books, reference books, and periodicals; non-print collections composed of CDs, DVDs and 
audiobooks; and an electronic-resource collection in the form of databases, e-books, streaming 
music, video and audiobooks, and various other services made available online. Materials in the 
collection are supplemented through access to the materials of the Peninsula Library System’s 
member libraries and by obtaining materials via interlibrary loan. Collections are developed in three 
major areas - for children, teens, and adults. The library supports the following international 
languages – Spanish, Chinese, Farsi and Japanese.  
Children’s Collections: A robust collection of print and media materials for children, items are 
provided in multiple formats for a wide range of interests, ages, and reading levels. Although the

library strives to provide basic curriculum support, we do not attempt to purchase in sufficient 
quantity for required reading or other district-wide projects.  
Teen Collection: A collection of books and magazines for teens, with awareness of the variety of 
interests, backgrounds, reading skills, and development levels within this age group. This book 
collection is predominantly fiction selected for recreational reading. The nonfiction titles in the 
collection are on subjects that meet the developmental needs of teens as well as titles of 
contemporary interest to this age group.   
Adult Collections: Fiction and non-fiction collections including books, CDs, DVDs, periodicals, and 
online materials, with an emphasis on popular works, basic reference, and homework support.  
Special Collections: All materials available at the Main Library. 
a. The Leon S. Benson Holocaust Studies Collection: This collection is dedicated as a living 
memorial for Leon S. Benson and all the survivors and victims of the atrocities of the 
Holocaust. 
b. Biotechnology Learning Center: The Biotechnology Learning Center at the Main Library 
provides access to print and online biotechnology resources. 
c. California Collection: This reference collection includes works of fiction and non-fiction 
about the history of California and its localities, with an emphasis on San Mateo County. 
d. Franklin Templeton Investments Business Resource Center: The San Mateo Public Library 
has the largest public library collection of business materials, in print and digital formats, on 
the Peninsula. 
e. The San Mateo County Genealogical Society Collection: The San Mateo County 
Genealogical Society supports the study of family history on the San Francisco Peninsula and 
beyond through its collections, educational programs, and research services. 
Digital Collections: The Library provides a variety of electronic resources for all age groups that are 
accessible via the Library’s webpage.  
Collection Management 
Collection maintenance and evaluation is important to ensure the materials, both print and digital, 
in the Library are balanced and responsive to community needs. The Library strives to maintain an 
up-to-date, useful collection. Statistical tools such as circulation reports help librarians to determine 
how the collection is being used, and community demographic information will be used to help keep 
abreast of changing community needs.  During the process of evaluation, the librarian will have the 
opportunity to identify subjects where materials are needed, missing titles that should be replaced, 
older editions of titles which should be updated, and subjects, authors, and titles that are no longer 
in demand in the community. Titles and items are withdrawn from the collection through periodic 
and systematic review by librarians. Materials may be withdrawn because they are worn, obsolete, 
or seldom used; superseded by a newer edition or better work on the subject; or physically 
damaged or in poor condition.  
Maintenance of the library's collection through constant re-evaluation by the library staff ensures its 
usefulness and relevancy to the community. This evaluation depends heavily on the staff's 
professional expertise in assessing the needs of the community and the content of the collection. 
Those materials determined to no longer be of value are withdrawn from the collection. Withdrawn 
items may be disposed of through book sales, giveaways, or recycling.

Intellectual Freedom & Access to Library Materials  
San Mateo Public Library fully endorses the principles documented in the Library Bill of Rights and 
the Freedom to Read Statement of the American Library Association. San Mateo Public Library does 
not censor library materials of any nature for any member of the community. Library staff and 
patrons may not impose their views, values, and biases upon one another.   
The Library's collection is the reflection of its diverse community, and inclusion in the library’s 
collection does not reflect endorsement of any specific contents or belief. Library materials are not 
identified or marked to show approval or disapproval based on its content. The library upholds the 
right of the individual to secure information, even though the content may be controversial, 
unorthodox, or unacceptable to others. Parents and guardians have the right and responsibility to 
make decisions about the materials suitable for their own children. The Library does not regulate 
what a child reads, views, or checks out. 
Request for Reconsideration 
 
Members of the San Mateo community wishing to recommend the removal of a particular item 
(physical or digital format) in the library collection may submit a Request for Reconsideration of 
Library Materials form, which will be reviewed by the Library Director and the staff in relation to the 
library's mission statement and the selection criteria of this collection development policy.  
 
Those seeking reconsideration must be a San Mateo Public Library cardholder residing within the 
City of San Mateo or Hillsborough. A title will be reviewed only once within a three-year period 
unless the content has undergone major revisions, and the title will remain on the shelf during the 
reconsideration process.  After evaluating journal reviews and other materials submitted by the 
patron as well as the staff, a response will be made by the City Librarian within 30 days of receiving 
the formal objection.  
 
Policy Review and Revision Statement 
This collection development policy will periodically be evaluated and revised every five years.
