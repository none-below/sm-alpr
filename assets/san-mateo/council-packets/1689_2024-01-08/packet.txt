SUBJECT:
31-57 S. B Street Commercial Development Project Art Proposal – Study Session
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and provide input to the applicant on the proposed location and design concepts for the public art associated with 
the commercial development project located at 31-57 S. B Street. 
BACKGROUND:
The proposed development at 31-57 S B Street (also referred to as “1st & B”), is a four-story, commercial (retail/restaurant) 
and office building proposed by Harvest Properties, the applicant. This project is currently under review as a planning 
application (PA-2022-089) and is anticipated to be scheduled for Planning Commission consideration in the first half of 
2024. In accordance with San Mateo Municipal Code Chapter 23.60, the project is subject to the Art in Public Places 
program, which requires all new commercial development projects and multi-family projects with a building permit 
valuation of three million dollars or more to plan for the inclusion of public art within the project site or payment of an in-
lieu fee. For this project, staff has recommended inclusion of public art on the rear (east) building façade facing the 
Downtown Caltrain Station, due to its prominent location as an entryway into the City from the Caltrain station and its 
high visibility within the Downtown Retail Core. 
The applicant is exploring three preliminary public art concepts for the rear building façade (Attachment 1) and is seeking 
input from the Civic Arts Committee on which option to pursue. The preliminary art concepts are as follows:
1.
Mosaic Tile by Rachel Rodi or Colette Crutcher – mosaic tile medium that utilizes abstract pattern or lettering that 
are expressions of nature or the artistic traditions of cultures.
2.
Painted Mural by Nicole Mueller or Apexer – colorful painted murals that may be applied on a wall or glass 
(window) surface.
3.
Sculpture by Jeremy Holmes – three-dimensional sculpture affixed on the exterior wall. Potential materials include 
wood or metal.
At this study session, the Civic Arts Committee should focus its review on the proposed concepts and provide feedback on 
the preliminary artists, materials, styles, and location. Following this study session, the applicant will have an opportunity 
to revise their public art concept based on the comments and input provided at this meeting. The applicant will return to 
the Civic Arts Committee to request approval for their proposed public art concept at a later date, after the Planning 
Commission has taken action on the proposed project.
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact as this is a private installation proposed within the project site. In accordance with San Mateo

Structures) since the proposed artwork consists of mosaic tiles, painted mural graphics or a sculpture affixed to an exterior 
building wall.
