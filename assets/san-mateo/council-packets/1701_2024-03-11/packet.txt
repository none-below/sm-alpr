SUBJECT:
Recreation Management Software Mobile Application Development - Feedback
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the development of a new recreation management software mobile application and provide 
feedback on design and navigation elements.
BACKGROUND:
The department currently uses Vermont Systems, Inc. - RecTrac and WebTrac (web-based) software for the registration of 
recreation classes, facility reservations, tickets sales, and many other processes. Last year Vermont System, Inc. announced 
they were partnering with Innovatise, GmbH to provide a white labeled mobile application (app) solution for its users. 
Innovastise is a pioneer in mobile marketing and the creator of myFitApp, an open, flexible, independent and digital 
marketing platform that seamlessly integrates with WebTrac and is customizable to each organization. 
The mobile app will add value to our users as it will provide access to online registration regardless of access to a 
computer. Staff have embarked on the development of this app and before moving on to the execution, we are requesting 
the commission's feedback on some navigation and design elements. We will be using our WebTrac site 
(www.sanmateorec.org) to guide this conversation as we intend for the app to be a mirror of the site.

SUBJECT:
Intergenerational Community Building - Presentation
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation regarding intergenerational programs and provide feedback.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo Age Friendly Action Plan identified Intergenerational Community Building to be foundational in 
paving the path to an age friendly city. Intergenerational connections assist all age groups in increasing self-esteem, sense 
of purpose, usefulness, and connection of the cycle of life in the community. They also promote intergenerational learning 
through sharing of skills, knowledge, and experience between the generations. As part of this effort, the San Mateo Senior 
Center will provide intergenerational programs such as intergenerational game activities and other special events or 
programs. The goal of this project is to be intentional about our efforts to bring together our community in all stages of 
their lives. 
Through the Age Friendly Action Plan, the city has a goal to implement more intergenerational programs and opportunities 
over the next two years.  City staff will collaborate with other City departments and community partners towards building 
a solid base of specific programs that target multiple age groups for years to come. 
On Wednesday, January 3, 2024 City staff presented on previous and current intergenerational programs offered and 
requested feedback from the San Mateo Parks and Recreation Commission.  Staff will be presenting similar information to 
the Senior Citizens Commission, along with feedback received from the Parks and Recreation Commission.
