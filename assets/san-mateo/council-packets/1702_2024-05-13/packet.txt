SUBJECT:
Age Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program – Feedback
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the development of the Age Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program and provide feedback 
on application and logo designs.
BACKGROUND:
In October 2020, the City of San Mateo took a significant step towards fostering an inclusive environment for all residents 
by committing to become an Age-Friendly City. This commitment was formalized through its partnership with the 
American Association of Retired Persons (AARP) Age Friendly Communities Network. In November 2022, the City 
demonstrated its dedication further by submitting its comprehensive Age-Friendly Action Plan to the AARP. This plan 
serves as a strategic roadmap, meticulously crafted by the Age-Friendly Task Force, to address key initiatives identified for 
enhancing the city's age-friendliness. Among the pivotal projects outlined in the Age-Friendly Action Plan is the pioneering 
Age-Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program. The primary objective of this innovative initiative is twofold: to enlighten 
local businesses about the significance of inclusiveness by evolving into age-friendly operations and to commend 
establishments that are already integrating age-friendly practices into their operations. 
With a collaborative effort between Parks and Recreation and Economic Development, a preliminary application for 
businesses along with initial logo designs has been created. The launch of this pilot program in the upcoming months 
signifies a crucial milestone. By engaging a small sampling of San Mateo businesses, the City aims to test the efficacy of the 
program's processes and logistics while gauging interest from stakeholders. This strategic approach allows for thorough 
evaluation and refinement before potential expansion in the future, ensuring the program's long-term viability and impact.
