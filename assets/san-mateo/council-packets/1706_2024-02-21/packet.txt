COMMUNITY RELATIONS COMMISSION – SPECIAL MEETING         
MINUTES – September 13, 2023
The September 13, 2023 Special Meeting of the Community Relations Commission (CRC) was called to order 
virtually over Zoom at 7:00 pm. Chair Crowell called the meeting to order.
ROLL CALL
Commissioners Nugent, Anand, Fitzgerald, and Crowell were present. Commissioner Boles was not present and is 
excused from attendance.
CITY STAFF PRESENT
Eloiza Murillo-Garcia, Housing Manager; and Nicholas Vu, Housing Specialist were present.  
CONSENT CALENDAR / APPROVAL OF MINUTES
The minutes of the April 19, 2023 meeting were approved. (Nugent/Anand; 4-0-0)
PUBLIC COMMENT
None
NEW BUSINESS
1. 2022-23 Consolidated Annual Performance Evaluation Report – Recommendation
Review the Consolidated Annual Performance Evaluation Report for submission to the Department of Housing and 
Urban Development. (Crowell/Anand; 4-0-0)
2. Community Needs Workshop for 2024-25 Community Development Block Grant Annual Plan
That the Community Relations Commission determine community priorities in preparation of the 2024-25 Annual 
Plan for the Community Development Block Grant Program. (Crowell/Nugent; 4-0-0)
A representative from Friends For Youth spoke about the need of serving disadvantaged middle and high school 
students in social and emotional learning through mentor services with positive adult role models. Additionally, 
there is an increased need for group sessions during school to process trauma with their peers. The waitlist is long 
and they are requesting funds to expand the program.
A representative from Peninsula Family Services spoke about the needs of the growing older adult community. They 
provide mental health support, peer counseling, and language support to elderly members of San Mateo. Their 
theme for the upcoming year is loneliness prevention. They are requesting funds to bolster the program since they 
currently run at a deficit.
A representative from Samaritan House spoke about the various programs that Samaritan House has partnered with 
the City on and how much the variety of assistance provided and client base has grown. The exponential increase in 
activity at the program office has generated more wear and tear on the facilities. Covid funds have run out but the 
need for assistance remains. Additional funds are requested to continue programs and improve facilities.
3. Election of Officers
Election of Chair and Vice-Chair for 2023 - 2024. Commissioner Crowell was nominated for the position of Chair. 
(Nugent/Fitzgerald; 4-0-0) Commissioner Boles was nominated for the position of Vice Chair. (Crowell/Anand; 4-0-0)
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
There were no reports or announcements from staff.
ADJOURNMENT
With no further business before the Commission, the meeting adjourned at 7:37 pm (Fitzgerald/Anand; 4-0-0).

SUBJECT:
2024 Community Funding Recommendations
RECOMMENDATION:
That the Community Relations Commission provide recommendations for which organizations should receive Community 
Development Block Grant (CDBG) funding for the 2024-2025 and 2025-2026 Program Years.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City of San Mateo provides Community Funding grants for non-profit organizations that provide community 
services that serve low and moderate income residents of San Mateo using federal Community Development Block Grant 
(CDBG) funds. Federal regulations cap the amount of CDBG funds that can be used for this type of activity to 15% of a 
jurisdiction’s total program funds. Based on this percentage, the CDBG public service budget is estimated to be 
approximately $111,000 for Fiscal Year (FY) 2024-25.
The CDBG jurisdictions within San Mateo County have worked together to provide a consolidated application process 
online through City Data Services (CDS), so agencies may apply to several jurisdictions at the same time. Each jurisdiction 
sets its own criteria for selection. The applicants and agencies that are selected will be awarded two-year grants for their 
programs. The second year of funding is contingent upon meeting all contractual obligations.
On September 13, 2023, the Community Relations Commission (CRC) held a community meeting to identify the Housing, 
Community Development and Public Service needs of San Mateo. At that meeting, the CRC established the following 
priorities for the Community Funding program:
•
Basic Human Needs
•
Youth Services
•
Senior Services
•
Preserving or Providing Affordable Housing
Last year, the FY 2023-24 CDBG budget for the public services program was $116,100, and the following applicants were 
selected to receive funding for their programs:
•
CALL Primrose - $12,900
•
Community Overcoming Relationship Abuse - $12,900
•
Friends for Youth - $12,900
•
Legal Aid Society of San Mateo County - $12,900
•
LifeMoves - $12,900
•
Ombudsman Services of San Mateo County, Inc - $12,900
•
Rape Trauma Services: A Center for Healing and Violence Prevention - $12,900

PROPOSAL REVIEW:
The City received a total of 14 applications seeking CDBG funding, with eight for public services, two for economic 
development/microenterprise, three for minor home repair programs, and one for fair housing services. Only the eight 
proposals that fall under the public services category will need to compete for funding under the 15% CDBG public service 
cap. City staff reviewed each application for completeness and confirmed CDBG eligibility. All organizations meet the 
requirements, with notes in the summary worksheet to support these findings. Elements of each proposal are 
consolidated on the Proposal Evaluation Summary 2024-25 (Attachment 1) as well as indicators of past and present goal 
performance.
The first step in the selection process is for the CRC to review and assign a preliminary score to each application. These 
scores are not binding, but provide the CRC with background information and a starting point for further discussion. The 
total amount of the funding being requested in these eight applications is $147,600.
The final recommendations on which proposals will receive funding will be a result of the CRC’s consideration of the 
quality of each proposal, how well it addresses the needs of the community, individual commissioner perspectives based 
on their community knowledge and experience, responses to any clarification questions to applicant agencies or public 
testimony, and the organizational experience and capacity of the applicant agency. These recommendations will be 
incorporated into San Mateo’s Annual Action Plan that will be approved by the City Council later this year in May.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The final CDBG entitlement to the City for the 2024-25 program year has not yet been confirmed by HUD, but staff is 
estimating $111,000 for this funding cycle. Future budget changes may impact the funding available for both program 
years and contracts will be amended in consideration of funding available. Since the anticipated CDBG funding for this year 
is less than the total amount being requested by the eight applicants, the CRC will need to determine how funding should 
be allocated, consistent priorities for the Community Funding program. Staff recommends that if an amount less than the 
applicant’s full request is granted, that the minimum be no less than $15,000. With those considerations, the CRC should 
provide recommendations for 2024-2025 CDBG funding for public service applications utilizing one of the following 
approaches:
•
Award grants, up to $111,000, to the applications that best meet the CRC’s public services criteria;
•
Award equal grants of $15,700 to the top seven applications; or
•
Award grant funding utilizing a different approach.
All grants will be subject to contract execution in compliance with HUD regulations and upon final Council authorization of 
the Business Plan for the City of San Mateo.

cause a physical change in the environment. (Public Resources Code Section 21065.)
