SUBJECT:
Appeal of a Zoning Administrator Decision to Approve a Third Story Addition to an Existing Two-story Single-Family 
Residence at 619 W. 39th Avenue (PA-2023-017).
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to deny the appeal of the Zoning Administrator’s decision to approve the Single-Family Dwelling Design 
Review (SFDDR) for a 438 square-foot third story addition to an existing two-story single-family residence at 619 W. 39th 
Avenue, based on the findings and subject to the conditions of approval.
BACKGROUND:
On March 20, 2023, Steve Lesley (Applicant), submitted Planning Application (PA) PA-2023-017 for a third story addition to 
an existing two-story, single-family residence, and worked with the City’s Development Review Board to address code 
requirements and consistency with design guidelines for the proposed project. Following a duly noticed public review 
period in which one comment letter was received, the Zoning Administrator approved the PA on January 3, 2024 
(Attachment 2). On January 12, 2024, Katherine Doughty (Appellant), a neighboring property owner, filed an appeal of the 
Zoning Administrator decision, citing concerns related to view and privacy impacts from the proposed addition and 
consistency with the surrounding neighborhood. The appeal form and accompanying letter are included as Attachment 3. 
San Mateo Municipal Code (SMMC) Section 27.08.090 authorizes the Planning Commission to take action on an appeal of 
a Zoning Administrator decision. Per the staff recommendation, a resolution to deny the appeal, based on the findings and 
conditions of approval, is included as Attachment 1.
Project Description
The project site is located at 619 W. 39th Avenue, southwest of Alameda de las Pulgas, on an upsloping lot. The site is 
zoned R1-B (One Family Dwellings) and has a parcel area of 6,165 square feet. A location map showing the project site and 
its vicinity is included as Attachment 4. 
The project is a 438 square-foot third story addition to an existing 1,891 square-foot two-story, single-family residence 
with an attached 693 square-foot two-car garage. The project also includes a 101 square-foot remodel of the second story 
and the legalization of an unpermitted 29 square-foot bathroom. Project plans showing the proposed site plan, floor plans, 
elevations and privacy diagrams are included in Attachment 5. 
Zoning Code and Design Review
The project is subject to the requirements of SMMC Chapter 27.18 for (R1 Districts – One-Family Dwellings), which 
includes development standards for floor area ratio (FAR), building height, daylight plane, and setbacks. The overall 
proposed floor area of 3,022 square feet is within the maximum allowable floor area of 3,033 square feet (0.5 FAR) for the 
property. The project proposes an overall building height of 27 feet, six inches measured to roof peak, and 24 feet

measured to the highest plate-line, both of which are measured from existing grade. These measurements are within the 
District’s height limits of 32 feet to roof peak and 24 feet to building plate line. The R1B District does not have a story limit, 
so a third story is allowed if it is within the specified height limits.  The project’s compliance with all applicable Zoning Code 
requirements is summarized in the Project Data Sheet (Attachment 6). 
The proposed construction of an upper story addition requires a Single-Family Dwelling Design Review (SFDDR) in 
accordance with SMMC 27.08.032. This review includes evaluating the project for compliance with the Zoning Code and 
consistency with the Single-Family Design Guidelines (Design Guidelines). Staff found the project to be in substantial 
conformance with the Design Guidelines because it is well integrated into the existing architectural character of the 
neighborhood and does not change the current configuration of the site, it is compatible with the existing residence and 
the surrounding neighborhood design character, and the addition is designed to minimize impacts to neighboring views 
and privacy. Additional details about the project’s conformance with the Design Guidelines are provided in the Resolution 
(Attachment 1).  
DISCUSSION:
Appeal
The appellant owns a neighboring property at 3909 Marshall Avenue, located upslope and to the rear of the project site. 
As noted in the letter provided with the Appeal Form, the appeal is focused on two areas of concern with respect to the 
City’s Single-Family Dwelling Design Guidelines: 1) conformance with the existing neighborhood, and 2) impacts caused to 
neighboring views and privacy. The Appellant also raises other concerns not regulated by the Design Guidelines or Zoning 
Code as explained below. Staff reviewed the Appellant’s concerns and the project in accordance with the Design 
Guidelines and made the following findings with regard to consistency with the Design Guidelines:
1.
Neighborhood Conformance: The Appellant expressed concerns that the addition of a third story does not conform 
with a neighborhood of existing one- and two-story homes. The Design Guidelines provide guidance on respecting 
neighborhood scale within Guideline IV.2, which states that when a common shape or size of building exists in a 
neighborhood, “respect the scale of the neighborhood through building sizes and shapes compatible with the houses 
in the neighborhood.” The Design Guidelines do not, however, place a limit on the number of stories. While third 
stories are not common in the neighborhood, the project does the following to respect the scale of the neighborhood:
a.
Complies with the Zoning District’s maximum building height of 24 feet to the plate-line and 32 feet to the roof 
peak. The project proposes a plate-line of 24 feet and a roof peak height of 27 feet, six inches.
b.
Due to the upslope of the lot, the residence will appear as a two-story residence from the side and rear 
elevations. The proposed third story also has a smaller footprint than the story below (438 square-feet 
compared to 1,891 square feet), further reducing the visual massing of the new upper story.
c.
The addition will maintain the existing materials (vertical board siding and brick veneer) and hipped roof shape 
of the home. These materials and roof design are commonly found throughout the neighborhood. The hipped 
roof further reduces the visible mass of the third-story addition.
2.
View and Privacy Impacts: The Appellant also expressed concerns over loss of view and privacy impacts from the upper 
story windows looking into neighboring yards and primary living areas, with the letter noting that the addition can be 
accomplished in ways other than through a third story addition. However, the project meets all applicable Zoning Code 
requirements, including the height limit, and the Zoning Administrator determined that the project substantially 
conforms with the Design Guidelines and limits impacts from the upper story addition as required by the Guidelines.
The Design Guidelines provide parameters for evaluating privacy and view impacts, including that windows should be 
carefully planned to account for neighboring privacy. The Design Guidelines do not prohibit view blockage but 
encourage additions to be designed to minimize view blockage, through methods such as:
a.
Configure living space where it would have less view impact; 
b.
Increase the setback of second story additions; 
c.
Lower roof plate heights; and,
d.
Choose roof forms that minimize mass.

To reduce impacts to views for neighboring properties uphill to the rear and right of the project site, the project 
locates the addition above the left-side of the existing second story. The addition has a relatively small footprint (438 
square feet) and a hipped roof with a slope of 4:12, both of which help to minimize the apparent massing and view 
impacts. The proposed third story windows are also offset from neighboring windows and do not present privacy 
impacts for neighboring yards and living areas. 
To visually demonstrate view impacts caused by the project, the applicant voluntarily installed story poles to 
demonstrate the volume and height of the proposed addition. Photographs of the story poles are shown on sheet A11 
of the project plans (Attachment 5), which are taken from neighboring living areas facing the project, including from a 
dining room window at 3909 Marshall Avenue (the Appellant’s property). The images taken from this view show that 
the proposed addition would be visible from the subject property and affect a portion of the view downhill. However, 
primary views toward the hills on either side and over the proposed addition are preserved. 
The Design Guidelines also encourage view equity where no single parcel should enjoy a greater view than other 
similar parcels. The proposed project does not appear to compromise the view of a neighboring parcel to enjoy a 
greater view, given that the project site is downhill from the Appellant’s property and designed with sensitivity to the 
existing views from neighboring properties.
3.
Other Concerns: The appeal letter speaks to other concerns not regulated by the City’s Design Guidelines or Zoning 
Code. The appellant notes a possible fire safety issue due to vegetation at the rear of the applicant’s property growing 
up into the power lines above. The appellant has raised this concern with the subject property owner and PG&E. It is 
staff’s understanding that the applicant will be responsible for maintaining the vegetation in this area to ensure fire 
safety standards are met. The letter also asserts that the addition will create a wind tunnel effect, however, the Zoning 
Code and Design Guidelines do not regulate this type issue for single-family projects. Lastly, the letter notes the 
appellant did not receive notice of the Neighborhood Informational Meeting until after the meeting took place, which 
was held on January 23, 2023. Mailed notices are required to be sent out at least ten days prior to the meeting date. 
The applicant’s application materials state that the notices were mailed out on January 11, 2023, and the City’s copy of 
the notice has a postmark date of January 12, 2023, which confirm that the ten-day requirement was met. The notice 
for the neighborhood meeting also states that neighbors can arrange an alternative time to meet with the applicant if 
they are unable to attend the neighborhood meeting, and the appellant confirmed that they received the notice and 
have been aware of the project but were not able to attend the Neighborhood Informational Meeting. The appellant 
also received the required legal notice of the pending Zoning Administrator decision. 
As discussed above and in the Resolution (Attachment 1), the project meets all applicable Zoning Code requirements and is 
in substantial conformance with the Single-Family Design Guidelines. Therefore, staff recommends that the Planning 
Commission find that the project meets all applicable policies and regulations, deny the appeal, and uphold the Zoning 
Administrator’s decision to approve the SFDDR for a third-story addition at 619 W. 39th Avenue.
