SUBJECT:
Recreation Management Software Mobile Application Development – Feedback 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the development of a new recreation management software mobile application and provide 
feedback on design and navigation elements.
BACKGROUND:
The department currently uses Vermont Systems, Inc. – RecTrac and WebTrac (web-based) software for the registration of 
recreation classes, facility reservations, ticket sales, and many other processes. Last year Vermont Systems, Inc. announced 
they were partnering with Innovatise, GmbH to provide a white labeled mobile application (app) solution for its users. 
Innovastise is a pioneer in mobile marketing and the creator of myFitApp, an open, flexible, independent and digital 
marketing platform that seamlessly integrates with WebTrac and is customizable to each organization. 
The mobile app will add value to our users as it will provide access to online registration regardless of access to a 
computer. Staff have embarked on the development of this app and before moving on to the execution, we are requesting 
the commission’s feedback on some navigation and design elements. We will be using our WebTrac site 
(www.sanmateorec.org) to guide this conversation as we intend for the app to be a mirror of the site. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact associated with this presentation.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This presentation is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that will not 
result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)

SUBJECT:
Proposed Parks and Recreation Fee Schedule for Fiscal Year 2024-25
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of the proposed Fiscal Year (FY) 2024-25 fee schedule for Parks and Recreation 
facilities and miscellaneous fees as provided in Attachment 1 of the Administrative Report.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City reviews and adjusts fees. Generally, fees are adjusted based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for the 
previous year. The Finance Department has identified the official CPI for this year to be 2.8%. The recommended fee 
changes for the Parks and Recreation Department are detailed in Attachment 1. The department has in most cases applied 
increases that are within the acceptable range. 
Picnic Reservations (Page 3):
A notable change this year is that we have classified our picnic rentals in small, medium and large categories in alignment 
with our web page listings of these sites. Several fees have been rounded up, to the nearest whole dollar amount.  Due to 
community interest for food vendors at picnic sites, a food vendor fee has been added.  Lastly a picnic rescheduling and a 
picnic cancellation fee have been added for picnic rentals to cover administrative costs of cancelling and rescheduling.
Indoor Reservations (Page 3):
An alcohol permit fee will be implemented for indoor reservations consistent with that of our picnic rentals. Indoor facility 
rentals with permitted alcohol will have a refundable deposit payable in case contracted rental hours are exceeded and/or 
there are facility damages. The language for our Classification A rentals has been updated to reflect prior approval by 
Director or approved delegate. Clarifying language has been added for facility rentals to reflect the need and fees 
associated with staffing a facility attendant and custodian during non-operational hours.  In the facility category table, 
facilities that are no longer available or are duplicates have been removed.  Rooms that are now available for rental have 
been added.  Services that we no longer offer have also been removed. Language pertaining to school rentals outside of 
San Mateo Union High School District and San Mateo-Foster City School District has been clarified.
 Athletics / Fields (Page 4):
Staffing fees for athletic events and activities such as tournaments have been added to the fee schedule.  This is to reflect 
the need for on-call staff to manage access and care for the facility for the booking.  In addition, tournament fees have

PARKS AND RECREATION
RECREATION COST RECOVERY AND PRICING POLICY*
The following shall serve as the policy guidance for establishing a Recreation Activities Cost Recovery Plan, developing annual activity budgets, and establishing fees to be charged for recreation activities. The specific 
elements of this policy are that:
The Park and Recreation Cost Recovery and Pricing Model shall be used.
In recognition of the wide variety of service values and revenue generating potentials of various activities, specific cost recovery thresholds shall be established for each recreation activity area at the 
most specific programming level that is administratively practical.
As part of the budgetary process, the Park and Recreation Commission shall review the Cost Recovery Plan and the performance of each activity compared to the plan and make any recommendations 
for changes to the plan.
Recreation Cost Tier Definitions
Direct: The specific “go-away” costs at the course or activity level. These include leaders, instructors and materials or services (e.g. transportation) specific to the course or activity.
Program Direct Overhead (DOH): Direct supervision of the program, and minor miscellaneous items such as supervisor mileage.
Facility Overhead (FOH): The combination of facility direct costs (the day-to-day costs to keep the building open- receptionist, custodial services, custodial products, utilities) and 
facility direct overhead (primarily facility supervision).
Operating Indirect Overhead (Op IOH): Division level and shared costs, such as departmental management, brochure, printing, photocopying, training, accounting support, 
technology, credit card discount costs, administrative office clerical support, computer, and recreation equipment sinking fund charges
The thresholds shall include both minimum and target levels of cost recovery.
Strategies for achieving the minimum and target thresholds shall be established by staff for each activity area. These strategies may include immediate or gradual action depending upon the best 
interests of maintaining a strong program, achieving the target within a reasonable time period and current fiscal conditions.
Activity areas unable to meet their minimum threshold after these strategies have been applied shall be reviewed by the Park and Recreation Commission for consideration of recommending 
appropriate action: reduce costs; reduce cost recovery target; eliminate activity.
Activities that have achieved or exceeded their target threshold shall be evaluated to determine if the target can/should be increased.
 The Cost Recovery Plan shall, where appropriate, also include specific other fee considerations (e.g. benchmarking to a specific competitor) that may supersede cost recovery target considerations.
Once a Cost Recovery Plan is established, and within that context, staff shall have the authority to set and adjust fees, except that no fee increase shall exceed 20% within a 1-year period without the 
review of the Park and Recreation Commission and approval of the City Council.
