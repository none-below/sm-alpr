SUBJECT:
Measure S Independent Accountant's Report - Review and Accept
RECOMMENDATION:
Accept the Independent Accountant's Report on Agreed-Upon Procedures Applied to Schedule of Measure S Revenues and 
Expenditures for the year ended June 30, 2023
BACKGROUND:
The City’s 1/4 cent transactions and use tax was first approved by San Mateo voters in 2009 via. Measure L, which was set 
to expire on March 31, 2018.  In November 2015, San Mateo voters approved Measure S, which extended the ¼ cent 
transactions and use tax through March 31, 2048.
In compliance with San Mateo Municipal Code 3.50.160, the Measure S Oversight Committee reviews the annual auditor's 
report.  As noted in the report for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2023, the auditor noted no exceptions to the agreed-upon 
procedures.  
The Measure S sub-fund recorded $7.3 million in total revenues, $2 million in total expenditures, and $3.5 million in 
transfers out, resulting in an increase of $1.8 million to the fund balance.  The Measure S sub-fund concluded the 2022-23 
fiscal year with a fund balance of $10.7 million, of which $6.3 million has been committed to capital improvement projects 
and the remaining $4.4 million is unrestricted fund. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budgetary impact resulting from this report.

commitment to any specific project which may result in a potentially significant physical impact on the environment. 
(CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(4).)

We have performed the procedures enumerated below on the accompanying Schedule of Measure S Revenues and 
Expenditures (the “schedule”) of the City of San Mateo, California (the “City”) for the year ended June 30, 2023. The 
City is responsible for the schedule.  
 
The City has agreed to and acknowledged that the procedures performed are appropriate to meet the intended purpose 
of meeting the requirements of City Ordinance 2015-11, Amending Chapter 3.50 of the City’s Municipal Code, 
“Transactions and Use Tax” To Extend the One-Quarter Cent Transactions and Use Tax Term for Thirty Years”. The

whether the procedures performed are appropriate for their purposes.

Years”.

SUBJECT:
Measure S Five-Year Forecast | Funding Requests for 2024-25
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive Measure S five-year forecast and review funding requests for 2024-25
BACKGROUND:
Based on current economic conditions and operational analysis, staff has updated the five-year forecast for the Measure S 
Fund, providing critical insight into fiscal sustainability beyond the current budget cycle.
Using the 2023-24 adopted budget as the baseline, revenue assumptions for the 1/4 cent transaction and use tax revenue 
are conservatively set at a modest rate ranging from 1% to 2%. It is projected that the fund’s revenues will grow from the 
current $7.3 million to $8.3 million over five years.
Measure S has historically supported Police, Parks & Recreation, and Library operations, with expenditures projected to 
grow steadily by 3% to 5% annually. To maintain the same service levels in fiscal year 2024-25, Measure S will need to 
provide approximately $2.2 million.
