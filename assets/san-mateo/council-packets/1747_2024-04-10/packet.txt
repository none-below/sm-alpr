SUBJECT:
Bicycle and Pedestrian Projects Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, acting as the City of San Mateo's Bicycle and Pedestrian Advisory 
Committee, receive an update on bicycle and pedestrian projects.
BACKGROUND:
Staff will present brief updates on the pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure projects and planning efforts.
Infrastructure Projects
Delaware Street Safe Routes to School Corridor: Staff was awarded funds under Cycle 5 of the California Transportation 
Commission’s (CTC) Active Transportation Program (ATP) for the Delaware Street Safe Routes to School bicycle project. 
This project will implement the Delaware Street Class IV separated bike lane and Class III bicycle boulevard as proposed in 
the 2020 Bicycle Master Plan. This project provides necessary safety improvements in an area with a high level of traffic 
stress and will improve safety and connectivity to two public schools accessed from Delaware Street. Further, this project 
improves north-west connectivity to the relocated Hillsdale Caltrain station. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant team are developing the preliminary concept design and will be conducting outreach 
in early summer to collect feedback on the design. Outreach will include pop-up events and direct engagement 
with staff from the San Mateo Foster City School District and schools near the project area.
28th Avenue Gap Closure Project: Staff was awarded funds from the San Mateo County Transportation Authority 
Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Cycle 5 to fund the design and environmental review for the 28th Avenue/Hillsdale 
Caltrain Station Bicycle Access Gap Closure project. The intent of the project is to evaluate options and develop design 
plans for the Class IV separated bike lane between Edison Street and El Camino Real identified in the 2020 Bicycle Master 
Plan and provide a safe path of travel for pedestrians and bicyclists across the El Camino Real intersection. The following is 
an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant team presented a summary of the November 2023 community workshop and an 
overview of design alternatives to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in January 2024. 
•
The project team is developing 35% design plans for Alternative C, which includes Class II bike lanes in each 
direction, bus boarding islands under the Caltrain tracks, and a pedestrian priority signal at the Delaware and 28th 
Avenue intersection.
•
Additional in-person community engagement is planned to collect feedback on the 35% design from the 
community.

Pavement Management Program Integration: Staff continues to integrate the implementation of bicycle facilities with 
scheduled pavement management projects where funding for design and construction is available and feasible. The 
following is an update on this effort. 
•
Pavement Management Program 5-B Project: This project includes installation of a bicycle boulevard on S. Grant 
Street from Concar Drive to 16th Avenue and will assess options for implementing traffic calming elements, 
including speed cushions, on South Grant Street. The Request for Proposals (RFP) to solicit construction services 
was released in late March and the construction contract is anticipated to be awarded in July 2024.
Poplar and Humboldt Traffic Signal Modification Project: This project will construct modifications to the traffic signals at 
the intersection of Poplar Avenue and Humboldt Street to implement protected left turn movements in all directions. The 
project will eliminate the conflict between left-turning vehicles and pedestrians at this intersection which has had seven 
recorded collisions involving pedestrians and left-turning vehicles between 2017 to 2021. Several of the traffic signal poles 
will be replaced to add the necessary left turn arrow signals. 
•
The Request for Proposals (RFP) for construction services is expected to be released in late spring 2024 with 
construction anticipated in summer and fall 2024.
School Zone Speed Limits: City Council’s strategic plan prioritizes implementation of bicycle and pedestrian projects that 
address safety issues. Part of this objective includes evaluating school zone speed limit reductions to address quality of-life 
and safety within neighborhoods. Speed limit reductions in school zones can help reduce the risk of severe or fatal 
conflicts between vehicles and active transportation users (e.g., cyclists and pedestrians) and can also help promote 
students and families walking or biking to school. City Council adopted a resolution in December 2021 identifying a total of 
38 roadway segments contiguous to 23 schools in San Mateo that qualify for speed limit reductions and authorized the 
reduction of posted speed limits in these zones. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
The construction contract for Phases 2 (Private elementary and middle schools) and 3 (High schools) was awarded 
at the January 2, 2024 City Council meeting. 
•
Construction for Phases 2 and 3 began in late March 2024 and is expected to be complete in early summer 2024.
19th Avenue/Fashion Island Class IV Bicycle Facility Project: This project is part of a joint effort between the City, San 
Mateo County Transportation Authority (SMCTA), and SamTrans to design and construct multimodal improvements as part 
of the 101/92 Interchange Project. The project includes feasibility, design, and construction of Class IV separated bicycle 
lanes on 19th Avenue and Fashion Island Boulevard, including intersection improvements, and is expected to connect 
directly to a proposed Mobility Hub at the existing Caltrans Park and Ride location being constructed in parallel. In addition 
to the proposed Class IV separated bikeway, the project includes smart corridor elements such as smart streetlights, air 
quality monitoring, traffic signal detection enhancements, and smart kiosks and digital bus stops with real-time transit 
information. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
Staff and the consultant team have developed conceptual alternatives for implementation of the bicycle facility, 
smart corridor elements, and the mobility hub.
•
The project team will be hosting a community workshop to collect feedback on the design alternatives in May 
2024. Additional information on the workshop will be shared closer to the date.
Hillsdale Boulevard at Alameda de las Pulgas Traffic Signal Project: This project will assess and design traffic signal 
operations at the Hillsdale Boulevard and Alameda de las Pulgas intersection to optimize safety for all modes, including 
pedestrians from nearby schools and bicyclists using the existing Class II bicycle lanes on Hillsdale Boulevard. The design 
aims to balance the needs of both current and future users and minimize congestion at the intersection. The following is 
an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant developed two conceptual design alternatives: a Pedestrian Enhancement Concept 
and a Protected Intersection Concept. 
o
Pedestrian Concept: Provides a more standard traffic signal implementation with improvements that 
generally enhance safety for pedestrians. 
o
Protected Intersection Concept: This is a relatively newer design prioritizing safety for both pedestrians 
and bicyclists. It provides greater protection at the intersection and lower stress crossing experiences 
through use of setbacks and space delineation.

•
The project team hosted a community workshop at the Hilldale Branch Library in late January. The 60 attendees 
were asked to review the design alternatives and identify preferred and less preferred elements for each 
alternative. The community expressed mixed feedback about the elements proposed in the project, including the 
need for a signal and the proposed curb extensions. The majority of attendees agreed that there were safety and 
congestion issues, such as the long crossing distances across Alameda de las Pulgas, crash history at the site, and 
the desire for crossing guards to improve safety for school age children. 
•
City staff presented the design alternatives to three parent-teacher school associations (PTA) (Hillsdale High 
School, Laurel Elementary School, and Abbott Middle School) in February and March 2024.
•
An online survey was posted on the project’s webpage in early March to gather additional input on the design 
alternatives. The survey closed on April 9, 2024.
•
City staff intend to hold a follow-up, informational community workshop to present the findings from the online 
survey and feedback heard from the PTA meetings and community workshop. 
•
The project team anticipates presenting an update to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in summer 
2024.
Downtown Parking Garage Pedestrian Safety Project: This project aims to improve pedestrian safety at the entrances and 
exits of Central Parking Garage, Main Street Parking Garage, and the 2nd and El Camino Real Parking Garage. The project’s 
design was completed in 2022 and incorporates feedback from the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, City staff 
from Public Works and Police Department, and the community through an online community survey. The design includes 
pedestrian-friendly improvements at and near the parking garage entrances and exits such as audible and visual 
pedestrian and vehicle warning devices, flexible delineators to provide physical separation between the drive aisle and 
walking paths, speed bumps, and updated pavement markings and traffic signs.
•
Improvements at the 2nd and El Camino Parking Garage will be installed as part of the 2nd and El Camino Parking 
Garage Improvement Project which will rehabilitate and remodel the parking garage. The planning application for 
the improvement project is currently under review.
•
The Request for Proposals (RFP) to solicit construction services for implementation of pedestrian safety 
improvements at Central Parking Garage and Main Street Garage closed on April 2, 2024. The construction 
contract is anticipated to be awarded this summer.
Planning Projects 
Citywide Complete Streets Plan: A collaborative team of staff from Public Works, Community Development, and the City 
Manager’s Office submitted an application to the Caltrans Sustainable Communities FY 2021-22 Grant Program for the San 
Mateo Complete Streets Plan and was awarded funding for the project in June 2021. This project brings together the City’s 
various active transportation plans, Local Roadway Safety Plan, and Green Infrastructure Plan to fully envision a 
multimodal transportation network and identify priority improvements for the City. The following is an update on this 
effort.  
•
City staff and the consultant team finalized the top ten priority corridors and kicked off the last phase of 
community engagement in February 2024. 
•
The project team held district-focused community workshops in late February and early March at North 
Shoreview Montessori, San Mateo City Hall, and Fiesta Gardens International School to present and collect 
feedback on the priority corridors. Each workshop focused on improvements in the specific council district(s), 
however, community members were welcome to attend any workshop. At the workshops, attendees were 
encouraged to review each priority corridor and provide feedback. 
•
City staff and the consultant team developed an online survey to collect additional feedback on the priority 
corridors. The survey was posted on the project’s webpage in early March and closed on April 5, 2024.
•
The project team is finalizing the draft Plan and working on the draft Design Manual which will include design 
standards and guidelines for implementing transportation improvements in the Plan. 
•
The draft Plan will be presented to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission and City Council in May 2024.
Citywide Transportation Demand Management (TDM) Ordinance: The Citywide TDM Ordinance envisions a comprehensive 
approach to TDM in San Mateo by recommending trip reduction goals to be established throughout the City to support

vehicles miles traveled (VMT) reduction and the goal of a multimodal transportation network. The project will propose 
updates to the San Mateo Municipal Code and develop program guidelines to provide detail and guidance for staff and the 
development community to implement trip reduction goals for projects in San Mateo. The project will evaluate existing 
TDM policies, consider best practices from like jurisdictions, and develop a framework for policy development. The 
framework will outline specific policy aspects on which Commission and Council will provide feedback to develop the final 
policy parameters. This project is funded through a technical assistance grant from the Metropolitan Transportation 
Commission (MTC). The following is an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant team hosted two focus group meetings in late February 2024 with local developers 
and the San Mateo Chamber of Commerce to give an update on the project and collect input on the proposed 
TDM Program and strategies for engaging/educating stakeholders impacted by the requirements.
•
City staff and the consultant team presented the proposed TDM Ordinance and guidelines in a joint meeting with 
the Planning Commission and Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission on April 9, 2024.
•
The proposed TDM Ordinance will be brought to the City Council as a study session on April 15, 2024.
Local Roadway Safety Plan: The Local Roadway Safety Plan (LRSP) analyzes collision patterns in the city and uses those 
findings to recommend prioritized safety improvements that work toward the goal of reducing fatal and severe injury 
crashes. Using input from the community and the LRSP’s Safety Partners, a list of prioritized projects has been developed 
and incorporated into a draft Local Roadway Safety Plan document. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
The draft LRSP was presented to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission on March 12, 2024. 
•
The draft LRSP will be presented at the City Council Study Session meeting on April 15, 2024, with anticipated 
adoption at the May 20th City Council meeting.
Complete Streets North Central Plan: As directed by City Council at the March 20, 2023 meeting, staff developed a scope 
for a Complete Streets North Central Plan that will be completed concurrently with the Citywide Complete Streets Plan. 
The scope for the Plan includes extensive engagement with the community to understand the lived experience and 
identify needs and goals for a community-specific transportation plan. The Plan will result in the development of priority 
projects for the neighborhood to address the goals and community needs. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant team summarized the feedback received during Phase 1 of the Plan’s outreach effort 
in the Defining Community Needs and Goals memo. The document organizes over 500 unique comments received 
into consolidated key themes to ensure improvements in the Plan address the unique needs and concerns 
expressed by the community.
•
The project team began Phase 2 of community engagement, including the second of three meetings with the Lived 
Experience Group (LEG) and focus groups with Spanish-speaking residents and the Home Association of North 
Central San Mateo (HANCSM). 
•
City staff and the consultant team presented an update to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission and 
City Council in March 2024.
•
The project team hosted two community workshops, with one each in Spanish and English, on March 28, 2024 at 
the Martin Luther King Community Center in collaboration with HANCSM. Attendees were asked to validate 
transportation needs heard from previous engagement events, discuss potential improvements and trade-offs, 
and provide input on priorities for the North Central neighborhood. 
•
The project team is working to identify recommended projects and programs to respond to the needs and 
concerns expressed by the community. 
•
The project team is developing the outreach strategy for Phase 3 community engagement which will focus on 
presenting potential improvements and prioritizing projects. Outreach will include a meeting with LEG members, a 
community workshop, and various outreach events focused on gaining feedback on identified projects in North 
Central.
