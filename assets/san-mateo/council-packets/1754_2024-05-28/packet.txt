SUBJECT:
Zoning Code and Map Amendments for By-Right Housing Opportunity Overlay District
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of an Ordinance amending San Mateo Municipal Code Title 27, Zoning, to add 
Chapter 27.94, Housing Opportunities Overlay District (H), which would allow certain types of housing projects by right on 
40 specified parcels, and amending the San Mateo Zoning Map to establish the “H” overlay on 40 parcels; and find that the 
Ordinance is exempt from further environmental review pursuant to California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Section 
15183 (Projects Consistent With A Community Plan, General Plan, or Zoning).
BACKGROUND:
The City is required to prepare an update to its Housing Element to affirmatively further fair housing and accommodate 
the City’s 7,015-unit Regional Housing Needs Allocation (RHNA) for the Sixth Housing Element Cycle (2023-2031). On April 
23, 2024, the Planning Commission recommended approval of the revised Housing Element, which is scheduled for 
adoption by the City Council on May 20, 2024. If adopted, the Housing Element would then be submitted to the State 
Housing and Community Development Department (HCD) for certification. 
Since 1969, California has required that all local governments (cities and counties) adequately plan to meet the housing 
needs of their community. Local governments meet this requirement by adopting housing elements, which are updated 
every 5-8 years. The housing element is a "chapter” of a city’s General Plan, which is also required by State law. However, 
it is the only element of the General Plan subject to State requirements for content, and it must be certified by HCD. State 
funding programs for transportation, infrastructure, and housing often require or consider a local jurisdiction’s compliance 
with housing element law. In addition, non-compliance with housing element requirements and targets could potentially 
affect a local jurisdiction’s ability to impose local zoning and land use controls over housing development and receive 
certain types of State funding and grants. 
The City’s revised Housing Element identifies how the City can accommodate its RHNA of 7,015 new units over the next 
eight years, and establishes goals, policies, and programs to help address the City’s current and future housing needs. 
Under Goal 1 – Production, the City commits to facilitate and support the production of new housing at all income levels, 
but especially affordable housing. Twenty policies with 52 implementing actions have been identified to support this goal. 
One of the identified policies under the Production goal, Policy H 1.10, commits the City to establishing a by-right housing 
designation for housing opportunity sites identified in a prior housing element, pursuant to Government Code 65583.2(c). 
Specifically, the implementation action within this policy requires the City to amend the Zoning Code and establish a by-
right zoning designation for housing development projects in which at least 20 percent of the units proposed are 
affordable to lower income households on these reused sites. Zoning for these sites must allow at least 30 units per acre to

comply with the State law. These amendments need to be adopted to ensure that HCD can certify the City’s revised 
Housing Element. The adoption of the by-right ordinance was delayed beyond the statutory deadline of January 31, 2024, 
due to General Plan and Housing Element adoption hearings in early 2024. The City advised HCD of this delay and 
coordinated with HCD on the Housing Element and by-right ordinance. City staff shared a draft of the Ordinance with HCD 
for review and input on May 3, 2024, and HCD provided preliminary approval on May 6, 2024. 
DISCUSSION: 
To implement the General Plan and Housing Element policies, State law requires that the City review and update the 
Zoning Code to ensure that the review processes and requirements are compatible with the objectives, policies, and 
programs specified in the General Plan. San Mateo Municipal Code section 27.060.040(c) requires the Planning 
Commission to review amendments to the Zoning Code and make a recommendation to the City Council. 
The proposed zoning code amendments (Attachment 1) would create a new chapter in the Zoning Code, namely Chapter 
27.94, Housing Opportunities Overlay District (H) (“Ordinance”). The Ordinance would create eligibility parameters and 
review processes for housing development projects that meet the by-right requirements of Government Code 65583.2(c) 
and Policy 1.10 of the 2023-2031 Housing Element. The Ordinance also amends the Zoning Map to create an H Overlay 
District on 40 identified parcels that were listed in prior housing elements as opportunity sites and thus qualify for this 
streamlined review process through the establishment of this overlay district. These sites include 35 nonvacant sites that 
were identified in the prior housing element and five (5) vacant sites that have been included in two or more consecutive 
housing elements and were not approved for development. See Attachment 2 for a Parcel Disposition Table and a Map of 
these sites. 
The proposed zoning code amendments would also allow housing development projects that propose 20% of the units as 
affordable to lower income households to be reviewed and approved without discretionary review or environmental 
review under the California Environmental Quality Act, pursuant to Government Code Section 65583.2(i). Qualifying 
housing projects compliant with the City’s Municipal Code and Objective Design Standards (approved by the Council on 
November 20, 2023) would be subject to Zoning Administrator approval without discretionary review or public hearings. 
All applicable standard conditions of approval may still be imposed on these projects. Since this approval would be 
ministerial, the Zoning Administrator decision for such projects would be final, and thus could not be appealed to the 
Planning Commission or City Council. This new process would allow further streamlining during the review process for all 
by-right housing projects proposed at these sites. 
Collectively, if developed at 30 units per acre, the 40 identified sites would result in 1,007 units, out of which 220 units 
would be affordable to the lower income households. The sites may be developed at higher or lower densities as allowed 
by the underlying base zoning. All requirements and standards associated with the underlying base zoning would remain 
unchanged, and existing permitted uses would not be affected by the creation of the H Overlay District. 
The zoning designation for by-right residential uses allowing 30 dwelling units per acre is within the density limits of the 
land use designations of the San Mateo General Plan 2040, and as set by Measure Y (the City’s voter approved density 
limits). The proposed code amendments implement the Goals, Policies, and Programs of the 2023-2031 Housing Element. 
Therefore, the proposed Ordinance is consistent with the City’s adopted General Plan. 
Next Steps 
Following the Planning Commission public hearing and recommendation, the City Council will hold a public hearing to 
consider introducing the Ordinance, which is tentatively scheduled in June/July 2024. If the Council introduces the 
Ordinance at the public hearing, it will be brought back for adoption at a subsequent City Council meeting and would 
become effective after 30 days. The final adopted version of the Ordinance will be shared with HCD to facilitate 
certification of the Housing Element. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Updates to the City’s Zoning Code are fully funded in the Community Development Department’s annual budget with no 
additional impact to the General Fund.

ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
The Environmental Impact Report (“EIR”) for the San Mateo General Plan 2040 analyzed new residential development of 
up to 19,764 new housing units and growth around the City through 2040.  On March 18, 2024, the City Council certified 
the EIR.  
In accordance with California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Guidelines Section 15183, the zoning amendment to 
establish a by-right residential overlay district and reclassify parcels with the overlay district (“project”) is exempt from 
further environmental review because the Project is consistent with the land use designation for the sites adopted as part 
of the General Plan 2040 for which an EIR was certified.  Also, the residential development density allowed by the 
Ordinance is consistent with the development density established by zoning or general plan policies.  
Applying Section 15183, there are no project specific effects that are peculiar to the project or the sites; there are no 
project specific impacts that the General Plan Update EIR failed to analyze as significant effects; there are no potentially 
significant off-site and cumulative impacts that the general plan EIR failed to evaluate; and there is no substantial new 
information which results in more severe impacts than anticipated by the general plan EIR.  Furthermore, any future 
development of sites specified in these code amendments would not constitute a “project” for CEQA purposes due to their 
non-discretionary nature, pursuant to Government Code Section 65583.2(i).  A detailed analysis in support of this CEQA 
determination is included as Attachment 3.

SECTION 1. Legislative Findings.  At a duly noticed public hearing on _______, 2024, the City Council of 
the City of San Mateo reviewed and considered the accompanying staff report and attachments thereto, 
the exemption from environmental review, together with comments received during the public review 
process, and hereby finds and determines that the proposed ordinance is consistent with the adopted 
General Plan in that it supports the development of housing and achieves the General Plan’s stated 
goals and policies.
SECTION 2. Chapter 27.94 is added to Title 27 of the San Mateo Municipal Code as follows:
Chapter 27.94 Housing Opportunities Overlay District (H)  
27.94.005 Purpose
The purpose of the Housing Opportunities Overlay District (H) is to establish by-right residential uses on 
sites identified to accommodate a portion of the housing need for lower income housing in the General 
Plan Housing Element, pursuant to Government Code Sections 65583.2(c) and (i).  This chapter 
establishes minimum development requirements and procedures for reviewing and approval of the by-
right residential development uses.
27.94.010 By-Right Residential Use Eligibility
The following housing development projects are eligible for by-right approval.  Housing development 
projects that are ineligible for by-right approval shall be subject to the requirements for residential 
development in the underlying zoning district. 
(a) Pursuant to Government Code Section 65583.2(c) and (h), housing development projects in 
which at least 20 percent of the units proposed are affordable to lower income households, as defined 
in Health and Safety Code Section 50079.5, and is a household whose income is equal to or less than 
eighty percent of the area median income, as published by the California Department of Housing and 
Community Development.  The housing development project shall be allowed at the density allowed in 
the underlying zoning district and shall be allowed a minimum density of at least 30 dwelling units per 
acre. A “housing development project” shall have the same meaning as defined in Government Code 
Section 65589.5(h)(2).  
27.94.020 By-Right Residential Use Requirements
(a) Site plan and architectural review approval is required under Chapter 27.08, Rules of 
Procedure. The Zoning Administrator shall be the approval authority for projects proposed pursuant to 
this chapter that comply with the City’s Municipal Code and Objective Design Standards. The Zoning 
Administrator shall not exercise any discretion in the review process for such projects and their decision 
shall be final.    
(b) The site plan and architectural review shall not constitute a “project” for the purposes of 
Division 13 (commencing with Section 21000) of the Public Resources Code.

--- page 16 ---
(c) Any subdivision of the site shall be subject to all laws, including, but not limited to Title 26 of 
the City of San Mateo Municipal Code (Subdivisions). 
(d) Notwithstanding Section 27.94.010, the following objective development standards shall 
apply to housing development projects built pursuant to this chapter:
(1) Development standards set forth in the underlying zoning district.
(2) For a project within another overlay district, the development standards of the overlay 
district.
(3) For development projects within an E2 Zoning District without an overlay district, the R4 
development standards.
(4) For development projects within a Specific Plan or within the TOD zoning district, the 
applicable development standards of the Specific Plan or TOD zoning district.
(5) Objective Design Standards for multi-family and residential mixed-use projects as adopted 
by City Council resolution.
(6) Applicable standard conditions of approval.
SECTION 3. Environmental Determination. In accordance with California Environmental Quality Act 
(CEQA) Guidelines Section 15183, the zoning amendment to establish a by-right residential overlay 
district and reclassify parcels with the overlay district (“project”) is exempt from further environmental 
review because the Project is consistent with the land use designation for the sites adopted as part of 
the General Plan 2040 for which an Environmental Impact Report (EIR) was certified by the City Council 
on March 18, 2024 and the allowed residential development density is also consistent with the 
development density established by zoning or general plan policies.  There are no project specific effects 
that are peculiar to the project or the sites; there are no project specific impacts that the General Plan 
Update EIR failed to analyze as significant effects; there are no potentially significant off-site and 
cumulative impacts that the general plan EIR failed to evaluate; and there is no substantial new 
information which results in more severe impacts than anticipated by the general plan EIR, all as further 
explained in the CEQA memorandum attached to the staff report. Furthermore, any future development 
of the sites pursuant to these code amendments would not constitute a “project” for purposes of 
Division 13 (commencing with Section 21000) of the Public Resources Code pursuant to Government 
Code Section 65583.2(i).

California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Exemption Evaluation 
Project Name: Housing Opportunities Overlay District Ordinance Amendments
Project Location: 40 Parcels Citywide
Lead Agency: City of San Mateo – Community Development Department
Date: May 22, 2024
Purpose
The purpose of this document is to evaluate the required findings under California Environmental 
Quality Act (CEQA) Guidelines Section 15183 (Projects Consistent with a Community Plan or Zoning) 
for purposes of assessing environmental impacts associated with proposed zoning ordinance and 
map amendments in order to establish a Housing Opportunities Overlay District, consistent with 
State law and City’s 2023-2031 Housing Element Policy 1.10.  Pursuant to CEQA Statute and 
Guidelines Section 15183, projects which are consistent with the development density established by 
existing zoning, community plan, or general plan policies for which an Environmental Impact Report 
(EIR) was certified shall not require additional environmental review, except as might be necessary to 
examine whether there are project-specific significant effects which are peculiar to the project or its 
site. This streamlines the review of such projects and reduces the need to prepare repetitive 
environmental studies.
Background
San Mateo General Plan 2040 EIR
As part of the City’s comprehensive general plan update process, an EIR was prepared in compliance 
with the CEQA Statute and Guidelines (California Code of Regulations §15000 et. seq.) and the 
regulations and policies of the City for the San Mateo General Plan 2040. On March 18, 2024, the City 
Council certified the Final EIR for the San Mateo General Plan 2040 and adopted the General Plan 
2040.  One of the primary purposes of General Plan 2040 is to plan for the growth and conservation 
of the City of San Mateo over a 20-year horizon. General Plan 2040 identified the location and 
allowed density and intensity of San Mateo’s land uses including housing and identified sufficient 
residential land to meet the current and future housing needs for people at all income levels. The 
General Plan 2040 EIR included buildout projections that included growth associated with current 
development projects in the planning entitlement pipeline and development of sites in the City’s 
Housing Element 2023-2031 Housing Sites Inventory. According to the buildout projections, the 
General Plan EIR accounted for a total of 21,410 housing units.

alternatives or feasible mitigation measures that would substantially lessen the significant impacts of

Proposed Project
The proposed project includes zoning ordinance and map amendments in order to establish a 
Housing Opportunities Overlay District, consistent with State law and City’s 2023-2031 Housing 
Element Policy 1.10. Policy H 1.10, commits the City to establishing a by-right housing designation 
(i.e., nondiscretionary review and approval) for prior housing opportunity sites identified in a prior 
housing element, pursuant to Government Code 65583.2(c). Specifically, the implementation action 
within this policy requires the City to amend the Zoning Code and establish a by-right zoning 
designation for housing development project in which at least 20 percent of the units proposed are 
affordable to lower income households on these reused sites and zoning for these sites must allow at 
least 30 units per acre to meet default density requirements for lower-income households comply 
with the State law.  
To implement the Housing Element Policy and compliance with Government Code 65583.2(c), the 
City is proposing to create a new chapter to amend Title 27, Zoning, of the San Mateo Municipal 
Code to establish the Housing Opportunities Overlay District (H) and to amend the Zoning Map of the 
City of San Mateo to reclassify 40 parcels that were previously identified as housing opportunity 
sites, with the “H” designation to allow by-right housing development. In addition to the zoning 
designation requiring the housing development provide for a minimum of 20 percent of the units for 
lower income households and allowing a density of at least 30 dwelling units per acre, Government 
Code 65583.2(i) specifies that the local government cannot apply any discretion in their review and 
the review would not constitute a “project” for purposes of the California Environmental Quality Act 
(Division 13 (commencing with Section 21000)) of the Public Resources Code.
Summary of CEQA Findings
To be eligible for exemption under CEQA Guideline Section 15183, a proposed project must be 
consistent with the development density established by existing zoning, community plan, or general 
plan policies for which an EIR was certified. The proposed zoning code and map amendments, to 
create the Housing Opportunity Overlay District (H), is consistent with the density established by the 
San Mateo General Plan 2040 and the related certified EIR because it will allow at least 30 dwelling 
units per acre which is lower than the maximum density allowed per the General Plan land use 
designation (see Attachment 2 of the agenda report).  
Section 15183 also requires that a city in approving a project meeting the requirements of this 
section of the CEQA Guidelines can limit its examination of environmental effects to the following 
items:
1. Are peculiar to the project or the parcel on which the project would be located.

current and future housing needs for people at all income levels.  The General Plan EIR included

SUBJECT:
Repeal Resolution for Design Review Process for Larger Development Projects
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of a resolution to repeal Resolution No. 67 (1996) Design Review Process for 
Certain Development Projects and determine the policy update is exempt from the California Environmental Quality Act 
pursuant to Section 15378(b)(2).
BACKGROUND:
The City implemented a design review process for larger development projects in 1991 (Resolution No. 7), with an 
amendment in 1996 (Resolution No. 67), that required third-party consultant review for a majority of larger projects, in 
response to community concerns to ensure that new developments upheld a standard of "superior design quality.” These 
adopted resolutions, included as Attachments 2 and 3, required design review to be conducted by a professional 
consultant to evaluate site layout, architecture and landscaping for compliance with adopted design guidelines and 
applicable policies; consider the project’s aesthetic and functional qualities; and the relationship of the project to 
neighboring properties and uses. 
The following types of projects are currently subject to this third-party design review requirement:
a.
Residential projects of six or more units.
b.
Commercial projects with 10,000 or more square feet of new space.
c.
Residential projects of three stories or greater.
d.
Any other projects determined by the Zoning Administrator.
In the 1990s, the City saw a significant number of new development proposals and there were not any adopted design 
guidelines, which resulted in controversies stemming from subjective comments based on project context and individual 
reviewers' professional perspectives. To address these concerns, design guidelines and policies were adopted and 
implemented to help improve the process, along with the requirement for third-party design review for all larger projects. 
In January 2022, the Planning Commission held a study session to provide input on improving the design review process. 
Most commissioners expressed support for the how the input from the third-party design review consultant assisted the 
Commission in its review of larger projects. More recently, in November 2023, in response to State laws that limited the 
ability of local jurisdictions to enforce subjective design guidelines on housing projects, the City adopted Objective Design 
Standards for qualifying residential and mixed-use projects. With Objective Design Standards, a third-party design 
consultant is no longer necessary to review for compliance.  Removal of the third-party design review requirement was 
also identified as a way to streamline the review process for housing development projects as part of the Sixth Cycle 
Housing Element preparation process.  The Housing Element, which was adopted by City Council on May 20, 2024,

CITY OF SAN MATEO
includes a policy to Streamline Housing Application Review (Policy H 1.6) with the following implementation action:
•
Update the City Council resolutions that establish the pre-application and development review processes to 
eliminate the pre-application requirement for housing projects and eliminate the third-party design review 
requirement for housing projects that meet objective standards.
With the adopted Objective Design Standards, project compliance can be determined as the staff-level for multi-family and 
residential mixed-use development applications, which eliminates the need for a third-party design review consultant and 
streamlines the review process for applicable projects.
The State of California also adopted several bills, such as Senate Bills (SB) 35, SB 167, and SB 330 to establish a streamlined 
review process for housing development projects. These laws limit local jurisdictions’ review of such projects to objective 
standards in that they are uniformly verifiable to an external and uniform benchmark or criterion, involve no personal or 
subjective judgment by a public official, and are knowable by both the applicant and City. 
DISCUSSION:
For all Site Plan and Architectural Review (SPAR) applications, projects are required to make certain findings, adhere to 
applicable policies, and meet design review guidelines. Staff conducts a compliance review as part of the application 
process and guides the design review consultant in providing comments consistent with applicable guidelines. In addition, 
projects below the thresholds outlined above are subject to staff-level design review. These projects include single-family 
residences, duplexes, small multi-family residential developments, and commercial projects with less than 10,000 square 
feet of new floor area.  In more recent years, Community Development has focused on increased training design review 
and support for Planning staff to support completion of architectural and site plan reviews without a third-party 
consultant. The consultant-led review process also requires multiple steps, which adds complexity, time, and cost to the 
process. 
In summary, transitioning to a staff-led design review process would streamline the design review process and save time

SUBJECT:
Update Requirements for Preliminary Application Review Requirements for New Development
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend that the City Council repeal Resolution No. 69 (2020) and adopt an Early Community Engagement Process for 
Development Projects, including changes to a mandatory Planning Commission study session requirement and 
simplification of the noticing and neighborhood meeting procedures for all planning applications; and determine the policy 
update is exempt from the California Environmental Quality Act pursuant to Guidelines Section 15378(b)(2).
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo (City) has a longstanding practice of encouraging public participation in the development review 
process and providing public notification that exceeds the minimums set by state law. The City's current Preliminary 
Application (Pre-Application) Planning Process was designed to foster early public awareness of new development 
proposals by sharing project details with the community, gathering feedback, and resolving potential issues before formal 
planning application submission. This approach aimed to reduce the likelihood of appeals. However, some of the required 
elements of the pre-application process add unnecessary time and costs to the project review process, particularly for 
certain housing projects that have a different standard of review based on state statutes. 
The current process has a two-tiered approach based on project size. The formal pre-application starts with a 
neighborhood meeting with mandatory staff attendance, followed by the submission of conceptual plans for staff review 
and input, followed by a Planning Commission study session. The notice radius for both meetings is 1,000 feet from the 
boundary of the project site. The informal pre-application requires a neighborhood meeting with notice sent to property 
owners within a 500-foot radius of the project site. 
In January 2022, the Planning Commission held a study session to provide input on improving the pre-application process. 
All planning commissioners were in favor of streamlining the process and requiring more conceptual-level plans and 
documentation for the study sessions. In addition, the Planning Commission discussed the option of scheduling the study 
sessions after the formal submission of the project, potentially reducing a project’s timeline since the pre-application 
process can take around four months to complete.
The City’s 2023-2031 Housing Element, which was adopted by the City Council on May 20, 2024, includes a policy that 
focuses on streamlining housing application review (Policy H1.6) and it includes the following implementation actions to be 
completed by 2024:
•
Update the City Council resolutions that establish the pre-application and development review processes to 
eliminate the pre-application requirement for housing projects.

--- page 58 ---
CITY OF SAN MATEO
•
Amend the Zoning Code to allow residential projects with up to 25 units to be approved administratively. On 
December 4, 2023, the City Council adopted the zoning ordinance to amend Municipal Code Title 27, which grants 
the Zoning Administrator the authority to review and approve up to twenty-five (25) residential dwelling units 
that meet objective design standards. 
Adoption of an Early Community Engagement Process in conjunction with simplification of pre-application requirements 
would greatly streamline the development review process, particularly for housing projects that meet objective standards.
DISCUSSION:
To achieve the Housing Element objectives and City Council priorities for streamlining development review while 
maintaining appropriate community engagement and notification, staff proposes the following modifications in four 
different areas of the current pre-application process. 
Simplifications of the Formal and Informal Pre-Application Process
The current formal and informal pre-application processes each involve distinct requirements for noticing and 
neighborhood meetings. The proposed policy (Attachment 1) streamlines this process by replacing the two-tiered 
approach with a consolidated framework consisting of three main components: noticing, neighborhood meetings, and 
Planning Commission study sessions. 
Modifications to Noticing Requirement
As outlined in the Background section, the current noticing radius differs between informal pre-application (500 feet) and 
formal pre-application (1,000 feet), measured from the project site boundary. The proposed policy maintains this noticing 
radius for regular planning applications (as defined in Section 1(b)) and large planning applications (as defined in Section 
1(c)). Furthermore, a new category of minor projects (defined in Section 1(a)) has been introduced, including single-family 
homes, which only requires notice to adjacent neighbors upon application submission. In the draft resolution, this category 
also includes other minor projects, such as second story addition and fence over seven (7) feet. However, staff is seeking 
guidance from the Planning Commission on whether noticing should be required for these minor projects.
The proposed notification procedures keep the requirement to notice the areas’ Homeowner Association and United 
Homeowners Association. The City in the past has also mailed these early notices to interested parties upon request, and 
can also email these notices to subscribed lists based on interest. The Planning Commission or Council has the discretion to 
recommend or adopt further policies that broaden or reduce the notification process.  
Modifications to Neighborhood Meetings
The proposed policy simplifies the neighborhood meeting requirements for all planning applications. Staff participation in 
these meetings would become optional, but the applicants are required to record the meeting and share the recorded 
video with the City. Apart from the noticing radius, which varies based on the project size, other requirements, such as 
meeting content, would be standardized and updated in the procedures manual.
Modifications to the Planning Commission Study Sessions
Housing Element Policy H 1.6 necessitates a change to exempt all housing projects, regardless of the project size, from the 
Planning Commission study sessions requirement. Based on the January 25, 2022 Planning Commission study session 
regarding the pre-application process, the Planning Commission highlighted the value of the study sessions in providing 
early input on projects. Therefore, the proposed policy requires a study session for certain large projects after the planning 
application submission. Qualifying housing projects that meet objective design standards would not be subject to this 
requirement. Applicants seeking early Planning Commission input may also request a study session prior to a planning 
application at the conceptual stages of the project. The target would be to schedule a Planning Commission study session 
within 90 days of an applicant’s request or application submittal.  Projects that require Planning Commission or Council 
approval would typically undergo the Planning Commission study session unless they are determined to be minor or 
procedural in nature by the Zoning Administrator. 
Currently, residential projects over 20 units require a formal pre-application review. The Zoning Ordinance, however, was

--- page 59 ---
CITY OF SAN MATEO
amended to allow Zoning Administrator approval for housing projects with up to 25 units that met objective design 
standards.  The proposed policy modifications to the pre-application process to focus more on early community 
engagement and Planning Commission input only where necessary would better align with the adopted codes and policies, 
and the City’s goal to streamline and simplify the development review process. 
Next Steps:
The proposed resolution to repeal and replace the pre-application process with an early community engagement process 
would be scheduled for Council review and approval in summer 2024. Once adopted, staff will update the Planning 
Application Guide and other handouts to outline procedures for implementing this resolution.

RESOLUTION NO 69 (2020) 
EXEMPTING APPLICATIONS FOR MODIFICATIONS TO PREVIOUSLY-APPROVED PLANNING APPLICATIONS FROM 
THE FORMAL PRE-APPLICATION PROCESS 
WHEREAS, the City Council of the City of San Mateo has adopted Resolution No. 98 (2008), which 
establishes pre-application procedures for planning applications; and 
WHEREAS, with limited exceptions, the City's current pre-application procedures require a 
neighborhood meeting and study session for planning applications; and 
WHEREAS, there is occasionally a need for applicants to seek modifications to approved planning 
applications subsequent to project approval; and 
WHEREAS, most applications for modifications are subject to the City's pre-application procedures; and 
WHEREAS, the City wishes to eliminate the requirement for a neighborhood meeting and study session 
for applications for modifications to approved planning applications and instead provide that requests for 
modifications proceed directly to the decision-making body; and 
WHEREAS, the City Council previously adopted resolutions amending the City's pre-application 
procedures to exempt requested modifications to the City's approved Wastewater Treatment Plant planning 
application and projects for which the one-year limitation on re-submittals has been waived; and 
WHEREAS, this proposed exemption will cover any application to modify a previously-approved planning 
application; and 
WHEREAS, the proposed Pre-Application Procedures attached as Exhibit A to this Resolution have been 
amended to memorialize all previous amendments; and 
WHEREAS, the City Council finds that amending the City's pre-application procedures is a general policy 
and procedure-making activity and not a project as defined by the California Environmental Quality Act, in 
accordance with CEQA Guidelines section 15378(b)(2); 
NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF SAN MATEO, CALIFORNIA, HEREBY RESOLVES 
that: 
1. The City's Pre-Application Procedures are amended to remove the requirement for neighborhood 
meetings and study sessions for applications to modify approved planning applications. 
2. 
Revised Pre-Application Procedures, as shown in Exhibit A to this Resolution, are adopted to replace the 
City's current Pre-Application Procedures. 
3. This Resolution shall take effect upon adoption.
