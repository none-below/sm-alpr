SUBJECT:
Capital Improvement Projects Update - Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the status of current Capital Improvement Projects.
BACKGROUND:
Capital Improvement Projects (CIPs) come forward as a part of the budget process where projects are identified, proposed, 
and costs are developed. Funding sources are identified where possible. The projects listed below are funded through the 
City’s General Fund (GF); Measure S (MS); Grants (GR); and Miscellaneous Fund (MISC). 
Projects that affect Parks and Recreation fall into two different areas of oversight, those overseen by Parks and Recreation 
project management staff and those overseen by Public Works project management staff. The following projects are those 
that are overseen by Public Works. 
Beresford ADA Entry
Description: Design and install a fully compliant ADA access route from the parking lot to the front door of the Beresford 
Recreation Center. 
Status/Timeline: In plan check. Construction scheduled between Summer through Winter 2024. 
 
Park Restroom IV- Casanova Park
Description: Renovations to the park restrooms are needed to replace old infrastructure and upgrade the facilities to 
comply with current accessibility standards required by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
Status/ Timeline: Under Construction. Completion in Summer 2024.
Park Restroom Phase V – Lakeshore and Shoreview
Description: Renovations to the park restrooms at Shoreview and Lakeshore Parks are needed to replace old infrastructure 
and upgrade the facilities to comply with current accessibility standards required by the Americans with Disabilities Act 
(ADA).
Status/Timeline: Shoreview: Under construction; Lakeshore: In plan check. Construction from Spring through Winter 2024. 
Lakeshore Community Center ADA Entry
Description: The project consists of improvements to the building entrance are required for safety and ADA compliance
Status/Timeline: On Hold.  Pending the development of Lakeshore Community Center Childcare Improvements.

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Community Center Rehabilitation
Description: This project will renovate the facility to ensure it can remain open and operable. This modernization ensures 
participants are taught a critical life and safety skill, learning to swim. This facility is designated as the community 
resilience center for the city in emergencies.
Status/Timeline:  Assessment in progress. Reports due Summer 2024.
 
King/Joinville Pool Improvements Assessment  
Description: The project involves a comprehensive assessment of the pool and implementation of capital improvements.
Status/Timeline: Assessment in progress. Reports due Summer 2024.
 
King Fire Alarm System
Description: The King Recreation Center was constructed in 1968 and has a wet sprinkler fire suppression system, an 
existing security alarm system but is currently not monitored by a Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP).
Status/ Timeline: Under Construction. Construction from Spring through Summer 2024.
 
King Small Kitchen 
Description: The small kitchen serves community groups and is used for classes and camps for storage and serving catered 
food.  The cabinets, fixtures, and appliances are due for replacement.
Status/ Timeline: Completion in April 2024. 
 
Washington Park Basketball Court  
Description: The basketball court is beyond the end of its useful life and due for replacement. The surface is made of 
composite material, which has collapsed in some areas and exposed cracks in others. 
Status/ Timeline: Under Construction. Completion by Summer2024.
 
Laurelwood Park Retaining Wall
Description: Two adjacent wood retaining walls that support the entrance from Glendora Drive are showing signs of 
deterioration and need to be evaluated for repairs.
Status/ Timeline: Assessment phase.  Engaged land surveyor and geotechnical engineer. Budget development for FY 2025-
26. Design in Summer 2025; Construction in Spring 2026.

SUBJECT:
2024 Summer Recreation Camps, Programs and Events Preview - Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the upcoming 2024 Summer Recreation Camps, Programs and Events.
BACKGROUND:
The summer season is the busiest time for Parks & Recreation, offering a variety of activities catering to all ages within the 
San Mateo community. Alongside our regular programming, the department provides in-house camps, contracted camps, 
teen volunteer opportunities, aquatics programming, and a diverse range of events across multiple locations. Additionally, 
we hire an extra 60-75 part-time staff to support our camps, aquatics, programs, and facilities during this bustling period.
Amidst the excitement of summer activities, we proudly observe National Parks & Recreation Month in July. This serves as 
an opportunity to advocate for the creation of strong, vibrant, and resilient communities through the transformative 
power of parks and recreation.
