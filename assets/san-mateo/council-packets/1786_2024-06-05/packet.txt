SUBJECT:
Age Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program – Feedback
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the development of the Age Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program and provide feedback 
on application and logo designs.
BACKGROUND:
In October 2020, the City of San Mateo took a significant step towards fostering an inclusive environment for all residents 
by committing to become an Age-Friendly City. This commitment was formalized through its partnership with the AARP’s 
Age Friendly Communities Network. In November 2022, the City demonstrated its dedication further by submitting its 
comprehensive Age-Friendly Action Plan to the AARP. This plan serves as a strategic roadmap, meticulously crafted by the 
Age-Friendly Task Force, to address key initiatives identified for enhancing the city's age-friendliness. Among the pivotal 
projects outlined in the Age-Friendly Action Plan is the pioneering Age-Friendly Business Certification Pilot Program. The 
primary objective of this innovative initiative is twofold: to enlighten local businesses about the significance of being age-
friendly and to commend those establishments that are already integrating age-friendly practices into their operations. 
With a collaborative effort between the Parks and Recreation and Community Development Department, a preliminary 
application for businesses along with initial logo designs has been created. The launch of this pilot program in the 
upcoming months signifies a crucial milestone. By engaging a small sampling of San Mateo businesses, the city aims to test 
the efficacy of the program's processes and logistics while gauging interest from stakeholders. This strategic approach 
allows for thorough evaluation and refinement before potential expansion in the future, ensuring the program's long-term 
viability and impact. 
This presentation was provided to the Senior Commission on May 13, 2024 and minor revisions have been made to the 
application and logo based on feedback received.
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact related to this presentation.
