SUBJECT:
Measure S Proposed 2024-25 Operating and CIP Budget – Approval Recommendation
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend the City Council adopt the Measure S proposed 2024-25 Budget
BACKGROUND:
At the February 22, 2024 Measure S Oversight Committee meeting, staff presented the Measure S Fund’s five-year 
forecast, including a preview of upcoming fiscal year’s revenues and expenditure projections. Public Works staff discussed 
the funding needs for capital improvement projects (CIP) and the Police Department presented the need of purchasing 
three police vehicles to support the department’s operations.
Incorporating existing and updated information, staff prepared the Measure S proposed 2024-25 budget for the 
committee’s review.
 
Revenues:
In summary, Measure S total revenues for 2024-25 are projected to be $7.2 million, primarily sourced from the city’s 
Measure S transactions and use tax ($7.1 million), along with a local grant supporting the City's Senior Transportation 
Program.  
Ongoing Operating Expenses:
Total expenditures are projected to be $2.4 million for 2024-25, supporting Police, Parks & Recreation, and Library 
operations:
•
$1.6 million in Public Safety/Police
•
$0.4 million for Community services
•
$0.2 million for Library services
•
A one-time budget item:  $182,000 to purchase three new police vehicles.
Over 75% of the $2.4 million in operating expenses are personnel costs, funding 6.51 full time equivalent (FTE) of staff 
positions:
•
Police Department
o
3.0 FTE Police Officer 
o
1.0 FTE Police Sergeant

CIP Projects:
Based on staff recommendation, the CIP projects that are recommended to be supported by Measure S are as follows for 
2024-25, totaling $3.7 million:
•
Marina Library Branch Improvement Project:  $2,100,000
•
Police Station Parking Deck Resurfacing Project:  $600,000
•
Gateway Park Pedestrian Bridge Improvements:  $300,000
•
Bermuda Drive Bridge Replacement Project: $300,000 
•
Quick-Build Program for Pedestrian and Bicycle Safety Improvements: $500,000
•
Smooth Street Program: ($500,000)
•
Parks & Recreation: Synthetic Turf Replacement: $400,000 
Transfers Out:
Transfers out is projected to be $6.6 million for fiscal year 2024-25, which are comprised of the following:  $3.7 million for 
capital improvement projects (CIP),  $2.6 million in transfers out for debt service payments related to the revenue bonds 
issued in 2020 to finance the cost of flood control and street rehabilitation, $0.3 million in transfers out to the General 
Fund for amortized repayment of costs it incurred on Measure S' behalf before Measure S funds were available. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
For 2024-25 fiscal year, Measure S projects revenues of $7.2 million, operating expenditures of $2.4 million, and transfers 
out totaling $6.6 million.  This results in a projected drawdown on Measure S fund balance of $1.8 million.
