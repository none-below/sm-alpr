ETHICS SAN MATEO

DEDICATED TO TRUTH, TRANSPARENCY AND ETHICAL CONDUCT IN CITY GOVERNMENT
12 June 2024

City Council
City of San Mateo
Via Email

RE: Planning Commission Applicant Interviews and Selection
Honorable Mayor and Councilmembers,

Ethics San Mateo has spoken, issued Statements of Position, and communicated by various media our
strong concern regarding conflicts of interest, bias, and prejudgment on the part of some of the current
Planning Commissioners.

We are not supporting the selection of any specific candidate to fill the current, or future, vacancy. We
insist that the selection be in accordance with the current Boards and Commissions Rules and
Procedures, specifically Chapter 2, Section 1. G.:

“Commissions serve to represent the general interests of the
entire City and not specific program areas or specific interest
groups.”

While it may be attractive to appoint someone with professional qualifications or a history of
participation in groups which actively express opinions regarding issues that may be in the purview of
the Planning Commission, it may be the forbearer of adding Commissioners with conflicts of interest and
bias. We do not presume that any of the current candidates fall into this category, however all MUST be
vetted to ensure that the residents of San Mateo are served as required by the rule stated above, as well
as with the highest of ethical behavior.

To accomplish this, you must carefully and thoroughly perform both a background investigation to help
determine the candidate’s ability to be fair and impartial, as well as ask questions about any potential
conflicts of interest or bias. Each and every decision made by the Planning Commission must be based
on the merits of the specific project or item, NOT a predetermined conviction. The residents of San
Mateo deserve planning decisions that are not based on the will of specific interest groups.

Ethics San Mateo appreciates this opportunity to input our strong message.

On behalf of the ESM Board of Directors,

David Cohen, President/CEO
Ethics San Mateo, A California Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation
DEDICATED TO TRUTH, TRANSPARENCY AND ETHICAL CONDUCT IN CITY GOVERNMENT

cc: Alex Khojikian, City Manager
Zach DahIDirector of Community Development
Manira Sandhir, Planning Manager

California’s housing crisis, and how that applies to the relatively affluent cities in the Peninsula, is 
undoubtedly complex. The state and local governments have understandably attempted to address the 
issue, which encompasses both a lack of housing and specifically the lack of affordable housing, by

local commission or city council meeting. We need to first think of how to more effectively do outreach 
to all members of our community and how to collect their opinions, even if in an informal setting. 
Ultimately, there is common ground among the different factions on this issue. At their core, all San 
Mateans value safety and the right to housing. It’s up to local officials to communicate these shared 
values and to remain transparent in their decision making with our city’s residents.
