CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
June 24, 2024
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Austin Volz, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:30 
p.m.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Mr. Volz led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Austin Volz, Sheila 
Canzian, Wendy McCall, and Richard Wysong.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
City Librarian James Moore advised the Board that the increase in library cards issued on 
the statistical reports was due to the Student Success Initiative records that were created for 
students from the San Mateo and Foster City School District.  A motion to approve the 
consent calendar was made by Ms. Canzian, seconded by Mr. Wysong, and the motion 
passed 4-0.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
OLD BUSINESS
Recommended Updates to the San Mateo Main Library Meeting Room & Facilities Rental 
Policy:  Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca presented an updated draft of the 
San Mateo Main Library Meeting Room & Facilities Rental Policy to the Board.  This draft 
included the recommendations the Board provided to Ms. Mendonca during the May Board 
meeting.  A motion was made to adopt the policy as presented by Ms. McCall, seconded by 
Mr. Volz, and the motion passed 
4-0.
6.
NEW BUSINESS
A. Fiscal Year 2022-2023 Impact Report: Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca 
presented some of the highlights that will be included in the San Mateo Public Library 
(SMPL) FY 22-23 Impact Report.  The Impact Report will include information on 
statistics, finances and grants, the Library Board of Trustees, SMPL volunteers, SMPL 
Foundation, SMPL Cultural Advisory Committees, and the programs and services SMPL 
provides for all age groups in the community.
B. Annual Rotation of President and Vice President Roles: The Library Board of Trustees 
conducted their annual rotation for the president and vice president roles for Fiscal Year 
2024-2025.  A motion was made by Mr. Wysong for Ms. Canzian to be president and 
Ms. McCall to be vice president, the motion was seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion 
passed 4-0.
7.

A.  City Librarian James Moore:

Annie Tsai has been chosen to fill the vacancy on the Library Board of Trustees.  
She will be sworn in at the end of July.  

Summer Learning Challenge is currently in progress for all age groups. It runs from 
June 1 to August 31. 

Storytime in Central Park has started and is scheduled on Wednesdays at 10:00 
a.m.  It is very popular with over 100 attendees at each session.

Mr. Moore will be attending the American Library Association conference in San 
Diego at the end of this week.

San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) will have a blood drive on July 1 from 10:00 a.m. – 
2:00 p.m.

It is estimated that the Marina Library repair project will cost approximately $900,000 
more than what has been allotted for the project.  During the upcoming fiscal year, 
someone will assist with applying for grants to help with the cost of the repairs.

The cost of the garage repair at the Main Library is now estimated at approximately 4 
million dollars, which is down from the original estimate of up to 10 million dollars.  
The Main Library sits on a creek and water needs to be continually pumped out.  The 
repair will need to be done within the next 10 years.  It will be a very technical 
process that will include a substance being injected into the concrete of the garage to 
prevent future leaks.
 
B. Board Members:

Mr. Volz visited the Library several times since the last Board meeting.

Ms. McCall attended the Juneteenth celebration on B Street and assisted at the 
Library booth with Supervising Librarian Aisha Washington.
C. Library Foundation Co-President Jeanne Back:

At the Foundation Board’s last meeting of FY 23-24, the Board said goodbye to their 
treasurer and secretary, and welcomed a couple of new Foundation Board members.

The Foundation gifted SMPL a $135,000 grant.  This amount is up since the onset of 
Covid, but still below pre-Covid levels.

The Foundation is working on expanding their donor family and having more events 
throughout the year.  There are currently 400 donors this year, which is up by 80 
from last year.

There are currently 75 Leadership Circle donors.  These are donors that help sustain 
the vitality of SMPL with unrestricted gifts and pledges of $1,000 or more annually.

The String Soirée fundraiser on May 6 at the Main Library was a success.

There will be an upcoming fundraiser on July 17 at the Main Library.  Tapas on the 
Terrace will be held on the Oak Room patio.  There will be a suggested donation of 
$20.00 at the door.

Mr. Moore offered the Foundation grant writing services and help with 
communication.  Also, Foundation members had a great conversation with City 
Manager Alex Khojikian.  

The Foundation has an opening for part-time help (2 days per week) in the office.  
Ms. Back asked the Library Board to help spread the word.  The Foundation would 
like to fill this position by August.

Special Collections Policy
[Revised 6-28-10, Renewed 9-23-19]

This policy identifies the special collections at the San Mateo Public Library and the process for
evaluating proposals to create new special collections at the San Mateo Public Library. A special
collection is defined as a focused set of library materials shelved apart from the general collection that
fulfills a specific community need or library role.

I. Guidelines for Establishing Special Collections

Special collection proposals can be formulated by members of the community or internally by Library
teams or staff members. Proposals shall be submitted to the City Librarian who will autherizethe

1. The proposed new collection’s definition should include a statement of scope indicating
circulating/reference, formats, age of audience, etc. The scope should be consistent with the
Library’s vision and mission.
