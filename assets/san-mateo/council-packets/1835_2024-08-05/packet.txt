Subject: Countywide Article 34 Measure

Hello Mayor, Council Members and City Manager,

| am writing to request that the City of San Mateo support us in encouraging the San Mateo County
Board of Supervisors to place a measure on the November ballot to allow local government agencies
to develop, construct, and acquire affordable rental housing for low-income persons pursuant to
Article 34 of the California Constitution. The Board of Supervisors directed the County Manager to
reach out to cities and get their input on placing this measure on the Ballot.

Why this matters:

e Advantageous: HEART and government agencies have certain advantages with respect to
property taxes and issuing bonds that make the acquisition and preservation of rental
housing more feasible than it is for nonprofit organizations. Agencies like HEART and
interested cities need to fill this housing gap.

*Effective: HEART's Board of Directors directed HEART to pursue funding and carry out a
program to acquire and preserve 5- to 25-unit apartment buildings. HEART is about to
complete its first acquisition, an 8-unit building in Redwood City. The per unit cost of this
acquisition is $350,000, significantly less than the cost of building a new unit which currently
costs over $1.1 million.

eBarrier: Article 34 makes it difficult for HEART and other government agencies to buy and
preserve existing housing, or to create new affordable housing directly by requiring projects
to be voted on by the residents of each community. We need a ballot measure to satisfy this
constitutional provision so that we can undertake this work without having to take each
project to a vote.

Please look at the attached fact sheet about Article 34. We hope you agree with us and tell the
County Manager and the Board of Supervisors that the City of San Mateo supports placing the
measure on the ballot.

Thank you,

Armando

Armando F. Sanchez
Executive Director

Housing Endowment and Regional Trust of San Mateo County

To whom it may concern:
I'm deeply concerned about the San Mateo County Supervisors' proposal to take control 
over city decisions, especially when it comes to low-income or homeless housing projects.
 Here's why I believe this shift in power should be opposed:
1. **Loss of Local Representation**:
 I value the fact that my city's elected officials are directly accountable to us, the residents. 
They understand our unique needs and concerns. If the county takes control, it undermines 
our local representation and moves decision-making further away from those who are most 
affected.
2. **Lack of Specificity and Oversight**:
 
 The proposed measure seems to give blanket approval for projects without providing any 
specific details. This concerns me because it means we wouldn't have the chance to review 
or challenge particular projects before they're approved. Transparency and oversight are 
crucial, and this proposal lacks both.
3. **Giving Up Local Rights**:
 
By allowing the county to take control, we would lose our ability to voice concerns and 
address grievances at the local level. I don't want to see the country preemptively approve 
projects that could negatively impact our community without the opportunity to evaluate 
them properly.
4. **Inconvenience and Accessibility**:
 
If the county takes over, we'd have to attend county meetings to express our opinions, 
which could be less accessible and less frequent than our city council meetings. This 
change could discourage public participation and make it harder for us to engage in 
meaningful dialogue about important issues.
5. **Preserving Local Governance**:
 
Most important:  I believe that cities and towns were established to provide local control and

the right to recall vote of the governor, state senators and assemblymen, and other
elected officials, and state consitutionally-protected voting right Proposition 11 (1972)
