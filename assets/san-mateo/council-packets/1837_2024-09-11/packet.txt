SUBJECT:
Bicycle and Pedestrian Projects Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, acting as the City of San Mateo's Bicycle and Pedestrian Advisory 
Committee, receive an update on bicycle and pedestrian projects.
BACKGROUND:
Staff will present brief updates on the pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure projects and planning efforts.
Infrastructure Projects
Delaware Street Safe Routes to School Corridor: Staff was awarded funds under Cycle 5 of the California Transportation 
Commission’s (CTC) Active Transportation Program (ATP) for the Delaware Street Safe Routes to School bicycle project. 
This project will implement the Delaware Street Class IV separated bike lane and Class III bicycle boulevard as proposed in 
the 2020 Bicycle Master Plan. This project provides necessary safety improvements in an area with a high level of traffic 
stress and will improve safety and connectivity to two public schools accessed from Delaware Street. Further, this project 
improves north-west connectivity to the relocated Hillsdale Caltrain station. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
City staff and the consultant team held two pop up events to at the project site in spring 2024 and had an online 
survey asking for feedback on the proposed improvements. The community expressed support for the Class IV 
protected bike lanes and pedestrian safety enhancements and preferred more robust safety measures, including 
use of bollards to protect bicyclists in the bike lane and at intersections. Community members suggested that the 
project should emphasize the benefits to public schools and areas with large student populations and expand the 
bike facilities to other destinations. The feedback was used to develop the 30% design.
•
The project team is developing the 95% design plans. The design is expected to be complete this fall 2024. 
28th Avenue Gap Closure Project: Staff was awarded funds from the San Mateo County Transportation Authority 
Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Cycle 5 to fund the design and environmental review for the 28th Avenue/Hillsdale 
Caltrain Station Bicycle Access Gap Closure project. The intent of the project is to evaluate options and develop design 
plans for the Class IV separated bike lane between Edison Street and El Camino Real identified in the 2020 Bicycle Master 
Plan and provide a safe path of travel for pedestrians and bicyclists across the El Camino Real intersection. The following is 
an update on this effort. 
•
The project team is reviewing the 30% design plans and planning level cost estimates for Alternative C, which 
consists of Class II and Class IV bike lanes, bus boarding islands under the Caltrain tracks, and a pedestrian priority 
signal at the Delaware and 28th Avenue intersection.
•
City staff and the consultant team are amending the scope to include in-person community engagement to collect

Pavement Management Program Integration: Staff continues to integrate the implementation of bicycle facilities with 
scheduled pavement management projects where funding for design and construction is available and feasible. The 
following is an update on this effort. 

Pavement Management Program 5-B Project: This project is currently in construction and includes installation of a 
bicycle boulevard on S. Grant Street from Concar Drive to 16th Avenue and traffic calming elements on South 
Grant Street. Construction of the bike facilities is expected to start in this fall.
School Zone Speed Limits: City Council’s strategic plan prioritizes implementation of bicycle and pedestrian projects that 
address safety issues. Part of this objective includes evaluating school zone speed limit reductions to address quality of-life 
and safety within neighborhoods. Speed limit reductions in school zones can help reduce the risk of severe or fatal 
conflicts between vehicles and active transportation users (e.g., cyclists and pedestrians) and can also help promote 
students and families walking or biking to school. City Council adopted a resolution in December 2021 identifying a total of 
38 roadway segments contiguous to 23 schools in San Mateo that qualify for speed limit reductions and authorized the 
reduction of posted speed limits in these zones. The following is an update on this effort. 

Construction work for the last two phases (Phases 2 and 3) was completed in June 2024. 

Outreach for the new speed limits was conducted in August 2024 and included dynamic parking signs posted at 
key streets. Yard signs were also distributed to schools to notify driver’s at pick up and drop off locations of the 
new speed limits.
19th Avenue/Fashion Island Class IV Bicycle Facility Project: This project is part of a joint effort between the City, San 
Mateo County Transportation Authority (SMCTA), and SamTrans to design and construct multimodal improvements as part 
of the 101/92 Interchange Project. The project includes feasibility, design, and construction of Class IV separated bicycle 
lanes on 19th Avenue and Fashion Island Boulevard, including intersection improvements, and is expected to connect 
directly to a proposed Mobility Hub at the existing Caltrans Park and Ride location being constructed in parallel. In addition 
to the proposed Class IV separated bikeway, the project includes smart corridor elements such as smart streetlights, air 
quality monitoring, traffic signal detection enhancements, and smart kiosks and digital bus stops with real-time transit 
information. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
Staff and the consultant team developed two conceptual design alternatives for the Class IV bike facility. Option A 
consisted of a two-way Class IV bikeway on the south side for the full length of the project limits. Option B 
included a two-way Class IV bikeway on the south side that transitioned to a one-way Class IV bikeway on either 
side of the street at the 19th Avenue and Fashion Island Boulevard intersection. Both alternatives have striping at 
the intersections to guide bicyclists across the intersection and in and out of the Class IV bikeway.
•
Phase 2 of community engagement was completed in May and June 2024. The project team hosted a community 
workshop at Fiesta Gardens International School on May 15th to collect feedback on the conceptual design 
alternatives. An online survey was available through June 28th for those who could not participate in the workshop 
and to provide an online option for feedback.
•
The project team selected Option B based on feedback from the community and are developing the design plans 
for the preferred alternative.
•
City staff are working with the San Mateo County Transportation Authority on two separate grant applications for 
funding construction of the final design.
Downtown Parking Garage Pedestrian Safety Project: This project aims to improve pedestrian safety at the entrances and 
exits of Central Parking Garage, Main Street Parking Garage, and the 2nd and El Camino Real Parking Garage. The project’s 
design was completed in 2022 and incorporates feedback from the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, City staff 
from Public Works and Police Department, and the community through an online community survey. The design includes 
pedestrian-friendly improvements at and near the parking garage entrances and exits such as audible and visual 
pedestrian and vehicle warning devices, flexible delineators to provide physical separation between the drive aisle and 
walking paths, speed bumps, and updated pavement markings and traffic signs. The following is an update on this effort.

Improvements at the 2nd and El Camino Parking Garage will be installed as part of the 2nd and El Camino Parking


On May 6, 2024, City Council approved the construction contract to Golden Bay Construction for pedestrian safety 
improvements at Central Parking Garage and Main Street Garage. Construction is anticipated to start in early 
winter 2024.
Third Avenue and Norfolk Street Intersection Safety Improvements: This project will design and construct improvements at 
the Third Avenue and Norfolk Street intersection to improve safety and efficiency for pedestrians, bicyclists, and drivers. 
The project aims to improve the walking and biking routes between the bike and pedestrian bridge on Third Avenue, 
existing Class II bike lane on South Norfolk Street, and the existing Class I bike path adjacent to the San Mateo Creek. The 
design will incorporate community feedback to ensure the final design responds to the safety concerns the community 
voices regarding crossing this intersection. The following is an update on this effort.

Online and postcard surveys were distributed to residents, nearby schools, bike advocacy groups, and on the City’s 
e-newsletter to collect feedback on the preliminary concept design in October 2023. The survey, which consisted 
of feedback from drivers, bicyclists, pedestrians, and transit riders, found the community has multiple safety 
concerns about the intersection, including pedestrian and bike visibility, conflicts between right-turning vehicles 
and pedestrians, and red-light violations from vehicles turning left on to eastbound Norfolk Street. 117 responses 
were received online and 54 responses from the mailed surveys.

City staff and the consultant team are currently reviewing the 65% design plans and anticipate completing the final 
design by fall 2024.

The City implemented interim signal phase changes to address ongoing safety concerns from the public.

Construction is expected in summer 2025.
Hillsdale Boulevard at Alameda de las Pulgas Traffic Signal Project: This project will assess and design traffic signal 
operations at the Hillsdale Boulevard and Alameda de las Pulgas intersection to optimize safety for all modes, including 
pedestrians from nearby schools and bicyclists using the existing Class II bicycle lanes on Hillsdale Boulevard. The design 
aims to balance the needs of both current and future users and minimize congestion at the intersection. The following is 
an update on this effort. 

City staff and the consultant team are developing the design for the protected intersection.

The design is expected to be complete in fall 2024.
Planning Projects 
Citywide Complete Streets Plan: A collaborative team of staff from Public Works, Community Development, and the City 
Manager’s Office submitted an application to the Caltrans Sustainable Communities FY 2021-22 Grant Program for the San 
Mateo Complete Streets Plan and was awarded funding for the project in June 2021. This project brings together the City’s 
various active transportation plans, Local Roadway Safety Plan, and Green Infrastructure Plan to fully envision a 
multimodal transportation network and identify priority improvements for the City. The following is an update on this 
effort.  
•
City staff and the consultant team are finalizing the draft Plan and working on the draft Design Manual which 
includes design standards and guidelines for implementing transportation improvements in the Plan. 
•
The draft Plan is estimated to be presented to City Council in winter 2024.
Citywide Transportation Demand Management (TDM) Ordinance: The Citywide TDM Ordinance envisions a comprehensive 
approach to TDM in San Mateo by recommending trip reduction goals to be established throughout the City to support 
vehicles miles traveled (VMT) reduction and the goal of a multimodal transportation network. The project will propose 
updates to the San Mateo Municipal Code and develop program guidelines to provide detail and guidance for staff and the 
development community to implement trip reduction goals for projects in San Mateo. The project will evaluate existing 
TDM policies, consider best practices from like jurisdictions, and develop a framework for policy development. The 
framework will outline specific policy aspects on which Commission and Council will provide feedback to develop the final 
policy parameters. This project is funded through a technical assistance grant from the Metropolitan Transportation 
Commission (MTC). The following is an update on this effort.

•
City staff and the consultant team are incorporating comments from the Planning Commission and Sustainability 
and Infrastructure Commission into the proposed TDM Ordinance.
•

Local Roadway Safety Plan: The Local Roadway Safety Plan (LRSP) analyzes collision patterns in the city and uses those 
findings to recommend prioritized safety improvements that work toward the goal of reducing fatal and severe injury 
crashes. Using input from the community and the LRSP’s Safety Partners, a list of prioritized projects has been developed 
and incorporated into a draft Local Roadway Safety Plan document. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
The Local Roadway Safety Plan was adopted by City Council on May 20, 2024 and is available for download on the 
City’s website at www.cityofsanmateo.org/SafetyPlan.
•
Staff is actively looking for opportunities to fund projects identified in the plan. 
Complete Streets North Central Plan: As directed by City Council at the March 20, 2023, meeting, staff developed a scope 
for a Complete Streets North Central Plan that will be completed concurrently with the Citywide Complete Streets Plan. 
The scope for the Plan includes extensive engagement with the community to understand the lived experience and 
identify needs and goals for a community-specific transportation plan. The Plan will result in the development of priority 
projects for the neighborhood to address the goals and community needs. The following is an update on this effort. 
•
The project team is identifying priority projects using the goals of the Plan and the needs and concerns expressed 
by the community. 
•
The project team is finalizing the outreach strategy for Phase 3 community engagement which will focus on 
prioritizing projects. Outreach will include a meeting with LEG members, a community workshop, and pop-up 
events in North Central. Preliminary designs showing the improvements in a graphical format will be developed for 
the top five priority projects and included in the final Plan.
Grants
Transportation Development Act (TDA) Article 3 Bicycle and Pedestrian Program: The TDA Article 3 Pedestrian and Bicycle 
Program funds specific projects that improve bicycling and walking conditions with the goal of reducing commute corridor 
congestion, making regional connections, enhancing safety and meeting local mobility needs. The improvements will 
reduce the barrier to transit for riders coming from the north and make accessing the station by foot safer and more 
comfortable. 

City staff submitted a grant application in November 2023 requesting $282,000 towards designing a new Caltrain 
entrance connecting to the northbound platform on the northeast side of the San Mateo Caltrain Station and 
improving pedestrian access to the station on Cypress Avenue.

The City was awarded full funding for the requested amount in May 2024 for the San Mateo Caltrain Station North 
Access Improvement Project. City Council formally accepted the grant at the July 15, 2024 meeting.
2024 Safe Travel Fellowship Grant: The San Mateo County Office of Education School Travel Fellowship creates an 
opportunity for cities to partner with their respective schools to support safe and active student travel. This program is 
funded by the California Office of Traffic Safety, the City/County Association of Governments of San Mateo County, and 
San Mateo County Health Policy and Planning. 

In coordination with Hillsdale High School staff, City staff submitted an application to improve pedestrian and 
bicycle safety around Hillsdale High School and were selected to participate in the program in January 2024.

A walk audit was conducted with Hillsdale High School students, parents, and staff in late March 2024 in which 
participants observed existing circulation and parking conditions and discussed potential safety improvements. 
The project team developed a walk audit report to summarize the walk audit findings and recommend 
infrastructure and programmatic improvements focused on improving safety and encouraging active 
transportation.

The project team installed a quick build project at Lansdale and Fernwood Streets in early August 2024 using cost 
effective and readily available materials to evaluate effectiveness of the recommended improvements. 

City staff submitted a grant application in late August 2024 for funding the design of the recommended 
improvements along 31st Avenue (see more below).

San Mateo County Cycle 7 Pedestrian and Bicycle Program: The San Mateo County Transportation Authority released a Call 
for Projects for grant funding from the Measure A and W Pedestrian and Bicycle Program on June 2024. The funds are 
intended to support projects that improve bicycle and pedestrian safety and comfort to encourage greater use of active 
transportation modes. City staff submitted two grant applications for two separate projects. 

Hillsdale High School/31st Avenue Pedestrian Safety Improvement Project: City staff submitted a grant application 
for the Small Capital Projects Category requesting $390,720 for designing pedestrian safety improvements on 31st 
Avenue from the Del Monte Street intersection to the Alameda de las Pulgas intersection. The improvements were 
identified through a walk audit with Hillsdale High School students, parents, and staff and are currently being 
tested through a quick build project installed in early August 2024. The grant application will advance the 
recommended improvements from the walk audit and quick build project into a construction-ready design that 
includes, but is not limited to, curb extensions, high-visibility and raised crosswalks, and a new signal and 
protected intersection at the Alameda de las Pulgas intersection.

19th Avenue/Fashion Island Class IV Bicycle Facility Project: In coordination with the San Mateo County 
Transportation Authority, City staff submitted a grant application for the Large Capital Projects Category 
requesting approximately $2 million for construction of the Class IV bikeway and smart corridor elements.
MTC Regional Measures 3 Grant Program: The MTC Regional Measures 3 Grant Program finances highway and transit 
improvements using funds from seven state-owned toll bridges. Voters approved Regional Measure 3 in June 2018 which 
increased tolls by $1 in 2019, 2022, and 2025 on seven state-owned bridges to fund highway and transit improvements. 
The grant program strives to help solve Bay Area’s growing congestion problems.

City staff are working with the San Mateo County Transportation Authority on a grant application (Due October 14, 
2024) requesting approximately $2 million for construction of the 19th Avenue/Fashion Island Class IV Bicycle 
Facility Project.
