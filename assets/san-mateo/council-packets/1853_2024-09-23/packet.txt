San Mateo . .
Public Library Meeting Minutes

1.

CALL TO ORDER
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to
order at 5:30 p.m.

PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL

Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, and Annie Tsai. Trustee Austin
Volz was present remotely by conference phone.

CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION

Regarding the Statistical Reports, City Librarian James Moore advised the Board
that the decrease of 9,722 visitors to the Main Library in July from the previous
month may be a mechanical problem with the gate counter. Mr. Moore has asked
staff to put in a service ticket and he will update the Board on the findings when
they are received. A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms.
McCall, seconded by Mr. Wysong, and the motion passed 5-0.

PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.

NEW BUSINESS

A. Welcome to New Library Board Trustee: Mr. Moore and the Board welcomed
new Trustee Annie Tsai. Ms. Tsai shared that she is a CEO of a fully remote
start up company which gives her flexibility to give back to the community. She
was president of the San Mateo Foster City Education Foundation for two years
and is still a member on their board. Ms. Tsai is a parent of young children and
a frequent library visitor.

B. Recommended Updates to the Special Collections Policy: Deputy City Librarian
Rukshana Singh presented recommended updates to the Special Collections

Policy and asked the Board if this policy should be incorporated into the
recently updated Collection Development Policy or remain as a separate policy.
The Board asked questions and offered feedback. Mr. Moore shared that the
Library has continued funding some collections, such as the Genentech
Foundation Biotechnology Learning Center, after the grant funding has been
depleted. Feedback from the Board included to keep this policy as a separate
policy, and to keep the non-circulating items such as photos, displays &
paintings in a separate area of the policy. The Board also asked if it would be
better to list the Funding Information Network as a special collection or in the
electronic resources list that the Library offers.

Special Collections Policy

This policy identifies the special collections at the San Mateo Public Library and the process for
evaluating proposals to create new special collections at the San Mateo Public Library. A special
collection is defined as a focused set of library materials shelved apart from the general collection that
fulfills a specific community need or library role.

1. The proposed new collection’s definition should include a statement of scope indicating
