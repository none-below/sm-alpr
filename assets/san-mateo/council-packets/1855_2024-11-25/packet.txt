San Mateo . .
Public Library Meeting Minutes

1. CALL TO ORDER
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order
at 5:30 p.m.

2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, and Austin Volz. Trustee Annie Tsai
had previously notified the Board that she was unable to attend.

3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION
Deputy City Librarian Rukshana Singh and the Board discussed the statistics. Ms.
Singh will confirm if the circulation statistics include ebooks and advise the Board.
Ms. Singh asked the Board to let her know if they would like the statistics in graph
form and if they do, how frequently they would like them. A motion to approve the
consent calendar was made by Mr. Wysong, seconded by Ms. McCall, and the motion
passed 4-0.

4. PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.

5. OLD BUSINESS

A. Status Report on the Marina Library Improvement Project: Tina Pham, Senior
Engineer with the City of San Mateo Public Works Department, presented a
PowerPoint presentation to the Board about the Marina Library Improvement
Project. The scope of the project includes structural foundation work, work to
ensure compliance with ADA standards, reconstruction of the outdoor deck,
replacement of the roof and addition of roof insulation, replacement of the heating,
ventilation, & air conditioning system, replacement of lighting, mechanical, &
electrical building systems, and full interior renovation. The construction phase is
scheduled for Summer 2025 to Summer 2026. There is a funding gap of 2.1
million dollars. Next steps include a funding gap analysis and work to secure the
additional funds. The Board offered to help with the fundraising efforts. Ms.
Singh told the Board that they will be kept informed and advised if there are some
steps they can take to assist with fundraising.

B. Draft Special Collections Policy: Ms. Singh presented an updated draft of the
Special Collections Policy to the Board. This draft included the recommended
updates that the Board provided at the September 23, 2024 meeting. Ms. Singh
made a correction to the draft that removed the language that new material is not
added to the special collections. A motion was made to adopt the updated policy
by Ms. McCall, it was seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion passed 4-0.

RECOMMENDATION:
City Librarian James Moore will review the draft San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) Strategic Plan Report with the Library 
Board of Trustees.
BACKGROUND:
The Library's current strategic plan reached the end of its usefulness at the end of FY 2023-2024. For the past several 
months, SMPL has conducted stakeholder engagement efforts to gather valuable insight into identifying the best way for 
SMPL to move forward in a way that assures its vitality and value to its community. The draft report is a result of the 
feedback gathered through community polling, stakeholder meetings, and workshops. The Board will have the opportunity 
to review the draft report and ask questions before it is ultimately shared with the wider community of SMPL patrons.
