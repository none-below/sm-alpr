Public Library Meeting Minutes

1.

CALL TO ORDER
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to
order at 5:33 p.m.

PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL

Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, and Austin Volz. Trustee Annie
Tsai had previously notified City Librarian James Moore that she was unable to
attend.

CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION

Mr. Moore advised the Board that the gate counter at Main has not been fixed. The
Peninsula Library System’s technology department will be making the repair as
soon as they can. A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms.
McCall, seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion passed 4-0.

PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.

NEW BUSINESS

Draft San Mateo Public Library Strategic Plan Report: Mr. Moore reviewed the
draft San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) Strategic Plan 2025-2028 with the Library
Board. During the strategic planning process, feedback was received from
community members and internal and external stakeholders about what they want
from their library in the future. The stakeholder team developed a SWOT analysis
for an internal assessment of where the library is today in terms of strengths,
weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. In addition, a PESTLE assessment was
conducted by the team to identify external forces that could impact library
operations including political, economic, social, technological, legal and
environmental. Mr. Moore advised the Board that the draft report is not in its final
format. The Board requested that Mr. Moore share the objectives once they are
added to the report. The Board asked questions including if there will be
prioritization of goals that can be accomplished more quickly. Mr. Moore said yes
and one of his first goals is to set up an area on the patio in Children’s that has
play equipment for neurodivergent children. He added that he would like SMPL to
become a leader on the Peninsula in offerings for neurodivergent children and
adults.

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS

A. City Librarian James Moore:
e Graphics are being added to the new community outreach van and a

charger has been installed in the parking garage at the Main Library.
