Public Library Meeting Minutes

1. CALL TO ORDER
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order
at 5:35 p.m.

2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, Austin Volz and Annie Tsai.

3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION
Regarding the statistics, City Librarian James Moore advised the Board that the gate
counter at the Main Library has been fixed. A motion to approve the consent
calendar was made by Ms. McCall, seconded by Mr. Volz, and the motion passed 5-0.

4. PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.

5. OLD BUSINESS
Update on Library’s Strategic Plan: Consultant Myra Jolivet provided an update to the
Board on the final steps of the San Mateo Public Library’s (SMPL) strategic plan. The
Board provided feedback on the cover design choices and asked whether the goals
should be more community focused rather than internally focused. Ms. Jolivet will
continue with the proofing and once a cover design is selected, the strategic plan will
be finalized.

6. NEW BUSINESS
Fiscal Year 2025-2026 Fee Schedule: Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca
presented the draft Fiscal Year 2025-2026 Fee Schedule to the Board. Currently, the
rental rate for businesses is the same for all three community rooms. The Board
asked if the fee can be higher for the Oak Room since it is a much larger than the
Laurel and Cedar Rooms. There was also discussion on whether the fee for recitals
should be higher. Mr. Moore and Ms. Mendonca will do research on this matter and
provide an update to the Board at the next meeting on February 24.

7. REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A. City Librarian James Moore:

e Mr. Moore is working with the City Attorney’s Office and Peninsula Library
System on guidelines regarding information requests for library card holders.

e SMPL is one of 60 libraries out of 1600 that applied for a Carnegie grant and is
moving on to the next step in the application process.

e SMPTL is still looking for a vendor to create the custom wrap for the new
outreach van.

e Mr. Moore shared that he has accepted another position and has submitted his
notice of resignation. His last day will be February 28. Deputy City Librarian

In August, the San Mateo County Genealogical Society (SMCGS)
launched its genealogy collection and services at the Main Library,
offering workshops, speaker programs, and consultations for family
history research. With a rich collection of books, journals, maps,
records, and online resources, SMCGS provides invaluable access
to research tools. Its First Families of San Mateo County program
highlights the area's historical roots. Located on the library's 2nd
floor, SMCGS remains a vital resource for those exploring their
family heritage and preserving it for future generations.

Support from the California State Library through the Parks Pass Program
has made it possible for the library to offer exceptional events, such as a
visit to the Folger Stables Historical Site. Participants explored the rich
history of the stables and toured the Carriage Room, a stunning museum
that highlights our local heritage, including the lumbering, farming, and
great estate eras. The program also provided an opportunity for a hiking
trip to Moss Beach in Pacifica, where attendees enjoyed the outdoors and
engaged with a knowledgeable park ranger.
