LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
February 24, 2025 
 
 Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order 
at 5:30 p.m. 
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive 
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees  
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, Austin Volz and Annie Tsai. 
 
3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION  
City Librarian James Moore and the Board discussed the visitor counts that have 
been down since the pandemic, and the increase in usage of electronic resources.  A 
motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. McCall, seconded by Ms. 
Tsai, and the motion passed 5-0. 
 
4. PUBLIC COMMENT 
There was not any public comment. 
 
5. OLD BUSINESS 
Fiscal Year 2025-2026 Fee Schedule: Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca 
provided the Board historical information regarding the Oak Room fees that were set 
for piano recitals and business events when the new Main Library opened in 2006.  
The Board asked questions and provided feedback.  A motion was made by Ms. 
Canzian to recommend the proposed 2025-2026 Fee Schedule that has increases 
based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI) to the City Council for adoption.  The 
motion was not seconded and was dropped.  A motion was made by Mr. Volz to offer a 
10% discount on the Cedar and Laurel Rooms for weekday rentals, it was seconded 
by Ms. Tsai, and the motion did not pass at 2-3.  A motion was made by Ms. McCall 
to recommend the proposed 2025-2026 Fee Schedule that has increases based on the 
CPI to the City Council for adoption, it was seconded by Mr. Wysong, and the motion 
passed 3-2. 
 
6. NEW BUSINESS 
Fiscal Year 2023-2024 Impact Report:  Senior Management Analyst Marcia Mendonca 
presented the Fiscal Year 2023-2024 Impact Report to the Board.  The Board asked 
questions and provided feedback for future impact reports.  Mr. Moore said that 
future impacts could possibly be in video form.  Suggestions for future content 
included including stories from community members of how the library has impacted 
their lives. 
 
7. REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS 
 
A.  City Librarian James Moore: 
• 
Mr. Moore shared attended the film and discussion event Free for All: The 
Public Library on February 23 in the Oak Room.

CITY OF SAN MATEO 
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
March 31, 2025 
 
 Special Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order 
at 5:30 p.m. 
 
2. ROLL CALL 
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive 
Assistant Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees  
Sheila Canzian, Wendy McCall, Richard Wysong, and Austin Volz.  Trustee Annie Tsai 
was not present and had previously notified Acting City Librarian Rukshana Singh 
that she was unable to attend. 
 
3. NEW BUSINESS 
City Librarian Appointment – Rukshana Singh: City Manager Alex Khojikian provided 
the Library Board of Trustees an update of the recruitment process for the City 
Librarian position and sought approval to appoint Acting City Librarian Rukshana 
Singh as City Librarian effective April 13, 2025.  Ms. Canzian was the former Director 
of Parks and Recreation for the City of San Mateo and has known Ms. Singh since 
2012.  Ms. Canzian shared her support in the appointment of Ms. Singh as City 
Librarian.  Foundation Co-President Alexandra Gillen and Foundation Board Member 
Ruthmary were present and also shared their support in the appointment of Ms. 
Singh as City Librarian.  A motion was made by Ms. McCall to approve the 
appointment of Rukshana Singh as City Librarian effective April 13, 2025, the motion 
was seconded by Mr. Wysong, and the motion passed 4-0.   
 
ADJOURNMENT 
Ms. Canzian adjourned the meeting at 5:47 p.m.

Creating Our Story
When I became City Manager, my biggest priority was building 
community in San Mateo. One of the ways we do that is through 
our library services. The San Mateo Public Library is more than just 
a place to check out books and do research; it’s the living room of 
our community, a safe, inclusive space where people of all ages 
come to connect over shared interests as well as grow together.
For many years, many predicted that libraries would become obsolete because of the 
internet, but libraries like ours have grown because we evolved into a multigenerational 
community hub for learning, collaboration and cultural activities. While the internet is a 
powerful tool, nothing can replace face-to-face conversations for building community, 
which is especially important at a time when so many people are divided or isolated.
I’m so proud of the work our strategic planning team has done to create a roadmap for the 
future to our libraries. I appreciate the countless hours they spent imagining new ways for 
our libraries to serve as gateways for connection and empowerment.  I’m grateful for our 
incredible external and internal partners who help us remain vigilant in our mission to make 
knowledge accessible to everyone in our communities. 
With their guidance, I’m confident that our library system will not only continue to thrive 
but serve as a model for the nation’s public libraries because we are willing to take bold 
steps toward the future. I commend our Foundation, Library Board, Volunteers, City 
Librarian, Staff, and all the external and internal stakeholders who work with us to create a 
better tomorrow through our libraries.
Alex Khojikian, City Manager

FULL CIRCLE: LITERALLY 
RISING FROM THE ASHES
The goal of the original San Mateo Public 
Library was simply to provide a “reading 
room” or place for leisure time socializing 
while improving minds. That was the modest 
vision that spurred the creation of the first 
version of the San Mateo Library.
It was established in one room of a small 
brick building called Library Hall. When fire 
destroyed the building, undaunted residents 
quickly built another library on the same site. 
Public support grew until in 1904, funded 
by citizens and administered by the city 
government, the San Mateo Public Library 
system we know today was created.
We continue to embody the past community 
spirit that, undaunted, resurrected the library 
from literal ashes because people knew that 
a place where community members could 
“socialize and improve minds” was essential to 
fostering a healthy, thriving community.
We also resolutely look forward by navigating 
a present and planning for a future that 
patrons of the “reading room”, could never 
have imagined. Community support and 
a commitment to service still define the 
character of the San Mateo Public Library 
and confirm that we will rise again and again 
to meet the challenges and boldly seize the 
opportunities the future holds.
