CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
May 27, 2025
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Sheila Canzian, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:30 
p.m.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Ms. Canzian led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees  Sheila Canzian, Wendy 
McCall, Richard Wysong and Austin Volz.  Trustee Annie Tsai had previously notified the 
Board that she was unable to attend this meeting.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. McCall, seconded by Ms. Tsai, 
and the motion passed 4-0.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
OLD BUSINESS
Continued Discussion of Naming Policy for Library Spaces:  City Librarian Rukshana Singh and 
the Library Board of Trustees continued their discussion regarding the creation of a naming 
policy that will inform how the San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) names its spaces in the future.  
Ms. Singh and the Board looked at some provisions from policies of other libraries for reference 
and agreed that a new policy for SMPL should be broad enough to include outside spaces, 
have a budgeting provision for continued upkeep of the space, include “useful life” language for 
the term of the naming so it can be renamed in the future if needed, and to make it a 
recognition and naming policy rather than just a naming policy.  Alison Joly, a member of the 
public, suggested that the City’s Code of Conduct/Ethics be kept in mind when drafting the 
policy, particularly in situations of name revocations.  Ms. Singh will prepare a draft policy for 
the Board to review at a future meeting. 
6.
NEW BUSINESS
A. Collection of Statistics:  Ms. Singh and the Board discussed what statistics should be 
collected and reviewed now that SMPL has a new 2024-2028 Strategic Plan that includes 
the goals of promoting literacy for all, creating beautiful and functional community spaces, 
increasing and securing stable funding, and proactively engaging the community.  The 
Board asked questions and offered feedback.  It was agreed that statistics will be reviewed 
quarterly instead of monthly, and to not include pre-pandemic comparisons any longer.  
Also, it was agreed that library staff would present information on library programs to the 
Board on a quarterly basis.  Alison Joly, a member of the public and a volunteer with SMPL, 
said that patrons at the Hillsdale Branch were very happy when the announcement was 
made that Children’s Storytime would be held at Beresford Park while the new playground 
at Central Park was under construction.
B. Proposal from EHDD Architecture:  Ms. Singh shared a portion of a draft proposal from 
EHDD Architecture for a new plan to complete projects at the Main Library with the Library 
Board of Trustees.  Ms. Singh is seeking to have the booklet “San Mateo Public Library – 
Possible Directions for Space Modification”, prepared by EHDD in 2017, updated.  The

REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A.  City Librarian Rukshana Singh:
•
The Marina Branch Library Restoration Project went out to bid last week and three bids 
were received out of the approximately 30 people that came to the pre-bid meeting.  
Construction is anticipated to start in August.  After the bids are reviewed, there will be 
a better sense of the budgeting and if fundraising will be necessary.
•
Recruitment is in progress for the Deputy City Librarian position.  Oral boards and the 
department interview will be held in July and Ms. Singh hopes to fill the position by the 
end of July.
•
Outreach for summer learning has started.  The Children’s team will be visiting eight 
elementary schools over the next three weeks.  Library staff will also visit the high 
schools.  Every student will receive a free book.
•
SMPL is in the top five for Best of Awards this year and we won last year.  There will be 
a Chamber of Commerce event ceremony on June 3 at the College of San Mateo.
•
Some highlights of SMPL’s DEI work includes outreach to youth at Hillcrest Juvenile 
Hall and English language learners at public high schools, outreach to Peninsula Jewish 
Community Center, working with veterans groups, planning more programming for 
seniors, held our first Spanish language gardening program, and outreach at different 
community events that SMPL had not participated in before including B Street Fiesta, 
PAL Family Night, Lunar New Year, Boba Day, an AAPI cultural event, and Fiesta 
Gardens Science Festival.  In addition, teen programming is always open to 
neuroatypical and neurotypical people, and people of all abilities.  Parents of 
neurodiverse children typically want their children to go to programs that are open to 
everyone, not programs open only to neurodiverse children.  
•
Ms. Singh attended the Pacific Library Partnership Directors meeting on May 16.  The 
theme for this meeting was advocacy and what is happening at the state and federal 
level.  Dr. Carla Hayden, the Librarian of Congress, was recently fired by President 
Trump.  When Dr. Hayden was the American Library Association President, she asked 
KG Ouye, SMPL’s City Librarian at the time, to join her “kitchen cabinet” to advise on 
the state of public libraries.  In addition, Dr. Hayden came to San Mateo to help with a 
fundraising event for the new Main Library.  Ms. Singh will be writing Congressman 
Kevin Mullin to let him know how about the special connection Dr. Hayden has to San 
Mateo and to express her displeasure with her firing.
•
There were 22 applications for the two Library Board Trustee positions.  The applicants 
who were selected will be announced at the City Council meeting on June 2.   In 
addition, the Library Board will now have two Youth Commissioners.  They will be 
present at the meeting on June 23.
    
B. Board Members:
•
Ms. McCall visited two libraries in the Washington DC area.  The area for teens in the 
Winchester, Virginia library was a large area, occupying approximately 25% of the 
building.
•
Mr. Volz will be resigning from the Library Board after the June 23 meeting and will be 
joining the San Mateo Library Foundation as a Board member.
