SUBJECT:
Fiscal Sustainability Strategy - Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the fiscal sustainability strategy developed in 2022 and provide feedback for policy updates.
BACKGROUND:
The current cost recovery strategy and policy was adopted in 2004. The current strategy identifies two thresholds of cost 
recovery for each activity a minimum and a target threshold; for example, Activity A may be expected to at a minimum 
recover all of its direct programmatic costs while the target threshold may be to recover all direct overhead costs as well 
and Activity B may have two entirely different thresholds. These thresholds are to be established based upon 
consideration of a number of factors including services, values, market conditions costs, demand, and the general fiscal 
condition of the City. In addition, it is recommended that additional fee considerations be identified where appropriate. 
For example, a specific activity may be in a specific market and therefore fees may be benchmarked to specific 
competitors. Although this system of cost recovery worked for the department for many years, it is very complex and 
requires a lot of administrative management.
Due to the lasting effect of the pandemic, the department saw a decrease in revenue. During this time the department 
shifted focus to providing low-cost human services that aligned with health mandates from the County and State. Services 
such as LEAP (Learning Education Assistance Program) which provided a safe environment for families to send their 
children to complete at home studies while schools remained closed.  Due to the changing economy staff thought it 
prudent to revisit its fiscal sustainability (cost recovery) strategy and began doing so by carrying out a holistic analysis of 
costs and associated revenue to ensure that high value services were being offered to the community and appropriate 
levels of subsidy were being provided for different service categories.
In April 2022, staff engaged 110%, Inc. to analyze the department’s cost recovery and fiscal sustainability position. After 
months of collaboration, compiling and analyzing revenue and expenses, the progress was presented to the Commission 
on November 2, 2022, for their feedback.  The Commission participated in an activity to prioritize eleven service categories 
(Attachment 1).  This process helped to design the Beneficiary of Service Model (Attachment 2) and laid the groundwork 
for current work on the development of a new fiscal sustainability strategy policy. It should be noted that the focus of this 
phase of our fiscal sustainability analysis is on recreational offerings. At this stage staff would like to present an update on 
the progress.
Next steps include bringing this update to the City Council for a study session. Staff will then return to the commissions 
and council for policy adoption and amendment of the fee schedule.

SERVICE CATEGORIES

Advanced Activities - Classes, clinics, workshops, and other led/instructed activities designed to advance or master a
skill.
Examples: Advanced Athletic Classes, Advanced Martial Arts, Advanced Art

Beginner Activities - Classes, clinics, workshops, and other led/instructed activities aimed at acquiring or learning a new
skill.
Examples: Beginning Dance, Fitness, Swim Lessons

Camps Contracted - Camps operated by third parties during school breaks, providing specialized enrichment for
children.
Examples: Science/Tech Camps, Musical Theater Camps, Dance Camps, Athletic Camps, Art Camps

Child Care — In-house childcare facilitated by city staff.
Examples: Summer Camps, Afterschool Care, Care on No-Schoo! Days

City-Wide Events —Large-scale events appealing to a broad community segment regardless of age, ability, or family
composition. These intensive events typically occur annually.
Examples: Summer Concert Series, Fourth of July, Movies in the Park, Winter Wonderland

Competitive Activities — Organized competitive leagues without guided instruction.
Examples: Adult Softball, Adult Volleyball

Drop-In Activities - Self-directed activities that do not require registration or instruction but include supervision or
oversight by staff and/or volunteers.
Examples: Rec Lap Swim, Open Gym, Games, Crafts, Senior Movies

Human Services - Services designed to support independence and community connection by providing or linking
resources for those in need.
Examples: Senior Transportation Services, Senior Lunch Services, Community Lectures

Parks and Facilities- Access to parks, park amenities, and recreation facilities for all community members.
Examples: City of San Mateo Parks such Casanova, Beresford Park, and Trinta.

Private/Semi-Private Activities - Individualized classes, clinics, workshops, and other led/instructed activities offered in
a private or semi-private setting to meet specific needs, interests or skill sets.

Examples: Private Music Lessons, Tech Tutoring, Private Swim Lessons

Rentals — Reservable outdoor/indoor spaces and facilities available for exclusive use by individuals or groups.
Examples: Recreation/Community Center Rooms, Sports Fields, Picnic Areas
