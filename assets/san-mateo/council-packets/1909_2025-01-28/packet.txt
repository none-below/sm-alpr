SUBJECT: 
General Plan 2040 Land Use Map Amendments
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of a Resolution to amend the General Plan 2040 Land Use Map to update the 
land use designation at 13 specified locations.
BACKGROUND:
On January 30, 2024, the Planning Commission recommended adoption of Strive San Mateo General Plan 2040 (General 
Plan 2040), which was adopted by City Council on March 18, 2024. General Plan 2040 consists of eight Elements and 
associated maps, which provide the foundation for future growth, change, and preservation in San Mateo. The Land Use 
Element sets the allowed land uses, heights, densities, and intensities (floor area ratio) for all parcels in the City. General 
Plan Table LU-1 Land Use Designations summarizes this information and is included as Attachment 2. The General Plan 
Land Use Map, Figure LU-1, acts as a visual representation of the land use designations detailed in the Land Use Element 
and is included as Attachment 3. During the General Plan 2040 adoption hearings and at a subsequent public meeting on 
implementation, the City Council, staff, and individual landowners identified 13 sites across the city where land use 
designations were either inconsistent with the existing and/or surrounding land uses, or required revisions to align with 
City Council direction on General Plan 2040. The 13 sites with recommended land use designation amendments are 
described in Table 1 below. 
Pursuant to Government Code Section 653534 and San Mateo Municipal Code Section 27.06.040(b), the Planning 
Commission must provide a recommendation to the City Council at a public hearing, prior to Council action on amending 
the General Plan. The resolution with findings to support the Planning Commission’s recommendation to the City Council is 
included as Attachment 1, with figures that show the recommended land use map amendments are included as Exhibit A. 
DISCUSSION:
Land Use Map Amendments
During the City Council adoption hearings, Council identified two sites: 1) the office buildings at 66 and 160 Bovet Rd. and 
2) the former Marriott hotel site at 1770 S. Amphlett Boulevard., as opportunities to redesignate to the Mixed-Use High 
land use designation to increase flexibility and housing production potential. Because these changes were not previously 
discussed at the Planning Commission recommendation hearing, they were not incorporated into the General Plan 
adoption, and direction was provided to bring the amendments back to the Planning Commission for consideration. Since 
General Plan adoption in March 2024, 11 additional locations throughout the city have been identified where updated

Measure T Consistency
At the time of adoption, General Plan 2040 included land use designations that exceeded the height, density, and intensity 
limits set by Measure Y, which was approved by voters on November 3, 2020. While Measure Y was in effect, any conflicts 
between these limits and the General Plan defaulted to Measure Y, restricting full implementation of the Land Use 
Element. Following extensive community outreach, on July 15, 2024, the City Council voted to place Measure T on the 
November 5, 2024, ballot. Measure T proposed repealing Measure Y, such that its limits within the ten General Plan Study 
Areas would be repealed (but largely retained elsewhere) to enable the implementation of the General Plan's Land Use 
Map. Voters approved Measure T, which became effective on December 19, 2024, 10 days after the City Council certified 
the election results.  
Measure T includes a provision allowing land use changes for eight sites listed in Table 1 above (Site Nos. 1,2,4,5,9, 10, 12, 
and 13) to be processed without voter approval if the proposed land uses align with those recommended in the ballot 
measure. These recommendations were chosen to reflect existing or surrounding land uses, or to implement City Council 
direction from the General Plan adoption hearing on March 18, 2024. However, Measure T requires these amendments, 
along with any future General Plan Land Use Map amendments, to undergo public hearings before the Planning 
Commission and City Council in compliance with state and local laws (Government Code Section 653534 and San Mateo 
Municipal Code Section 27.06.040(b)). As the land use designations of these eight sites align with the recommended 
designations in Measure T, and the proposed land use map amendments for all 13 sites are being processed as required by 
state and local laws, all recommended land use designations in Table 1 are consistent with Measure T and the General 
Plan’s Land Use Element.
Consistency with State Law – No Net Loss Requirement
The proposed General Plan Land Use Map amendments are consistent with state laws relating to No Net Loss 
requirement. The No Net Loss requirement, as implemented under the Housing Crisis Act (California Government Code 
Section 66300), prohibits a city from “enacting a development policy, standard, or condition with respect to land where 
housing is an allowable use that would change the General Plan land use designation, specific plan land use designation, or 
zoning of a parcel or parcels of property to a less intensive use or reducing the intensity of land use within an existing 
General Plan land use designation, specific plan land use designation, or zoning district in effect at the time of the 
proposed change unless the city concurrently changes the development standards, policies, and conditions applicable to 
other parcels within the jurisdiction to ensure that there is no net loss in residential capacity.”

Of the 13 sites identified above, the Arbor Rose Condominiums (Site No. 7) and the adjacent vacant parcel at 1050 S. 
Amphlett Boulevard (Site No. 8) are the only sites with recommended land use designations that would decrease the 
residential density. These changes would shift the designations of both sites from Regional Commercial (up to 50 du/ac) to 
Residential Low II (20-35 du/ac) to align with existing residential uses, and to Service Commercial/Light Industrial, where 
housing is a prohibited use, to match the service commercial/light industrial uses that exist along this section of S. 
Amphlett Boulevard. These two land use map changes would result in a reduction of 77 units of density capacity. The 
proposed changes in land use designations on the remaining 11 sites, however, would result in an increase of 1,751 units 
of density capacity. Because of the net increase of 1,674 units of capacity, the land use designation changes at these two 
locations would not affect the City’s overall residential development capacity pursuant to the Housing Crisis Act.
Interim Implementation 
Through the adoption of General Plan 2040, new land use designations have been established with increased densities, 
heights, floor area ratios, and uses that do not align with current zoning districts and their associated development 
standards. Updates to the Zoning Code to align with the newly created land use designations through a comprehensive 
update will commence later this year and are expected to take up to two years to complete. In the interim, any 
development standards for parcels with land use designations that do not align with their current zoning district or that 
lack a corresponding zoning district (e.g., Quasi-Public) will default to the General Plan. While the underlying zoning will 
remain in effect until updated, any development standards that are inconsistent with the heights, densities, intensities and 
uses contained Table LU-1 of the Land Use Element would be superseded. An interim Zoning Code amendment to clarify 
that any inconsistencies between the Zoning Ordinance and the General Plan shall defer to General Plan 2040 is currently 
being prepared, with a public hearing before the Planning Commission anticipated for February 11, 2025.
Next Steps 
Following the Planning Commission public hearing and recommendation, the City Council will hold a public hearing to 
consider the General Plan Land Use Map amendments, which is tentatively scheduled for March 17, 2025. If approved, the 
General Plan amendments will be effective one day after the date of the action.

was certified by the City Council on March 18, 2024, and filed with the State Clearinghouse (SCH No. 2022010160). 
Pursuant to CEQA Guidelines Subsection 15168(c), the amendments can be approved as being within the scope of the 
program EIR. The proposed land use changes are consistent with the type of allowable land uses, overall planned density 
and building intensity, geographic area analyzed for environmental impacts, and covered infrastructure as described and 
evaluated in the EIR. The increase in development capacity (1,674 units) resulting from the proposed General Plan
