Re: Request to Defer Further Review of Baywood Nomination
Dear State Historic Preservation Officer Julianne Polanco: 
The City of San Mateo (City) understands that the Baywood District nomination submitted by the San Mateo Heritage 
Alliance (SMHA) was recently reactivated. Considering the significant community concerns this nomination has raised, 
we, the San Mateo City Council respectfully request the following:
•
Notification: Please promptly inform the City when (date) the nomination was reactivated.
•
Correspondence: Kindly copy the City on all correspondence between SMHA and the OHP regarding this 
nomination.
•
Deferral: We ask that further OHP review be deferred for at least 18 months, as we work through a local 
process.
By way of context, the City recently allocated funds to develop a local process for evaluating and designating historic 
resources, including updates to related ordinances. This initiative is intended to foster broader community 
engagement and build consensus around our historic preservation efforts. Should the nomination advance at the state 
level before our local process is fully developed, it could inadvertently undermine these efforts—not only in Baywood 
but across the entire City of San Mateo.
Although we understand that the City Council will have a 60-day period to comment once the nomination is agendized 
by the OHP, we may not be in a position to offer comprehensive feedback until our local process is complete, which 
we anticipate will take approximately 18 months. Moreover, we are concerned that additional investments of 
resources by both the OHP and SMHA might limit receptiveness to alternative perspectives.
In light of the fact that several Baywood residents have expressed their concerns through letters of objection, as 
outlined in California State Public Resources Code 5024.1(f).4, it seems unlikely that the district will be listed on the 
National Register. Pursuing the nomination amidst noticeable local opposition may draw unfavorable attention at both 
the local and state levels, which could affect broader historic preservation efforts in San Mateo. The Council believes 
that taking additional time for local discussions and community engagement could lead to a more positive and 
constructive outcome for everyone involved. 
Since Baywood is not in imminent danger of losing its historic character, we trust that you will agree there is no urgent 
need to proceed at this time. We appreciate your thoughtful consideration of our request to maintain the current 
pause on the nomination until the City is in a better position to participate.
