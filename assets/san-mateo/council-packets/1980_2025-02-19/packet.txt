BACKGROUND:
Community Funding Program
Each year, the City of San Mateo (City) receives an entitlement grant from the United States Department of Housing and 
Urban Development (HUD) for the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program. The City grants CDBG funds to 
local organizations that serve low-income residents (below 80 percent of Area Median Income, (AMI)) of San Mateo.
The City uses CDBG funds to assist the public in the following categories: public services, economic development, 
community development, fair housing administration, minor home rehabilitation, and public facilities. These are outlined 
in the City’s CDBG Annual Action Plan, a document adopted by the City Council and submitted to HUD that outlines the 
budget and goals for the upcoming CDBG program year. 
In recent years, the City has also received a Permanent Local Housing Allocation (PLHA) grant from the California 
Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD); this program follows a Five-Year Plan.
 
Through the Community Funding program, the City releases an annual Notice of Funding Availability (NOFA) to solicit 
applications for both CDBG and PLHA. The NOFA is coordinated with other CDBG jurisdictions in San Mateo County, 
allowing agencies to apply online via a consolidated application and to receive CDBG allocations from multiple 
jurisdictions. Each jurisdiction sets its own criteria for selection.
The Community Relations Commission (CRC) reviews Community Funding applications and makes funding 
recommendations for the upcoming program year. 
CDBG 2025-26 Entitlement
The estimated amounts in each CDBG funding category are outlined below. These amounts are based on an estimated 
total amount of $753,586 in CDBG entitlement funds for the prior (2024-25) program year, plus unspent funds from prior 
years. Please note that federal regulations cap the amount of CDBG funds that can be used for public service activities to 
15% of a jurisdiction’s total entitlement, and administration to 20% of the total entitlement. The estimated amounts 
available in each category are as follows:

PLHA Entitlement
The City receives annual funding from HCD through the PLHA program for housing-related activities. Grant allocations by 
activity area are set in a Five-Year Plan adopted by City Council and submitted to HCD. During the five-year cycle, 
applicants who received funding in the previous program year are required to apply as a “renewal” to continue receiving 
PLHA funds. However, amendments have been made to the Five-Year Plan to direct PLHA funds to new activities, or to 
adjust the distribution of funding between grant recipients, based on staff recommendations. 
PLHA applications do not require CRC review, and this breakdown of funds is provided to the CRC for informational 
purposes only. In FY 2024-25, the City’s total PLHA amount was $293,103, and four programs received PLHA funding:
•
Human Investment Project, Inc. (HIP): Affordable Housing Programs - $20,000
•
LifeMoves: Rapid Rehousing - $181,907
•
Samaritan House: Safe Harbor Shelter - $15,000
•
Bridge Housing Corporation: Montara - $61,541
•
Administration: $14,655
The amount of PLHA funding available for Year 5 is $224,653.
CDBG Proposal Review for Program Year 2025-26
CDBG Community Funding runs on a two-year cycle, meaning that the application is only open to 2024-2025 CDBG 
grantees to re-apply and “renew” their funding for the 2025-2026 program year (with the exception of public facilities 
applicants). 
The City received a total of 13 applications by the January 21, 2025 NOFA deadline. Seven are for public services, two for 
economic development/microenterprise, two for minor home repair programs, one for fair housing services, and one for 
public facilities improvements. 
City staff conducted a preliminary review of applications for completeness and compliance with CDBG requirements. A 
summary of each proposal is included in the Proposal Evaluation Summary in Attachment 1. At this meeting, the CRC 
should review and score each application out of 100 points using the Evaluation Criteria in Attachment 2. 
The CRC’s funding recommendation for the 2024-25 program year was to fund all public services applications by the same 
amount. As the 2025-26 program year is a “renewal” year, staff recommends maintaining this distribution of funds. Staff 
also recommends allocating the same amount in the other CDBG activity areas of fair housing, minor home repair, and 
economic development as was allocated in 2024-25. Staff proposes to reduce CDBG administration to $60,000 and to 
allocate approximately $449,850 to City Street & Sidewalk Improvements (public facilities), which is the balance of 
available CDBG funds after other allocations are made.

Budget Contingency
As in prior years, at the time of this CRC meeting, the total entitlement amount has not been confirmed by HUD. 
Therefore, grant amounts are provided as estimates subject to a future adjustment. In the case that the entitlement 
amount is less than the previous year, the City must determine how to allocate this reduced funding to the applicants. 
Likewise, the City must account for how a higher entitlement amount will be distributed among subrecipients (while 
maintaining the 15% public service and 20% administration caps).
With this funding uncertainty, the CRC may make funding recommendations using the folloiwng draft contingency 
language:
If CDBG allocation is less than estimated:
•
Reduce the amount awarded for all public service applications equally to remain under the 15% cap. If the amount 
awarded per application is below $10,000, award fewer public service programs.
•
Retain the amounts for minor home repair, economic development, and fair housing awards.
•
Reduce the amount allocated to the City-led public facilities Street & Sidewalk Improvements project.
•
Reduce CDBG administration so as to remain under the regulatory cap of 20%.
If CDBG allocation is more than estimated:
•
Increase the amount awarded to public service applications equally while remaining under the 15% public service 
cap. 
•
Maintain the same award amount for minor home repair, economic development, and fair housing programs. 
•
Increase CDBG administration while remaining under the regulatory cap of 20%.
•
Any remaining additional funds will be used for public facilities.

implementation impeded the ability of our same staff to also conduct home previews that they normally would have been conducting to be sure that our pipeline was full ahead of contract execution.
