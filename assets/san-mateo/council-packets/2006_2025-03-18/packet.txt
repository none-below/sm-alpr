Statement of Decision by the San Mateo Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in the Matter of an 
Appeal of the Public Works Director’s Decision to Grant a Wireless Permit
(401 Dorchester Road, Permit # WC-2024-000246)
Section 1. Introduction
San Mateo Municipal Code (SMMC) Chapter 17.10 establishes reasonable, uniform and comprehensive 
standards and procedures for wireless facilities deployment, construction, installation, collocation, modification, 
operation, relocation, and removal within the City's public right-of-way, consistent with and to the extent 
permitted under federal and California state law (“Ordinance”).  To that end, SMMC Section 17.10.040 requires 
a wireless permit, City of San Mateo (“City”) pole license (if the proposed facility is located on a City owed pole), 
various other permits and approvals, and fees to be submitted prior to the installation of any proposed wireless 
facility.
SMMC Section 17.10.070(a)-(b) specifies the Public Works Director (“Director”), or designee, shall approve, 
conditionally approve, or deny an application for a wireless permit upon a finding that: 
(1) the applicant and the proposed wireless facility complies with all the requirements of SMMC Chapter 
17.10, and the design standards and application requirements in effect at the time of application; 
(2) the application is complete; 
(3) the proposed wireless facility complies with all application federal, state, and local laws regulations, 
rules, restrictions, and conditions; and 
(4) the proposed wireless facility will be in compliance with all applicable Federal Communications 
Commission (“FCC”) rules and regulations relating to Radio Frequency (“RF”) emissions.  
SMMC Section 17.10.070(g) provides that an applicant, or property owners or residents within 500 feet of any 
proposed facility, may appeal the Director’s decision to approve or deny an application for a wireless permit 
with five calendar days of the decision to the City’s Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission 
(“Commission”).  However, appeals based on the environmental effects from RF emissions that comply with all 
applicable FCC regulations are not allowed.  The appellant bears the burden of proof to establish the basis for 
seeking a reversal of the Director’s decision.  The Commission’s decision shall be the final administrative 
decision for the City.
On November 15, 2024, Crown Castle (“Applicant”), working on behalf of Verizon Wireless, submitted an 
application for a wireless permit for a proposed wireless facility to be installed on a replacement wood utility 
pole in the San Mateo Park neighborhood, approximately 150-feet north of the West Poplar Avenue/Dorchester 
Road intersection on a replacement wood utility pole (No ID) within the public sidewalk (“Application”). This 
Application is subject to the 2021 Design Standards and Application Requirements and Ordinance in effect at the 
time of application.
On January 31, 2025, the Director conditionally approved the Application.  On February 4, 2025, the City 
received an appeal from Mr. Sam Shank (“Appellant”) asserting various reasons why the Application should be 
denied.  The appeal hearing was subsequently held at a Commission regular meeting on March 12, 2025.

At the appeal hearing on this matter, City staff provided a presentation to the Commission which described the 
proposed wireless facility, staff’s determination that the Application was complete, and a response to 
Appellants’ assertions.  City staff prepared an Agenda Report which provides a summary of staff’s responses to 
the Appeal, which is summarized below.  During its review, City staff also reviewed all possible alternative site

In the presentation to the Commission, City staff also categorized the Appellants’ appeal categories, with 
responses to each, which includes:
1)
Category 1– Alternative Site Analysis.  
Appellants assert that the Applicant failed to show why only existing poles within the 200-foot 
radius from the proposed location are technically feasible and why other sites within a 500-foot 
radius is not technically feasible.  
Staff determined the Applicant provided sufficient information in the Alternative Site Analysis to 
approve the application because, based on staff’s own experience as well as consultation with a 
retained RF consultant, the reasonable range for these small wireless facilities is between 200 to 
300 feet, with 300 feet being under the most ideal conditions without physical interference.  
Here, the Applicant’s objective appears to be improved coverage and capacity in the immediate 
area of the proposed location and since the signal attenuates rapidly after 200 feet, it would not 
allow the Applicant to achieve this objective if other locations beyond 200 feet are considered.  
In addition, trees and other interferences limit the effective range in this area.
2)

Section 1. Introduction

San Mateo Municipal Code (SMMC) Chapter 17.10 establishes reasonable, uniform and comprehensive
standards and procedures for wireless facilities deployment, construction, installation, collocation, modification,
operation, relocation, and removal within the City's public right-of-way, consistent with and to the extent
permitted under federal and California state law. To that end, SMMC Section 17.10.040 requires a wireless
permit, City of San Mateo (“City”) pole license (if the proposed facility is located on a City owed pole), various
other permits and approvals, and fees to be submitted prior to the installation of any proposed wireless facility.

SMMC Section 17.10.070(a)-(b) specifies the Public Works Director (“Director”), or designee, shall approve,
conditionally approve, or deny an application for a wireless permit upon a finding that:

(1) the applicant and the proposed wireless facility complies with all the requirements of SMMC Chapter
17.10, and the design standards and application requirements in effect at the time of application;

(2) the application is complete;

(3) the proposed wireless facility complies with all application federal, state, and local laws regulations,
rules, restrictions, and conditions; and

(4) the proposed wireless facility will be in compliance with all applicable Federal Communications
Commission (“FCC”) rules and regulations relating to Radio Frequency (“RF”) emissions.

SMMC Section 17.10.070(g) provides that an applicant, or property owners or residents within 500 feet of any
proposed facility, may appeal the Director’s decision to approve or deny an application for a wireless permit
within five calendar days of the decision to the City’s Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission
(“Commission”). However, appeals based on the environmental effects from RF emissions that comply with all
applicable FCC regulations are not allowed. The appellant bears the burden of proof to establish the basis for
seeking a reversal of the Director’s decision. The Commission’s decision shall be the final administrative
decision for the City.

a wireless permit, City of San Mateo (“City”) pole license (if the proposed facility is located on a City owed pole), 
various other permits and approvals, and fees to be submitted prior to the installation of any proposed wireless 
facility.
SMMC Section 17.10.070(a)-(b) specifies the Public Works Director (“Director”), or designee, shall approve, 
conditionally approve, or deny an application for a wireless permit upon a finding that: 
(1) the applicant and the proposed wireless facility complies with all the requirements of SMMC Chapter 
17.10, and the design standards and application requirements in effect at the time of application; 
(2) the application is complete; 
(3) the proposed wireless facility complies with all application federal, state, and local laws regulations, 
rules, restrictions, and conditions; and 
(4) the proposed wireless facility will be in compliance with all applicable Federal Communications 
Commission (“FCC”) rules and regulations relating to Radio Frequency (“RF”) emissions.  
SMMC Section 17.10.070(g) provides that an applicant, or property owners or residents within 500 feet of any 
proposed facility, may appeal the Director’s decision to approve or deny an application for a wireless permit 
with five calendar days of the decision to the City’s Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission 
(“Commission”).  However, appeals based on the environmental effects from RF emissions that comply with all 
applicable FCC regulations are not allowed.  The appellant bears the burden of proof to establish the basis for 
seeking a reversal of the Director’s decision.  The Commission’s decision shall be the final administrative 
decision for the City.
On October 22, 2024, Crown Castle (“Applicant”), working on behalf of Verizon Wireless, submitted an 
application for a wireless permit for a proposed wireless facility to be installed at the east corner of the El 
Camino Real/Baldwin Avenue intersection on an existing City-owned streetlight pole (Pole ID# 2805) within the 
public sidewalk (“Application”). This Application is subject to the 2021 Design Standards and Application 
Requirements and Ordinance in effect at the time of application.
On February 28, 2025, the Director conditionally approved the Application.  The City thereafter received 
identical appeals from Mary Bianco, Kevin Lewis, and Senovia Grismore and George Grismore (collectively 
“Appellants”) asserting various reasons why the Application should be denied.  The appeal hearing was 
subsequently held at a Commission regular meeting on March 12, 2025.

Based upon the information presented at the hearing on this matter, the Commission makes the 
following findings:
1)
The Director conditionally approved the Application for a wireless permit on February 28, 2025.
2)
The City received timely appeals from Appellants asserting various reasons why the permit application 
should be denied.
3)
The Appellants have the burden to establish a basis for reversal of the Director’s decision approving 
Application.
