SUBJECT:
City Council Strategic Plan Workshop  
RECOMMENDATION:
Engage in a facilitated discussion to begin development of the Strategic Plan as agreed upon on October 7, 2024, before 
returning with a draft Plan for Council review and further direction in Summer 2025.
BACKGROUND:
On October 7, 2024 the City Council reviewed staff’s proposal to develop a Strategic Plan, which will be a living document 
providing a framework to help guide the implementation of existing planning documents for the City Council to use in 
determining both short and long term priorities. It will guide the Annual Budget process and integrate key elements from 
large and long-term planning documents, such as the General Plan and Capital Improvement Projects. The October 7, 2024 
Agenda Report provided details and is attached for reference (Attachment 1). The Council directed staff to move forward 
with next steps to onboard a consultant and plan workshops to develop, and review at key milestones, the Strategic Plan. 
To inform the Plan, the Council will rely upon staff and information from the General Plan (i.e. the Ten Big Ideas), community 
“Conversations” outreach effort, 2025 Community Survey, and 2024 Faith/community Leaders Round Table meeting, which 
are summarized in Attachment 2.  
The Strategic Plan will utilize the following elements: 
•
Mission Statement: the guiding benchmark for Council decisions, developed and approved by the City Council.
•
Initiatives: major categories reflecting the long-term concerns and interests of the City, developed by the City Council.
•
Priorities:  key initiatives identified by the Council that will guide both direction and financial decisions.  These priorities 
may span several years yet are reviewed annually as part of the Annual Budget process.
•
Goals:  high-level outcomes established by the Council, related to Priorities, with typically 2 – 4 goals per Priority.
•
Activities:  approved and funded projects and programs that align with Priorities and Initiatives as developed by staff, 
reflecting Council interests and direction.
•
Reports:  regular reports to Council reflecting progress and outcomes on approved Activities.  Though not a part of the 
Strategic Plan document, they are critical to the effectiveness of the process and for the Council in decision-making.
It is anticipated that the Strategic Plan will be implemented as part of the Fiscal Year 2026/27 process.  To accomplish this, 
the following steps are needed:

SUBJECT:
Discussion and Adoption of Strategic Planning Process for the City Council.
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve the following: (1) incorporate a robust Strategic Plan into the Annual Budget Process, starting with the Fiscal Year 
2026- 2027; (2) develop the Strategic Plan during an off-site workshop by City Council in February 2025; (3) continue the 
Blue Sky goal-setting workshop to include key Council projects in the Fiscal Year 2026- 2027 annual budget process.
BACKGROUND:
The City Council is responsible for reviewing and approving an annual budget that funds all city services. This process 
involves several months of public and Council input and review, concluding with a final approval in June, in preparation for 
the Fiscal Year commencing on July 1.
Currently, the process involves the following: 
•
Blue Sky goal-setting workshop to gather initial public input, identify City Council priorities, and announce major 
department initiatives. 
•
Preliminary budget to indicate ongoing projects, resources, staffing, facilities, legal requirements and projects, and 
City Council priorities identified during the Blue Sky goal-setting process. 
•
Finalized proposed budget presented to City Council.
•
Approval of the final budget.  
The adopted budget provides the financial framework for the fiscal year’s projects and ongoing operations and services.  
Additionally, the City Council may approve necessary adjustments to the budget in response to either unforeseen 
opportunities or challenges.
 
The City Council often faces complex challenges when various demands and interests, regulatory requirements, fiscal 
restraints, and aging infrastructure combat with long-term planning. This impacts the City Council’s overall vision and 
priorities for the City, making it difficult to balance immediate needs with long-term goals.   
 
A robust Strategic Plan would offer a comprehensive framework for City Council to utilize when making decisions for both 
long-term and immediate priorities. It would also guide the Annual budget process and integrate key elements from large 
and long-term planning documents, such as the General Plan and Capital Improvement Projects for greater efficiency and 
impact. 
 
Proposed Strategic Plan Elements
Staff is proposing a Strategic Plan that will incorporate the following elements:

The initial Strategic Plan would be developed under the following process, and then reviewed annually thereafter for any 
modifications.
1.
Approval of Concept and Framework: Council approves the overall Strategic Plan concept.
2.
Mission Statement, Strategic Initiatives, and Strategic Priorities: Plan to develop and approve during an off-site 
workshop, tentatively planned for February 2025.
3.
Goals: City Council will approve of Goals in the fall of 2025.
4.
Activities: City Council will approve of Activities and incorporate in the Annual Budget process thereafter.
The off-site workshop will be facilitated by a strategic planning consultant, and staff will provide City Council with resources 
that include General Plan categories (Land Use, Housing, Circulation, Economic Development, Communications and 
Outreach, Sustainability and Climate Change Resilience, Infrastructure and Facilities), Community Conversations summaries, 
and community survey results.  Utilizing these resources, the Council will both identify the long-term Strategic Initiatives and 
the Strategic Priorities.
 
Adopting this new Strategic Plan process will not interfere with the current Blue Sky goal-setting process for the forthcoming 
fiscal year. Council members will submit their top three priorities for consideration and provide the community the 
opportunity to submit public comment. In future years, the Blue Sky goal-setting process will be modified to align with the 
new Strategic Plan priorities identified by City Council while also ensuring opportunities for community input in accordance 
with both law and Council practices.
Staff recommends approving the proposed Strategic Plan process and parameters.

San Mateo City Council and Staff,
My name is Jayden Wan. I'm seventeen years old, and a senior at Burlingame High. I
spoke at the Council's 2/3 Meeting and their Blue Sky Session.
In light of the City’s process for long-term visioning, I’d like to address the discrepancy
between the Council’s proposed priorities and its actual action, specifically on the
topic of sustainability, safety, and bike/pedestrian infrastructure.
Communities across the Bay Area both in and outside the City of San Mateo are
mobilizing to support the Humboldt bike lanes. That's because the decisions made in
the City of San Mateo set rippling precedent for other cities locally, and removing bike
lanes in this city enables the removal of bike lanes in MY city. Other cities follow in
the footsteps of San Mateo, for better or worse.
I expect that the Council back down on their decision to remove the bike lanes 
for the following reasons and many more.
It is indisputable that bike lanes make streets safer for bikers, pedestrians, 
homeowners, and cars. They also reduce vehicle congestion and negative 
greenhouse gas emissions. 
The pilot program to install bike boulevards on Fremont/Idaho is an insufficient 
supplement for bikers locally, who deserve dedicated bike LANES. 
The Council has NOT involved youth in this decision-making process, and has 
not considered how removing the bike lanes would disenfranchise students at 
many local schools of their safety and mobility. 
In 10, 20, 50 years, the current councilmembers' terms will have expired and the 
City will undoubtedly return to this issue of bike and pedestrian infrastructure. 
Bike culture should and will have popularized increasingly by then, and by not 
thinking long-term, San Mateo would be hindering its capacity to provide for the
