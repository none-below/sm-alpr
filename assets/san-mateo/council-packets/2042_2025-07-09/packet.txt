SUBJECT:
Bicycle and Pedestrian Projects Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, acting as the City of San Mateo's Bicycle and Pedestrian Advisory 
Committee, receive an update on bicycle and pedestrian projects.
BACKGROUND:
Staff will present brief updates on current pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure projects, planning efforts, and recently 
awarded grants. An update was last provided to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in September 2024. 
PROJECTS
Downtown Parking Garage Pedestrian Safety Project: This project improves pedestrian safety at the entrances and exits of 
Central Parking Garage, Main Street Parking Garage, and the 2nd and El Camino Real Parking Garage. The project’s design 
was completed in 2022 and incorporates feedback from the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, City staff from 
the Public Works and Police Departments, and the community through an online community survey. The design includes 
pedestrian-friendly improvements at and near the parking garage entrances and exits such as audible and visual
pedestrian and vehicle warning devices, flexible delineators to provide physical separation between the drive aisle and
walking paths, speed bumps, and updated pavement markings and traffic signs. The following is an update on this effort.
•
Construction was completed in winter 2025.
Delaware Street Safe Routes to School Corridor: Staff was awarded funds under Cycle 5 of the California Transportation
Commission’s (CTC) Active Transportation Program (ATP) for the Delaware Street Safe Routes to School Corridor project.
This project will implement the Delaware Street Class IV separated bike lane and Class III bicycle boulevard as proposed in
the 2020 Bicycle Master Plan. The project provides necessary safety improvements in an area with a high level of traffic
stress and will improve safety and connectivity to two public schools accessed from Delaware Street. Further, this project
improves north-west connectivity to the Hillsdale Caltrain station and will directly connect to the future bike facility on 28th 
Avenue. The following is an update on this effort.
•
City staff and the consultant held two community meetings in April 2025 to share the proposed project 
improvements. The meetings, held at Fiesta Gardens International School and The Nueva School, asked for 
feedback on the bikeway design.
•
City staff presented the project at the May 5, 2025 City Council Study Session and to the San Mateo County 
Exposition and Fair Association Board of Directors on May 28, 2025.
•
City staff will be hosting another community meeting on the final design this summer. 
•
The final bikeway design will be complete this summer.

The bid process for the construction contract is anticipated to start in late summer 2025 followed by

Pavement Management Program Integration: Staff continues to integrate the implementation of bicycle facilities with
scheduled pavement management projects where funding for design and construction is available and feasible. The
following is an update on this effort.
•
Pavement Management Program 3-A Project: This project includes installation of two bike facilities: Class II bike 
lanes on Campus Drive from Hillsdale Boulevard to Live Oaks Drive and South B Street from 9th to 16th avenues. 
The bike facility on South B Street is identified as a high priority in the adopted Bicycle Master Plan and the 
Campus Drive facility is a medium-low priority. The bike facilities are expected to be installed in late summer 2025 
following the pavement work. One travel lane in each direction were removed on Campus Drive to accommodate 
the bike lanes. One lane in each direction is sufficient for the low vehicle volumes on Campus Drive. In addition, 
narrower and fewer vehicle lanes will help reduce speeding and improve safety along the corridor. No parking or 
travel lanes will be removed to accommodate the bike lanes on South B Street.
•
Pavement Management Program 4-A Project: This paving project will install Class II bike lanes on 5th Avenue 
between El Camino Real to Laurel Avenue. The bike lanes on 5th Avenue are a high priority in the adopted Bicycle 
Master Plan. Construction is anticipated to start in fall 2025. No travel lanes will be removed as part of this effort. 
Up to 7 parking spots may be removed for this project on westbound 5th Avenue, immediately east of El Camino 
Real. 
•
Pavement Management Program 5-B Project: This project will install a bicycle boulevard on South Grant Street 
from Concar Drive to 16th Avenue as well as traffic calming elements on South Grant Street. This bike boulevard is 
identified as a medium-high priority in the adopted Bicycle Master Plan. The bicycle boulevard will be constructed 
this summer and will not remove any travel lanes or parking. Additionally, this paving project has completed 
updating the striping and pavement markings for the existing Class II bike lanes on Norfolk Street from Lodi 
Avenue to Kehoe and Dale to Dakota Avenue. 
Peninsula Avenue Corridor Improvements: There are multiple projects planned for Peninsula Avenue aimed at improving 
the corridor for all modes. In the short term, the roadway markings from San Mateo Drive to Humboldt Street will be 
refreshed. In the medium term, local funds will be allocated as part of the FY25-26 budget to improve the Peninsula and 
Delaware Avenue intersection, which was identified as a priority intersection in the Local Roadway Safety Plan. 
Improvements will include improved lighting, high-visibility crosswalks, curb extensions, and a left turn arrow to separate 
left turn movements from pedestrians crossing the roadway. In the long term, the Peninsula Corridor Complete Streets 
Project will assess improvements including bike lanes, green infrastructure, signal optimization, and repaving. The 
following is an update on these efforts.
•
Refresh Roadway Markings: Roadway markings on Peninsula Avenue from San Mateo Drive to Humboldt Street 
are anticipated to be refreshed in summer 2025. The markings that will be refreshed include travel lane lines, 
arrows, and crosswalks.
•
Peninsula/Delaware Traffic Signal Modification Project: The request for proposal process to procure a consultant 
to assist with the design phase will be initiated this month (July 2025). Construction is expected in late 2026.  
•
Peninsula Corridor Complete Streets Project: City staff anticipate starting the planning and conceptual phase of the 
project in FY25-26. This phase will focus on understanding the impacts on the corridor, improvements that can be 
achieved, and planning level estimates for a complete streets project on the Peninsula Corridor. 
28th Avenue Gap Closure Project: Staff was awarded funds from the San Mateo County Transportation Authority
Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Cycle 5 to fund the design and environmental review for the 28th Avenue/Hillsdale
Caltrain Station Bicycle Access Gap Closure project. The intent of the project is to evaluate options and develop design
plans for the Class IV separated bike lane between Edison Street and El Camino Real identified in the 2020 Bicycle Master
Plan, providing a safe path of travel for pedestrians and bicyclists across the El Camino Real intersection. The project will 
also develop plans for a Class II bike lane from El Camino Real to Kyne Street. The following is an update on this effort.
•
The project scope was amended in April 2025 to remove the Delaware intersection and include additional

The project team will host a community meeting at Nueva High School on July 15, 2025 to gather feedback on the

Third Avenue and Norfolk Street Intersection Safety Improvements: This project is constructing improvements at the Third 
Avenue and Norfolk Street intersection to improve safety and efficiency for pedestrians, bicyclists, and drivers. The project 
will improve the walking and biking routes between the bike and pedestrian bridge on Third Avenue, existing Class II bike 
lane on South Norfolk Street, and the existing Class I bike path adjacent to the San Mateo Creek. The design incorporates 
community feedback to respond to safety and mobility concerns regarding crossing this intersection. The project is funded 
in part by the federal Community Development Block Grant (CDBG). The following is an update on this effort.
•
City staff held a community meeting in early January 2025 to present the proposed safety improvements and 
respond to potential concerns from the community. The concerns included the scale of outreach conducted, 
potential for delay and congestion with “no right turn on red” restrictions, and lack of a pedestrian crossing where 
the new bike crossing is proposed at the San Mateo Creek Trail. After the meeting, a frequently asked questions 
document responding to the concerns raised during the meeting was posted on the project webpage.
•
The first phase of construction occurred from February to April 2025 and implemented a new bikeway on Norfolk 
Street between 3rd Avenue and the San Mateo Creek Trail. 
•
The second phase of construction started in early June and will wrap up this month (July 2025). 
•
The third phase of construction is scheduled in fall/winter 2026 and will include traffic signal modifications and 
signal operation changes. 
Hillsdale Boulevard at Alameda de las Pulgas Traffic Signal Safety Project: This project will assess and design traffic signal
operations at the Hillsdale Boulevard and Alameda de las Pulgas intersection to optimize safety for all modes, including
pedestrians from nearby schools and bicyclists using the existing Class II bicycle lanes on Hillsdale Boulevard. The design
aims to balance the needs of both current and future users and minimize congestion at the intersection and will include 
safety improvements such as a new traffic signal with pedestrian signals and protected corners and pedestrian safety 
islands to provide a safe space for bicyclists and pedestrians to wait before crossing the roadway. The following is an 
update on this effort.
•
The final design is expected to be complete in fall 2025.
•
The project team will host a community meeting in the fall when school is back in session. 
•
Construction is anticipated in summer 2026.

Poplar and Humboldt Traffic Signal Project: This project will implement safety improvements at the Poplar Avenue and 
Humboldt Street intersection. This intersection was one of the top three priority intersections for safety improvements in 
the City’s 2024 Local Roadway Safety Plan due to the number of collisions at this location. The design includes various 
improvements to make traveling through the intersection safer for all modes, including new left turn signals to separate 
left-turn traffic from pedestrian crossings, leading pedestrian intervals, and upgraded lighting. The following is an update 
on this effort.
•
The design phase was completed in April 2025.
•
The construction contract was awarded at the June 16, 2025, Council meeting.
•
Construction is expected to begin this summer and finish in the fall. 
Hillsdale High School/31st Avenue Pedestrian Safety Improvement Project: The City was awarded funding from the San 
Mateo County Transportation Authority Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Cycle 7 to design pedestrian safety improvements 
on 31st Avenue from Del Monte Street to Alameda de las Pulgas. The improvements were identified through a walk audit 
with Hillsdale High School students, parents, and staff and were tested through a quick build project installed in early 
August 2024. The project will advance the recommended improvements from the walk audit into a construction-ready 
design that includes, but is not limited to, curb extensions, high-visibility crosswalks, and a new signal and protected 
intersection at the Alameda de las Pulgas intersection. Outreach will be conducted with the school and adjacent 
neighborhood as the design progresses. The following is an update on this effort.
•
The City was awarded $390,720 in grant funding from the SMCTA Cycle 7 Bike and Pedestrian Program in winter 
2024. 
•
City staff are waiting for the approved supplemental agreement from the San Mateo County Transportation 
Authority.
•
City staff will bring the project to City Council for approval to appropriate the necessary funds to cover the local 
match this summer.
Downtown Caltrain Station North Access Improvement Project: The City was awarded funding from the MTC 
Transportation Development Act Article 3 grant program to design a new Caltrain station entrance connecting to the 
northbound platform on the northeast side of the San Mateo Caltrain Station and improve pedestrian access to the station 
on Cypress Avenue. The project was identified as a high-priority project in the City’s adopted Transit-Oriented 
Development Pedestrian Access Plan due to its proximity to the station. By constructing a new entrance, the distance 
Caltrain riders from the North Central neighborhood must walk will be reduced by approximately one long block. The 
project will also improve Cypress Avenue to provide pedestrian-friendly access to the station through implementing new 
crosswalks, upgraded lighting, and wayfinding signage. Direct outreach with residents on Cypress Avenue is included in the 
project scope. The following is an update on this effort.
•
An agreement was awarded to a consultant in April 2025 to assist with the design phase of the project.
•
The project team held a kickoff meeting in early May 2025.
•
City staff and the consultant team are preparing three design alternatives for the project corridor that will be 
shared with the community at a future community outreach meeting.
PLANNING EFFORTS
Citywide Transportation Demand Management (TDM) Ordinance: The Citywide TDM Ordinance envisions a comprehensive
approach to TDM in San Mateo by recommending trip reduction goals to be established throughout the City to support
vehicles miles traveled (VMT) reduction and the goal of a multimodal transportation network. The project will propose
updates to the San Mateo Municipal Code and develop program guidelines to provide detail and guidance for staff and the
development community to implement trip reduction goals for projects in San Mateo. The project will evaluate existing
TDM policies, consider best practices from like jurisdictions, and develop a framework for policy development. The
framework will outline specific policy aspects on which Commission and Council will provide feedback to develop the final
policy parameters. This project is funded through a technical assistance grant from the Metropolitan Transportation
Commission (MTC). The following is an update on this effort.
•
In coordination with MTC and the consultant team, City staff extended the project agreement term and updated

Citywide Complete Streets Plan: A collaborative team of staff from Public Works, Community Development, and the City
Manager’s Office submitted an application to the Caltrans Sustainable Communities FY 2021-22 Grant Program for the San
Mateo Complete Streets Plan and was awarded funding for the project in June 2021. This project brings together the City’s
various active transportation plans, Local Roadway Safety Plan, and Green Infrastructure Plan to fully envision a
multimodal transportation network and identify priority improvements for the City. The following is an update on this
effort.
•
City staff amended the agreement with the project consultant to extend the agreement term.
•
As per our 6/2/25 Annual Transportation Review to City Council, staff will be combining the North Central 
Complete Streets Plan (NCCSP) into the Citywide Complete Streets Plan. Synchronizing the NCCSP with the 
Complete Streets Plan allows the City to more cost-effectively rescope and finalize the NCCSP.
•
The planning effort is on hold but expected to start up again in FY25-26. The North Central Complete Streets Plan 
is currently on hold for multiple reasons, including the need for Council direction on the Humboldt Bike Lanes, the 
recent cancellation of the Peninsula Interchange Project that requires other approaches to address the circulation 
and safety needs that were the drivers for that project, the need to understand circulation impacts of one or more 
potential rail crossing closures on the western edge of the North Central neighborhood as the City pursues 
creation of a Train Horn Quiet Zone, and the need to plan for bi-jurisdictional improvements for vehicle circulation 
and bicycle/pedestrian safety on Peninsula Avenue. 
Humboldt Bike Lane Alternatives Project: At the February 3, 2025 City Council meeting, Council members directed staff to 
explore Idaho and Fremont Streets as an alternative bike route to a portion of the Humboldt Street bike lanes. The project 
includes several opportunities for community members to participate, including three community meetings, an online 
survey, and at public meetings to the Commission and City Council. The following is an update on this effort.
•
The first of three community meetings was held at the Martin Luther King Jr. Community Center on June 18th from 
6pm to 9pm. Following a presentation by City staff, participants provided feedback on their experience traveling 
on Humboldt, Fremont, and Idaho streets and discussed potential alternatives to the existing bike lanes on 
Humboldt Street in breakout groups. 
•
The project team are incorporating feedback from the community meeting into a concept design.
•
An online survey is available on the project webpage and will close on July 11, 2025. The intent of the survey is to 
capture feedback from those who were unable to attend the June 18th community meeting.
•
The second community meeting will take place in late August 2025.
•
Staff will return to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in winter 2025 with the findings of the 
community outreach.
•
Staff will return to City Council in early 2026 to present the findings of the community and seek direction on the 
Humboldt bike lanes.
RECENTLY-AWARDED GRANTS
Housing Incentive Pool Grant (HIP): This program provides transportation grants to promote the production and 
preservation of affordable housing. MTC distributes $71 million of this funding to 15 jurisdictions that issued certificates of 
occupancy for the greatest number of eligible housing units from 2018 to 2022. The City was amongst the top 15 
jurisdictions awarded Housing Incentive Pool funds. 
•
The City was awarded $959,000 in HIP funds in March 2025 to design and construct the Local Roadway Safety 
Priority Package 1 project, which includes improvements at priority intersections identified in the Local Roadway 
Safety Plan: El Camino Real at 22nd and 27th avenues, Humboldt Street at Indian, Tilton, and Santa Inez Avenues, 
and Hillsdale Boulevard at Franklin Parkway and Norfolk Street. Pedestrian safety improvements at these locations 
will include improved lighting, flashing beacons, and high-visibility crosswalks.

SUBJECT:
Central El Camino Multimodal Plan - Introduction
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive an introduction to the Central El Camino Multimodal Plan being developed in partnership with SamTrans and the 
Cities of San Mateo, Belmont, San Carlos, and Redwood City.  
BACKGROUND:
SamTrans is initiating a new planning effort with the Cities of San Mateo, Belmont, San Carlos, and Redwood City called the 
Central El Camino Real Multimodal Plan (Central El Camino Plan). 
The project aims to address safety and mobility issues on El Camino Real (SR-82). Project goals include creating a cohesive, 
locally defined vision for multimodal improvements on El Camino Real; identifying corridor needs and evaluating 
alternatives; selecting a preferred alternative that meets local and regional needs; building on SamTrans' 2022 Bus Speed 
and Reliability Study; and complementing local planning efforts. 
Key challenges include sidewalk gaps, lack of low-stress bicycle facilities, crosswalk gaps, and narrow sidewalks. 
The project will interface with the Grand Boulevard Initiative (GBI), which SamTrans recently relaunched with a focus on 
delivering multimodal improvements. Design options from the Central El Camino Plan will feed into a coordinated Caltrans 
process led by the GBI. 
Themes from past outreach include: 
•
Complete Streets: safety and connectivity gaps for walking and bicycling
•
Pedestrian Access: difficult crossings, narrow/obstructed sidewalks, and limited bus stop amenities 
•
Safety: concerns about speeding, red light running, and crossing conditions 
The project incorporates input from local and regional plans including: 
•
San Mateo Complete Streets Master Plan (in progress) 
•
San Mateo Bicycle Master Plan (2020) 
•
Caltrans District 4 Bike Plan (2018) 
•
C/CAG Bicycle and Pedestrian Plan (2021) 
•
SamTrans Bus Speed and Reliability Study (2022) 
•
Transit Orientated Development (TOD) Pedestrian Plan (2022) 
SamTrans will conduct public engagement in three phases:

CITY OF SAN MATEO
Phase 1 (May–July 2025): Identify corridor gaps and needs
Phase 2 (Jan–Feb 2026): Present corridor alternatives and trade-offs
Phase 3 (Spring 2026): Present preferred alternative and implementation plan 
Planned Phase 1 engagement includes community pop-ups, meetings with community-based organizations, multilingual 
listening sessions, an online survey, and presentations. For San Mateo, SamTrans hosted a pop-up table on the 4th of July 
at Central Park from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. More information is available at: SamTrans.com/CentralElCamino. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
This project is funded by SamTrans and a Transit Performance Initiative (TPI) grant awarded by the Metropolitan 
Transportation Commission (MTC). The study cost is $732,000, funded by a $407,000 MTC Transit Performance Initiative 
grant and $325,000 in District funds. The City of San Mateo will provide in-kind staff support.
