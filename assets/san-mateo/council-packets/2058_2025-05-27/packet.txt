SUBJECT:
Measure S Proposed Fiscal Year 2025-26 Operating and CIP Budget – Approval Recommendation
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend the City Council adopt the Measure S Proposed Fiscal Year 2025-26 Budget.
BACKGROUND:
At the April 21, 2025 City Council meeting, Finance staff provided a preview of the Fiscal Year (FY) 2025-26 General Fund 
operating budget. The presentation highlighted a projected General Fund budget deficit of $15.2 million. This structural 
deficit is due to operating costs outpacing revenue growth. In the same meeting, Public Works staff also reviewed the 
City’s overall needs for capital improvement project (CIP). 
Building on that discussion and new information available, staff has developed the FY 2025-26 Measure S (a General Fund 
sub-fund) proposed budget for the committee’s review and feedback.
 
Revenues:  In summary, Measure S total revenues for 2025-26 are projected to be $7.1 million, primarily sourced from the 
city’s Measure S transactions and use tax ($7.0 million), along with a local grant supporting the City's Senior Transportation 
Program.  
Ongoing Operating Expenses:   Total expenditures are projected to be $2.2 million for FY 2025-26, supporting Police, Parks 
& Recreation, and Library operations:
•
$1.6 million in Public Safety/Police
•
$0.4 million for Community services
•
$0.2 million for Library services
Over 75% of the $2.2 million in operating expenses are personnel costs, funding 6.51 full time equivalent (FTE) of staff 
positions:
•
Police Department
o
3.0 FTE Police Officers 
o
1.0 FTE Police Sergeant 
•
Parks & Recreation Department
o
0.16 FTE Community Services Supervisor 
o
1.0 FTE Community Service Coordinator
•
Library 
o
0.75 FTE Library Assistant II

Transfers Out:
Transfers out is projected to be $5.6 million for fiscal year 2025-26, which are comprised of the following:  $2.6 million for 
capital improvement projects (CIP),  $2.7 million in transfers out for debt service payments related to the revenue bonds 
issued in 2020 to finance the cost of flood control and street rehabilitation, $0.3 million in transfers out to the General 
Fund for amortized repayment of costs it incurred on Measure S' behalf before Measure S funds were available. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
For 2025-26 fiscal year, Measure S projects revenues of $7.1 million, operating expenditures of $2.2 million, and transfers 
out totaling $5.6 million.
