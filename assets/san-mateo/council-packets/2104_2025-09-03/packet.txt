Capital Improvement Projects Update - Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the status of current Capital Improvement Projects.
BACKGROUND:
Capital Improvement Projects (CIPs) come forward as a part of the budget process where projects are identified, proposed, 
and costs are developed. Funding sources are identified where possible. The projects listed below are funded through the 
City’s General Fund (GF); Measure S (MS); Grants (GR); and Miscellaneous Fund (MISC).  
Projects that affect Parks and Recreation fall into two different areas of oversight, those overseen by Parks and Recreation 
project management staff and those overseen by Public Works project management staff. The following projects are those 
that are overseen by Public Works.  
Central Recreation Center Roof Improvements 
Description: Roof Replacement 
Status/Timeline: Project Awarded / Construction in September 2025 thru October 2025.  
Emergency Field Lighting Upgrades – King Park 
Description: Installation of Pathway Lighting 
Status/Timeline: Project Bidding in September 2025; Construction in October 2025 thru November 2025.  
Emergency Generator Upgrades – King Community Center 
Description: Installation of Backup Generator Transfer Switch 
Status/Timeline: Complete
  
Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Recreation Center – Resiliency Center Project 
Description: This improvement project will bring the facility up to current standards including additional features that will 
allow the facility to qualify as a Community Resilience Center. 
Status/Timeline: Grant Applications are in progress. Design phase begins in early 2026.  
King/Joinville Pool Improvements 
Description: The City adopted a phased approach to repair and upgrade the facilities, as follows: Phase 1 Immediate Term-
Implement critical repairs to operate the pool facilities during the peak summer season; Phase 2 Short Term-Design and 
implement code compliance upgrades; Phase 3 Long Term-Strategic discussions regarding the future of Joinville and the

SUBJECT:
Annual Parks Survey - Informational
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the results of last year's Annual Parks Survey.
BACKGROUND:
The Parks and Recreation Department holds an annual parks survey aimed at gathering community feedback to help 
inform future decisions regarding local parks. The questions asked help staff understand usage of our parks, current 
perception of the existing amenities and equipment, and what respondents would like to see more of in our parks. 
Last year's survey took place from March 19, 2025, to June 30, 2025. There were two new open-ended follow-up questions 
added based on commission feedback and staff analysis in order to garner more in-depth information to help guide future 
department initiatives regarding accessibility & safety in our parks. It was promoted via signage in all City of San Mateo 
parks and community centers, social media posts, and City newsletters, ultimately garnering over 600 responses.

There is no budget impact related to this informational item.
