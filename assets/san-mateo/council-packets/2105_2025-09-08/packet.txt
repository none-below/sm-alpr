SUBJECT:
Annual Parks Survey - Informational
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the results of last year‘s Annual Parks Survey. 
BACKGROUND:
The Parks and Recreation Department holds an annual parks survey aimed at gathering community feedback to help 
inform future decisions regarding local parks. The questions asked help staff understand usage of our parks, current 
perception of the existing amenities and equipment, and what respondents would like to see more of in our parks. 
Last year's survey took place from March 19, 2025, to June 30, 2025. There were two new open-ended follow-up questions 
added based on commission feedback and staff analysis in order to garner more in-depth information to help guide future 
department initiatives regarding accessibility & safety in our parks. It was promoted via signage in all City of San Mateo 
parks and community centers, social media posts, and City newsletters, ultimately garnering over 600 responses.

There is no budget impact related to this informational item.

SUBJECT:
Senior Programming – Informational
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive an update on current senior programming, results from the 2025 Senior Center Satisfaction Survey, and the City 
Council’s upcoming Senior Hubs initiative included in the Fiscal Year 2025–2026 Council Priorities.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo’s Parks and Recreation Department continues to provide and enhance programs that promote 
health, wellness, social connection, and community engagement for older adults. 
Over the past year, congregate nutrition programs have provided 8,400 meals at the Senior Center and Dr. Martin Luther 
King Jr. Community Center, contributing significantly to the health and well-being of participants. Additionally, the Get 
Around program has delivered 10,248 rides, improving transportation access and independence for older adults. 
Intergenerational programming has also grown, including a community egg hunt and the inception of Friday afternoon 
games, which foster social connections across age groups. Recreation programming occurred at the San Mateo Senior 
Center primarily, with some fitness and enrichment programs at Beresford Recreation Center, and Mobile Recreation 
programs at Edgewater Isel, Rotary Haciendas, and Rotary Floritas Senior apartments. 
To help assess the impact of current offerings and guide future planning, staff conducted the 2025 Senior Center Program 
Survey. The results reflect satisfaction among current programs and services: 
• 86% of participants reported that programs have improved their quality of life 
• 92% were satisfied with the instructors 
• 80% felt more connected to their community 
• 92% reported being overall satisfied with the programs they attend 
Survey feedback also highlighted areas of interest for new or expanded offerings, including day trips, fitness and low-
impact sports, lectures, and games. This input will help inform the City’s new Senior Hubs initiative, identified as a Fiscal 
Year 2025–2026 City Council Priority. Senior Hubs will serve as additional access points for services and programs beyond 
the traditional senior center model, with expanded sites at the Beresford and Martin Luther King Jr. community centers. 
These locations will explore offering scheduled coffee and tea socials, and staff will continue working with community 
partners to expand hub programming. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
This informational update to the Senior Advisory Commission does not have a fiscal impact on program updates.
