CITY OF SAN MATEO
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
October 27, 2025
 Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
Wendy McCall, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order at 5:33 
p.m.
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Ms. McCall led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Wendy McCall, Richard 
Wysong, Annie Tsai, Alison Joly and Sheila Canzian, and Youth Commissioner Erin Finn.  
Youth Commissioner Oliver Baker was not present.
3.
CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION 
Board members proposed two corrections to the minutes: ethics training is due December 16, 
not September 16, and Microsoft Publisher is available to the general public.  A motion to 
approve the consent calendar with the proposed corrections was made by Ms. Joly, seconded 
by Ms. Canzian, and the motion passed 5-0.
4.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was not any public comment.
5.
NEW BUSINESS
A. Overview of the Circulation and Technical Services Division:  Supervising Library Assistant 
Jon Jung provided the Board with a PowerPoint presentation of the Circulation and 
Technical Services Division, the Library’s largest division with 47 employees.  Circulation 
staff provide a variety of services including customer service, account registration, holds 
processing, shelving, oversight of the Technology Lending+ collection and the State Parks 
Pass collection, loan management, and more.  Technical Services staff perform many tasks 
including purchasing, receiving, cataloging, mending, quality control, repacking, end 
processing, and withdrawing.  Mr. Jung has implemented a Library Assistant Shadowing 
Program that provides other library staff an opportunity to learn the tasks that Circulation 
and Technical Services staff perform.  Ms. McCall said that school librarians may be 
interested in the information that is provided in the shadowing program.  Mr. Jung said he 
may be able to provide some on-demand type of instruction.   
B. Revisions to Small Conference Room Use Policy: City Librarian Rukshana Singh presented 
proposed revisions to the Small Conference Room Use Policy.  The proposed revisions 
include that 1) the patron who registered for the room must be physically present during the 
time the room is booked and 2) food is not permitted inside the rooms.  Ms. Canzian made 
a motion to adopt the proposed revisions to the policy, Ms. Tsai seconded the motion, and 
the motion passed 5-0.
6.
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
A.  City Librarian Rukshana Singh:

•
San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) received a $10,000 Carnegie Foundation grant.  
Some of these funds may be used for SMPL’s 20th anniversary activities in 2026.
•
A group of library staff and Board members met with a team from EHDD Architecture, 
including the original architect of the Main Library, Jennifer Devlin-Herbert.  The 
architects from EHDD will synthesize our feedback and come up with a new plan for the 
spaces that were identified as needing to be repurposed.  These spaces include the 
café, teen lounge, computer lab, jobseekers area, and business area.  Hopefully, some 
of these projects will be funded if a bond measure is passed in 2028.  The new plan will 
provide visuals and updated costs that can be used in communication and fundraising 
efforts.
•
Our new Deputy City Librarian Thomas McMahon will attend the November 24, 2025 
Board meeting.
•
The Annual Staff Development Day was very successful.  The day included a workshop 
on artificial intelligence, a safety drill, and a murder mystery activity.
•
Library managers will give a presentation to City Service Academy participants next 
week.
•
Baker and Taylor, our main book provider, is going out of business.  We are in the 
process of finding a new primary vendor.  In addition, we can order books from Amazon 
and process them in house.  We were outsourcing the end processing to Baker and 
Taylor, but with Amazon we will have to do in-house processing.  The shadowing 
program that Mr. Jung has implemented will help with more staff knowing how to 
perform this task. 
•
Last Monday, Ms. Singh, along with other Pacific Library Partnership (PLP) library 
directors attended a PLP Legislative Advocacy workshop with Assemblymember Diane 
Papan and her staff to ask for library funding to be restored in Governor Newsom’s 
budget in January.  Ms. Singh invited the Board to attend a future PLP Legislative 
Advocacy workshop.
•
The Marina Branch Library Restoration Project is going well.  The satellite site at the 
Shoreview Recreation Center is up and running for patrons to pick up their books on 
hold. 
B. Board Members:
•
Ms. McCall attended a portion of Staff Development Day.  She was impressed with the 
speaker’s presentation on artificial intelligence and the relevant examples provided.
•
Ms. Joly attended an event at Meadow Heights Elementary School, a Senior Advisory 
Commission event, and the Halloween party at the Main Library.  She said there was a 
huge turnout for the Halloween party and some people had to be turned away because 
the Oak Room was at capacity.  The party featured a magician and arts and crafts 
activities.  
•
Ms. Canzian attended the Foundation’s Harvest Happy Hour event.  Ms. Canzian said 
she will not be able to attend the Board meeting on November 24.
•
Mr. Wysong attended the facilities upgrade meeting with the architects.
•
Ms. Tsai attended a school tour at Abbott Middle School, the Foundation’s Harvest 
Happy Hour event, and the facilities upgrade meeting with the architects.
•
Ms. Finn attended the facilities upgrade meeting with the architects and provided her 
input.

This past year has been one of transformation and possibility for the San Mateo
Public Library. We developed a new strategic plan and vision that will guide our work
for the next three years. Our new vision, to cultivate boundless potential through
exploration, learning, and connection, reflects our deep commitment to serving the
evolving needs of our community. We are already making meaningful progress
toward the goals we set in our strategic plan. 
 
In April, the library hosted the third annual San Mateo Celebrates event during
National Library Week. This citywide arts and culture festival brings people of all
ages and backgrounds together to connect through shared experiences at the
library. As an intergenerational “third space,” the library plays a crucial role in
addressing social isolation, especially at a time when loneliness has been declared
a national epidemic. In a city like San Mateo, where the senior population is growing,
these inclusive, community-building efforts are more important than ever and align
with City Council priorities. 
 
San Mateo Celebrates is one example of how the library reaches new audiences, but
outreach is part of our work year-round. Over the past year, library staff engaged
with residents across the city and expanded access through the purchase of a new
outreach van, which will deliver library services and resources directly to
neighborhoods. We continue to approach our work through an equity lens, ensuring
that everyone in San Mateo feels welcomed and supported at the library. 
 
Construction began on the Marina Library’s structural improvement project, ensuring
the branch will continue to serve the community for generations. Recognizing that
our branches are vital community anchors, we expanded programming at the
Hillsdale branch, including the launch of a popular romance book club and crochet
group. 
 
Looking ahead, we remain committed to promoting literacy, re-imagining spaces to
meet evolving needs, and expanding outreach. Above all, our goal is to ensure the
San Mateo Public Library continues to be a place where every member of our
community can discover, connect, and learn.

San Mateo Public Library Foundation
A Healthy Library = A Healthy Community
This year, the San Mateo Public Library Foundation (SMPLF) deepened their
commitment to the library and the diverse communities it serves. SMPLF proudly
hosted San Mateo Celebrates, a citywide arts and culture festival that united
patrons across generations and added a vibrant touch to the National Library
Week festivities.
SMPLF also played a key role in the library’s Strategic Planning process, offering
valuable insights to help shape its direction over the next three years.
In addition, SMPLF’s financial contributions enabled the library to deliver enriching
programs for children, teens, and adults alike. SMPLF’s theme for the year ahead,
A Place for Everyone’s Story, will help build on the library’s commitment to
providing enriching and inclusive services. 
For more information, please visit the San Mateo Public Library Foundation at
smlibraryfoundation.org

The library hosted a special version of the film, Free to All: The Public Library. The
film tells the story of the quiet revolutionaries from the pioneering women behind
the “Free Library Movement” to today's librarians who provide service to the public
despite working in a contentious age of closures and book bans. 
EVENTS & PROGRAMS
The film chronicles those who created a civic institution where everything is free
and the doors are open to all. The film was followed by a discussion with the
filmmakers, Dawn Logsdon and Lucie Faulknor, library staff, and a Library Board
member.

As the Library’s 2018-2023 strategic plan came to a close, the work began to set the
course for 2024-2028.
STRATEGIC PLAN
MISSION
San Mateo Public Library is a gateway to
empowerment and understanding. We make
knowledge accessible and offer free
enriching experiences and welcoming civic
spaces for people of San Mateo,
Hillsborough, and neighboring communities. 
VISION
To cultivate boundless potential through
exploration, learning, and connection.
GUIDING PRINCIPLES
Intellectual Freedom 
Adaptability & Innovation 
Equity of Access 
Embrace Diverse Perspectives 
Welcoming Environment 
GOALS
To promote literacy for all ages and
abilities 
To create beautiful and functional
community spaces 
To increase and secure stable funding 
To proactively engage community
Through a series of planning sessions with committed stakeholders, feedback from
almost 700 surveys (in 3 languages), and careful consideration of upcoming
challenges, a new guiding document was created to let everyone connected with
the library know who we want to become and what we want to accomplish.

An exciting new chapter is underway with the restoration of the Marina Branch
Library. This comprehensive project, expected to span approximately 16 months,
will deliver critical upgrades to enhance the safety, accessibility, and longevity of
the facility. Planned improvements include: 
MARINA BRANCH
RESTORATION PROJECT
Foundation and structural repairs 
Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing upgrades 
Accessibility improvements
Exterior and landscaping improvements
Newly updated interior 
Relocated deck
This project is supported in whole or in part by funding provided by:
A Community Project Funding Grant, administered by the U.S. Department
of Housing and Urban Development; and 
A Member Agency Grant administered by the Peninsula Clean Energy
Authority.
