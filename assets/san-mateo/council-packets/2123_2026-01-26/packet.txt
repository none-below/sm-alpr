LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
November 24, 2025 
 
 Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Wendy McCall, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order 
at 5:32 p.m. 
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Ms. McCall led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Executive Assistant 
Dena Gomez conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Wendy 
McCall, Richard Wysong, Annie Tsai, Alison Joly and Youth Commissioner Oliver 
Baker.  Board Member Sheila Canzian and Youth Commissioner Erin Finn were not 
present. 
 
3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION  
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. Joly, seconded by Ms. 
Tsai, and the motion passed 4-0.  City Librarian Rukshana Singh, Deputy City 
Librarian Tommy McMahon and the Board discussed the statistics, including the 
digital service Hoopla.  San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) had to decrease the per 
patron usage allowance from 10 titles per month to 5 due to the increasing cost of the 
service.  Hoopla charges per use and every month 80 to 100 new patrons begin using 
Hoopla.  The bill last month was over $8,000. 
 
4. PUBLIC COMMENT 
There was not any public comment. 
 
5. NEW BUSINESS 
A. Introduction of New Deputy City Librarian Thomas (Tommy) McMahon: Deputy 
City Librarian Tommy McMahon introduced himself to the Library Board of 
Trustees and provided information about his professional background.  He has 
worked for libraries throughout San Mateo County for almost 20 years.  Most 
recently, he was an adult services manager for Burlingame Public Library for 10 
years.  The Board welcomed Mr. McMahon to SMPL. 
B. Fiscal Year 2024-2025 Impact Report: Senior Management Analyst Marcia 
Mendonca presented a draft of the Fiscal Year 2024-2025 Impact Report to the 
Library Board of Trustees.  The Board provided feedback including suggestions to 
add a notation that the Marina Branch Library is temporarily closed, to include a 
percentage change in statistics from the previous fiscal year (for future impact 
reports), and to show a conversion of volunteer hours to full time employee hours.  
Ms. Mendonca will incorporate the suggestions and publish the report by Giving 
Tuesday on December 2.

Library Rules of Conduct Policy 
[Revision Adopted by the Library Board of Trustees, 1/26/2026]  
 
 
The Library is a resource for all members of the community and its purpose is to provide 
information services, programs and educational, informational, and recreational materials 
to the public. Any behavior that disrupts the Library’s ability to provide a safe and 
welcoming environment for the public and a supportive workplace for library employees 
shall be grounds for immediate action. Follow-up may include requesting person(s) to 
leave the Library; barring future services, including admission (temporarily or 
permanently); and calling the police.  
 
Prohibited behaviors in the Library may include but are not limited to the following: 
 
• 
Leaving personal items unattended [the Library is not responsible for personal belongings 
• 
      lost or stolen on Library property*] 
• 
Consuming food and beverages (other than liquid in a container with lid) outside of 
      designated eating zones at the Library. 
• 
Sleeping [as a safety precaution, staff will awaken persons sleeping on Library property*] 
• 
Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways 
• 
Bringing animals other than service animals inside the Library 
• 
Entering the Library without wearing shoes or a shirt 
• 
Strong pervasive odors  
• 
Smoking on Library property* 
• 
Not setting cell phones or devices on silent mode; and speaking or listening on cell phones 
or devices in 
      spaces other than designated “phone zones” 
• 
Leaving children unattended [Library employees are not responsible for unattended children] 
• 
Using rollerblades, scooters, or skateboards on Library property*** 
• 
Soliciting or panhandling 
• 
Using the restroom inappropriately, such as bathing or laundering  
• 
Monopolizing Library space or resources including moving furniture 
• 
Viewing obscene content on library computers or personal devices; or other violations of the 
      Computer Use Policy 
• 
Causing a public disturbance, which includes but is not limited to use of abusive or obscene 
language, talking loudly, or making excessive noise  
• 
Disobeying the direction of a library staff member 
• 
Harassment of any kind toward other library users and library staff  
• 
Bringing bicycles (or any wheeled transport that cannot be folded and/or safely stored away) 
      inside the Library 
• 
Bringing large, oversized, or bulky sets of personal belongings that cannot be safely stored 
away 
• 
Bringing personal items judged to be unsanitary, pose a health or safety risk, or disruptive to 
library operations 
• 
Vandalizing or misusing furniture, equipment, or materials 
 
To prevent the theft of Library materials, state law authorizes the detention for a reasonable period 
of any person using these facilities when there is probable cause to believe that the person being 
detained is unlawfully removing Library materials from the premises. Staff has legal authority to 
conduct a limited and reasonable search of purses, bags, parcels, briefcases and other packages

** If brought inside the library, scooters and skateboards must be carried; scooters must be in the folded position. 
 
 
Enforcement of Violations 
 
To ensure a safe and welcoming environment for the public and a supportive workplace for 
library employees, violations of the Rules of Conduct are enforced by Library employees with 
assistance by the San Mateo Police Department. Suspensions are determined by the type of 
infraction and are issued by the City Librarian. The suspension period will be contingent on the 
nature of the violation and the Library will consult with law enforcement officials and the City 
Attorney’s office. The City Librarian reserves the right to make individualized determinations as 
to the appropriate level of enforcement.  
 
Library staff will document instances of minor violations or behavioral misconduct. Staff 
may direct patron to leave the library if verbal warnings are not heeded followed within 
the same day and patron will not be allowed to enter until the following day. A written 
warning will be issued by the City Librarian or designee if misconduct continues over a 
period of time. A suspension (banning) letter will be issued by the City Librarian if the 
written warning is not heededfollowed.   
 
For level three violations stemming from criminal activity, library staff will summon the San Mateo 
Police Department in response to criminal activity in the Library. A suspension (banning) letter 
will may be issued by the City Librarian to the patron at the time of the arrest. Due to the 
egregious nature of criminal activity, the Library will not issue a warning letter will not be issued. 
The suspension period will be contingent on the nature of the violation and the Library will 
consult with law enforcement officials and the City Attorney’s office.  
Appeal Process 
 
Suspensions may be appealed to the City Librarian within seven days of receipt issuance of the 
suspension letter. The appeal will be reviewed at the next scheduled Library Board meeting, 
during which time the suspension will remain in effect. The Library Board will decide if the 
suspension will be upheld, reduced, or extended. The Library Board’s decision will be final. 
Library patrons whose library privileges are suspended cannot use any of the San Mateo libraries 
while serving a suspension.  
 
Level One: Minor Violations-examples may include but are not limited to the following: 
 
• 
Leaving personal items unattended;  
• 
Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways 
• 
Sleeping 
• 
Speaking on cell phone in areas not designated as phone zones 
• 
Bringing animals other than service animals;  
• 
Entering without wearing shoes/shirt 
• 
Using rollerblades, scooters, skateboards on Library property 
• 
Leaving children unattended  
 
Suspension Period: If behavior violations at this level persist, a suspension letter may be 
issued for disobeying the direction of library staff (see level two violations below). 
 
Level Two: Violations stemming from behavioral misconduct-examples may include but 
are not limited to the following:
