CITY OF SAN MATEO 
LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES 
January 26, 2026 
 
 Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
Wendy McCall, President of the Library Board of Trustees, called the meeting to order 
at 5:30 p.m. 
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Ms. McCall led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance. Ms. McCall 
conducted roll call and present were Library Board Trustees Wendy McCall, Richard 
Wysong, Alison Joly, Sheila Canzian, and Youth Commissioners Oliver Baker and 
Erin Finn.  Board Member Annie Tsai had previously notified City Librarian 
Rukshana Singh that she was unable to attend this meeting and was not present. 
 
3. CONSENT CALENDAR ADOPTION  
A motion to approve the consent calendar was made by Ms. Joly, seconded by Mr. 
Wysong, and the motion passed 4-0.  Ms. Canzian brought into question the following 
bullet point: 
• 
“The City Attorney’s office is reviewing the draft Naming Policy.  They have 
expressed caution about the Board approving names.” 
Ms. Singh commented that instead of a Naming Policy, a list of recognition 
opportunities will be created with the San Mateo Public Library Foundation. 
 
4. PUBLIC COMMENT 
There was not any public comment. 
 
5. NEW BUSINESS 
A. Draft 2025-2026 San Mateo Public Library Work Plan: Ms. Singh presented a draft 
of the 2025-2026 San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) Work Plan, showcasing 
changes to the format compared to the 2024-2025 version.  The new template 
focuses on highlighting major department initiatives.  Ms. Singh will provide 
updates to the Board of Trustees after the next update to the 2025-2026 SMPL 
Work Plan, which is planned for February 2026. 
B. Draft Revisions to the San Mateo Public Library Rules of Conduct Policy: Ms. 
Singh presented draft revisions to the SMPL Rules of Conduct Policy.  Under 
advisement of the City Attorney’s office a new section highlighting an appeal 
process is being added.  The Library Board of Trustees will be the body to hear 
appeals made by patrons who have received a suspension letter.  Instructions for 
the appeal process will be better defined in the document after further advisement 
of the City Attorney’s office.  Other revisions to the document not directly related 
to the appeal section were agreed upon unanimously, after short discussions 
seeking clarification of intent for various revisions. 
 
6. REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS 
 
A.  City Librarian Rukshana Singh: 
• 
Ms. Singh presented pictures of the Marina Library renovation project.  These 
images highlighted the reinforcement of the structure, and the process of 
installing helical piles.
