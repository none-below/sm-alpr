SUBJECT:
Parks and Golf Operations Electric Backpack Leaf Blower Deployment – Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive Update on Electric Backpack Leaf Blower Transition 
BACKGROUND:
Parks and Golf staff use gas-powered leaf blower equipment to remove debris throughout outdoor maintenance areas. 
Staff rely on leaf blowers almost daily as a primary source to remove debris from pathways and parking lots. Parks staff 
began testing electric maintenance tools in 2020, to support efforts and identify opportunities for equipment and tool 
replacements. Subsequently, the division purchased a variety of electrical maintenance tools, and a large area sweeper 
called a Mutihog Compact Sweeper to reduce backpack blower use, estimated to be a savings of 14 hours of labor per 
week. In 2023, Parks staff worked with Public Works staff to invest in facility power upgrades costing $25,000. 
In 2024, City staff worked with Peninsula Clean Energy (PCE) to support the funding needs to purchase twenty-three (23) 
Toro electric backpack leaf blowers, ninety-two (92) batteries, and sixteen (16) battery chargers. The equipment was 
delivered in August of 2025, replacing all gas-powered backpack blowers in the parks and golf divisions. Four locations at 
parks have been added to reduce travel time for replacement batteries. Staff received training and deployed electric 
equipment by October 2, 2025. Gas-powered backpack blowers have been decommissioned.
Parks staff initial experience has been debris removal is slower particularly in wet weather.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Fiscal Year 2023-24 $25,000 in upgrades to Park Yard facility for charging infrastructure. Fiscal Year 2024-25 Electric 
Backpack Blowers total cost: $47,174 paid for by PCE Grant. Ongoing costs are projected to average $10,000 per year for 
replacement batteries by year three (3).
