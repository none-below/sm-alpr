2025 Summer Recreation Camps, Programs, and Events Review - Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation reviewing 2025 Summer Recreation Camps, Programs, and Events. 
BACKGROUND:
Summer represents the department’s busiest operational period, requiring expanded staffing, enhanced program 
coordination, and increased facility usage. During this season, the Parks and Recreation Department scales up its services 
to meet heightened community demand through a broad range of camps, aquatics activities, volunteer programs, special 
events, and enrichment opportunities. 
In April 2025, staff provided the Commission with an overview of planned summer operations, outlining scheduled 
programming, special events, staffing needs, and projected revenues. This follow-up item summarizes and evaluates the 
2025 summer season. 
Highlights from the 2025 season include: 
•
Enrollment: 8,663 total summer program enrollments, a 4% increase from 2024. 
•
Aquatics Programming: Swim lesson planning resulted in 2,034 enrollments, a 5% increase from 2024. 
•
Seasonal Staffing: The department hired over 30 new part-time staff in camps and aquatics and trained over 100 
staff including returning employees. Also, staff developed 73 youth in our Leaders-in-Training program. 
•
Community Events: The Central Park Music Series, Fourth of July in the Park, Movies in the Park, and Central Bark 
drew large crowds to several parks throughout the City.
•
Survey Results: Program surveys were distributed to patrons, with results indicating favorable satisfaction levels. 
o
Includes 90% of swim lesson participants stating the program improved their quality of life and 100% of 
our Art and STEM participants indicating they would recommend the camp to others. 
This background is intended to support the Commission’s review of 2025 summer operations and overall performance.
