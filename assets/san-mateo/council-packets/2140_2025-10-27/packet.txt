Proposed Legislative Advocacy Policy for City of San Mateo 
SUBJECT:    Proposed State Legislative Platform 
PURPOSE 
The intent of the City of San Mateo Legislative Platform is to convey to state legislators, 
policymakers, and the public where the City stands on important policy issues. This 
Platform provides the direction needed for the City to engage on state initiatives that 
affect the City’s key priorities, programs, and/or operations. In line with the City’s 
mission, the Legislative Platform reaffirms the City’s commitment to serving the San 
Mateo community through collaboration, innovation, and professionalism.  
San Mateo strives to provide safe, clean, and attractive neighborhoods; successful 
businesses and solid tax base; a community where residents can flourish and youth are 
nurtured; orderly planning, development; and open, participative, and effective City 
Government. The City of San Mateo prides itself on being a vibrant, diverse community 
that is committed to equity and inclusivity, and stands firmly against all forms of racism, 
anti-immigrant sentiment, xenophobia and implicit bias. San Mateo is a community that 
is inclusive of all people, regardless of skin color, gender, sexual orientation or ethnicity. 
Respect, integrity, transparency and inclusivity are central values in the City, and those 
values inform the City’s legislative advocacy. 
Additionally, the City of San Mateo is a progressive community that aims to preserve the 
environment, provide economic well-being, and ensure social equity for our residents 
and businesses. Empowering local decision making and fiscal sustainability are 
underlying principles of this Platform.  
OFFICIAL POSITIONS FOR ADOPTION 
Possible positions that can be adopted on legislation include: 
Support: A support position indicates to the Legislature, regulatory agencies, 
and other stakeholders that the City is in favor of the proposed legislation in 
question. 
Sponsor: A sponsor position indicates to the Legislature, regulatory agencies, 
and other stakeholders that the City is not only in strong support of the legislation 
in question but is publicly leading the advocacy effort. This position is only used 
when a Member of the Legislature has agreed to author a specific legislative 
proposal on behalf of the City.
Oppose Unless Amended: An oppose unless amended position indicates to the 
legislature, regulatory agencies, and other stakeholders that the City is against

the proposed legislation in question, unless requested changes are adopted to 
address the City’s policy-based concerns. 
Oppose: An oppose position indicates to the Legislature, regulatory agencies, 
and other stakeholders that the City is against the proposed legislation in 
question. This position is often used only when/ if amendments or other policy 
compromises cannot be reached. 
Neutral: A neutral position indicates to the Legislature, regulatory agencies, and 
other stakeholders that the City has formally removed their prior adopted position 
due to changes being made to the legislation or proposal (e.g., a bill that the City 
formally adopted an “oppose unless amended” position  which was amended to 
address the City’s concerns, therefore the City is formally withdrawing its 
opposition and moving to a “Neutral” position). A neutral position therefore 
indicates that the City is impartial on the legislation in question.
Watch: A watch is an internal position which indicates that the City is closely 
monitoring and evaluating the legislation in question. Should factors change, the 
City reserves the right to adopt a formal external position.
Community Development and Housing
The City of San Mateo takes pride in its communities and works to maintain and improve 
the quality of neighborhoods and expand the City of San Mateo's affordable housing. 
Through production and preservation, utilizing public and private funding sources, the 
City has grown into a diverse and modern innovation hub with plentiful options for 
housing, employment, shopping, and leisure. 
The City is working to implement policies and programs proscribed in its certified 
housing element to achieve production goals to create a robust mix of affordable and 
market-rate housing options to house community members at all income levels.  
San Mateo appreciates collaboration with State and regional partners to achieve 
measurable success. In that spirit, San Mateo outlines the following guiding policy 
principles for community development and housing: 
1.
Support comprehensive housing legislation that addresses all contributing factors 
to California’s housing crisis.
2.
Support legislation that enables the City to meet its Regional Housing Needs 
Allocation (RHNA) goals, including measures that extend compliance timelines, 
provide housing funding, and increase flexibility in eligible housing types—such 
as granting RHNA credit for each bedroom in multi-unit developments.
3.
Support efforts to preserve existing affordable housing and protect housing 
options for extremely low-, very low-, low-, and middle-income residents.

Support incentives for housing production across all income levels—market rate, 
middle, low, very low, and extremely low—while maintaining local land use

developments.

Transportation and Public Works 
The City of San Mateo understands the importance in ensuring residents have access to 
safe, resilient, equitable, and sustainable public infrastructure. The City oversees the 
maintenance of 210 miles of City streets, 87 signalized intersections, over 7,000 street 
lights, and 11 publicly-owned parking lots and garages.  
The City believes that close alignment with regional transportation partners is critical to 
meeting its goals. San Mateo outlines the following policy principles for transportation: 
1.
Support legislation that promotes innovation in emerging technologies, 
particularly those that creatively address congestion and improve overall 
transportation system management.
2.
Support legislation and funding for traffic signal synchronization initiatives to 
enhance safety at rights-of-way.
3.
Support efforts to maximize SB 1 funding for local street and road rehabilitation 
projects that reduce congestion and improve safety.

Support legislation and funding mechanisms that advance a regional, multimodal,

Parks, Recreation, and Community Services
The City of San Mateo’s parks and urban forests are important natural resources that 
provide community members with access to nature, encourage healthy lifestyles, and 
support a mixture of active and passive recreation opportunities. The City’s Park system 
includes more than a dozen neighborhood parks, nine community parks, six 
recreation/community centers, two pools, the Shoreline regional park system, several 
small “mini” parks, and an estuary lagoon for boating. Combined, the San Mateo 
community has access to approximately 795 acres of parks and open space. The City 
hosts diverse activities from senior programming, dance, music, and fitness classes to 
youth sports and camps to encourage healthy community engagement.
