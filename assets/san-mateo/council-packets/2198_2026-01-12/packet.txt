BACKGROUND
In 2019, the Age Friendly Task Force was formed to 
prepare San Mateo’s application to the AARP, under 
the guidance of CAFÉ, which had a contract with the 
County to assist cities with their applications
The San Mateo Application to the AARP was submitted 
to the AARP on September 24, 2020. 
By submitting the application San Mateo committed to 
actively engage with residents and local advocates to 
make San Mateo an Age-Friendly place to live. 
The AARP required that San Mateo submit an Action 
Plan within two years of acceptance into the program.
San Mateo submitted its 2022-2025 Action Plan to 
the AARP in November 2022.

PROJECT 1 
 
SAFE WALKING ROUTES FOR SENIORS - AND 
EVERYBODY
This project aims at accelerating the repair of sidewalks that 
represent tripping and falling hazards for seniors caused by 
sidewalks that are lifted, cracked, blocked, or otherwise 
damaged. Unsafe sidewalks are a hazard and a deterrent to 
walking by residents of all ages, not just seniors.
The Age-Friendly Task Force under the leadership of Vince 
Siminitus developed a map showing where seniors are most 
likely to be walking.  
The map was provided to the Public Works Department which 
is incorporating it in its larger effort to assess and improve 
sidewalk safety in San Mateo.
  It is also being used by the Police Department in its 
enforcement of sidewalk parking restrictions.

Age-Friendly San Mateo News # 39
Uber and Lyft for Seniors
Some older adults have been hesitant to start using rideshare services to 
get around.  Both Uber and Lyft have now rolled out special programs to make it 
easier for seniors and their families to use their services.  It will take a little effort to 
set up and get used to, but it will make it easier for you to go to events, see the 
doctor, visit your friends or go shopping.
Lyft Silver, https://www.lyft.com/rider/silver, offers more access to cars 
that are easy to get into and out of, a user-friendly app with larger fonts, and live 
phone support.  If you need help scheduling a ride you can talk to a real person. 
Caregivers or family members can upload gift cards for payment and monitor rides 
to make sure the older adult has safely reached his or her destination.  
Uber Senior Accounts, https://www.uber.com/us/en/ride/seniors/, are part 
of Uber’s Family Profile feature. Family members can book rides, track trips, and 
manage the account. The Senior Account app interface is streamlined with larger 
text and fewer buttons and can be linked to Medicare Flex cards for eligible medical 
rides.  It also is possible to schedule an Uber ride without a smart phone by calling 
833-USE-UBER (873-8237).  
Older adults who live in San Mateo also may want to sing up for the Get 
Around program, https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/3795/Get-Around-Senior-Rides-
Program .  Residents of other cities may be eligible for the Got Wheels program, 
https://peninsulafamilyservice.org/got-wheels/ .  Both programs offer a limited 
monthly number of $5 rides with Serra Yellow Cab. 
This information is brought to you by the San Mateo Age-Friendly Coalition.  
Contact agefriendlysanmateo@gmail.com if you would like to learn more about our 
work.

Key Project

intergenerationail Community Building

Intergenerational connections assist all age groups in increasing self-
esteem, sense of purpose, usefulness, and connection to the cycle of life in
the community. They also promote intergenerational learning through the
sharing of skills, knowledge, and experience between the generations. As
part of this effort, the San Mateo Senior Center will provide
intergenerational programs such as Teen and Senior Theater, Game Nights,
and other special events. The goal of this project is to be intentional in our
efforts to bring together our community in all stages of their lives. The Age-
Friendly Task Force will work with San Mateo Parks and Recreation staff,
library staff, schools, and community members to build a solid base of
specific programs that target multiple age groups for years to come.

PROJECT 4 
AGE FRIENDLY 
BUSINESS 
RECOGNITION/ 
CERTIFICATION
Businesses that adapt their services and physical 
locations to be age-friendly can expand their 
customer base, and revenue, while helping older 
adults remain active and live independently. 
Beginning in 2022, the Age Friendly Task Force, 
under the leadership of Vince Siminitus, designed 
this program, including  developing the concept, 
proposing the Age-Friendly criteria, drafting the 
application and proposing a workflow.
In 2023 the City decided to take the lead on 
implementing a pilot for this program aiming for 
20 enrollees.

❖Age Friendly Coalition volunteers visit 
businesses, explain the program and invite 
owners to apply.  Businesses can also apply 
directly through the City’s website.
❖A volunteer team, trained and led by Vince 
Siminitus, then verifies applicants’ compliance 
with the age friendly criteria. 
❖To date, 23 businesses have been enrolled.

GOAL PSF-7 
DELIVER PUBLIC SERVICES AND FACILITIES THAT SERVE THE NEEDS OF 
SENIORS, ARE AGE FRIENDLY, AND ALLOW SAN MATEO RESIDENTS TO AGE 
IN PLACE. 
POLICIES 
Policy PSF 7.1 Universal Design. Encourage 
Universal Design, a design concept that encourages 
accessibility for people of all ages, in new residential 
construction and major remodels. 
Policy PSF 7.2 Healthy Aging. Support institutions 
and initiatives that promote healthy aging, both at 
home and in care centers. 
Policy PSF 7.3 Outreach to Seniors. Provide 
regular and timely communication to seniors and 
aging adults about the services, programs, and other 
opportunities available to these groups of people. 
Use age-appropriate outreach channels to 
disseminate information.
ACTIONS
Action PSF 7.4 Age Friendly City. Support the City’s commitment 
to becoming an Age-Friendly City by continuing to implement the 
Age Friendly Action Plan. 
Action PSF 7.5 Comprehensive Senior Services. Study the 
effectiveness of existing senior services and explore ways to 
increase and strengthen these services in coordination with senior 
service providers. Comprehensive services include addressing 
senior nutrition, mental health, and transportation. 
Action PSF 7.6 Senior Volunteers. Continue the volunteer 
program by recruiting/encouraging participation of seniors with 
certain skills and experience. 
Action PSF 7.7 Caregiver Support. Collaborate with private, 
nonprofit, faith-based and public community service organizations, 
including the County of San Mateo, to offer support for caregivers of 
seniors and people with disabilities.

WHERE ARE WE IN THE PROGRAM CYCLE?
The Age-Friendly Process and Program Cycle

Members of the AARP Network of Age-Friendly States and Communities
commit to an assessment process and cycle of continuous improvement,
the steps of which typically require the member community to:
1. Establish a way — such as through a commission, advisory panel or
focus groups — to include older residents in all stages of the age-friendly
planning and implementation process

2. Conduct a community needs assessment (AARP can provide survey
examples, templates and an online tool in English and Spanish)

3. Develop an action and evaluation plan based on the assessment results
4. Submit the plan for review by AARP

5. Implement and work toward the goals of the plan

6. Share solutions, successes and best practices with AARP

7. Assess the plan’s impact and submit progress reports

8. Repeat!
