SUBJECT:
Senior Transportation “Get Around” Program - Informational 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation on the Senior Transportation “Get Around” program and provide feedback on potential options to 
reduce program expenses for long-term sustainability.
BACKGROUND:
The “Get Around” Program provides on-demand transportation services for qualified City of San Mateo residents aged 60 
and over. Each month a rider is eligible for eight (8) one-way trips or four (4) round-trips to destinations within the cities of 
San Mateo, Burlingame, Foster City, Belmont, Hillsborough, San Carlos and Redwood City. Additional locations outside of 
the cities listed include the Veterans' Hospitals in Menlo Park and Palo Alto and Stanford University Medical Centers. All 
rides must begin or end in the City of San Mateo. 
On February 2, 2026, the City Council approved an amendment with Serra Yellow Cab of Daly City, Inc. to add an additional 
$200,000 in funding from Parks and Recreation Department salary savings to cover projected costs for the remainder of 
the fiscal year. 
The Senior Transportation “Get Around” program has experienced significant growth over the past several years, adding 
approximately 500 new riders annually and reaching over 2,000 riders to date. This growth reflects strong community 
demand and program success; however, it has also resulted in rapidly rising and unpredictable costs. Additionally, the 
City/County Association of Governments (C/CAG) Lifeline Transportation Program Cycle 7 funding will conclude on June 30, 
2026. This funding currently reimburses 80% of program costs. 
To stabilize demand and mitigate further unbudgeted cost increases, a waitlist was implemented on January 12, 2026. To 
further sustain the program, other considerations must be made to keep the program aligned with the $250,000 Measure 
S funds allocated to its operations. 
The current fiscal year is projected to provide more than 13,000 rides, while the approved allocation of $250,000 can 
support approximately 8,200 rides. At present, the program serves an average of 284 riders per month, with each rider 
taking an average of 3.8 rides. 
To remain within the allocated budget, the program can serve either: 
• 170 riders with a maximum of four rides per month; or 
• 285 riders with a maximum of two rides per month 
Updates to the program are necessary to ensure it can continue to serve the most vulnerable community members
