31-57 S. B Street Commercial Development Project Public Art Proposal
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve the proposed public art design for the public art associated with the approved commercial development project 
located at 31-57 S. B Street.
BACKGROUND:
On July 23, 2024, the Planning Commission approved the proposed development project including a four-story, 
commercial and office building (the ‚ÄúProject‚Äù) at 31-57 S B Street (also referred to as ‚Äú1st & B‚Äù), proposed by Harvest 
Properties (the ‚ÄúApplicant‚Äù). In accordance with San Mateo Municipal Code Chapter 23.60, the Project is subject to the Art 
in Public Places program, which requires all new commercial development projects and multi-family projects with a 
building permit valuation of three million dollars or more to plan for the inclusion of public art within the project site or 
payment of an in-lieu fee. 
On January 8, 2024, the Civic Arts Committee discussed the proposed Project as a Study Session item. At that time, the 
Planning Application (PA-2022-089, Site Plan and Architectural Review and Tentative Parcel Map to consolidate the 
parcels) for the Project was under review and the applicant was seeking feedback on potential placement and conceptual 
public art installation. Staff recommended the inclusion of public art on the rear (east) building fa√ßade facing the 
Downtown Caltrain Station due to its prominent location as an entryway into the City from the Caltrain station and its high 
visibility within the Downtown Retail Core. The Committee reviewed three different art concepts and provided feedback to 
the applicant.
Based on the feedback from the Committee and the approved Planning Application, the Applicant is proposing a painted 
mural by artist Nicole Mueller on the east fa√ßade of the building facing the Downtown Caltrain Station, similar to location 
that was shown at the previous Study Session. The proposed design, artist background, and other materials relating to the 
estimated cost of the public art installation is included in Attachment 1. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact as this is a private installation proposed within the project site. In accordance with San Mateo 
Municipal Code Chapter 23.60, the applicant is responsible for the installation costs and maintenance of the proposed 
artwork.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This art design approval is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that will 
not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)

in San Francisco, CA. Her large-scale paintings, 
murals, and public art installations are driven by 
process and inspired by interiority, ideas of place, 
and the elusive nature of memory. Her paintings,

surrounding environment, capturing an emotional

through scale, color, and gesture in her work.
Her works combine color and form into an 
individualized visual language characterized by 
animated gestures, expansive compositions, and 
organic, lyrical palettes.   
Mueller‚Äôs work in the public realm becomes an 
extension of her studio practice, pushing her 
explorations of scale, process, and material with 
large murals or installations in mosaic or glass.

The landscapes around San Mateo, and throughout Northern California, were of particular inspiration 
for this project. The proximity to the Bay, the movements of water, the directional artery-like forms of 
tidal marshes when viewed from up above all informed the direction of this mural for 1st & B. 
A palette of nature-inspired hues and more subdued tonal range of greens balance the warmth of the 
surrounding architecture with references to the landscape. Depth is created in the layering of organic 
forms that abstractly mimic the movements of water; the sway, ebb and flow, and shifting fields of 
vision. These visual references to currents and directional tidal pools influenced the mural‚Äôs 
composition while alluding to the ever-shifting landscapes around San Mateo.
Combining inspiration from the surrounding landscape with Mueller‚Äôs unique approach to color and 
form, the artist uses her visual language as a painter to create a highly specific sense of place with 
each work. Her interests lie in painting as a filter and vehicle for translating experience, or capturing the 
essence of a place, in ways that are intuitive, emotional, and more connected to the intangible qualities 
of a place rather than literal depictions of it.
Mueller‚Äôs work transforms into shifting explorations of internal vs. external landscapes, the elusive and 
illusory nature of memory, experience, and how these can be woven together into dynamic, layered 
spaces that are open-ended, abstract reflections of place.
