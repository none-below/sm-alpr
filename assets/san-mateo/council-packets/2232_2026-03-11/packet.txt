2. San Mateo Countywide Transportation Plan Update 
Staff from the City/County Association of Governments presented on the San Mateo Countywide 
Transportation Plan update. 
 
Public Comment: Mike Swire emphasized the need to improve bike safety and connectivity to the downtown 
station so more people can bike instead of drive. He added that although the Peninsula project was canceled 
due to cost, it would have provided an important connection. 
 
Commissioners asked questions. 
 
Commissioner’s comments included: Commissioners highlighted the importance of regional and cross-county 
connections. There was emphasis that as the community grows with more housing, transportation challenges 
must be addressed proactively with a comprehensive, well-executed plan and clear metrics to track progress. 
Stressed the need for protected bike lanes, first- and last-mile connections, and better coordination across 
cities to ensure seamless and safe connections. Some Commissioners also underlined the importance of 
accurately representing people without cars in survey data to support equity goals and called for future 
presentations to include more detailed data on youth, schools, and how different groups are impacted. 
 
3. Storm Drain Master Plan Update 
Staff provided an update on the City’s Storm Drain Master Plan efforts and requested Commissioner feedback. 
 
Public Comment: None 
 
Commissioners asked questions. 
 
Commissioner’s comments included: 
Recognized that recurring ponding and localized flooding significantly affect resident safety, daily life, and 
confidence in city services, and supported increased funding for maintenance and mitigation. Recommended 
prioritizing geographically significant and high-visibility areas such as areas near schools, parks, gathering 
spaces, and major corridors where impacts are most apparent. Encouraged refining project criteria to better 
account for visibility, resident experience, and public trust alongside technical measures. Emphasized the 
importance of clearer communication about how stormwater funds are used to alleviate resident frustration. 
Advocated for pursuing state and federal funding, bonding, and other revenue strategies to address structural 
funding gaps. Called for more robust and creative public engagement efforts to build community understanding 
and long-term support for infrastructure investment. Commissioners responded to staff’s request for feedback, 
expressing support to continue allocating a limited portion of annual funding for minor storm water projects 
that may not be captured as high priority in the master plan.

SUBJECT:
Bicycle and Pedestrian Projects Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive an update on City of San Mateo's bicycle and pedestrian projects.
BACKGROUND:
Staff will present brief updates on current bicycle and pedestrian infrastructure projects, planning efforts, and recently 
awarded grants. An update was last provided to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission on July 9, 2025. 
The City of San Mateo is committed to improving transportation safety for everyone, whether walking, biking, rolling, 
driving, or taking transit. Through completed plans and ongoing capital projects, the City advances safety with targeted 
intersection improvements, school-area enhancements, bicycle network upgrades, and corridor-level investments. 
Together, these efforts support a shared goal: reducing severe and fatal crashes while making streets safer and more 
comfortable for all users. Key initiatives which inform the City’s major transportation capital improvement efforts are as 
follows: 
•
The completed Bicycle Master Plan (BMP) serves as a blueprint for expanding and improving the San Mateo bicycle 
and mobility network in the coming years, after being approved by City Council in 2020 following robust 
community engagement, existing conditions and data analysis, and planning and design work.
•
The completed Local Roadway Safety Plan (LRSP) analyzed traffic safety performance, crash patterns, and 
recommended strategic and proven solutions to improve safety performance at priority locations throughout the 
City. It then identified high-priority locations based on crash history, stakeholder collaboration, and community 
feedback.
CAPITAL PROJECTS
Delaware Street Safe Routes to School Corridor: Funds awarded by Cycle 5 of the California Transportation 
Commission’s (CTC) Active Transportation Program (ATP) enable implementation of a Class IV separated bike lane and 
Class III bicycle boulevard on Delaware Street as proposed in the BMP. The project provides necessary safety 
improvements in an area with a high level of traffic stress and will improve safety and connectivity to two public schools 
accessed from Delaware Street. Further, this project improves north-west connectivity to the Hillsdale Caltrain station 
and will directly connect to the future bike facility on 28th Avenue. The following is an update on this effort:
•
Design was completed in summer 2025. This phase included multiple community meetings with residents and 
stakeholders, as well as a presentation to the City Council in May 2025.

facilities identified in the BMP with scheduled pavement management projects, where feasible. PMP projects with

below, if applicable; typically, implementation of BMP projects into PMP projects are intended to involve minimal 
parking or travel lane removal. Projects which have more significant impacts are instead identified for a separate 
implementation effort, with a more robust community engagement effort. 
The following is an update on this effort:

PMP 3A:

Class II bike lanes on Campus Drive from Hillsdale Boulevard to Live Oaks Drive (Medium-Low priority

sufficient for the low vehicle volumes on Campus Drive.

•
The bike facilities were completed in Fall 2025 following the pavement work. Striping repair work will be 
completed in Spring 2026.

PMP 4A-1:

Class II bike lanes on 5th Avenue from El Camino Real to Ellsworth Avenue. (High Priority from BMP). Up

from S Humboldt St to S Amphlett Blvd; Delaware St from Birch Ave to Sunnybrae Blvd; Peninsula Ave 
from Bayshore Blvd to Airport Blvd; Norfolk St from Darwin Ave to Roberta Dr.; and Bridgepoint Pkwy 
Bridgepoint Cir to Bridgepoint Cir. (Various Priorities from BMP). 
•
Construction began in Fall 2025. The project is currently suspended due to inclement weather, and the 
contractor will continue in late Spring 2026. 
•
PMP 4A-2: 
•

•
PMP 6B-2:

Class II bike lanes on W. 3rd Avenue from Dartmouth Rd to El Camino Real. Installation is anticipated in

Avenue, aimed at improving the corridor for all modes:
•
Roadway Marking Refresh: Roadway markings, including lane lines, arrows, and crosswalks, on Peninsula 
Avenue from El Camino Real to US-101 were refreshed in summer 2025 to provide greater visibility. 
•
Peninsula/Delaware Intersection Safety Improvements: This LRSP priority project includes improved lighting,

Peninsula Corridor Multi-Modal Complete Streets Study: The City of San Mateo, in partnership with the City of

19th Avenue/Fashion Island Multimodal Improvement Project: This project is a joint effort between the City and SMCTA, 
and will design and enhance multimodal travel along a key east-west corridor by improving bicycle and pedestrian facilities 
and refining traffic operations on 19th Avenue and Fashion Island Boulevard. The project supports safer, more comfortable 
biking connections across the city, while also significantly improving congestion along the corridor. The following is an 
update on this effort: 
•
The project team held public meetings on July 15, 2025 (Nueva High School, 35% design feedback) and 
November 5, 2025 (College Park Elementary School, responses to feedback), with an online survey available. 
Staff have additionally hosted a number of focused stakeholder meetings for the project. A final community 
meeting is scheduled for in-person on April 7, 2026, and virtually on April 16, 2026, to review the completed 
design.
•
The project recently secured an additional $16,457,217 from the 2025 SMCTA Highway Program. Staff have 
submitted a request for an additional $3 million in RM3 funding.  With these additional funding sources, the 
project will be fully funded through construction.
•
The Design Engineering Evaluation Report has been submitted to Caltrans. Once that is completed staff can 
finalize the design phase which includes the 100% design plans and right-of-way certification with Caltrans. 
The design phase is estimated to be completed in November 2026. 
Third Avenue and Norfolk Street Intersection Safety Improvements: Targeted safety upgrades at this key intersection are 
designed to improve visibility, clarify vehicle movements, and reduce conflict points between vehicles, pedestrians, and 
bicyclists. This project responds to needs identified through the LRSP and supports safer access in the downtown area by 
improving connectivity between the bike and pedestrian bridge on Third Avenue, the existing Class II bike lane on South 
Norfolk Street, and the existing Class I bike path adjacent to the San Mateo Creek. The project is funded in part by the 
federal Community Development Block Grant (CDBG). The following is an update on this effort: 
•
Phase 1 construction took place from February to April 2025, installing a new bikeway on Norfolk Street 
between 3rd Avenue and the San Mateo Creek Trail, along with signal pole foundations and other 
underground electrical work.
•
Phase 2 construction occurred from June to August 2025, completing drainage improvements, 3rd Avenue 
median modifications, paving (on portions of Norfolk Street and the new bikeway), slurry sealing, and striping.

Hillsdale/31st Avenue Safe Routes to School: The City was awarded funding from the SMCTA Pedestrian and Bicycle

and were tested through a quick build project installed in early August 2024. The project will advance the 
recommended improvements from the walk audit into a construction-ready design that includes, but is not limited to, 
curb extensions, high-visibility crosswalks, and a new signal and protected intersection at the 31st Ave and Alameda de

City Council approved the supplemental funding agreement appropriation and execution in September 2025.

Downtown Caltrain Station North Access Improvement Project: The City was awarded funding from the MTC 
Transportation Development Act Article 3 grant program to design a new Caltrain station entrance connecting to the

PLANNING EFFORTS
Citywide Complete Streets Plan: The Complete Streets Plan identifies policies, goals, and priority projects to improve 
mobility, equity, connectivity, and sustainability and build a better circulation network for the City’s future. These 
policies and goals will guide how future transportation projects are planned, designed, constructed, and maintained. It 
also serves as a unifying framework that aligns and connects the City’s existing transportation plans. In 2023, City staff 
and the consultant team completed Phases I and II of the community engagement plan and began the process of 
developing a Complete Streets North Central Plan (NCCSP). Work continued on both plans through 2024, with 80% of 
the Complete Streets Plan and 85% of the NCCSP completed by the end of 2024. The following is an update on this 
effort:
•
City staff amended the consultant agreement to extend the term and, per the June 2, 2025, Annual 
Transportation Review to City Council, will incorporate the North Central Complete Streets Plan (NCCSP) into 
the Citywide Complete Streets Plan for more cost-effective rescoping and finalization. 
•
Work on the Citywide Complete Streets Plan and NCCSP is temporarily paused due to staff resource limitations 
but is anticipated to resume in FY 2026-27. 
•
Upon resumption, the NCCSP will incorporate Council direction from the February 2, 2026, meeting on 
Humboldt Street bike lanes; the Peninsula Interchange Project cancellation; and potential impacts of rail 
crossing closures related to the Train Horn Quiet Zone. The effort will also need to be coordinated with other 
concurrent planning studies, like the Peninsula Corridor Multi-Modal Complete Streets Study. 
Humboldt Bike Lane Alternatives Project: Staff conducted robust community outreach following February 3, 2025, 
City Council direction to explore the feasibility of bicycle boulevards on Idaho Street and Fremont Streets as alternative 
bike routes to a portion of the Humboldt Street bike lanes. This included three workshops, multiple online surveys, 
input incorporated into the concept designs, and a final mailed survey to capture community feedback. The following 
is an update on this effort:
•
Staff presented outreach findings to the Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission in December 2025 and 
to City Council in February 2026. 
•
At the February 2026 City Council meeting, Council directed staff to retain the Humboldt Street bike lanes 
while pursuing: lighting upgrades; traffic calming measures on Humboldt Street; a focused Residential Parking 
Permit Program (RPPP); enhanced enforcement of parking and speeding in North Central (including after-
hours); feasibility study for making Humboldt one-way; exploration of a rideshare program for the 
neighborhood; and application of lessons learned to future bike boulevards in the City. 
AB 413 Daylighting Efforts: In accordance with AB 413 Daylighting Law requirements, parking is prohibited on the 
approach side of crosswalks at intersections. Although red curb is not required for the state law to be enforceable, 
adding red curb in parking prohibited areas helps to raise driver awareness about this State law. Preventing parking 
increases unobstructed sightlines so drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians can better see one another, reducing potential

The City has completed AB 413 red curb painting (daylighting for crosswalk visibility) around all school zones

RECENTLY AWARDED GRANTS
Housing Incentive Pool Grant (HIP): This program provides transportation grants to promote the production and 
preservation of affordable housing. MTC distributes $71 million of this funding to 15 jurisdictions that issued certificates 
of occupancy for the greatest number of eligible housing units from 2018 to 2022. The City was amongst the top 15 
jurisdictions awarded Housing Incentive Pool funds.
•
The City was awarded $959,000 in HIP funds in March 2025 to design and construct the Local Roadway Safety 
Priority Package 1 project, which includes improvements at priority intersections identified in the LRSP.  This 
project was discussed above.
SMCTA Highway Program Funds: Voter-approved local sales tax revenues from Measure A (½-cent tax, reauthorized 
2004) and Measure W (additional ½-cent congestion relief tax, 2018), administered by the San Mateo County 
Transportation Authority. These funds support highway infrastructure in San Mateo County to reduce congestion, 
improve safety, and enhance throughput on corridors like Highways 101 and 280, via competitive calls for projects 
including interchange modifications and ramp improvements. In the most recent round (2025 Call for Projects, awarded 
January 2026), the TA approved nearly $190 million (approximately $189.9 million) in Measure A and Measure W 
funding for highway and complete streets projects, the largest such release to date.
•
The 19th Avenue Fashion Island Multimodal Improvement project is the third top-ranked project in the recent 
Highway Program funding cycle. The project was awarded approximately $16.450M in funding for the 
construction phase. SMCTA also recommended the allocation of $3M in Regional Measure 3 funding for this 
project which is not expected to be finalized until summer of 2026. This project is discussed above.
•
The Peninsula Corridor Multi-Modal Complete Streets Study is the second top-ranked project in the recent 
Highway Program funding cycle. The project was awarded $1.695M in funding for the planning phase of this 
project.  This planning study is discussed above.

Highway Corridor Grant Awards
34
Grant Information
• The SMCTA Highway Program provides Measure A and Measure W 
funding to reduce congestion and improve safety along major 
commute corridors
Update
• The City was awarded $16.45M for the 19th Avenue/Fashion Island 
Boulevard Multimodal Improvements Project, supporting the 
construction phase of a Class IV separated bikeway and related 
pedestrian and intersection safety improvements along the corridor.
• The City also awarded $1,695,000 in SMCTA Highway Corridor funding 
for the Peninsula Corridor Multimodal Project, supporting planning 
and design for multimodal and circulation improvements in the US-
101/SR-92 interchange area
