Library Rules of Conduct Policy 
[Revision Adopted by the Library Board of Trustees, 3/2/26]  
 
 
The Library is a resource for all members of the community Any behavior that disrupts the 
Library’s ability to provide a safe and welcoming environment for the public and a 
supportive workplace for library employees shall be grounds for immediate action. Follow-
up may include requesting person(s) to leave the Library; barring future services, 
including admission (temporarily or permanently); and calling the police.  
 
Prohibited behaviors in the Library may include but are not limited to the following: 
 
• 
Leaving personal items unattended  
• 
Consuming food and beverages (other than liquid in a container with lid) outside of 
      designated eating zones  
• 
Sleeping  
• 
Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways 
• 
Bringing animals other than service animals inside the Library 
• 
Entering the Library without wearing shoes or a shirt 
• 
Strong pervasive odors  
• 
Smoking on Library property* 
• 
Not setting cell phones or devices on silent mode; and speaking or listening on cell phones 
or devices in spaces other than designated “phone zones” 
• 
Leaving children unattended  
• 
Using rollerblades, scooters, or skateboards on Library property** 
• 
Soliciting or panhandling 
• 
Using the restroom inappropriately, such as bathing or laundering  
• 
Monopolizing Library space or resources including moving furniture 
• 
Viewing obscene content on library computers or personal devices; or other violations of the 
      Computer Use Policy 
• 
Causing a public disturbance, which includes but is not limited to use of abusive or obscene 
language, talking loudly, or making excessive noise  
• 
Disobeying the direction of a library staff member 
• 
Harassment of any kind toward other library users and library staff  
• 
Bringing bicycles (or any wheeled transport that cannot be folded and/or safely stored away) 
      inside the Library 
• 
Bringing large, oversized, or bulky sets of personal belongings that cannot be safely stored 
away 
• 
Bringing personal items judged to be unsanitary, pose a health or safety risk, or disruptive to 
library operations 
• 
Vandalizing or misusing furniture, equipment, or materials 
 
To prevent the theft of Library materials, state law authorizes the detention for a reasonable period 
of any person using these facilities when there is probable cause to believe that the person being 
detained is unlawfully removing Library materials from the premises. Staff has legal authority to 
conduct a limited and reasonable search of purses, bags, parcels, briefcases and other packages 
if a patron does not voluntarily surrender unlawfully removed Library materials. (CA Penal Code 
490.5) 
 
* Library property includes outdoor areas adjacent to the Library building.

Enforcement of Violations 
 
To ensure a safe and welcoming environment for the public and a supportive workplace for 
library employees, violations of the Rules of Conduct are enforced by Library employees with 
assistance by the San Mateo Police Department. Suspensions are determined by the type of 
infraction and are issued by the City Librarian. The suspension period will be contingent on the 
nature of the violation and the Library will consult with law enforcement officials and the City 
Attorney’s office. The City Librarian reserves the right to make individualized determinations as 
to the appropriate level of enforcement.  
 
Library staff will document instances of minor violations or behavioral misconduct. Staff may 
direct patron to leave the library if verbal warnings are not followed within the same day and 
patron will not be allowed to enter until the following day. A written warning will be issued by the 
City Librarian or designee if misconduct continues. A suspension (banning) letter will be issued 
by the City Librarian if the written warning is not followed.   
 
For violations stemming from criminal activity, library staff will summon the San Mateo Police 
Department in response to criminal activity in the Library. A suspension (banning) letter may be 
issued by the City Librarian to the patron at the time of the arrest. Due to the egregious nature of 
criminal activity, a warning letter will not be issued. 
 
Appeal Process 
 
Suspensions may be appealed in writing to the City Librarian within seven days of receipt of the 
suspension letter. The appeal will be reviewed at the next scheduled Library Board meeting, 
during which time the suspension will remain in effect. The Library Board will decide if the 
suspension will be upheld, reduced, or extended. The Library Board’s decision will be final. 
Library patrons whose library privileges are suspended cannot use any of the San Mateo 
libraries while serving a suspension.  
 
Level One: Minor Violations-examples may include but are not limited to the following: 
 
• 
Leaving personal items unattended  
• 
Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways 
• 
Sleeping 
• 
Speaking on cell phone in areas not designated as phone zones 
• 
Bringing animals other than service animals;  
• 
Entering without wearing shoes/shirt 
• 
Using rollerblades, scooters, skateboards on Library property 
• 
Leaving children unattended  
 
Suspension Period: If behavior violations at this level persist, a suspension letter may be 
issued for disobeying the direction of library staff (see level two violations below). 
 
Level Two: Violations stemming from behavioral misconduct-examples may include but 
are not limited to the following: 
 
• 
Soliciting or panhandling  
• 
Viewing obscene content on library or personal computers/devices 
• 
Using the restrooms inappropriately such as bathing and laundering 
• 
Monopolizing library space and resources

•

• 
Disobeying the direction of library staff

Possession and/or use of alcohol or illegal drugs 
•
