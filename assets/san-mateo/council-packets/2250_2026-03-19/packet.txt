SUBJECT:
Measure S Five-Year Forecast and Capital Improvement Project Requests
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive Measure S five-year forecast, consider FY 2026-27 budget projections, and recommend the transfer of $300,000 
from the Los Prados Court Lighting Improvement project to the Main Library Lighting project.
BACKGROUND:
Measure S Fund – Five Year Forecast 
Based on current economic conditions and operational analysis, staff has updated the five-year forecast for the Measure S 
Fund, providing critical insight into fiscal sustainability beyond the current budget cycle. 
Using the FY 25-26 adopted budget as the baseline, revenue assumptions for the 1/4 cent transaction and use tax revenue 
are conservatively set at a modest rate ranging from 1% to 2%. It is projected that the fund’s revenues will grow from the 
current $7 million to $7.6 million over five years. 
Measure S has historically supported Police, Parks & Recreation, and Library operations, with expenditures projected to 
grow steadily by 3% to 4% annually. To maintain the same service levels in fiscal year 2026-27, Measure S will need to 
provide approximately $2.2 million.

For the new fiscal year 2026-27, staff projects $7 million in revenues, $2.2 million in operating expenditures, $2.6 million in 
transfers out for debt service payments related to the revenue bonds issued in 2020 to finance the cost of flood control 
and street rehabilitation, and $0.3 million in transfers out to the General Fund for amortized repayment of costs it incurred 
on Measure S' behalf before Measure S funds were available. 
As shown in the table below, there is limited remaining capacity in the Measure S Fund for FY 26-27. This is mostly due to 
higher levels of budgeting for capital improvement projects in FY 24-25 and FY 25-26. However, there is available capacity 
in future years for between $2.35 - $1.65 million million per year. 
Based on the information available at the time of this report, city departments have the following new funding requests 
for capital improvement projects, totaling $.7 million, for the next fiscal year 2026-27: 
•
Public Works: $.55 million for capital improvement projects
•
Parks & Recreation: $.15 million for capital improvement project
Measure S Fund – FY 25-26 Funding
The Facilities Division within Public Works is proposing to transfer $300,000 in Measure S funds from The Los Prados Court 
Lighting Improvement Project to the Main Library lighting project. The Los Prados Court Lighting Improvement project was 
originally funded to enhance lighting at the tennis and basketball courts at Los Prados Park. However, facilities staff 
implemented an alternative solution that met the lighting requirements at Los Prados Park without the use of Measure S 
funds. Thus, they are recommending the transfer of these funds to the existing project to update the lighting control 
system at the Main Library. The energy-efficient lighting solution at the Main Library is designed to automate and manage 
lighting throughout the building, but many components of the existing lighting control system have exceeded their useful 
life and are now considered obsolete. Due to the unique nature of this system, the Main Library Lighting project needs 
additional funding to complete the lighting upgrades at the Main Library.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Staff recommends the committee review the five-year forecast, consider FY 2026-27 budget projections, and recommend 
the transfer of $300,000 from the Los Prados Court Lighting Improvement project to the Main Library Lighting project. This 
fund transfer will not affect the overall Measure S Fund balance.

the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(4).)
