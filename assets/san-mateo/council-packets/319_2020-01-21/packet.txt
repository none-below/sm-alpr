SUBJECT:
25th Avenue Grade Separation - Parking Track Location Update 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive update on Caltrain Parking (Set-Out) Track evaluation and provide direction to staff on preferred alternative for 
Caltrain to consider.
BACKGROUND:
The 25th Avenue Grade Separation project was a vision established over 20 years ago to improve east-west connectivity 
across the Caltrain railroad tracks. The project raises the tracks at the 25th Avenue crossing, which will allow additional 
east-west street connections at 28th and 31st Avenues in conformance with the Rail Corridor Plan. The Hillsdale Caltrain 
Station will also be relocated northward between 28th and 31st Avenues as part of these improvements. As a result, the 
existing Caltrain parking track located in Bay Meadows must be relocated to another location in San Mateo. This relocation 
has been part of the overall project budget and is estimated to cost $2 million.
Caltrain chose the original location for the replacement parking track to be on the west side of the railroad tracks between 
10th Avenue and 14th Avenue. Caltrain completed design and environmental work for the project and had begun 
earthwork activities at the location.  At that point, residents in the area contacted City Council to inquire about the work. 
In response, Caltrain held a public meeting on June 11, 2019 to present the proposed location to residents in the 
immediate vicinity. The negative feedback from that meeting prompted discussion between Caltrain staff and City staff to 
evaluate potential alternative locations for the new Caltrain parking track. 
Caltrain created an opportunity on its website for members of the public to provide additional considerations for the 
parking track relocation. This resulted in multiple meetings between Caltrain and City staff to review all 28 alternatives 
identified. The alternatives analysis considered the operational viability using the nine Caltrain operational criteria. The 
City provided input on the financial viability. For an option to be considered viable, both operational and financial viability 
criteria must be met. The result of the alternatives analysis identified four alternatives at two locations that met all 
necessary criteria. 
Location 1 - West side of tracks between 10th Avenue and 14th Avenue: 
•
Option 8 – Provide access from 14th Avenue with no additional mitigations
•
Option 9 – Provide access from 14th Avenue with additional mitigations (e.g., trees, concrete wall)
•
Option 23 – Provide access on 9th Avenue with additional mitigations (e.g., trees, concrete wall)
Location 2 - Between 14th Avenue and the Caltrain Hayward Park Station
•
Option 27 – Provide access on 17th Avenue with no mitigation measures

On October 8, 2019, Caltrain held a second public meeting to review the design standards, efforts taken to analyze the 28 
alternatives, and present the viable alternatives. Additionally, Caltrain presented more accurate information about the 
purpose and use of the parking track. Approximately 70 residents attended the meeting. While there continued to be 
negative feedback from many residents, others became more receptive to a resolution as a result of the information 
presented. Meeting attendees were given a sticker to provide staff with their preference between the two viable options. 
Option 27 received 17 stickers and Option 9 received 5 stickers. Staff notes that a resident created an additional option of 
“Not in Hayward Park” that received 30 stickers. Two showed a preference for Option 7 (between 10th and 14th but on 
the east side of the tracks) and one wrote in by hand preference for Option 13 (location is in Belmont).
During the October 8, 2019 meeting, staff was asked whether the City could prohibit Caltrain from replacing the parking 
track altogether. From a legal standpoint, Caltrain is exempt from the City’s zoning and building regulations by virtue of its 
status under the Public Utilities Code. Additionally, from a practical standpoint, Caltrain has informed the City that it is 
infeasible to not replace the parking track due to operational needs.
Staff had originally planned to hold a study session on October 21, 2019, but City staff had to postpone the study session 
because of new information that arose three days before the meeting. The new information would require Caltrain to 
clear right-of-way encroachments, which would remove the playground area from a preschool. Since the cancellation of 
that meeting, the City held small focus group meetings to speak with a small group of community members. Three focus 
group meetings were held, November 6, 2019, November 20, 2019, and December 6, 2019. The City also hosted another 
community meeting on January 13, 2020 to inform the neighborhood of the latest news on the parking track. There were 
many new attendees at this meeting and the vast majority in attendance were opposed to the parking track being in 
Hayward Park. At the end of this meeting two members of the focus group presented a PowerPoint detailing their 
perspective on the issues and took questions from those in attendance.
Staff finds Option 9 as the most fiscally viable option. Should Caltrain move forward with this location, staff additionally 
recommends construction of a concrete masonry wall along the track location and creating access to the site from 9th 
Avenue. Community members asked about extending the wall at 10th and 14th Avenues to eliminate access at these 
locations. Staff will be discussing the possibility of this change with Caltrain and will provide undated information at the 
study session.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The 25th Avenue Grade Separation project has budget of $2 million to relocate the parking track. Option 9 with mitigation 
of a concrete masonry wall will cost an estimated additional $1,100,000. This would raise the budget of the parking track 
relocation to approximately $3.1 million.
Funding for the 25th Avenue Grade Separation Project is based on a co-op agreement between the City, Caltrain (the 
Peninsula Corridor Joint Powers Board), and the California High Speed Rail Authority. That agreement provides that the 
City is responsible for project funding above the $180 million amount committed by the parties. As mentioned above, the 
project budget included $2 million for the parking track. With 18 months remaining in the construction schedule, staff 
wants to ensure that any increased expenditures for the parking track do not pose undue financial risk on the overall 
project budget, and specifically the City. Therefore, at this point staff recommends limiting additional expenditures on the 
parking track to mitigation work on Option 9. 
Any additional funds required above the project budget of $180 million would need to be allocated from the City’s 
Transportation Impact Fee (TIF) Fund, which has already committed to a $12 million contribution to the 25th Avenue 
Grade Separation project.  Relocating the parking track outside of the originally selected location between 10th and 14th 
Avenues would require additional City funding from the TIF and would hamper the City’s ability to address other needed 
improvements to our transportation system.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This informational meeting is not a project subject to CEQA, because it can be seen with certainty that it will not cause a 
physical change in the environment. (Public Resources Code Section 21065.)

Set Out Track Criteria:
1. All MOW setout tracks shall have a minimum storage capacity of 950 feet. The storage capacity shall be measured from the “clear point” of the switch or the clear of the signal, whichever is more restrictive. Setout tracks shall be double ended with derails at both mainline turnout entry locations.
2. All MOW setout tracks shall be electrified. Current Peninsula Corridor Electrification Program (PCEP) design standards shall be incorporated for all elements.
3. All MOW setout tracks shall include a means of access for rubber tire trucks (40’ tractor trailer trucks) for deliveries and pickups.
4. When relocating and/or removing a set out track, construction staging shall be implemented to allow for maintenance of way access to the set out track at all times.
5. Avoid relocating a set out track to a different mainline track. Set out tracks shall be relocated to the same main line when possible.
6. All setout tracks shall include a remote controlled (via CCF) power switch machine.
7. All turnouts for new setout tracks shall be constructed using flange bearing or “jump” frogs. 8. Setout tracks shall be located a maximum of 3 miles from each adjacent (north and south) set out track.
9. Turnout should not be constructed on curved track due to excessive maintenance issues on the switch.

I am writing in opposition of the plan to locate the new Caltrain Maintenance track to 14th-Hayward 
Park.  
 
My name is Bry, and I am a home owner in 19th ave. We have lived in our house for over 5 years now 
and have seen the transformation of our neighborhood with Station Park Green, the office buildings on 
Calaroga, and now the AAA building. My family and I believe in transit oriented development and are 
supportive of the Passage development as well.  
 
However my sons’ preschool Universe of Colors on B St, a very diverse and Spanish immersion preschool 
would have their entire back yard removed if the new Caltrain Maintenance track is built btwn 14th St-
Hayward Park. This would probably effectively shut down the preschool. 
 
Universe of Colors is a great, diverse school. Between the 30 different children that go there, ranging 
from 2 years to after school care, they probably speak 8 different languages. Every day their learn about 
each other’s unique culture/heritage.  
 
Universe of Colors is one of only two Spanish Immersion preschools in San Mateo (to my knowledge). 
My sons will attend Sunnybrea or Fiesta Gardens for school. Both of those schools are made up of a 
large percentage of Spanish speaking families. Universe of Colors has helped my family/sons prepare for 
attending either public school. And as you are aware their is a shortage of preschools in San Mateo.  
 
Choosing to locate the maintenance track between 14th-Hayward Park also goes against Transit 
oriented development. I understand the homeowners btwn 10-14th st do not want the track behind 
their house. However this effects maybe 40 houses and wouldn’t effect the automotive shops on the 
east side of the tracks.  
 
Locating the track to 14th-Hayward Park would effect Hundreds of residents. There are apartments on B 
street, on the east side of the tracks Station Park Green with 600 units, the new AAA condo building ~73 
units, and the proposal for another condo on the Caltrain parking lot. This would probably make crossing 
the tracks harder too.  
 
Transit Oriented Development is all about having amenities nearby. Preschool for families is one of 
those. We walk our son to school and then hop on the train! That’s what it is all about. If Universe of 
Colors closes, I’ll probably have to drive my kid across town or to another town for preschool because 
there is such a huge shortage.  
 
Please do not approve locating the maintenance track to 14th-Hayward Park. It will economically hurt 
the B street neighborhood and the community.  
 
Thank you for taking the time to read this,  
 
Bry Myers  
19th ave resident
