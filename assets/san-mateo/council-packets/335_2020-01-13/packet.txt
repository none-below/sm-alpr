Having been born in Texas along the Mexican border, raised in Mexico, and then spending the majority
of my adult life in the USA, my work is greatly informed by my binational Mexican/American heritage, my
queer identity, and the liminal intersection of the LGBTQI and Latino communities of San Francisco. |
have explored these communities and concepts through varied and diverse artistic approaches, making
murals, posters, sculptures, and art installations that in some way satisfy my own personal curiosity,
respond to the immediate context, honor the message, and impact the spectator. As | continue to
explore the power of public art, | hope to use my work to inspire individuals with the pride and love that
| feel for my two countries of heritage, my LGBTQ! community, and to bring focus to the duality of
millions of Mexican-Americans living and traveling between both sides of this border.
