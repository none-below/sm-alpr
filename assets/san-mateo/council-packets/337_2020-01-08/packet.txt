    2. Citywide Bicycle Master Plan - Draft Plan Review 
Sue-Ellen Atkinson, Principal Transportation Planner, along with Megan Wooley-Ousdahl, consultant 
from Toole Designs, asked the Commissioners to review and comment on the Draft 2020 Bicycle 
Master Plan.  
  
Public Comment.   
The following spoke to the item:  Mark Eliot, Adam Loraine, Michael Ragan, Drew, and Foster Kinney. 
Emails from Mark Eliot and Kelly Moran were also submitted as public record.  
Commissioners asked staff to come back to the Commission and incorporate their comments before 
the Draft Bicycle Master Plan is presented to the Council.  Comments included a project list; a list of 
priorities along with a funding strategy; look at other funding options; include “micro-mobility” as part of 
the Master Plan; look at senior mobility; and a plan to encourage the County hospital staff to use 
bicycles or other modes of transportation.  
Staff will come back to the Commission and incorporate Commissioner comments as well as evaluate 
comments received during the comment period, which ends December 13, 2019.

SUBJECT:
Presentation by the Bay Area Water Supply and Conservation Agency
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive a presentation from the Bay Area Water Supply and Conservation Agency (BAWSCA) regarding the regional water 
system and BAWSCA’s responsibilities.
BACKGROUND:
The Bay Area Water Supply and Conservation Agency (BAWSCA) was created on May 27, 2003 to represent the interests of 
24 cities and water districts, and two private utilities, in Alameda, Santa Clara and San Mateo counties that purchase water 
on a wholesale basis from the San Francisco Regional Water System.  CalWater, who is the retail water purveyor for the 
City of San Mateo, is one of BAWSCA’s member agencies.
BAWSCA has the authority to coordinate water conservation, supply and recycling activities for its agencies; acquire water 
and make it available to other agencies on a wholesale basis; finance projects, including improvements to the regional 
water system; and build facilities jointly with other local public agencies or on its own to carry out the agency’s purposes.
BAWSCA will provide an overview of the San Francisco Regional Water System, how San Mateo receives water, and 
BAWSCA’s roles and responsibilities.
