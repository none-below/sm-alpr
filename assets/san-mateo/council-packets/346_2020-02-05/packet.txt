Review of Non-Resident Surcharge Policy for Recreation Programs 
Bob Palacio, Community Services Manager, provided an overview of the staff report and explanation of the current 
policy. Commissioners posed questions and offered comment. Commissioners expressed concern that residents 
may have an expectation of receiving a “perk” for being city residents and that removing the surcharge might cause 
courses to fill more quickly with non-residents thus prohibiting residents from registering for their preferred course. 
Sheila Canzian, Director, gave more detail regarding exclusions to the non-resident surcharge and explained that the 
courses currently with the largest waitlist, such as summer camps and day care, already exclude the non-resident 
surcharge. Commissioners suggested doing more research into cities, like Redwood City, that do not impose a non-
resident surcharge to see if it has had positive impact on their registration volume. It was also suggested to research 
comparable course pricing in neighboring cities to see if our courses are not already more competitive in pricing 
even with the non-resident surcharge. Commissioners agreed that it would be good to offer city residents priority 
registration as a perk if the non-resident surcharge is removed. After reviewing the 3 options: 1. Leave the policy as 
is 2. Adjust the policy 3. Discontinue the Non-Resident Surcharge, commissioners agreed that removing the non-

SUBJECT:
Proposed Parks and Recreation Fee Schedule for FY 2020-21
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of the proposed FY 2020-21 fee schedule for Parks and Recreation facilities and 
miscellaneous fees as provided in Attachment 1 of the Administrative Report.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City reviews and adjusts fees. Generally, fees are adjusted by the Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) for the 
previous year. The Finance Department has identified the official COLA for this year to be 3.00%. The recommended fee 
changes for the Parks and Recreation Department are detailed in Attachment 1and outlined below. Many fee increases are 
expected to be no larger than the COLA increase. Typically, fee increase recommendations are based on information 
gathered from market surveys and user meetings. In some cases, it has become practice to maintain fees at the same rate 
and then recommend a more reasonable increase as opposed to raising them incrementally every year.  
Recommended Fee Changes: For 2021 most fee increases are in line with the COLA increase and where necessary with 
rounding applied for transaction simplification.  The only fees that have increased higher than COLA are in Section VII. 
Parks and Landscape Resources. The fee for a Heritage Tree permit is proposed to increase from $75 to $80 which is a 6.6% 
increase. The Heritage Tree permit fees were last raised in FY 10-11 and staff does not believe that a modest $5.00 
increase is over-burdensome for applicant. The cost of a Tree Replacement deposit is also proposed to increase from $425 
to $475 which is an 11.7% increase.  The last increase in this fee was made in FY 15-16 when it was raised from $350 to 
$425.  The proposed $50 increase reflects the increased costs we are experiencing when hiring contractors to plant street 
trees.  
 Park and Landscape Resources (Page 3) Staff has made revisions to the fee schedule to reflect changes in the Park in-
Lieu/Park Impact Fees. These fees are adjusted based on a calculation that includes an annual determination of the 
median land value. The median land value decreased this year by 3% and thus the fees dependent on it also decreased. 
Park in-lieu/Park Impact Fees are administered per the City’s Municipal Code, Chapter 26.64 Dedication of Land for 
Community Purposes (Attachment 5). This code outlines how City staff are to calculate the fees each year by discussing 
various elements of the fee calculation. Some important elements of the code include: 
•
“In order properly to serve the subdivision, two acres are needed for each 1,000-estimated population as 
determined by applying factors per dwelling structure types as outlined in the City’s fee schedule in effect at the time the 
fees are to be paid.”
•
“The fair market value of land in the City, used to calculate each subdivider’s in-lieu fee and determined by the 
Parks and Recreation Director or designee, will be included in the City’s fee schedule adopted by City Council resolution.”

Using these pieces of the Municipal Code, City staff develops a fair market value of land in the City and multiplies it by the 
two acres per 1,000 estimated population and the most updated population factors per dwelling unit type. Fee = 
population factor per dwelling unit type X 2 acres/1000 population X median land value The calculation for the FY 2020-21 
Park in-Lieu/Impact Fee is detailed in Attachment 2. Both the median home value and the population factor per dwelling 
unit have been updated and this is reflected in the new fees.
In the past Commissioners have requested staff to study more broad policy changes to the current In Lieu Fee ordinance.  
Last year, the City Council directed staff to undertake a comprehensive review of all developer impact fees, including Park 
In Lieu.  This current effort is being led by the Community Development Department with input from the various 
departments.  In light of this on-going effort,  staff is not proposing any substantive changes other than those resulting 
from changes in land value or population factor. 
Last, staff is recommending a modest $1.00 per round increase in golf green fees.  This represents the first rate increase 
since FY 10-11.  Many factors led to staff not recommending green fee changes over the years, including decrease in play, 
condition of the course, etc.  The most recent green fee study from surrounding courses suggests that a modest $1.00 per 
round increase would still keep Poplar Creek at comparable market rates.  
BUDGET IMPACT:
Most recommended changes would not have a significant impact on the Department’s budgeted revenue outside of COLA. 
For fees related to Park in Lieu or Park Impact, the impact is always unknown since revenue depends on economic factors 
enabling a project to proceed and is out of our control.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This agenda item is not a project subject to CEQA, because it can be seen with certainty that it will not cause a physical 
change in the environment. (Public Resources Code Section 21065.)
