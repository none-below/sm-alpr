MINUTES – November 20, 2019 
The November 20, 2019 Regular Meeting of the Community Relations Commission (CRC) was called to order at 
City Hall, Conference Room C, at 7:05pm. 
 
ROLL CALL 
Commissioners Fernandez, Hsiung, Mahesri, Rivera, and Torrijos were present.  
 
CITY STAFF PRESENT 
Sandy Council, Housing Manager and Brian Alexander, Management Fellow were present.   
 
CONSENT CALENDAR / APPROVAL OF MINUTES 
The minutes of the May 15, 2019 meeting were approved. (Fernandez/Torrijos; 3-0, Hsiung, Mahesri abstained) 
The minutes of the August 21, 2019 meeting were approved (Torrijos/Hsiung; 3-0, Fernandez, Mahesri abstain) 
 
PUBLIC COMMENT 
None 
 
NEW BUSINESS 
1. Community Needs Assessment for 2020-21 Annual Plan 
After a brief presentation from staff, members of the community and representatives from non-profit organizations 
provided comment on needs to be prioritized in the coming program year. Staff summarized comments received 
before the meeting as well. All comments summarized below: 
• 
Via email and in-person, the Central Neighborhood Association commented that CDBG funds could be 
used for capital improvements, traffic mitigation, and community outreach. More specifically: 
o The 3rd Avenue overpass needs improved pedestrian lighting, especially given the SamTrans FCX 
shuttle stop.  
o There are empty planting strips in the public right of way that could be improved and trip hazards 
on sidewalks caused by the roots of street trees.   
o Signage should be added to prohibit right turns on red at the 4th and Fremont intersection to allow 
residents to safely back out of driveways. 
o The intersection of 4th and Grant would benefit from a flashing yield light at the pedestrian 
crossing. 
o A neighborhood clean up day with City Staff to clean up trash and debris from the streets and 
planting strips on 3rd, 4th, and 5th Avenues between Railroad Avenue and South Amphlett. 
• 
HIP Housing thanked the City for support of their programming and provided a number of statistics. They 
provided information on the most significant challenges their clients are facing and an update on the 
number of homeshares in San Mateo.  
• 
The Legal Aid Society of San Mateo County spoke about the rise in no-fault evictions as landlords respond 
to the passage of AB1482. They also thanked the City for the passage of the emergency ordinance on 11/4 
which they believed to be a big help to their clients who were previously unprotected. Legal Aid also 
relayed client statistics.  
• 
Representatives from the Intercommunal Pre-School on 2nd Avenue spoke about their program and 
thanked the City for past support. The program is licensed for 29 children, primarily from the North 
Central neighborhood, and needs lighting and structural improvements. 
• 
A community member spoke about her increasing rent and expressed concern about a lack of coordinated 
resources for those struggling to afford housing in San Mateo. 
• 
Rebuilding Together Peninsula thanked the City for their support and provided an update on their 
programs for low-income homeowners and community facilities. They relayed the importance of serving 
seniors and those living with a disability. 
• 
The Renaissance Entrepreneurship Center gave an update on their program and expressed concerns about 
the lack of affordable commercial space for small businesses in San Mateo. The Renaissance 
Entrepreneurship Center would like to bring an incubator to San Mateo and sees affordable commercial 
space as a necessity to make that work.

SUBJECT:
2020 COMMUNITY FUNDING RECOMMENDATIONS
RECOMMENDATION:
That the Community Relations Commission provide recommendations for organizations to receive Community 
Development Block Grant funding for the 2020-2021 and 2021-2022 Program Years.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City of San Mateo provides Community Funding grants for non-profit organizations that provide community 
services that serve low and moderate income residents of San Mateo using federal Community Block Grant Funds (CDBG). 
The federal regulations cap the amount of CDBG funds that can be used for this type of activity to 15% of the program 
funds.  The CDBG public service budget is estimated to be $117,000 for 2020-21.
The CDBG jurisdictions within San Mateo County have worked together to provide a consolidated application process 
online through City Data Services (CDS), so agencies may apply to several jurisdictions at the same time. Each jurisdiction 
sets it own criteria for selection. Those programs selected are awarded 2-year grants for their programs. The second year 
of funding is contingent upon meeting all contractual obligations.  
On November 20, 2019 The Community Relations Commission (CRC) held a community meeting to identify the Housing, 
Community Development and Public Service needs of San Mateo. At the meeting the CRC established the following 
priorities for the Community Funding program:

Basic Human Needs

Youth Services

Senior Services

Preserving or Providing Affordable Housing
PROPOSAL REVIEW
City staff reviewed the applications for CDBG eligibility.  The CRC members reviewed and gave each application a 
preliminary score.  These scores are not binding but provide the CRC background information and a starting point for 
further discussion.    Most organizations met the requirements and some notes are provided in the summary worksheet. 
The City received a total of 14 proposals for public services, one for economic development/microenterprise, four for 
minor home repair programs, and one for fair housing services. One program, Youth for Change was determined ineligible 
for CDBG funds. 
Elements of each proposal are consolidated on the Proposal Evaluation Summary 2020-21 (Attachment A) as well as 
indicators of past and present goal performance.

The final recommendations will be a result of the Commission’s consideration of the quality of the proposal and how well 
it addresses the needs of the community, individual commissioner perspectives based on their community knowledge 
and experience, responses to any clarification questions to applicant agencies or public testimony, and the organizational 
experience and capacity of the applicant agency. These recommendations will be incorporated into San Mateo’s Annual 
Action Plan that will be approved by the City Council.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The final CDBG entitlement to the City for the 2020-21 program year has not yet been confirmed by HUD. Currently staff is 
estimating $117,000 for this funding cycle.  Future budget changes may impact the funding available for both program 
years and contracts will be amended in consideration of funding available. Staff generally recommends that awards be no 
less than $15,000 per agency.
With those considerations, the options for CDBG grant awards for public service applications for 2020-2021 are:

7 service grants at $16,700 each, or

8 service grants at $14,600 each
All grants will be subject to contract execution in compliance with HUD regulations and upon final Council authorization 
of the Business Plan for the City of San Mateo.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This community funding program is not a project subject to CEQA, because it can be seen with certainty that it will not 
cause a physical change in the environment. (Public Resources Code Section 21065.)
