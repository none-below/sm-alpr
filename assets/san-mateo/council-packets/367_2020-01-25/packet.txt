RECOMMENDATION:
Review the current Council Strategic Plan and identify new Council objectives for consideration for fiscal year 2020-21.
BACKGROUND:
The City Council goal setting process includes a three-step deliberation process resulting in the adoption of a Strategic Plan 
that allows for multi-year resource planning. The January 25, 2020 meeting represents the first step in the process, a City 
Council workshop to determine strategic initiatives and objectives for consideration for the upcoming fiscal year. The 
meeting will include public comment period at the start of the meeting. The meeting will then be a facilitated discussion 
among the Council with the goal of revising and updating the Strategic Plan developed last fiscal year. 
Staff has provided updates on the implementation status of the Council objectives from fiscal year 2019-20 (Attachment 
1). The highlighted items represent Council objectives that staff plans to have completed prior to the end of the fiscal year 
or items that staff will not address this fiscal year that the Council may want to consider whether or not to remain on the 
list. In addition, staff has provided a list of Council-directed objectives that were added after the adoption of the fiscal year 
2019-20 Strategic Plan (Attachment 2). The City Council will review both lists and decide which items to retain and/or 
adjust and which ones can be removed.
As part of the consideration process for new priorities to include in the Strategic Plan, staff is providing a list of major 
department initiatives identified for fiscal year 2020-21 (Attachment 3). This list highlights the significant new work plan 
items that each department plans to address next year that Council may not otherwise be aware. Members of the City 
Council provided their lists of potential new objectives for consideration to assist with the prioritization process 
(Attachment 4). The list also includes new priorities that staff recommends that Council consider adding to the Strategic 
Plan. The Council will be asked to review this list and identify which items to include in the Strategic Plan and what priority 
to assign them.
The second step in the goal setting process, scheduled for March 2, 2020, will be a study session where staff will propose a 
work plan to address each of the objectives in the Strategic Plan, provide feedback on resource requirements, and suggest 
modifications to the proposed objectives. The final step in the process, which will occur at the March 16, 2020 City Council 
meeting, will be a consent calendar item where City Council will approve the Strategic Plan with the proposed objectives 
for the 2020-21 fiscal year. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact from this agenda item. The objectives identified by the Council will be taken into consideration 
as part of the development of the fiscal year 2020-21 budget.

The City of San Mateo has a significant number of important priorities that are carried over from 2019 and 2018.

completion. These top priorities include:
*
1.
2030 General Plan: Successfully completing our General Plan with broad community input.
*
2.
Traffic Mitigation: Continuing local traffic mitigation efforts to ease worsening traffic congestion.
*
3.
Affordable Housing: Continuing efforts to encourage and incentivize the construction of more 
affordable housing units. 
*
4.
Pension Liability Management: Continuing to explore options to pay down our unfunded pension 
liability and prioritize new expense options that do not expand our liability.
5.
Prioritization of Existing Infrastructure Plans: Prioritizing our existing infrastructure plans (Central Park 
Master Plan, Recreation Facilities, Library Facilities, Bicycle Master Plan, Corp Yard, etc.), identifying funding 
options for each, and beginning the process of raising funds for top priorities.
*
6.
New Water Treatment Plant Construction: Ensuring the successful completion of our $1 Billion Clean 
Water Program.

within our downtown and design and implement a pilot program.

2.

cigarettes/vaping devices within city limits.

Dear City Council Members, 
 
During your workshop on Saturday, January 25 2020, please consider these two points: 
 
1. Quality of Life | Neighborhoods | Traffic 
 
Everything related to traffic congestion, management, and mitigation can be improved by prioritizing

2. Framing the Future | Social Leadership 
 
The Police Department and Public Works Department are acquiring surveillance technologies in an ad 
hoc fashion without much public discussion about the privacy ramifications. These technologies are 
currently limited to the PD’s mobile automated license plate readers and body worn cameras. However, 
the Council recently approved a new parking enforcement contract that includes more ALPRs and the PD 
intends to install fixed ALPRs this year without further Council review. The PD is also starting to use 
doorbell and other video from citizens for criminal investigations. 
 
These surveillance technologies are clearly useful for law enforcement. They also have the potential to 
be very invasive by constantly tracking large numbers of law-abiding citizens. In the future, ALPRs, 
BWCs, and doorbell video may be easily augmented with facial recognition systems making them even
