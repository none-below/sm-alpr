BACKGROUND:
Project Description
The pre-application has been submitted to redevelop a currently developed 30,994 square-foot lot with a new mixed-use 
development (total 56,200 square feet) with 48 residential rental units (53,535 square feet) and a commercial office 
space (2,665 square feet). The residential component consists of a combination of 1 bedroom / 1 bath and 2 bedroom / 2 
bath living units with private open space balconies, organized as four stories of stacked flats over one level of 
subterranean parking, with each floor served by an elevator. The commercial office component occurs as a portion of the 
ground level located adjacent to O'Farrell Street, with three stories of residential flats above it. Amenities include a 
ground level fitness room, mail room, bicycle parking and a large south-facing outdoor court. 
The project proposes a total number of 71 residential parking spaces and 8 commercial parking spaces.  The project also 
includes long-term bicycle storage for 53 bicycles.  Bicycle parking consists of 5 short-term spaces and 53 long-term 
spaces for the residential units and 1 short-term and 1 long-term space for the commercial space.
The proposed architectural style is modern and features solid building elements broken up with various vertical and 
rectilinear forms and flat planes that are carried throughout the building except for the commercial component on the 
ground level which is curvilinear in shape. The windows are recessed primarily due to the prominent vertical columns and 
arranged in rectangular sets which are present on all the building elevations.
Seven heritage Redwood trees exist on the project site along the side (west) property line. The applicant proposes to 
remove all of these heritage trees as part of the project’s scope of work.  Project Plans are included as Attachment 1 and 
the applicant’s Project Description Letter as Attachment 2.

Site Description
The project site is comprised of one parcel, totaling 30,994 square feet located at the northeast end of the cul-de-sac 
along O’Farrell Street. O-Farrell Street is surrounded by the City of San Mateo City Hall on the southwest, a four-story 
office structure (Diadodi Building) with a two-story parking structure serving City Hall on the west, a one-story office 
building over underground parking on the east (just south of the subject parcel), as well as a multifamily residential 
complex over below grade parking on the east. In addition, adjacent immediately to the east of the subject site is a 
recently constructed multi-family residential project over underground parking. Presently, the subject site includes a one-
story office building with at grade parking.  
Several easements exist along the western portion of the property including: 1) an existing 14- foot wide non-exclusive 
ingress and egress easement; 2) 4-foot by 4-foot public utilities easement adjacent to the easement #1; 3) a 5-foot wide 
public utilities easement within the subject project and adjacent to the cul-de-sac bulb; 4) a road easement within the 
bulb of the cul-de-sac along O’Farrell Street;  5) a 5-foot wide public utilities easement which runs along the eastern side 
of the property; and 6) a 10- foot wide public utilities easement to the north of the property.  Three of the existing 
easements, #1, 3, and 6 noted above would be vacated with the proposed development. All of rest of the easements are 
remaining, #2, 4, and 5, and are not in conflict with the proposed development.
The applicant is proposing to remove all of the existing seven Redwood trees on-site, heritage trees, located on the west 
side of the property.   
The project site is zoned E1-1/R4 (Executive Park/High Density Multi-Family Overlay) and the General Plan land use 
designation is Executive Office/High Density Multi-Family. (Refer to Attachment 3, Location Map.)
Applicable Code and Policy Review 
The City’s General Plan includes specific policies for the Twentieth Avenue Vicinity in the Beresford Park planning area 
pertaining to the site.  Proposed development is reviewed for conformance with applicable policies and guidelines set 
forth in the General Plan, municipal code, and other applicable regulations.
A Factual Data Sheet has been prepared that evaluates the project for consistency with the specific zoning regulations for 
the project (see Attachment 4). Specific findings for code and policy compliance are included in Attachment 5, Findings 
for Approval. In order for the City to approve the project, specific Findings for Approval must be met. 
General Plan
The General Plan Land Use Diagram designates the project site as Executive Office/High Density Multi-Family.  A 
preliminary list of applicable General Plan policies is provided as Attachment 6, these include PA 8.2 - Twentieth Avenue 
Vicinity, pertaining to encouraging office uses with high density residential, though allow commercial expansion limited to 
minimizing neighborhood impacts as a mix of multi-family and low-scale commercial. This policy also indicates high-
density residential development with heights up to four stories is allowed (residential permitted by itself).   The proposed 
project is consistent with these land use designations.  This area is not designated as a large scale commercial node due 
to its proximity to residential neighborhoods and limited peak period roadway capacity. 
Additionally, Policy 2.9 from the City’s adopted Housing Element states, “Provide for the development of multi-family 
housing to create a diversity of available housing types.”  This project would provide rental housing units at market rate as 
well as below market rate units. 
Protection of heritage trees and preservation of existing stands of trees or groves of trees in the design of new projects 
and planting of additional trees are key policies in the City’s Conservation and Open Space Element of the General Plan. 
The applicant is proposing to remove seven existing Redwood trees, protected heritage trees, located on the west side of 
the property, and four Oak trees, protected heritage trees, of the 23 on-site trees, and enhance the development with 
new landscaping and additional trees.

Zoning Code
The project proposes to redevelop a site located in the E1-1/R4 (Executive Park/Residential Overlay District) zoning 
district with a multi-family residential and commercial office development which requires it to comply with the 
development standards of the R4 zoning district.  The building can be constructed to a maximum of 45 feet (measured 
from finished grade to highest plate line) with a maximum permitted floor area ratio of 2.0 (or 61,987 square feet).  
Setbacks are required to provide a minimum of half the building height or 15 feet, whichever is greater, along the street 
frontage, side yards and rear yards. In this case, 22 feet as the building height is 44 feet and 4 inches. The E1 zoning 
district requires a minimum of 35 percent at-grade open space be provided. This includes decorative outdoor landscape 
elements, water features, paved or decorated surfaces of rock, stone brick, or other similar material (excluding 
driveways, parking, loading, or storage areas), and sculptural elements.
State Density Bonus Law
California Government Code Section 6915 outlines density bonus standards for residential projects that include certain 
percentages of below-market-rate housing units. As presently designed, the applicant proposes to dedicate 10% of the 
base 36 residential units to the very-low-income category, which would comply with the City’s affordable housing 
requirements and would make the project eligible for a density bonus request, reduced parking standards, incentives or 
concessions, and waivers. 
As such, the applicant proposes to exceed the maximum base of 36 dwelling units by providing twelve additional units for 
a total of 48 dwelling units, which is within the maximum density of 50 DU/ac including the 32.5% state density bonus. At 
this time, the applicant is requesting one concession and no waivers.

Housing Accountability Act
At least two-thirds of the square footage of this project is dedicated to residential use. Therefore, this project is subject to 
the state Housing Accountability Act (HAA), which became law in 2017 and is codified at Government Code Section 
65589.5. The HAA was intended by the State Legislature to curb the capability of cities to deny, reduce the density of, or 
render infeasible housing development projects. For all housing projects, whether affordable or not, the key provision 
requires that if a housing project complies with all “objective” general plan, zoning, and subdivision standards, the 
decision-making body may only deny or reduce the density of a housing development project that is consistent with the 
City’s objective development standards if it makes certain findings. Please see Attachment 5 for more information 
regarding the required findings.
Bicycle Master Plan
The City’s Bicycle Master Plan identifies existing and proposed bike lanes within the City.  Per the Bicycle Master Plan, the 
nearest existing bikeway is along Alameda De Las Pulgas, a Class III signed bicycle route.  The Bicycle Master Plan 
Identified West 20th Avenue as a community suggested ‘bikeway’ facility for a future improvement which would be just a 
block from the subject site. 
Pedestrian Master Plan
The project will be evaluated for consistency with the City’s Pedestrian Master Plan which identifies design guidelines for 
sidewalk standards to enhance the safety, convenience, and mobility of pedestrians within the City. 
For the O’Farrell Street frontage, the applicable sidewalk standard (A.10 – Sidewalk Standards – Mixed Use Type B Street 
Parking) requires a 4-6 foot furniture/planter zone and 8-10 foot through zone (i.e. accessible, unblocked right-of-way for 
pedestrian walkability) for the frontages along O’Farrell Street. The applicant will need to demonstrate compliance with 
the through zone width and frontage requirements of the Pedestrian Master Plan upon submittal of the formal 
application. The plans appear to meet these guidelines. The City’s Pedestrian Master Plan, A.10 Sidewalk Standard – 
Mixed Use Type B Street Parking is included in Attachment 8.
Items for Discussion
Design Review – Site Design and Building Design
The proposed preliminary design has been reviewed by the City’s design review consultant, Larry Cannon, AIA, of Cannon 
Design Group, for consistency with the General Plan’s Urban Design Element.  The design review analysis is included as 
Attachment 9.  The Design Review Consultant has indicated that it is a difficult site to develop efficiently. It is a relatively 
small site which is narrow and deep, and a portion of the site is encroached upon by the pavement bulb of the cul-de-sac. 
The site’s required property line setbacks further limits the building’s footprint. The goal of retaining the existing 
Redwood trees on the northwest edge of the property is also a constraint. The following are listed as important issues to 
consider when redeveloping this site:
        1.    The setbacks provided from three of the site property lines are less than required.
        2.    The existing Redwood trees would not be saved and incorporated into the project.
        3.    The three on-street parking spaces interrupt the sense of entry to the building and make pedestrian access 
difficult.
        4.    The entry lobby is not enclosed which seems undesirable for a multifamily development of this quality.
        5.    The residential elevator is not well located relative to the building entry as it is far away from the street entry, 
units on the ground floor would experience hallway noise from upper floor tenants arriving and departing, and

might be at the other end of the structure. Also, the bike storage rooms on the upper floors are located near the 
westerly stair, but a long corridor away from the elevator.
        6.    The stair circulation near the residential lobby is awkward as the access at the entry involves coming into the

        7.    The outlook from units adjacent to the elevator would not be pleasant with views across a narrow space to a 
blank wall.
        8.     The facades visible from Highway 92 are “boxy” with no variation in height, the buffer landscaping with the

Heritage Tree Removal
As indicated previously, protection of heritage trees and preservation of existing stands of trees or groves of trees in the 
design of new projects and planting of additional trees are key policies in the City’s Conservation and Open Space Element 
of the General Plan. The applicant is proposing to remove protected heritage trees including seven existing Redwood 
trees located on the west side of the site, and four existing Oak trees situated on the eastside of the property. 
The applicant should consider a site design which accommodates and preserves the Redwood trees and Oak trees in a 
manner which would allow them to thrive and benefit this development.  
Office Component
The office component of the project is not necessary to satisfy zoning development standards as the zoning district 
permits multi-family residential development by itself. It does not need to be a mixed-use project. The office element 
crowds the proposed development.  Elimination of the office component, which would require less parking, would enable 
a design which could preserve the Redwood trees to the west, provide a softened landscaped site entrance and an 
enhanced design which can accommodate all the features of the development in a more welcoming, and aesthetically 
pleasing manner to better fit this rather small, narrow and deep site.

Housing Accountability Act
At least two-thirds of the square footage of this project is dedicated to residential use. Therefore, this project is subject to 
the state Housing Accountability Act (HAA), which became law in 2017 and is codified at Government Code Section 
65589.5. The HAA was intended by the State Legislature to curb the capability of cities to deny, reduce the density of, or 
render infeasible housing development projects. For all housing projects, whether affordable or not, the key provision 
requires that if a housing project complies with all “objective” general plan, zoning, and subdivision standards, the 
decision-making body may only deny or reduce the density of a housing development project that is consistent with the 
City’s objective development standards if it makes certain findings. Please see Attachment 5 for more information 
regarding the required findings.

Heritage Tree Removal
As indicated previously, protection of heritage trees and preservation of existing stands of trees or groves of trees in the 
design of new projects and planting of additional trees are key policies in the City’s Conservation and Open Space Element 
of the General Plan. The applicant is proposing to remove protected heritage trees including seven existing Redwood 
trees located on the west side of the site, and four existing Oak trees situated on the eastside of the property. 
The applicant should consider a site design which accommodates and preserves the Redwood trees and Oak trees in a 
manner which would allow them to thrive and benefit this development.  
Office Component
The office component of the project is not necessary to satisfy zoning development standards as the zoning district 
permits multi-family residential development by itself. It does not need to be a mixed-use project. The office element 
crowds the proposed development.  Elimination of the office component, which would require less parking, would enable 
a design which could preserve the Redwood trees to the west, provide a softened landscaped site entrance and an 
enhanced design which can accommodate all the features of the development in a more welcoming, and aesthetically 
pleasing manner to better fit this rather small, narrow and deep site.

WE ARE PROPOSING TO BUILD A FOUR-STORY MIXED-USED BUILDING AT 2850 SOUTH EL CAMINO REAL. 
CURRENTLY THE SITE IS COVERED BY A 13,000 SQ. FEET ONE-STORY BUILDING. 
THE PROPOSED BUILDING WILL HAVE AN UNDERGROUND 51 CAR GARAGE, 7,500 SQUARE FEET OF RETAIL AT 
GROUND FLOOR AND 18 TWO-BEDROOM RENTAL APARTMENT UNITS ON SECOND THROUGH FOURTH FLOORS. 
THERE WILL ALSO BE AN OFFICE ON THE SECOND FLOOR ADJACENT TO THE SECOND-FLOOR APARTMENT 
UNITS. 
THE APPROXIMATELY 27,850 SQUARE FEET BUILDING SITE IS IN CENTRAL SAN MATEO WITHIN A SHORT 
WALKING DISTANCE FROM THE RECENTLY DEVELOPED HILLSDALE SHOPPING CENTER AS WELL AS HILLSDALE 
CALTRAIN STATION.  BY REMOVING ALL EXISTING SURFACE PARKING AND PLACING THEM UNDERGROUND, WE 
WILL CREATE A PEDESTRIAN FRIENDLY PATHWAY BETWEEN EDISON STREET TO THE WEST AND EL CAMINO 
REAL. 
OF THE 18 APARTMENT UNITS IN THE PROPOSED PROJECT, 3 APARTMENT UNITS WILL BE DESIGNATED 
AFFORDABLE FOR LOW-INCOME RESIDENTS.  IN ACCORDANCE WITH SAN MATEO CITY CHARTER AND 
MUNICIPAL CODE, SECTION 27.24.040 (A), THE ALLOWABLE UNIT COUNT IS 13.  THROUGH DESIGNATING 20% OF 
THE ALLOWABLE TOTAL, 3 OUT OF THE 13 UNITS AS AFFORDABLE FOR LOW-INCOME RESIDENTS, THIS 
PROJECT QUALIFIES FOR A DENSITY BONUS OF 35%, INCREASING THE NUMBER OF UNITS FROM 13 TO 18 
UNITS.

PLANTING DESIGN & WATER USE
INTENT STATEMENT
  The planting design aimed to be both people and
environment friendly. All the plants selected are
low-water-use plants according to WUCOLS
rating. The plants selection was based on their
climate adaptability, seasonal aesthetic
characteristics and pollinator benefits. Many of
them are California natives which require little
water after established and provide beneficial
habitats  for wildlife. Throughout the planting
area, we incorporate Sedum spathulifolium - a
plant essential for the survival of the
endangered butterfly native to the San Mateo
area, the San Bruno Elfin.
  No plants are used that are considered as
invasive in the regions listed by the CAL-IPC.
  The irrigation system will be automatic, low gallon
use drip system. All new trees will be irrigated
with bubblers on a separate circuit,
  The remote control valves will have integral
pressure regulators to prevent fluctuations and
ensure constant application rates to minimize
over or under watering. The electronic irrigation
controller will have season adjustments, multiple
programs and application cycles / start times. A
weather sensor will be installed to prevent
irrigation during rain periods. A flow sensor and
master valve will be connected to the controller
to allow automatic shut off of any valve circuit
or main line in the event of a pipe brake to
prevent water waste.

Presentation: 
Yen Chen from YC+D Architecture described the project.  The proposed design is a new 4-story 
mixed-use building with underground garage, retail at level 1, office space to be used as leasing 
office at level 2, and (18) 2-bedroom apartment units at levels 2 through 4.   
The project site is located on El Camino Real within close walking distance from Hillsdale Caltrain 
Station and Hillsdale Shopping Center.  Currently, the project site is mostly surface parking and 
has an existing older building that will be demolished.  The lot to the north of site is Macy’s 
Furniture store and to the south is occupied by Bank of America.  To the west of site along 
Edison Avenue is a residential neighborhood.  The existing driveway off El Camino Real will be 
eliminated as part of the design.  
126 of 131

--- page 127 ---
The project site is long and narrow (nearly 400-ft long by less than 70-ft wide) and therefore the 
design proposes a long and narrow building placed along the southern portion of the property.  
This allows the design to provide a landscaped walk path along the northern edge of the site 
that connects Edison Avenue to El Camino Real.  The landscaped path has planting, lighting and 
seating features accessible to not only the public but also serve as outdoor amenity to the 
tenants and residents of the development. The landscape design for the site will take advantage 
of a variety of local plants with color and texture.  Short term bicycle parking is located at the 
northeast corner of the site. Covered long term bicycle storage is provided at the western end of 
the building, south of the walk path.  
The project proposes 2 driveways along Edison Avenue, one to provide ramp access to 
underground parking, the other will accommodate loading, emergency vehicle access and waste 
collection.    
The building is setback from the street and steps back on both El Camino and Edison sides to 
break down the scale of the building on two ends.  Units with south facing balconies are 
staggered between levels, which further contribute to achieving façade articulation to break up 
the long building mass. 
Leasing office on the 2nd floor and residential units on levels 2 through 4 are connected to two 
sets of exterior stairways and elevators through an exterior walkway along the north side of the 
building. Screens along the walkway provides privacy to apartment entrances as well as 
additional façade articulation. 
The project uses a simple palette of materials in stucco plaster, metal, and glass. Wood is used 
as an accent material to add warmth and contrast to the overall building design.   
 
Comments, Questions, and Answer Session: 
Q. Is the project considering solar panels on the roof? 
YCD A. We are allocating roof area for solar panels to comply with city’s “Mandatory Green Building 
Measures”.  
Q. The project proposes 18 units; why can’t the proposed building go above 4 stories?  
YCD A. The allowable height in our zone is between 40 and 55 feet; the proposed building height is 48 
feet which encompasses 3 levels of residential over a concrete podium at grade to be used as retail. 
Q. Will the project consider straightening the jog in Edison sidewalk? 
YCD A. The ramp access down to underground garage cannot be shortened or shifted eastward due to 
various site constraints without compromising the number of parking stalls provided.  Due to the height 
difference between El Camino Real and Edison, the ramp slope is currently at 18%, which is the 
maximum allowable slope.  Also, the narrow width of the site makes it impossible to provide a 2nd level 
of underground parking.  Other constraints include existence utility vault within sidewalk.  
Q. Why is the proposed building sited towards south?

storefronts along the length of the site. There is also a 6-ft wide AT&T telecommunication easement
