SUBJECT:
2020-21 Budget Development – Preliminary Review
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and provide direction on the informational report for the development of the fiscal year 2020-21 budget.
BACKGROUND:
On January 21, 2020, staff provided the City Council with an update on the City’s financial status. Key components included 
a General Fund update for the current year, which showed revenues flat to original estimates, and expenditures that were 
about $3 million over the adopted budget, primarily due to adjustments approved for Fire Station 25 and the Police 
substation and sleeping quarters. The City Council provided direction to move forward with funding discretionary 
expenditures that totaled $5.4 million, including $2 million in support for capital improvements, $2.4 million additional 
discretionary pension payment, and $1 million housing set-aside. A comprehensive pension update was also provided, 
which showed the City’s required contributions increasing over the next ten years. 
On March 16, 2020, health officers from San Mateo County and five other counties in the Bay Area issued a shelter-in-
place order in response to the coronavirus pandemic. From a financial perspective, the shelter-in-place order halted major 
sources of General Fund revenues in unprecedented speed, including sales tax, hotel tax, property transfer tax, and golf 
and recreational fees. The uncertainty from the pandemic also caused huge volatility in the financial markets, and CalPERS’ 
investment returns as of the end of March was -4%. Returns at the end of June will likely fall short of the 7% expected rate 
of return and likely create an additional unfunded pension liability for the City. 
Actual performance for the current year is being closely monitored, and thus far, revenues are projected to fall short of the 
original estimates by approximately $10 million. As conditions change rapidly, the City is also responding quickly. For the 
current year, short-term recommendations to adjust expenditures without causing a major impact to service levels include 
freezing vacant positions and suspending the additional discretionary pension payment.  Staff is also recommending 
suspending the discretionary housing set-aside in the current fiscal year as well.
These strategies carry into the 2020-21 budget and corresponding long-term plan. Even so, revenues continue to fall short, 
and a structural imbalance remains. To address this, a combination of net reductions and strategic draws on the 
unassigned fund balance is incorporated into the 2020-21 budget and long-term plan. In the meantime, a Fiscal 
Sustainability Plan is being developed, which will include options for operational adjustments that will close the structural 
budget deficit, and will be brought forward for Council discussion and evaluation at a future date. 
Another recommended adjustment in response to the changing conditions is to pivot away from the current two-year 
budget cycle into an annual budget cycle. In the current two-year cycle, which has been in place since 1986, the focus in 
budget development shifts each year between the operating budget and the capital budget. With current conditions that

delivering the budget to the City Council in May, with public hearing and budget adoption in June.

summary of proposed appropriations for 2020-21, as well as comprehensive financial information at the fund level and the 
department level.
