3.
City-Owned Downtown Affordable Housing and Parking Garage Sites (PA-2019-033)
Phillip Brennan, Associate Planner, Nevada Merriman, MidPen Housing, and David Israel, BAR Architects, provided 
a presentation on technical information and the redesign of the seven-story multi-family residential building and 
parking garage proposed for 480 E. 4th Avenue and 400 E. 5th Avenue.
The Commission asked questions of staff and the applicant regarding AB-1763; clarification of shadow studies; 
impact on neighboring structures; resident versus public parking in the parking garage; parking survey; parking 
agreement; percentage of units prioritized for public employees; accommodation for residents with intellectual 
disabilities; seven and eight floor building impacts and worker’s resource center.
Public Comment via telephone: Diane Pettit; Kalisha Webster; Jordan Grimes, Peninsula for Everyone; Alex 
Melendrez, Housing Leadership Council; and Drew. 23 comments were read into the record from Laurie 
Watanuki; Daniel Schonberg; Jonathan New; Evelina Chang; Joseph Devoto; Evan Adams; Elizabeth Hall; Daryl 
Rose; Claudia; Chet Lexvold; Kelsey Banes; Adam Nugent; Jeremy Smith; Daniel Tam; Carol Steinfeld; Joshua Hugg; 
Vilma Olivera; Adam Loraine; Robert Fruchtman; Isaac Adam Gendler; John Lovell; Valerie Rynne; and Archan 
Luhar.
The Commission expressed appreciation for the community participation and noted that the feedback is received 
and is being heard. Staff and the applicant responded to additional questions. Commission discussion and 
deliberation ensued. Generally, there was a positive response to the redesign and the new color palette. Interest 
was expressed in incorporating childcare on-site, as well as making the learning center and community room open 
to the public. Appreciation was expressed of the well thought out details of redesign, solar panels, roof top deck 
and courtyard. The Commission expressed concern regarding the potential impacts of shadows casting down from 
the tall residential building on neighboring properties and it was suggested the project design team look for ways 
to reduce building height including using eight-foot ceiling heights.
The Commission provided additional comments including that the South Railroad elevation needs more 
articulation and visual interest. A comment was made that the industrial appearance and rectilinear lines of the 
design do not meet the needs for a transitional architectural style between a traditional neighborhood and the 
historic downtown. Concern was expressed to ensure that adequate parking for residents as well as guest parking 
for downtown businesses be addressed. Positive response to the pedestrian bridge becoming a signature feature 
downtown. There was a preference expressed for a green palette alternative and a redesign to include more 
outdoor space with more balconies. Overall support was expressed for the 100% affordable housing project and 
appreciation of the work from City staff and MidPen.

SUBJECT:
Design Review for Concar Passage Mixed Use Project (PA-2018-052)
RECOMMENDATION:
That the Planning Commission review the proposed design, receive public comments, and provide input to the applicant 
and staff.
BACKGROUND:
California Coastal Properties submitted a formal Planning Application on September 20, 2018 to redevelop the Concar 
Shopping Center with a mixed-use development. The project site is zoned TOD (Transit Oriented Development) and is located 
within the Hayward Park Station Overlay Zone of the Rail Corridor Transit Oriented Development Plan.  Project entitlements 
include: Site Plan and Architectural Review; Site Development Planning Application; Vesting Tentative Map; and a 
Development Agreement.
The Planning Commission, at its March 27, 2018 meeting, reviewed the project on a preliminary basis, received public 
comments and provided input to the applicant and staff.  Since that meeting, the applicant has worked on several design 
iterations in response to comments from the Commission, public, staff and the City’s peer review architect. The purpose of 
this Study Session is to receive public comments on the revised design and have the Planning Commission review the design 
and consider the adequacy of the revisions to the design.
Project Site and Description
The project site is located on Concar Drive, between South Grant Street and South Delaware Street.  The site is approximately 
14.5 acres and currently occupied by Concar Shopping Center, which includes Trader Joe’s, Peninsula Ballet Theatre, Ross 
Dress for Less, The Pantry, Rite-Aid, T.J. Maxx, Shane Co., and 7-Eleven. The project proposes to demolish the existing 
commercial buildings and construct 961 multi-family dwelling units and approximately 40,000 square feet of 
commercial/retail space. The project includes 73 low income housing units, 38 moderate income housing units, associated 
parking and 3 acres of community open space. Commercial uses include Trader Joe’s, Peninsula Ballet Theatre, 7-Eleven and 
a new SEED food hall, with other retail space and a daycare facility for approximately 70 children. The current project plans 
containing the architectural and landscape drawings are included as Attachment 1.
The site is comprised of eight parcels (APNs 035-243-090, -140, -160, -170, -190, -200, -210, -220), located at 640, 666, 678, 
690 Concar Drive, 1820, 1850 S. Grant Street and 1855 S. Delaware Street, San Mateo.
Design Review
As noted above, the Planning Commission held a meeting on March 27, 2018 to review the design of the proposed 
development and provide comments to the applicant and staff.  The following comments were received at that meeting:

The project architect, Pieter Berger, MVE Partners, prepared a memo (Attachment 2) dated May 19, 2020 outlining the 
project revisions made in response to the Planning Commission study session, summarized as follows:

Parking has been revised to be either at grade or below grade and entirely screened by buildings.

Trader Joe’s location includes immediately adjacent parking at an accessible corner of the site.

Depot Way has an increased width for better visibility and a fully signalized intersection is included at Depot Way 
and Concar with pedestrian crossing to the Concar Park. 

The central open space has been substantially increased with an active Arts Plaza and a passive Park Plaza.

Transit, shuttle, ride-share and deliveries are included along Depot Way, internal to the site.

The building massing has been revised, including dividing original building 4 at Concar and N. Grant into two buildings 
with separation and articulation. This has been done to reduce the visual appearance from surrounding streets

The building architecture has been revised to create more visual variety with a greater range of horizontal and 
vertical gestures along with more variety of color and materials.

Additional improvements include bike lanes, two protected bike intersections, and a new protected pedestrian 
intersection.
The project has undergone several rounds of staff review by development departments as well as several reviews by the 
City’s Design Review Consultant, Larry Cannon, Cannon Design Group.  Specifically, the applicant has revised the plans in 
response to five rounds of review comments from City departments and four rounds of review comments from Mr. Cannon.  
The letter dated March 25, 2020 from Cannon Design Group (Attachment 3) summarizes the design revisions completed 
since 2018.  Mr. Cannon notes that staff has worked closely with the applicant since the Planning Commission’s March 2018 
Study session to address the Commission’s concerns.  He states that the while the project has retained the essence of its 
original land use and site planning framework, the parking layout, open space details and architectural design have been 
greatly refined.  Mr. Cannon has no further recommendations for changes.
Staff recommends the Planning Commission review the revised design and consider if the design addresses changes as 
previously requested and if any further revisions are required prior to formal Planning Commission consideration.  
Next Steps
Following this Study Session, the applicant and project architect will have the opportunity to revise the design based on 
feedback given by the public and the Planning Commission. Staff anticipates that this project will be presented to the 
Planning Commission as a Public Hearing in the summer of this year.

Style Summary

To achieve variety and diversity in architecture, 5 distinct yet compatible architectural styles are
proposed. Each of them holds a unique identity, while coming together to embrace a public pas-
sage across the site in an east/west direction and celebrate the vitality of the neighborhood by
shaping a public plaza in the center of the site. A variety of massing compositions and facade artic-
ulations are introduced to enhance the pedestrian experience and bring in a dynamic building ap-
pearance. Visual interest is achieved through the use of secondary elements, enhanced materials
and consistent color palette, which cohesively tie together all the buildings.

Consultant Larry Cannon of Cannon Design Group:
Overall, the Planning Commissioners felt that the mix of housing was appropriate, and much needed in the city. The 
concerns and comments from one or more of the commissioners are summarized below:

The inward facing assembly of buildings seems to create a barrier to the surrounding community - doesn’t seem 
welcoming.

The open space is not well sized to be useful - especially when Depot Way is proposed to run through it.

A larger open space would be desirable.

Concern was expressed that the Trader Joe’s location was located at the worst place for it on the block, and that 
it would make traffic worse.

Parking appeared limited on Depot Way for delivery and service vehicles.

More below grade parking would be desirable.

Would like to see better developed open green spaces.

Internal open space should be visible to the surrounding community and should have things to activate the space 
and attract neighboring residents - central open space needs to provide significant amenities - for neighborhood, 
not just for the residents of this project.

Would like to see more differences in the style, materials and colors of the buildings. Like to see more interesting 
buildings. Would like to see more setbacks and articulation of the structures.

Current building designs appear more suited to office use and a Highway 92 frontage than to the adjacent 
neighborhood.

Buildings look too similar in terms of materials and colors - want to see more difference in building styles, 
materials and colors.


Would like to see more setbacks from the public right-of-way - overbearing if too close to the street. The South 
Grant Street residential frontages deserve to have some separation from the street (setbacks, stoops, 
landscaping) - buffer by as much green as possible or better materials.

A reduction in the number of buildings would be desirable.


One opinion was stated that the project needs some significant community benefit to the surrounding

After the March 27th, 2018 Study Session, the applicant has undergone 4 rounds of resubmittals in response to comments 
made as well as coordination with City Staff. Substantial Design Revisions are as follows:

All parking has been revised to either be at grade or subterranean and 100% screened by architecture and 
building uses. The previous larger “wrapped” parking garages, where in some cases were exposed along 
Highway 92, have been removed.  This change to podium parking (below the buildings) allows for greater variety 
appearance and massing versus the more dense, monolithic designs of the previous wrap buildings.  

The Trader Joe’s location has been sited in the proposed community to ensure a proprietary on-grade parking 
field, at grade directly under the building, providing all the requisite parking immediately adjacent to the store.  
Back of house loading, trash compacting, and mechanical equipment is sited on Passage Way, remotely from the 
majority of the proposed residential units and adjacent (but not visible from) Highway 92.  The store is both 
accessible from all major roadways for off-site customers and has clear pedestrian paths through the project for 
customers that choose to walk or bike.  Lastly, it is sited in the first phase of the project to allow for the existing 
Trader Joes to remain open while the new store is being built.  All these factors were considered for the 
proposed location and ultimately were the preferred choice for Trader Joes.  

The central open space has been substantially increased as well as the street sections along the internal Depot 
Way have increased in width to allow for better visibility from Delaware Street and Concar Drive.  A fully 
signalized intersection at the new Depot Way and Concar will also include pedestrian crossings, north to 19th 
Avenue Park, allowing a safer crossing than the current condition with access to the public park, restaurant 
offerings and Trader Joes.  

Improvement to the Landscape programming further detail out the central open space into an Active Arts Plaza 
adjacent to Building 2 and a more passive Park Plaza adjacent to Building 4.  The pavement section where Depot 
Way intersects with the park has been raised and treated with special pavement to make for a more friendly 
pedestrian crossing.  In addition, significant design improvements were made to the stairway transitioning from 
the park at the Arts Plaza up to the podium crossing through Building 2 in order to allow for a more inviting 
pedestrian connection from the park to the Food Hall.

Transit, shuttle, ride-share and package delivery drop-offs were designed and identified along Depot Way to 
maximize the efficiency of these activities in relationship to the proposed Depot mobility hub and adjacent to 
the community parcel package delivery areas.


Revisions in building massing have been introduced to visually reduce the impact from the surrounding public 
streets. Most notably through the introduction of an additional building in Building 5 as well as the massing 
separation introduced in Building 4. 

All internal building courtyards have been revised and increased in size. Landscape podium level plazas in both 
the private and public realm have been improved as well. 

A full revision in Building Architecture has occurred to create more visual variety. A greater range of horizontal 
and vertical gestures along with more variety of color and materials has been introduced to reduce the visual 
perception of large continuous buildings. 

Two significant buildings on the corners of Delaware/Concar and Grant/Concar were designed with elements 
evoking an Eichler architectural style to not only provide unique variety on the most visible corners but to also 
complement the Eichler style homes in the 19th Avenue Park neighborhood immediately to the north.  

Significant community benefit is being provided through a proposed Development Agreement with bike lane 
improvement, two protected bike intersections, a new protected pedestrian intersection on Grant Street, on-
site day care, the inclusion of a studio and performance space for the Peninsula Ballet Theater and an additional 
36 affordable units (proposed at the moderate income level) beyond the required 73 units (very low income 
units).  
The revisions laid out above have been well received by City Staff as well as Cannon Design Group. Please see the attached 
Site Plan and Rendering comparison for reference.

STAFF EVALUATION AND CONCERNS
Based on the Planning Commission’s concerns, staff advised the applicant that the proposed designs likely looked too 
much the same and seemed very boxy in form and detail, as follows:
Building Forms and Articulation: The designs for the buildings are well developed with subtle variations between 
the four architectural styles. However, they are very boxy and repetitive in form with limited height varia-
tion. The facades facing Highway 92 especially appear monolithic. Overall, the designs are very much like the 
structures on the adjacent Station Park Green project. In the study session, the commissioners expressed the desire 
for more variety in style, materials and colors. As the design stands at this point, the overall impression is one of a 
single “Project” rather than one of of varied structures within a visually rich “Sense of Place”.

CURRENT BUILDING DESIGNS
The current building designs provide substantial variety. While the original designs had a strong horizontal emphasis and 
a somewhat boxy look, the current designs have a mixture of horizontal and vertical forms and a greater variation in color 
and materials in order to reduce their visual perception of large, continuous buildings. Also, while the buildings have 
largely remained the same height as the original proposal, substantial articulation of the building parapets reduces the 
feeling of single large buildings.

SUMMARY
Staff has worked closely with the applicant since the Planning Commission’s March 2018 Study Session to address the 
commission’s concerns. While the project has retained the essence of its original land use and site planning framework, 
the parking layout, open space details and architectural design have been greatly refined. Major changes have included:
•	 A substantially larger central open space and a more graceful link between the grade level and the podium of Build-
ing B2.
•	 More below grade parking.
•	 Enlarged and refned interior residential courtyards.
•	 Increased building design articulation and visual variety with an improved residential scale and character.
•	 Improved Highway 92 facades and elimination of the exposed parking structure facade on that frontage.
The applicant will continue to refine the design and landscaping details as they prepare the final contract documents.
I have no further recommendations for changes.
