SUBJECT:
Affordable Housing Funding Strategy
RECOMMENDATION:
Provide feedback on priority uses of the City’s various affordable housing funding sources.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo has a history of utilizing many tools to support affordable housing. From the 1980’s until 2012, the 
former Redevelopment Agency (RDA) provided a steady stream of funding specifically to assist affordable housing.  During 
those years the City used a combination of RDA Housing funds and federal HOME program funds primarily for new 
construction of rental and first-time homebuyer units, acquisition and rehab of existing housing of rental units, as well as 
ongoing support for housing-related services.  In the 1990’s the City adopted its Below Market Rate (BMR) inclusionary 
program, which also has provided rental and for sale units to the affordable housing stock.  The BMR program has 
provided 25% of the City’s affordable units.
Since the dissolution of redevelopment agencies and the decrease in federal housing funds, the City’s main housing 
resources effectively dried up, although the City continues to collect smaller annual amounts in the form of loan 
repayments of loans made during those prior years.  Since that time, the City has been diligent in pursuing other resources 
and has also adopted a Commercial Linkage Fee in 2016. Recent legislation has established a number of housing funds 
available from the State of California.  As a result, the City now has several resources available, each with its own set of 
requirements as to allowable uses and income targets.  Staff seeks Council direction on the types of housing programs that 
should be prioritized so that staff can more effectively complete funding applications as they become available and 
develop program guidelines as needed.
AFFORDABLE HOUSING PROGRAMS
Various potential affordable housing assistance programs are described below representing production, preservation and 
protection (aka “the 3 P’s”). 

Multifamily New Construction- support construction of new ownership or rental units by partnering with a 
nonprofit or market rate developer.  Funding can be used for site acquisition, predevelopment soft costs or actual 
construction. 

Acquisition/Rehabilitation- costs to acquire and/or rehabilitate existing housing units and place rent restrictions to 
ensure affordability.

Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)- financial assistance to build ADU’s to property owners in exchange for rent 
restrictions on the ADU for a certain time period. Funds could also be used to legalize ADU’s built without permits 
in exchange for rent restrictions.


Down Payment Assistance- down payment loans for homeowners to purchase a home on the open market.  Given 
the market costs of for-sale housing, the financial assistance required per unit is high and not recommended at 
this time.

Rent Assistance- rent deposit assistance or short-term or emergency rental assistance through a nonprofit partner.

Voluntary Landlord Participation in Section 8- The Housing Authority has already implemented streamlining and 
modest financial incentives for landlords, but other ideas include: funds to make property improvements, reserves 
for damages, or assistance with security deposits.  This item is identified as a work item in the 2017 Assessment of 
Fair Housing Plan. Since that time, State legislation has been passed that prevents landlords from discriminating 
tenants who have Section 8 vouchers as a source of income.

Housing/Homeless Services- funds to local service providers who provide housing and homeless prevention-
related services such as shared housing, legal assistance, supportive assistance to special needs, etc. Currently 
these agencies have to compete with other service providers for the limited Community Development Block Grant 
Community Funding (CDBG) program.

Summary
In July and August 2020, staff will submit applications for the new programs: Permanent Local Housing Allocation (PLHA) 
Fund and Local Housing Trust Fund Match. The PLHA will be an ongoing grant based on a formula allocation and requires a 
five-year plan for use of the funds. Since the funding is relatively small but regular, staff recommends it be used for 
programs and projects that do not require long-range planning.  Good uses are homeless support services, including short-
term rent assistance, and it could be a good source for assistance for the construction of ADU’s.  If the Council is interested 
in an ADU program, staff proposes that a pilot loan program be developed and tested. 
The Local Housing Trust Fund Match is a competitive State grant that can be used as matching funds to our Commercial 
Linkage Fee Fund.  It is to be used for development, acquisition, or rehabilitation of housing or as down payment 
assistance.  If awarded, staff recommends using the grant to leverage the current commitment of $3.8 million for the 
Downtown Opportunity Sites. These funds are best used with structured long-term affordability agreements.
Staff requests that City Council provide feedback on priorities for the use of the various funds.

May 29, 2020 
Honorable Mayor Goethals and Council Members  
Thank you for the June 1 study session on this important issue.  My comments are: 
1.      $7.5 million of available housing fund money is earmarked for the Downtown 
Opportunities Site, which is a good thing for a great project.  However, the total money 
available in the Housing Fund, as shown in the table in the staff report, is only $7.254 million. I 
assume that the City is counting on more money to accumulate. 
2.     I recommend a pilot ADU program and I suggest that City staff, among all the other things 
they are doing right now, receive statements of interest from City of San Mateo Property 
Owners interested in participating in a City wide ADU construction project.  If the City is able to 
demonstrate to granting agencies that there is bona fide interest among many residents, this 
might be a strong way to receive a grant that could be distributed to the participants. 
3.     Whatever the City could do to influence the US Congress for grants, or tax relief in the way 
of tax credits for ADU construction (with a grant deed rider) would be greatly appreciated. 
Thank you again for dealing with this difficult issue.  
Robert Whitehair
