RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to amend the San Mateo - Foster City Public Financing Authority Joint Exercise of Powers Agreement 
with regard to the assignment of roles for the Treasurer-Auditor-Controller and the Clerk; to amend insurance language; 
and to outline payment changes for the extended roles.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo and the Estero Municipal Improvement District/City of Foster City formed the San Mateo – Foster 
City Public Financing Authority (PFA) in February 2016 for the purpose of financing capital improvements for the member 
agencies. In 2019, the PFA issued $270 million bonds to finance the member agencies’ respective shares for the Clean 
Water Program. In February 2020, the PFA issued $46 million in lease revenue bonds to finance the City of San Mateo’s 
North Shoreview Flood Improvement Project and various street rehabilitation/reconstruction projects. A joint exercise of 
powers agreement outlines the PFA’s governance and operating structure. 
Staff from Foster City and San Mateo are jointly proposing several amendments to that agreement. First, the agreement 
currently states that the member agencies’ City Clerks shall serve as Secretary to the PFA Board on a rotating two-year 
term. The agreement also states the member agencies’ Finance Directors shall serve as the PFA’s Treasurer-Auditor-
Controller on a rotating four-year term. Both terms are scheduled to end in June 2020. Foster City staff has expressed a 
desire to have the City of San Mateo’s City Clerk and Finance Director continue serving as the Secretary and Treasurer-
Auditor-Controller and City of San Mateo staff is able and willing to do so. The proposed amendments would allow for this 
arrangement, at the option and expense of the City of Foster City. 
Secondly, staff is proposing to change the insurance requirements for PFA Board and Officers. Currently the agreement 
requires the PFA to maintain errors and omissions liability insurance with a minimum of $1 million in coverage on a “claims 
made” basis. The proposed amendment would change that requirement to $1 million on an “annual aggregate” basis and 
reflects the availability of errors and omissions liability coverage in the insurance market. The amendments were approved 
by the respective city councils of each member agency on April 20, 2020. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
The City Clerk of the City of San Mateo has served as Secretary of the PFA since inception, as the EMID Clerk requested San 
Mateo to serve as PFA Secretary for the initial partial year and subsequent two-year term. The fiscal year ending June 30, 
2020 marks the end of the City’s Finance Director’s term as Treasurer-Auditor-Controller and the City Clerk’s term as 
Secretary. Upon approval of the amended JPA, in fiscal year 2020-21, EMID will pay $14,362.49 to the City of San Mateo 
for the first year of the four-year term in which it would have been responsible for Treasurer-Auditor-Controller duties and 
first year of the two-year term for which it would have been responsible for Secretary duties. The cost of providing

members of the Authority, and additional members may be added pursuant to Section 14.

The Authority shall continue to exercise the powers herein conferred upon it until termination 
of this Agreement, and thereafter shall continue to exercise only such powers as to enable it to 
pay and discharge all costs, expenses, and charges legally incurred hereunder, and to dispose 
of, divide and distribute any property required as a result of the joint exercise of such powers. 
 
Section 1617.  Disposition of Assets; Property and Money 
 
Upon termination of this Agreement under Section 13, all costs, expenses, and charges legally 
incurred by the Authority shall be paid and discharged; and the Authority shall sell such 
property as may be necessary and shall distribute to the federal or State government such 
property and funds as are lawfully required; the balance of such property and any surplus 
money on hand shall be distributed or returned in proportion to contributions made by the 
affected parties except to the extent otherwise agreed upon by the affected parties.

Section 1819. Amendments 
 
This Agreement may be amended at any time by the written agreement of the parties to this 
Agreement. Notwithstanding the foregoing, (a) any amendment required to add a new 
member that is not an Associate Member shall only be executed by the new member and the 
members of the Authority that are not Associate Members and (b) any amendment required to 
add a new member that is an Associate Member shall only be executed by the new Associate 
Member and the Authority. 
 
Section 1920. Advances 
All costs of administering the Authority shall be shared equally by the members, and to 
the extent provided by the Governing Body, the Associate Members. 
Contributions or advances of public funds and of personnel may be made to the Authority by 
the members for any of the purposes of this Agreement. Payment of public funds may be made 
to defray the cost of any such contribution. Any such advance made in respect of a revenue- 
producing facility may be made subject to repayment, and shall be repaid, in the manner 
agreed upon by the members, and the Authority at the time of making such advance as 
provided by section 6512.1 of the Act. 
 
The members may allow the use of personnel in lieu of other contributions or advances to 
the Authority. 
 
Section 2021. Notices. 
 
Notices and other communications hereunder to the parties shall be sufficient if delivered to the 
clerk or secretary of the governing body of each party. 
 
Section 2122. Severability

jurisdiction to be illegal or in conflict with any law of the State of California, or otherwise be 
rendered unenforceable or ineffectual, the validity of the remaining portions of provisions 
shall not be affected thereby. 
 
Section 2223.  Entire Agreement 
 
This Agreement contains the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior 
understanding between them with respect to the subject matter of this Agreement. There are 
no promises, terms, conditions or obligations, oral or written, between or among the parties 
relating to the subject matter of this Agreement that are not fully expressed in this Agreement. 
This Agreement may not be modified, changed, supplemented or terminated, nor may any 
obligation under this Agreement be waived, except by written instrument signed by the 
parties.
