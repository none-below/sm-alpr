City of San Mateo Age-Friendly-Community Project Ideas
Age Friendly Cities Task Force
1. Safe Sidewalks/Pedestrian Safety
a. Expand crossing time on specific intersections. 
b. Increase # of flashing light pedestrian crossings.
c. Increase enforcement/education of DMV laws pertaining to pedestrian 
safety. 
d. increase enforcement/education of DMV laws pertaining to auto/bicycle 
co-existence. 
2. Age Friendly Business Incentivization/Certification Training
a. Encourage businesses to provide event/meeting space. 
b. Create criteria that businesses can meet to receive certification
3. Coordinate Communication of Senior Services/Events/age friendly policies
a. Increase communication about existing services such as but not limited to:
i. Housing Maintenance resources. 
ii. Host HIP Housing workshops 
iii. Provide information about transportation options 
iv. Provide information and increase access to computer/technology 
assistance
v. Increase information about how to develop Accessory Dwelling Units 
(ADUs) 
vi. increase information about strategies to locate and apply for 
affordable housing for low-income as well as middle income renters 
needing below market rents.

Safer Senior Pedestrian Routes
11 of 24

--- page 12 ---
Project Description
Prioritize the repair of sidewalks and other 
safety hazards on designated walking routes 
from high concentrations of senior housing to 
high-need destinations. This will promote 
walking for exercise, business and 
socialization.

--- page 13 ---
Current San Mateo 
Sidewalk Repair Program
“Properly maintained sidewalks allow for safe pedestrian travel, add value to 
individual properties, and decreases the liability faced by a homeowner in 
the event someone sues after being injured by damaged sidewalk that is in 
front of their residence.
Safe sidewalks are a priority for the Department of Public Works. There 
are 380 miles of sidewalks in the City of San Mateo and, in order to help 
maintain them, we have developed a 15-Year Sidewalk Repair Program to 
inspect and schedule repairs in an effective and cost-efficient way.”
13 of 24

--- page 14 ---
Benefits
•Safer routes encourage walking by seniors 
for business, exercise and socialization, with 
a reduced risk of falls and injuries. 
•It addresses a MAJOR issue from Age-
Friendly focus groups and interviews and 
integrates with the existing City Sidewalk 
Repair program.
14 of 24

--- page 15 ---
Process
1. Identify high concentrations of senior residents in the city.
2. Identify major destinations frequented by seniors. 
3. Map walking routes between the above two. 
4. Determine data capture method and hazard classification system.
5. Recruit and train volunteers (seniors) to walk mapped routes and 
identify sidewalk hazards.
• Also capture issues with intersection signal timing, pedestrian markings, 
flashing crosswalk lights on the same routes
6. Locate, photograph, categorize and classify hazards. 
7. Report findings to Public Works for action.
15 of 24

--- page 16 ---
Action Options
• Sidewalk Issues
• Where the owner is responsible, send out notices to correct damage 
from tree roots, vegetation intrusion, parking and basketball hoop 
blockages, etc.
• Incorporate low priority repairs into the existing 15 year neighborhood 
repair cycle.
• Where the City is responsible or the City and owner share 
responsibility, prioritize repair response based on severity of hazard, 
normal schedule of repair and availability of funds.

Action Options, Cont’d
• Sidewalk Issues
• Note - The current City Sidewalk Repair Program already provides for 
correction of sidewalk hazards “when they become apparent” outside 
the normal 15 year neighborhood cycle. 
• This project provides a mechanism for making hazardous 
conditions on heavily travelled senior pedestrian routes 
”apparent” and prioritizes them for correction over others with 
lower utilization and less community benefit.
• Other hazards on same routes 
• These will be directed to the appropriate party within or outside Public 
Works for action.

AGE-FRIENDLY BUSINESS CERTIFICATION
                 A City of San Mateo and
                   San Mateo Area Chamber of Commerce    
             Collaboration
18 of 24

--- page 19 ---
Project Description
Implement a high profile program where City 
businesses that are confirmed to be “Age-
Friendly” are certified. They visibly promote 
themselves as such in the community and are 
prominently supported in that effort by the City 
and Chamber of Commerce
19 of 24

--- page 20 ---
Benefits
• Age-Friendly businesses increase their long term viability prospects 
by preferentially attracting our community’s fastest growing 
demographic.
• Seniors treated with respect and with positive experiences spread 
the word about those businesses in their personal networks.
• Prominent promotion of the program’s participants and successes 
incentivizes more businesses to seek certification.
• The City’s resulting Age-Friendly business environment becomes a 
retention and recruiting tool for senior residents and businesses. 
     WIN! – WIN! – WIN!
20 of 24

--- page 21 ---
Concept
•Businesses that meet pre-defined criteria apply for 
Age-Friendly certification.
•Senior volunteers “confirm” criteria are satisfied.
•Businesses are awarded a sticker to post in their window 
upon confirmation.
•The City and Chamber promote the program and 
recognize/celebrate qualifying businesses.
21 of 24

--- page 22 ---
AGE-FRIENDLY CRITERIA
• Basic Qualifications
• Training – of employees/staff on how to effectively interact with seniors
• Physical Environment – conditions such as lighting, noise, safety, signage, accessibility
• Available Services – transportation, hours, discounts, phone access, delivery options
• Bonus Points
• Hiring Practices – senior owned/managed; presence in customer-facing roles
• Community Support – space availability for meetings, financial support, volunteer projects
22 of 24

--- page 23 ---
Process
1. Define City and Chamber roles and responsibilities and initial program scope. 
Develop implementation plan and timeline.
2. Communicate plan and recruit participants to develop the details. 
3. Develop Age-Friendly criteria and weighting scheme for certification.
4. Develop application form, workflow, process and success metrics.
5. Recruit and train senior volunteer Age-Friendly “checkers”.
6. Pilot the system with motivated in-scope businesses.
7. Adjust the criteria and process as needed based on pilot outcomes. 
Consider ongoing feedback needs or opportunities. Periodic renewals?
8. Roll-out with major publicity acknowledging the program, participants and 
first businesses to qualify.
