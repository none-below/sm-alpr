SUBJECT:
Proposed Revisions to Heritage Tree Ordinance
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on presentation of Heritage Tree Ordinance issues and proposed revisions.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo Heritage Tree Ordinance (SMMC Chapter 13.52) was first adopted in 1968 to ensure a significant 
and thriving population of large healthy trees in San Mateo for public enjoyment and environmental sustainability, while 
honoring the reasonable economic enjoyment of private property. Since first adopted, the Heritage Tree Ordinance has 
been revised several times to improve its relevance to the times and to enhance its efficacy toward achieving its intended 
purpose. Over the past five years, Parks staff has observed some elements of the ordinance that: 

Are difficult to interpret and confusing to the public 

Create tree preservation loopholes 

Are difficult to administer and enforce 

Are not in accordance with current industry best practices 
With the aim of enhancing clarity, increasing and maintaining canopy, and increasing effectiveness of ordinance 
implementation, staff has begun the process of drafting updates to the ordinance that serve these ends. 
The proposed updates are informed by staff and resident experiences, and by a study of ordinances adopted this year in 
Menlo Park and Atherton. 
To solicit input in most effectively updating San Mateo’s ordinance, Staff intends to present to the public at two or more 
upcoming Park and Recreation Commission meetings. At these meetings, staff will present on aspects of the ordinance 
that could use refinement and raise for discussion proposed changes to better meet its intent and purpose. The 
presentations will be announced to the public on the City’s website and Nextdoor.com. The public can comment at the 
meeting and/or via email to the Managing Arborist. During the first presentation at the July 1 Park and Recreation 
Commission meeting, the Managing Arborist will present on six (6) issues with the language of the current ordinance, 
providing context, examples, and proposed remedies. The list of topics to be covered can be seen in the attached table 
(Att 1 - Table of Issues to be discussed at 1 July 2020 Meeting).
 Once all the proposed revisions have been reviewed, staff, in conjunction with the City Attorney’s office will prepare a 
revised ordinance that will return to the Commission for review and approval prior to being forwarded to the City Council 
for adoption.
