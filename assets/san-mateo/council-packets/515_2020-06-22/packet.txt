Borrowing Policy Addendum:  City Librarian James Moore advised the Board that the Library needs

B.
City Librarian Report:  

There will be a phased re-opening plan for the Library, beginning with curbside pickup 
service starting the first week of June

The California State Library approved a request from the Pacific Library System (PLS) for 
$15,000 in emergency collection funds to purchase additional OverDrive titles to be shared 
amongst all PLS libraries.

The California State Library invited the San Mateo Public Library to participate in a funding 
opportunity of $5,000 for crisis collection funding to address the increased demand from 
our community for e-resources while the Library is closed due to the pandemic.

The City Manager’s recommendation to address the $7,800,000 structural deficit with 
expected revenue losses of approximately $18,000,000 and $11,000,000 in each of the next 
two fiscal years respectively includes savings from freezing new capital spending and 
employee compensation, strategic elimination of some positions, and passage of TOT 
(Transit Occupancy Tax on short term rentals) ballot measure.  An end of year furlough for 
employees is also being considered.  The goal is to minimize service level impacts.

The Library has been offering many virtual programs during the closure.  

Upgrades have been made to the Library’s webpage, and work on this project will continue.

LIBRARY BOARD OF TRUSTEES
May  29, 2020
Special Meeting Minutes
1.
CALL TO ORDER 
 The meeting was called to order at 10:00 a.m. by President Dr. Lester Chen. 
2.
PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL
Dr. Lester Chen led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call.  Board Members Dr. Lester Chen, Elizabeth De Winter, Kathy Shields and 
Don Hill were present.  Board Member Lisa Diaz Nash was present after roll call.
3.
STUDY SESSION
Library Rules of Conduct Policy – Addendum – Rules for Curbside Service : City Librarian James 
Moore reported that he shared this proposed addendum with Bahareh Abdollahi from the City 
Attorney’s Office and received approval, and the Library managers had no objections.  Discussion 
ensued about necessary edits to the addendum including deletion of the section that advises 
patrons to remain in their car, and an end date of the addendum.  Trustees asked questions of Mr. 
Moore and further discussion ensued regarding details of the curbside service, the marketing of it, 
and the possibility of enlisting the help of volunteers.  A motion was made to adopt the addendum 
with the following amendments: 1) remove the requirement for patrons to remain in their car and 
2) the expiration of this addendum will be decided upon a future decision by the Board of Trustees.

Moved:     Dr. Chen, Seconded: Ms. De Winter

Ayes:         Dr. Chen, Ms. Diaz Nash, Ms. De Winter, Mr. Hill and Ms. Shields

Notes:        None
Public Comment – Karen Herrel asked Mr. Moore what the bigger plan is for the Library’s reopening.  
Mr. Moore explained that the reopening of the Library must be done in a very thoughtful and safe 
manner and in phases, with the first phase beginning on June 1 with limited curbside pickup service 
for materials that have been on hold and ready for pickup since the shelter in place health order 
went into effect.  Mr. Moore shared that the San Mateo Public Library is aligned with or even ahead 
of other libraries in the Pacific Library Partnership (PLP) for curbside pickup service.  Of the forty-two 
libraries in the PLP, only the Watsonville Public Library has implemented curbside pickup service.
4.   ADJOURNMENT
       Dr. Lester Chen adjourned the meeting at 10:56 a.m.

SUBJECT:
COVID-19 Closure Library Fine Suspension Policy.
RECOMMENDATION:
Library Board will review policy options for ending or extending suspension of fines on library materials due to COVID-19 
safety concerns and the consequent library closure.
BACKGROUND:
When San Mateo County issued a shelter-in-place order beginning Friday, March 14th, the library decided to suspend the 
issuing on fines on all materials already checked-out to patrons until June 30, 2020. With that date approaching and the 
library still being closed, the Library Board members and City Librarian will discuss the possible need to extend the fee-free 
period and, if so, the creation of specific policy to provide clear guidance to staff and the public.
