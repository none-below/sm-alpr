A.
Audio Recording Library Board Meetings:  City Librarian James Moore shared information that
he received from City Clerk Patrice Olds regarding options for recording audio of the Library 
Board meetings, and making these recordings accessible to the public.  A possible option is to 
use the recording function of Zoom while conducting remote meetings.  When meetings are 
able to be held in person again, additional options include continued use of the recording 
function of Zoom, use of a digital recorder, and a staff member assigned to record the meeting 
using a laptop or an iPhone.  A motion to begin saving the audio recordings of the meetings and 
make them available to the public was made by Ms. Shields, seconded by Ms. Diaz Nash and the 
motion did not pass at two ayes and three noes.

Moved:  Ms. Shields, Seconded: Ms. Diaz Nash

Ayes:      Ms. Shields and Ms. Diaz Nash

Noes:     Mr. Hill, Ms. De Winter and Dr. Lester Chen
A motion to continue to record the meetings in Zoom was made by Ms. Diaz Nash, and since she 
did not receive a second to her motion, Ms. Diaz Nash withdrew her motion. 
       
A motion to find out if it is possible to have the meeting minutes translated into Spanish and 
included in the Library’s e-newsletter as a link was made by Dr. Chen, seconded by Mr. Hill, and 
the motion passed 4-1.

       B.
City Librarian Vision Presentation for Fiscal Year 2020-2021:  Mr. Moore presented his vision for 
              FY 2020-2021 and shared that even in the midst of a pandemic and a lean budget to work with,
              there are opportunities to adapt and thrive.  Priorities include employee training and
              development; building upon the Library’s outreach program; continuing to work on improving
              the user experience; and to ensure that the Library is a place with access to information that
              addresses equity, social justice, diversity and inclusion, and a forum to promote civil
              discourse on these important issues.
C.
City Librarian Budget Review for Fiscal Year 2020-2021:  Mr. Moore reviewed the budget for FY 
2020-2021 and shared that the Library will need to achieve a mandated savings of $130,000. 
Additionally, the Library normally receives approximately $300,000 per fiscal year from the 
Foundation, and this year the Foundation is only guaranteeing “flow-through” grants that 
amount to less than $40,000 and can only be used for very specific purposes as instructed by the 
grantor.  Mr. Moore’s priority is to maintain quality service, and he shared his plan to achieve 
the savings by leaving some per diem positions vacant and reducing discretionary spending.  
There will also be savings due to lower utility and salary costs resulting from the mandatory 
closure of the buildings and the need to furlough employees.
