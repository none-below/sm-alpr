Central Park Fallen Hero's Memorial Design 
Sheila Canzian, Director and Gina Chavez of RRM Design Group presented on the preferred design, the 
remembrance Poppy, for the Central Park Fallen Hero's Memorial. Commissioners posed questions and offered 
comment. Commissioners agreed it would be helpful to have a model or image that better portrayed the design 
from all angles, including from the Rose Garden, as it was difficult to visualize what the public would see if they 
approached the memorial from different areas in the park. Commissioners expressed some concern as to the 
modern design elements/materials clashing with the natural look of the park and possibly being mistaken for an 
art installation instead of a memorial. Gina Chavez explained that although the material for the columns is 
stainless steel that the rest of the design would integrate the natural colors in the area. She added that stainless 
steel was the best option in order to have flexibility and consistency when adding names to the memorial after

SUBJECT:
Proposed Revisions to Heritage Tree Ordinance (Part 2)
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on presentation of Heritage Tree Ordinance issues and proposed revisions.
BACKGROUND:
At the July 1, 2020 meeting of the Park and Recreation Commission, the Managing Arborist presented on six (6) issues in 
which Heritage Tree Ordinance could be updated to enhance clarity, increase and maintain tree canopy, and improve the 
effectiveness of ordinance implementation. 
At the September 2, 2020 meeting, he will present on an additional four (4) issues providing context, examples, and 
proposed remedies. The list of topics to be covered can be seen in the attached table (Att 1 - Table of Issues to be 
discussed at 2 September 2020 Meeting). This presentation has been announced to the public on the City’s website and 
Nextdoor.com. The public can comment at the meeting and/or via email to the Managing Arborist. 
Once all the proposed revisions have been reviewed, staff, in conjunction with the City Attorney’s office, will prepare a 
revised ordinance that will return to the Commission for review and approval prior to being forwarded to the City Council 
for adoption.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The proposed review of the Heritage Tree Ordinance will not have any budget impact.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
In accordance with Public Resources Code Section 21065, providing feedback to staff regarding potential ordinance
changes is not a project subject to CEQA because the City is not taking action at this time.
NOTICE PROVIDED
All meeting noticing requirements were met.

13.52.025 (a) Tree 
Protection and 
Preservation 
Applicants for Planning Applications and 
Building Permits only required to disclose 
Heritage Trees on subject property. Can 
result in lack of protection for Heritage Trees 
on adjacent property that may be impacted 
by construction. 
Require disclosure of all HTs on 
subject property and HTs on 
adjacent properties that are within 
30 feet of proposed construction

13.52.025 (b) Tree 
Protection and 
Preservation 
The trigger for requirement of tree 
protection plan is "exterior construction 
work" [near tree]. Applicants often think 
"construction work" means the final 
footprint of their project. This overlooks the 
myriad of activities associated with 
construction that may damage trees, such as 
staging, access, and material storage. 
Replace use of term "exterior 
construction work" with 
"construction activities" and 
provide non-exhaustive list of 
example activities. 
13.52.070 
Notification 
Requires notification and 10-day appeal 
window for ALL trees. In the case of dead 
trees this results in unnecessary delays and 
potentially hazardous situations 
Allow Managing Arborist to waive 
notification and appeal window 
when s/he has determined that 
tree is Dead or Dangerous. 
13.35 Street Trees 
and 13.52 Heritage 
Trees 
Currently two separate Muni Code chapters 
with many provisions that apply to both 
Heritage Trees and Street Trees. Customer 
service experience and ordinance upkeep 
could be improved by consolidation of both 
chapters into single chapter to cover both 
classes of "protected trees." 
Combine provisions of Heritage 
Tree and Street Tree Ordinances 
into single chapter to cover both 
classes of protected trees and 
name it "Tree Preservation 
Ordinance" or similar.
