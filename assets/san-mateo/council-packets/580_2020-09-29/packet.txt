6.   NEW BUSINESS 
A. Potential Policy Implication of Circulating Chromebooks and MiFi Units During Pandemic:  City 
Librarian James Moore introduced Supervising Library Assistant Miguel Sanchez to the Board. Mr. 
Sanchez shared an overview of the number of Chromebooks and MiFi units in the Library’s 
collection, including those that still need to be returned from checkouts prolonged by the March 
16, 2020 COVID-19 shutdown.  In addition, 35 new Chromebooks have been purchased but not 
shipped yet due to a nationwide shortage.  
 
Board members indicated their desire for a system to be implemented whereby any Chromebooks 
currently on-hand (2) are made available to patrons as soon as possible during curbside hours.  
Discussion ensued on possible methods to reserve and checkout Chromebooks and MiFi units, the 
amount of available staffing to help with this service, and the possibility of volunteers helping.   
 
Mr. Moore advised that the curbside service during this pandemic is considerably more labor- 
intensive compared to normal Library operation when patrons can pick out their own materials 
from the shelves.  During the shutdown, Library staff must manually pull all the holds and manually 
check them back in once the items have been quarantined for four days.  These are not tasks that 
volunteers can perform.  
 
 
4 of 30

--- page 5 ---
The Board said they would prefer a system where a patron can reserve tech items by phone, rather 
than an online reservation system, given the digital divide.  Mr. Sanchez reported that patrons who 
currently have tech items checked out have been sent written notification to return these items, 
and he hopes to have all or most of these items returned by mid-September.  Mr. Moore agreed to 
have Library staff follow-up on the email reminders already sent to these patrons with phone call 
reminders.  This topic will be discussed further at the September 29, 2020 meeting.  
B. Policy Implications of Conducting Limited Activity by the Friends of the Library During the COVID-19 
Pandemic: Mr. Moore reported that there are volunteers who would like to return to their 
volunteer duties at the Library, and internet sales of books should be resumed with the assistance 
of one or two volunteers working at a time in the building.  Discussion ensued about the possibility 
of accepting donations again.  Library Foundation Executive Director Lynne Esselstein shared 
information that she received regarding the process that Redwood City Public Library Friends of the 
Library volunteers are currently using to accept donations.   
 
A special Library Board meeting will be scheduled prior to the September 29, 2020 regular meeting 
to review and address any necessary policy updates for these Friends of the Library activities.

Special Meeting Minutes 
 
1. CALL TO ORDER  
 The meeting was called to order at 6:05 p.m. by President Lisa Diaz Nash.  
 
2. PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE & ROLL CALL 
Lisa Diaz Nash led those in attendance with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Executive Assistant Dena 
Gomez conducted roll call.  Board Members Lisa Diaz Nash, Elizabeth De Winter, Kathy Shields, Don 
Hill and Dr. Lester Chen were present.  
 
3. STUDY SESSION 
COVID-19 Policy Review : City Librarian James Moore clarified the purpose of this meeting is to 
review the COVID-19 policies for any needed updates for accepting limited donations for the Friends 
of the Library bookstore.  Mr. Moore said some services can be started on a trial basis without a 
formal policy, like the Internet Café that was started very quickly but is now paused due to the poor 
air quality from the wildfires.  In addition, the Library is still waiting for many of the loaned out 
Chromebooks to be returned by patrons, and has yet to receive the 35 new Chromebooks that were 
ordered due to a nationwide shortage.  Therefore, the Technology Lending Program is not able to 
resume yet. 
 
Library Foundation Executive Director Lynne Esselstein shared that the Foundation members and 
the Friends of the Library volunteers are looking forward to future donation days to raise money for 
the Library, and to also keep the connection with our community members.  Ms. Esselstein said that 
members from the Foundation will be volunteering at these donation days, and the books that are 
donated will be sold through the Friends of the Library bookstore on Amazon. 
 
Discussion ensued on what policies should be updated and/or adopted for patrons donating items 
to the Library, and for volunteers working at the Library during the COVID-19 pandemic.  A motion 
was made by Ms. Shields to remove the word “non-medical” before the words “face coverings” on 
the addendum to the Library Rules of Conduct dated May 26, 2020, seconded by Dr. Chen, and the 
motion passed 5-0. 
 
 
Moved:     Ms. Shields, Seconded: Dr. Chen 
 
Ayes:         Ms. Shields, Dr. Chen, Ms. Diaz Nash, Ms. De Winter and Mr. Hill 
 
Noes:       None 
 
A motion was made by Ms. Shields to add a donation limit of 1 banker’s box or 2 grocery bags per 
person to the addendum to the Library Rules of Conduct dated May 26, 2020, seconded by Ms. De 
Winter, and the motion passed 5-0. 
 
 
Moved:    Ms. Shields, Seconded: Ms. De Winter 
 
Ayes: 
   Ms. Shields, Ms. De Winter, Ms. Diaz Nash, Mr. Hill and Dr. Chen 
 
Noes:    None

A motion was made by Dr. Chen to adopt a new policy for volunteer rules of conduct during COVID-
19, seconded by Mr. Hill, and the motion passed 5-0.  The rules require volunteers to take their 
temperature before coming to the Library, and to not come to the Library if they have a fever; wear 
masks; stay 6 feet apart from others; and wear gloves and/or use hand sanitizer at the beginning of 
their shift and regularly throughout their shift.  
 
 
Moved:    Dr. Chen, Seconded: Mr. Hill 
 
Ayes:        Dr. Chen, Mr. Hill, Ms. Diaz Nash, Ms. De Winter and Ms. Shields

Key Considerations for a Limited Reopening in November 2020 
  
 
We do not aim to reopen until elections are over  
  
 
We need to decide what services we will provide 
o Can open smaller browsing collections of high-interest items  
o Computer use - can use meeting rooms if needed; there are data ports there, but they 
have poor circulation  
o Could open computer use to Children's area but would need to restrict to outside of 
storytime hours; would need to remove/restrict furniture because Children's are hi 
touch areas; also, can have doors open for airflow   
  
 
We aim to provide these services, and can pull out of browsing collection if that one is too 
challenging at this time  
o Self-pickup of holds  
o Computer use (might want to consider restricted hours for high risk populations)  
o Browsing collections  
  
 
 
Need to determine level of service on computers - might need to have a roaming IS person in 
addition to someone staffed at desk  
o Would need portable sneezeguard for computers 
o Technically it is possible to set up remote assistance but the people who need help 
would have a hard time  
o Perhaps volunteers could assist    
o Need face shields for staff  
  
 
Staff at different levels could help curate browsing collections - "Staff Picks" stand   
  
 
Could set up Friends' book sale area for limited sale area or browsing collection; might be an 
issue of overflow from other PLS libraries if we open doors for browsing  
  
 
Limit people in building - appointment based or drop-in; staff person at door to limit access  
  
 
Can look to other organizations (museums, Los Gatos Public Library, retail) for guidance  
  
 
Consider the staggering of reintroduction of services - holds, then computers, then browsing  
  
 
Keep areas separate to keep patrons more contained  
  
 
If there are 9 computers, can assign 7 to appointment based and 2 walk in (express stations?) 
  
 
Need to determine hours of operation; this will affect staff schedules  
  
 
Need to reshape our service model based on not having on call-staff; whatever services we 
introduce now will need to be sustainable, including flexibility in scheduling
