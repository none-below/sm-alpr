SUBJECT:
City of San Mateo Conflict of Interest Code – 2020 Biennial Update
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to update the City of San Mateo’s conflict of interest code to account for the addition, deletion, and 
modification of positions listed as “designated employees.”
BACKGROUND:
The Political Reform Act of 1974 requires, among other things, that local government agencies adopt and maintain a 
conflict of interest code. Like most cities, the City of San Mateo many years ago adopted a “model” conflict of interest 
code prepared by the Fair Political Practices Commission. The model code establishes the substantive requirements 
relating to the disclosure of financial interests and the requirements for disqualification from participation in decisions in 
which City employees and other officials might have a private, financial interest. Cities adopting the model code need only 
adopt a list designating those employees subject to the conflict of interest code’s financial interest disclosure 
requirements and specifying the scope of interests those designated employees must disclose. 
In addition to imposing the requirement to adopt a local code including the listing of designated employees, the Political 
Reform Act requires cities to review their codes on at least a biennial basis (to be conducted each even numbered year) to 
insure that the code reflects the organization’s current staffing. 
A review of the City of San Mateo’s conflict of interest code revealed that revisions were required due to the addition of 
positions not previously listed, and the deletion of positions that have been eliminated or which do not meet the decision-
making threshold to necessitate the need to file. The exhibit attached to the resolution included with this report reflects 
these changes. 
The list of designated employees and officials does not include council members, planning commissioners, the city 
manager, the city attorney, and the finance director. These positions are not listed in the local code because they are 
already required by state law to disclose their financial interests. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
There is no budget impact with this item.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This item is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that will not result in 
direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)

SUBJECT:
COVID-19 Rent Deferral and Expense Reduction – Agreement 
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve an agreement with PAR3SM, LLC for COVID-19 rent deferral and miscellaneous expense reductions and authorize 
the City Manager to execute the agreement in substantially the form presented. 
BACKGROUND:
The City entered into an agreement with PAR3SM, LLC in April 2019 to provide food and beverage services at Poplar Creek 
Golf Course.  Both the City and PAR3SM invested in initial improvements and renovations to the restaurant, bar, and 
banquet spaces, include painting, new floor coverings, remodeled bar area, etc., as well as the installation of some new 
kitchen equipment.  PAR3SM was on track to produce $2 million in gross revenue sales in its first year of operation before 
the start of COVID-19. 
In response to the outbreak of COVID-19, the City declared a state of emergency on March 16, 2020, followed shortly 
thereafter with a shelter in place order issued by the San Mateo County Health Officer that required all non-essential 
businesses to close immediately in order to slow the spread of the disease.  On March 17, Poplar Creek Golf Course and 
PAR3SM closed for business.  On May 7, Poplar Creek Golf Course was allowed to re-open under county/state guidelines; 
however, food and beverage service, other than take out and “grab and go” self service items did not resume until early 
June when limited outdoor dining was approved.  Similar to other restaurant operators, PAR3 has struggled with outdoor 
dining based upon numerous weather and air quality factors as well as limited available space on the outdoor patio.  
Although recent changes have allowed for limited indoor dining, the bulk of PAR3’s business is their bar and banquet 
business, which will remain impacted for the near term.  As such, they have had problems generating sufficient income to 
cover rent, salaries, and general operating expenses.  
Staff is proposing a number of modifications to the existing lease agreement with the goal of keeping PAR3 an integral part 
of the golfing experience at Poplar Creek.  These include:

Deferral of rent payments for February and March 2020 until April 2021

Waiver of rent beginning April 1, 2020 until either April 1, 2021 or until restaurant, bar, and banquet operations 
can resume at 50% capacity, whichever occurs first.  Rent will be calculated as a percentage income, as described 
in the lease, rather than payment of a base rent

Full rent payment (either percentage or base, as described in the lease) will resume when indoor operations are 
allowed to resume at 100% capacity

City will pay for one-half of the sewer service charges for the remainder of the lease

City will pay for janitorial and paper products associated with cleaning and stocking the main clubhouse restroom 
during the months of November 2020 to March 2021, up to a maximum of $10,000 
29 of 186

--- page 30 ---
CITY OF SAN MATEO

City will reimburse PAR3SM up to $63,000 for the cost of facility improvements incurred by them prior to opening 
so long as the restaurant and on-course snack shack remain open from November 1, 2020 through March 31, 
2021. 
Staff recommends approval of these modifications as it is unlikely that PAR3SM will be able to generate enough income to 
pay rent arears for the previously deferred rent or cover all anticipated operating expenses given the limited seating 
capacity that restaurants are likely to sustain for the foreseeable future.  Staff’s hope is to retain PAR3SM as a valued 
partner at Poplar Creek, as it would be challenging to find a replacement food and beverage operator given the 
environment. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
The value of the rent concessions and expenditure reductions is approximately $205,000.  At the close of the first quarter 
of fiscal year 2020-21, golf greens fee revenue is approximately $141,000 ahead of the same period from fiscal year 2019-
20.  Lease revenues from the golf course are deposited in Fund 19 and are considered part of the General Fund.

Recreation Center Family Changing Room Construction – Contract 
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to approve the alternative purchasing procedure for the contract with Angeles Contractor, Inc. for 
construction to convert part of an existing storage area in the Martin Luther King Recreation Center into a family changing 
room in the amount of $95,685.23; establish a contingency reserve in the amount of $10,000; and authorize the Public 
Works Director to execute the contract in substantially the form presented and issue change orders within the contingency 
amount.
BACKGROUND:
The Martin Luther King Jr. (MLK) Recreation Center is located in the North Central neighborhood in San Mateo and is home 
to many youth activities including the Police Activities League, drop-in basketball, several dance and fitness classes, and a 
swimming pool. 
The swim center was constructed in the late 1960’s and originally designed to fit the programs and social norms of that 
era. As a result, separate dressing rooms and bathrooms for each gender were created. Since then, the need for dressing 
areas has changed and includes accessibility, family accommodations, and gender neutrality. Due to a policy that restricts 
persons older than seven years of age from entering the dressing rooms of the opposite gender, parents with children of 
the opposite gender are separated while dressing and walking to the pool. Therefore, the creation of a gender-neutral 
family changing area will meet the safety and convenience needs of all the patrons. 
The contractor selected for project implementation utilizing the job order contracting process is Angeles Contractor, Inc. 
The job order contract competitive procurement method for construction services provides fixed-price unit rate quotes 
and was approved by the City Council via resolution on March 18, 2019. The swim center is open for the summer months 
from June through August. Therefore, construction is scheduled to be performed during the off-season starting in late 
2020 and will take 90 days to complete. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Sufficient funds for the construction contract and contingency are available in the King and Joinville Changing Room 
Improvements Project.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This project is categorically exempt from CEQA as an “existing facility,” because it consists of the operation, repair, 
maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, 
mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of use. (CEQA Guidelines Section 
15301.)

SUBJECT:
Asphalt Patch Truck Purchase – Contract
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to approve an alternative purchasing process for the contract with PB Loader Corporation in the 
amount of $258,049 for the purchase of one PB Loader Model BC-6 Asphalt Patcher mounted on a 2021 Freightliner M2 
Truck Chassis; and authorize the Public Works Director to execute the purchase contract in substantially the form 
presented. 
BACKGROUND:
The Public Works Department utilizes various specialty vehicles to perform essential services. One of these is a truck 
mounted asphalt patch truck for street repairs and maintenance. The existing truck has exhausted its useful life and needs 
replacement. Public Works has determined that to meet City goals, the replacement asphalt patch truck should be of 
greater capacity. For specialized equipment such as the asphalt patch truck, there are few procurement sources available 
to governmental agencies and are typically limited to contracts with either Sourcewell (formally NJPA) or the State of 
California General Services Division (GSD). Both Sourcewell and GSD conduct competitive bidding processes to select 
vendors and establish pricing on vehicles and equipment. The City can rely on the competitive bids obtained by Sourcewell 
and GSD in lieu of obtaining its own independent bids.
Public Works has selected PB Loader Corporation as the manufacturer that produces the best equipment for the City's 
needs. PB Loader is the sole source on the West Coast for PB Loader Equipment. Staff sought Sourcewell pricing through 
MME and PB Loader to assure best pricing available. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Sufficient funding is available in the Vehicle Replacement Fund (Fund 81).

environment and are therefore not projects subject to CEQA.

chassis, and authorize the Public Works Director to execute the purchase contract in substantially the form presented. 
BACKGROUND:
The Public Works Department utilizes various specialty vehicles to perform essential services. One of these is a truck-
mounted VAC-CON pipe chaser truck for sewer repairs and maintenance. The existing truck has exhausted its useful life 
and needs replacement. 
For specialized equipment such as the VAC-CON pipe chaser truck, there are very limited procurement sources available to 
governmental agencies and are typically limited to contracts with either Sourcewell (formally NJPA) or State of California 
General Services Division (GSD).  Both Sourcewell and GSD conduct competitive bidding processes to select vendors and 
establish pricing on vehicles and equipment.  The City can rely on the competitive bids obtained by Sourcewell and GSD in 
lieu of obtaining its own independent bids.
Public Works has selected VAC-CON, Inc., as the manufacturer that produces the best equipment for the City’s needs. 
Municipal Maintenance Equipment (MME) is the sole source in Northern California for VAC-CON Equipment. Staff sought 
Sourcewell pricing through MME and VAC-CON, Inc., to assure best pricing available. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Sufficient funding is available in the Vehicle Replacement Fund (Fund 81).

RECOMMENDATION:
Approve a contract with Sonsray Machinery of San Leandro for the purchase of one CASE 580SN Backhoe in the amount of 
$137,259.09; establish a contingency reserve in the amount of $14,000.00; and authorize the Public Works Director to 
execute the purchase contract in substantially the form presented and issue any change orders within the contingency 
amount. 
BACKGROUND:
The Public Works Department utilizes two backhoes to perform a variety of projects and general maintenance. One of the 
backhoes sustained a critical failure and was recently replaced. The second backhoe has reached the end of its service life 
and is due for replacement. Products from several manufacturers were evaluated as a replacement including CAT, Kubota, 
and John Deere. Staff determined CASE best meets the City’s needs based on the City’s experience with this equipment 
and its capabilities, as the current and previous backhoes are manufactured by CASE. Staff determined the CASE 580SN 
with options as specified in the attached agreement meets the needs of the department. 
For specialized equipment, such as backhoes, there are limited procurement sources available to government agencies and 
typically are limited to either Sourcewell (formerly NJPA) or State of California General Services Division (GSD). Both 
Sourcewell and GSD conduct competitive bidding processes to select vendors and establish pricing on vehicles and 
equipment.  The City can rely on the competitive bids obtained by Sourcewell and GSD in lieu of obtaining its own 
independent bids. The State GSD does not maintain a contract for purchasing CASE backhoes, so the City is relying on 
pricing obtained by Sourcewell. 
Staff identified two CASE dealers in Northern California and both were contacted to submit quotes for the CASE 580SN.  
Sonsray Machinery of San Leandro submitted a quote of $137,259.09, and Sequoia Equipment of Fresno submitted a 
quote of $142,858.47 via their Sourcewell contracts.  Staff recommends purchasing the CASE 580SN from Sonsray 
Machinery. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Sufficient funding is available in the Vehicle Replacement Fund (Fund 81). The contingency of $14,000 is to account for the 
potential annual price increase at the time of equipment delivery.

SUBJECT:
State Route 92 and 82 Interchange Landscape Improvements Project – Agreement
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve a cooperative agreement between the City of San Mateo and California State Department of Transportation for 
the State Route 92/State Route 82 Interchange Landscape Improvements Project for oversight and review services at no 
cost to the City of San Mateo and authorize the Public Works Director to execute the agreement in substantially the form 
presented. 
BACKGROUND:
Roadway improvements to reconfigure the State Route 92/State Route 82 El Camino Real (SR 92/SR 82) highway 
interchange were completed in August 2018 as part of a joint project between the City of San Mateo (City) and California 
State Department of Transportation (Caltrans). The City and Caltrans now desire to complete the next phase of the project, 
which involves restoration of landscaping within the interchange limits. The landscaping project includes clearing and 
grubbing, light grading and contouring, ground cover and tree planting, and irrigation improvements. 
The City is required to enter into a cooperative agreement with Caltrans in order to complete landscaping improvements 
within Caltrans right of way and obtain Caltrans review and oversight of the project. The City is currently under contract 
with BKF Engineers to design and develop construction documents for the landscape project. Public outreach for input on 
landscaping concepts will be conducted during the design phase.  Staff anticipates completing design and advertising for 
construction in Fall 2021, with completion of the project anticipated in Fall 2022.  Staff recommends approval of the 
cooperative agreement for this project.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Caltrans will be responsible for the cost of their staff time incurred for project oversight under this agreement. The design 
services and City staff costs are funded by Traffic Impact fees. The construction phase is partially funded and staff is 
currently in discussions with the San Mateo County Transportation Authority to identify the needed funds remaining. 
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This action of approving the Cooperative Agreement is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or 
administrative activity that will not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines 
Section 15378(b)(5).) 
The landscape restoration received environmental clearance when the project level initial study and negative declaration 
for the highway improvements project was approved in May 2014. Environmental clearance for the landscape work will be 
re-validated during the design phase.

SUBJECT:
Mobile Parking Revenue Collection – Agreement
RECOMMENDATION:
Approve an agreement with PaybyPhone Technologies Inc. for mobile parking revenue collection services in the amount of 
$219,000 and authorize the Public Works Director to execute the agreement in substantially the form presented.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo executed an agreement with IPS Group, Inc. in October 2019 for procurement and construction of 
the Downtown Parking Technology Project. As part of that project, IPS Group will install upgraded pay stations and parking 
meters throughout the downtown paid parking area. The pay station and meters will accept payment through cash, credit, 
and mobile payment sources.
The City of San Mateo previously initiated a pilot project with PaybyPhone Technologies, Inc. for mobile parking revenue 
systems in 2016. Following successful implementation in two surface parking lots, the City expanded the pilot to a parking 
garage and integrated with parking enforcement to fully test the capabilities of the system.
While IPS Group offers a mobile payment option, the City has opted to implement the PaybyPhone program throughout 
the downtown because it is a vendor that is already in service and well utilized by downtown users and is one that is 
common in local jurisdictions. Under this agreement, PaybyPhone will coordinate with IPS Group to integrate with all pay 
station and parking meters that will be installed in the downtown, offering patrons the option to pay for parking via their 
smartphone. By using a service currently implemented in the City, and that is common in nearby jurisdictions, the City 
hopes to increase the number of users opting for mobile payment.
There is a one-time setup fee of $1,500 to integrate the PaybyPhone system in the downtown. The City has historically 
absorbed credit card fees for parking transactions; similarly, the City will absorb the $0.25 transaction fee cost for each 
PaybyPhone transaction for the foreseeable future to encourage economic activity in the downtown. When more normal 
business operations resume, the City may re-evaluate this decision and modify to pass the transaction fee to the user. 
The agreement is estimated at $219,000 for the three-year term.  This assumes the City is paying the transaction fee for 
the full term and is based on an estimate of future users and expected annual transactions. If the City opts to pass the 
transaction fee to the user in the future, the City will no longer pay PaybyPhone directly. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
The one-time setup fee for implementation of PaybyPhone services throughout the Downtown paid parking area is $1,500. 
The City will pay transaction fees for PaybyPhone transactions ($.25 per transaction), estimated at $73,000 annually. 
Should the City opt to pass the transaction fee to the consumer, the City will no longer pay annually for this service.

SUBJECT:
Hillsdale Caltrain Station Bicycle Access Gap Closure – Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Funding
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to authorize an application with the San Mateo County Transportation Authority for Measure A and W 
Pedestrian and Bicycle Program Funding for the Hillsdale Caltrain Station Bicycle Access Gap Closure project.
BACKGROUND:
On August 7, 2020, the San Mateo County Transportation Authority released a Call for Projects for grant funding from the 
Measure A and W Pedestrian and Bicycle Program. The funds through this grant program are intended to support projects 
that improve bicycling and walking accessibility and safety to encourage greater use of active transportation modes. 
Measure A was reauthorized in 2004 and is a half-cent sales tax for transportation improvements. Three percent of 
revenue from Measure A is available through this grant program. Measure W was authorized by voters in 2018 and is a 
new half-cent sales tax measure for transportation improvement implementation. Five percent of Measure W revenue is 
available through this grant program. 
Staff proposes submitting a grant application for the environmental and design phase of the Hillsdale Caltrain Station 
Bicycle Access Gap Closure project. The project will serve to connect the 28th Avenue Bicycle Boulevard (scheduled to 
begin construction in November 2020) to the new Hillsdale Caltrain Station at 28th Avenue via a short segment of Class IV 
separated bicycle lanes. The project will also include intersection and signal modifications to create greater safety and 
comfort for bicyclists and pedestrians to cross El Camino Real and access the new station via a Class I bicycle and 
pedestrian path that is being constructed as part of the current grade separation project. The grade separation project 
Class I bicycle and pedestrian path will provide access for bicyclists and pedestrians to the new station platform at 28th 
Avenue. The project estimate is $170,000 for design and environmental review, including Caltrans coordination and review 
for required permitting to implement modifications at the El Camino Real intersection.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The submittal of the grant application for $170,000 has no impact to the General Fund. If the application is selected for 
funding, there is a 10 percent minimum local match requirement (estimated at $17,000). Staff intends to allocate funds 
from the Measure A Local Streets and Transportation funding to fulfill the local match in the FY 2021-22 budget.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This project was identified in the 2020 Bicycle Master Plan, which was determined to be Categorically Exempt under CEQA 
per Section 15304 Minor Alterations to Land – Class 4, Subsection (h).

SUBJECT:
Clean Water Program Costs – Loan Approval
RECOMMENDATION:
Adopt a Resolution to approve documents and actions relating to a San Mateo - Foster City Public Financing Authority 
Water Infrastructure Finance and Innovation Act Loan.
BACKGROUND:
The City of San Mateo (City) and the City of Foster City/Estero Municipal Improvement District (District) are co-owners of a 
wastewater treatment plant (the “WWTP”) operated by the City of San Mateo. Ownership and operation are governed 
pursuant to an operating joint exercise of powers agreement. Under that agreement, as amended, the City pays 75.12% of 
the capital costs associated with WWTP improvements. 
In 2009, the Regional Water Quality Control Board issued Cease and Desist Order No. R2-2009-0020 requiring the City, the 
Town of Hillsborough, and the Crystal Springs County Sanitation District to take specific actions to eliminate the 
occurrence of sanitary sewer overflows. In order to comply with the Cease and Desist Order, the City developed a 
preliminary 10-year Integrated Wastewater Master Plan (Clean Water Program) calling for a major upgrade to the WWTP 
and improvements to the City’s collection system. Current estimated costs for all improvements are approximately $1 
billion. The City’s share is $854 million, of which $340.6 million is for collection and $513.9 million is for the WWTP. The 
$513.9 million includes the shares attributable to Hillsborough ($30.8 million), the Crystal Springs County Sanitation 
District ($13.6 million) and the County of San Mateo ($760,000) (collectively, the Regional Customers). The Regional 
Customers will repay the City over time for their respective shares via an annual capacity charge. 
The City and the District will require financing to complete these improvements. The City and the District established the 
San Mateo – Foster City Public Financing Authority (Authority) in 2016 to facilitate the financing of both agencies’ capital 
contributions to the WWTP upgrade project. The Authority may be used by either member to facilitate any capital 
financing.

The plan of finance consists of six funding sources: (1) loans to each city for the WWTP from the United States government

enterprise, (5) a loan from the State of California issued under the State’s Revolving Fund (SRF) loan program, and (6)

will be its next source of funding. 
Cash – Pay-go financing, which carries no interest charge, is the least expensive way to finance capital projects. The City’s 
municipal advisors have developed a cash flow and debt financing financial projection model to estimate the optimal 
combination of loans (WIFIA and SRF), revenue bonds, and cash for the City’s share of the WWTP and its collection system 
capital needs. Free cash flow not necessary for reserves and the collection system will be applied to WWTP capital costs. 
This amount is expected to be approximately $172.3 million through 2027. 
Revenue Bonds – The Authority sold $270 million in revenue bonds in 2019 for Clean Water Program costs. The bonds 
were marketed with an original issue premium (the coupons attached to maturity dates were higher than market yields for

$20 million was applied to pay off a sewer enterprise State Street Bank line of credit, $18.2 million to reimburse the sewer

BUDGET IMPACT:
The sale of the WIFIA Bond will cause the sewer enterprise to assume additional debt service payments. This debt is 
necessary to fund a portion of the City’s share of WWTP capital costs. The WIFIA Bond will be repaid over 30 years with 
increasing debt service, beginning in fiscal year 2025-26. The rate at which debt service increases is designed to use 
projected future surplus more effectively, keep debt service coverage (revenues pledged for debt service divided by debt 
service) relatively constant in a range between 1.55x and 1.65x (see chart on next page), and avoid large near-term sewer 
service rate increases. The interest rate on the WIFIA Loan is currently estimated at 1.32%, which is lower than what the 
City could obtain in the municipal bond market. In addition, the WIFIA Bond can be prepaid in whole or in part on any 
payment date without penalty, which is a more advantageous optional redemption term than what is available in the 
municipal bond market.
