5.   OLD BUSINESS
Main Library Limited Reopening Plans:  City Librarian James Moore provided details on the plans for a 
limited reopening at the Main Library:

Limited reopening date is set for December 1, 2020.

Staff will not handle cash until further notice.  All fine payments can be done using debit/credit 
cards at the self-service checkout stations.

Reopening will be phased in carefully, and social distancing, the use of masks, and hand 
sanitizer will be required.

For the first few weeks, services will be limited to self-checkout of holds.  Patrons will also be 
able to submit applications online for library cards, and pick them up in person.  A maximum of 
eight people will be allowed in the lobby at a time, and the restrooms and parking garage will 
not be open.

For the second phase, limited computer use will be offered with one-hour appointments and 
30-minute drop-in time slots offered.  The maximum number of patrons allowed in the building 
during this phase will be increased to fifteen.  Other services being considered in this phase are 
same-day requests for materials, a limited browsing collection, and reopen the restrooms.  A 
date has not been set for this phase yet.

6.   NEW BUSINESS
A.
Library Rules of Conduct Policy Review:  Mr. Moore and the Library Board members reviewed the 
Library Rules of Conduct Policy to see where amendments are appropriate to accommodate a 
limited reopening of the Main Library on December 1, 2020.  The policy addendum that was 
created when curbside service was implemented, and later updated for the Donation Day at the 
Library, includes rules to protect the health and safety of staff and patrons during the coronavirus 
pandemic.  These rules include: 1) patrons should not come to the Library if they are sick, 2) 
patrons should remain 6 feet apart, and 3) patrons should wear a mask.  Since these services have 
only been offered outside of the Library building, it was agreed that the addendum should be 
amended to add the requirement that patrons and staff socially distance and wear a mask inside 
the building as well.  Other amendments that were agreed upon include the requirement to use 
hand sanitizer, to prohibit food and drink inside the Library, and to include the self-screening 
requirements that are listed in the City of San Mateo’s COVID-19 Workplace Protection Policy.  In 
addition, the Board members requested that the Library Volunteers Rules of Conduct Policy During 
COVID-19 Pandemic be amended to include the self-screening requirements that are listed in the 
City of San Mateo’s COVID-19 Workplace Protection Policy.  Mr. Moore agreed to these requests 
and advised that he will prepare these two draft policies and send them to the Board members to 
review at least one week before the next meeting on November 23, 2020.
B.
San Mateo Public Library Computer, Internet and Wireless Access Policy Review:  Mr. Moore and 
the Library Board members reviewed the San Mateo Public Library Computer, Internet and Wireless 
Access Policy to see where amendments are appropriate to accommodate the second phase of the 
limited reopening of the Main Library that would allow limited computer use.  Mr. Moore said 
computer use would be initially available for a maximum of one hour per day on an appointment 
basis, and 30 minutes per day on a drop-in basis.  Dr. Chen asked if it would be possible for a longer 
maximum time limit.  Mr. Moore said he will consult with the management team and update the 
Board members with their feedback at the next meeting on November 23, 2020.

Library Rules of Conduct Policy
[Revision Adopted by the Library Board of Trustees, 9/14/20] 
The Library is a resource for all members of the community and its purpose is to provide 
information services, programs and educational, informational, and recreational materials 
to the public. Any behavior that disrupts the Library’s ability to provide a safe and 
welcoming environment for the public and a supportive workplace for library employees 
shall be grounds for immediate action. Follow-up may include requesting person(s) to 
leave the Library; barring future services, including admission (temporarily or 
permanently); and calling the police. 
Prohibited behaviors in the Library may include but are not limited to the following:

Vandalizing or misusing furniture, equipment, or materials

Harassment of any kind toward other library users and library staff 

Causing a public disturbance, which includes, but is not limited to, use of abusive or obscene 
language; talking loudly; or making excessive noise 

Viewing obscene content on library computers or personal devices; or other violations of the 
Computer Use Policy

Disobeying the direction of a library staff member

Leaving children unattended [Library employees are not responsible for unattended children]

Blocking entrances, exits, aisles, walkways, or stairways

Monopolizing Library space or resources including moving furniture

Bringing bicycles inside the Library

Using rollerblades, scooters, or skateboards on Library property**

Using the restroom inappropriately, such as bathing or laundering 

Strong pervasive odors 

Sleeping [as a safety precaution, staff will awaken persons sleeping on Library property*]

Soliciting or panhandling

Smoking on Library property*

Bringing animals other than service animals inside the Library

Not setting cell phones or devices on silent mode; and speaking on cell phones or devices in 
spaces other than designated  “phone zones”

Consuming food and beverages that are purchased outside the Library

Leaving personal items unattended [the Library is not responsible for personal belongings 
lost or stolen on Library property*]

Entering the Library without wearing shoes or a shirt.
To prevent the theft of Library materials, state law authorizes the detention for a reasonable 
period of any person using these facilities when there is probable cause to believe that the 
person being detained is unlawfully removing Library materials from the premises. Staff has 
legal authority to conduct a limited and reasonable search of purses, bags, parcels, briefcases 
and other packages if a patron does not voluntarily surrender unlawfully removed Library 
materials.  (CA Penal Code 490.5)
* Library property includes outdoor areas adjacent to the Library building.
** If brought inside the library, scooters and skateboards must be carried; scooters must be in the folded position.

Enforcement of Violations
To ensure a safe and welcoming environment for the public and a supportive workplace for library 
employees, violations of the Rules of Conduct are enforced by Library employees with assistance by the 
San Mateo Police Department. Suspensions are determined by the type of infraction and are issued by the 
City Librarian. Library patrons whose library privileges are suspended cannot use any of the San Mateo 
libraries while serving a suspension. The City Librarian reserves the right to make individualized 
determinations as to the appropriate level of enforcement.
Level One:  Library staff will document instances of minor violations. Staff may direct patron to leave the 
library if verbal warnings are not heeded within the same day; and patron will not be allowed to enter until 
the following day. A written warning will be issued by the Deputy City Librarian if misconduct continues over 
a period of time. A suspension (banning) letter will be issued by the City Librarian if the written warning is 
not heeded.

entering without wearing shoes/shirt; using rollerblades, scooters,

Librarian if misconduct continues over a period of time. A suspension (banning) letter will be issued by the

1. This Order will take effect immediately and will continue to be in effect until it is extended,

2. As used in this Order, a “Face Covering” means a covering made of cloth, fabric, or other soft or 
permeable material, without holes, that covers only the nose and mouth and surrounding areas of the 
lower face.  A covering that hides or obscures the wearer’s eyes or forehead is not a Face Covering.  
Examples of Face Coverings include a scarf or bandana; a neck gaiter; a homemade covering made 
from a t-shirt, sweatshirt, or towel, held on with rubber bands or otherwise; or a mask, which need 
not be medical-grade.  A Face Covering may be factory-made or may be handmade and improvised 
from ordinary household materials.  The Face Covering should be comfortable, so that the wearer 
can breathe comfortably through the nose and does not have to adjust it frequently, so as to avoid 
touching the face.  For as long as medical grade masks such as N95 masks and surgical masks are in 
short supply, members of the public should not purchase those masks as Face Coverings under this 
Order; those medical grade masks should be reserved for health care providers and first responders.  
In general, even when not required by this Order, people are strongly encouraged to wear Face 
Coverings when in public.  Also, for Face Coverings that are not disposed of after each use, people 
should clean them frequently and have extra ones available so that they have a clean one available 
for use.

Chromebook Café
We started Chromebook Café with 5 Chromebooks from the Children’s Makerspace so it would not 
affect the Chromebooks used by the community through the lending program. Chromebook Café was 
offered from September 1st until October 1st, then again October 14th – November 5th.  These 
Chromebooks were managed by Adriana Valencia, Supervising Librarian for Children’s and Literacy 
Services, and were not on the Google Management Software as the Chromebooks offered through 
Alternative Lending. As a result, they could not be used long term outside the library without staff due 
to the increased possibility of theft.
Program Overview:
We positioned four, six foot tables along the building by the parking garage elevator, along with one 
chair at each table. These tables and chairs were provided by Mateo (library outreach vehicle) and 
typically used for outreach. In addition, we had a Purell hand sanitizer dispensing stand available that 
was shared with the Storytime in the Park program.
Members of the public could drop in to use a Chromebook for 30min intervals, if no one was waiting, 
they could stay longer. We did not record any sign ups, so no email or library card was required. Prior to 
starting their session, patrons must review the written policy for computer use and Wi-Fi use, as well as 
agree to maintain social distancing. A copy was provided with each table for their reference. Hand 
sanitization was encouraged but not required as the computer and attached mouse were wiped down 
after each user. Users could use the web browser but were not able to download apps or software. If 
the user wished to enable sound, they needed to use their own headphones. 
Staff cleared the downloads and cache as well as browsing history after each use. Staff had access to an 
alcohol solution for wiping down the technology and area between users to meet the increased 
sanitization needs.
After the first two weeks, staff experienced issues with connecting to the building’s Wi-Fi and shifted to 
using a Mi-Fi instead, to offer more consistent internet connectivity. A Mi-Fi was used for the rest of the 
pilot period through the first week of November. 
After 3 weeks, staff decided to offer free printing limited to three pages in black and white. This 
additional free service did not increase participation in the pilot by large numbers. During the first 
month, the program had only six members of the public actually use the Chromebook Café.  Six other 
participants took advantage of the service after being referred by Project Read to meet with a staff or 
their tutor for help accessing their own Chromebooks. During the last half of the pilot, there were an 
additional 8 users whom took advantage of internet access and printing.
At the end of the entire program there were a total of 14 (actual) Chromebook Café users.
Recommendation:
We would not recommend continuing to offer Chromebook Café at the Main Library. There was not 
sufficient community interest to continue this program in the current capacity providing for social 
distancing. Efforts should be redirected to prepare to offer computer use when the library is able to

If there is a desire to continue, then the program would require access to Chromebooks from the Tech

Considerations for alternative programs:
Should there be a desire to expand such services, the following alternatives could be considered:
1.
Providing internet access off site at higher traffic areas in the communities that need them. 
For example working with community partners such as the King Center, local parks or 
marketplaces with ample parking lots to accommodate an outdoor setup. 
Additional considerations: dedicated chromebooks from the tech lending program, 
access to portable electricity, safety items (PPE), protection from the elements and 
space.
2.
Provide users access to pay for their own print jobs with less restrictions. This would mean 
providing for payment terminals at the site and easier access to the printer for black and 
white or colored print jobs. This would be difficult to do considering our current 
circumstances where staff are not comfortable accepting payment and there are no 
portable payment terminals.
3.
Having staff provide internet or device instruction. This is currently not possible with the 
required social distancing mandates using the resources currently available.
