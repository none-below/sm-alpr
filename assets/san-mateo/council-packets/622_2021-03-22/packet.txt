5.   NEW BUSINESS
A.  Introduce New Library Board Member Rod Linhares:  City Librarian James Moore introduced new 
Library Board Member Rod Linhares to the Board.  Mr. Moore shared that Mr. Linhares has been active on 
the San Mateo Library Foundation Board for the past 4+ years, and his work with the Foundation has 
demonstrated his passion and enthusiastic support for the Library.  
B.  Homebound Delivery Pilot Program and Sustainable Senior-Specific Outreach and Delivery Program:  
Mr. Moore provided an overview of the pilot home delivery service that was recently implemented.  Home 
delivery of library materials will be offered once a month during the pandemic while the libraries are 
closed to the public, and will be limited to ten items per patron.  The delivery will be contactless and 
scheduled with a delivery window.  Materials eligible for delivery include books, audiobooks, music and 
movies that are owned by San Mateo Public Library (SMPL).  The requirements for a patron to qualify for 
this service are: 1) they must be a resident of San Mateo or Hillsborough; 2) they possess or be eligible for 
a SMPL library card; and 3) be unable to visit the library because of a temporary or long-term illness, injury, 
impairment, medical condition, or disability.  Discussion ensued on the possible use of Mateo, the library’s 
community outreach vehicle, for this pilot home delivery service.  It was agreed that the use of Mateo 
would not be practical for this pilot home delivery service, but could possibly be used for future outreach, 
including outreach to seniors.  Mr. Moore shared that enhanced outreach to seniors was the priority for 
the Library that was identified by the City Council at their annual goal-setting meeting in January.  Mr.

Moore said there will be future discussion with City Council regarding improving outreach to seniors, and 
that this topic will be discussed in more detail during future Board meetings.
C.  Library Operational Outlook for Calendar Year 2021:  Mr. Moore highlighted the key initiatives from the 
Library’s 2020-2021 Work Plan including: 1) improve Library user experience; 2) build a sustainable
 outreach/marketing program; and 3) equity, diversity, inclusion, and social justice engagement.  Mr.
 Moore said that a date has not been set for the Library’s plans for a limited reopening, but that staff has
 made the necessary preparations for the lobby to reopen once San Mateo County moves to a less
 restrictive color coded tier.  Discussion ensued on whether just the lobby or the entire building should be
 accessible upon reopening.  Board members provided feedback and suggestions including availing 
 outside areas for patron use at all three library locations, such as the courtyard at the Main Library and
 the parking lots at Hillsdale and Marina Libraries.  Mr. Moore said there will be further discussion on this
 topic at future Board meetings.
D.  Day in the District Overview: Mr. Moore provided an overview of the annual Day in the District event.  
There will be three legislators this year and the meetings will be virtual.  The first has been planned for 
March 11 at 3:30pm with Senator Josh Becker.  The dates for the other two meetings with Congresswoman 
Jackie Speier and Assembly member Kevin Mullin are to be determined.  Once the dates are set, Mr. 
Moore will advise the Board.  Since the meetings are limited to 30 minutes, Mr. Moore and the Board 
discussed what priority for libraries should be discussed with the legislators.  Mr. Moore and the Board 
agreed on advocating for Broadband Equity, Lifelong Learning as well as Equipping California Libraries to 
Address Disaster and Infrastructure Needs.  Mr. Hill made a motion to advocate for Lifelong Learning that 
emcompasses early education, adult & youth literacy, career support, economic development, after-school 
programming, veterans services and assistance to vulnerable populations, Dr. Chen seconded this motion, 
and the motion passed 5-0. 

Moved:  Mr. Hill, Seconded: Dr. Chen

Ayes:      Mr. Hill, Dr. Chen, Ms. Diaz Nash, Ms. De Winter and Mr. Linhares

Noes:     None
          
7.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Reports by Board Members:

Ms. De Winter said curbside service is going great and she appreciates the flexibility of being able 
to get her materials even if she has arrived after her appointment time.

Mr. Hill spent some time talking with Monika Lee, a member of the City of San Mateo Senior 
Commission, and learned that the City of San Mateo has applied to be an Age Friendly City.  Ms. 
Lee is on a task force that has been created to begin to identify and build connections with 
organizations in San Mateo that can help the City’s Age Friendly Initiative, and Mr. Hill suggested 
that Ms. Lee make a presentation to the Board.  The Board agreed to invite Ms. Lee to the next 
meeting on March 22.

Mr. Linhares shared that he is eager to advocate on behalf of the library.

Dr. Chen expressed his appreciation that Mr. Linhares prepared thoroughly for his first Board 
meeting by reading all the meeting materials and asking thoughtful questions. 

Ms. Diaz Nash thanked the Foundation Board and staff for being able to deliver such a rich grant to 
the Library.  Per the minutes of the November 23, 2020, Ms. Diaz Nash asked about the possibility 
of a resolution to honor two Library donors, and also about doing something to honor former 
Board Member Kathy Shields.  A discussion ensued about what the past practice has been in these 
two types of occurrences, and it was decided that to maintain consistency these two actions would 
not take place.  Ms. Diaz Nash also made a suggestion to add a discussion on how the Library will 
function post pandemic as an agenda topic for the March meeting.

Report by Lynne Esselstein, Executive Director of the Foundation:

In January, the Foundation wrote a check to the Library for $130,000 as one of the two biannual 
grants that the Foundation gives to the Library.

The Foundation received the second round of Paycheck Protection Program funding.  To qualify for 
this funding, a 25% diminution of overall quarterly comparisons must be demonstrated.  This 
funding will cover overhead, and ensure that donors’ donations can go to the Library.

The Authors Gala Spring Fundraiser will be held on April 20 and will be virtual this year.  The April 
20 date is noteworthy because it was on this date in 1905 that San Mateo Public Library received a 
Carnegie grant to build the first library.  Admission to the Authors Gala will be free, but attendees 
will be encouraged to donate a minimum of $25.00.  There will be fundraising throughout the 
event.  Creative programming is being planned including a poetry contest for children in grades 4 
through 8.  Librarians will help promote the contest and the theme will be “Eureka”, a California 
centric theme that is tied in with the two featured authors who have written westerns and have 
ties to California.  
Report by City Librarian James Moore:

Mr. Moore has been talking with volunteers from AARP regarding the annual tax preparation 
service they provide at the Library.  Planning is in progress for a way to provide this service in a 
safe way.  The date to begin this service has been delayed and will most likely start sometime in 
March and run through June.

SUBJECT:
Standing Calendar for Library Board Meetings
RECOMMENDATION:
The City Librarian will provide information on the setting of a standing calendar of Library Board meetings for each 
calendar year.
BACKGROUND:
The City Council and all other San Mateo Boards and Commissions publish their meeting dates as a standing calendar, 
instead of setting a new calendar of dates each year. Currently, the Library Board meetings are set on a fiscal year 
calendar, while all other City Boards and Commissions are set on a calendar year schedule. To get aligned with the rest of 
the City, The Library Board needs to post on the City website that meetings are held on the 4th Monday of each month, 
with no meeting in December, and set a standing meet calendar based on the entire calendar year. Also, the Library Board 
would need to post that if the 4th Monday falls on a holiday (such as Memorial Day does some years), then the meeting 
would be held on the following Tuesday.
