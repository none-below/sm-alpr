5.   OLD BUSINESS
A.  Update on Library Reopening Planning:  City Librarian James Moore shared the reopening plan 
for the Library.  Beginning March 29, there will be a staggered approach to reintroducing services, 
beginning with in-person pickup of holds and checkout in the lobby and limited computer use by 
appointment.  Service at the branches will start at the same time as the Main Library and the hours 
will be the same as the current curbside hours are with the exception of an added time slot of 10:00 
a.m. to 2:00 p.m. on Mondays at Main.  No appointment will be necessary for hold pickup.  The 
maximum number of patrons allowed in the building at the Main Library will be limited to thirteen 
at one time and COVID-19 safety precautions must be followed.  The next phase of reopening will be 
to open all three floors at Main for browsing with limited access to computers and limited 
occupancy.  Branches will also open simultaneously to match services with limited computer access 
and limited occupancy.  Consideration will be given to reopening the Friends of the Library book sale 
area.  The anticipated date of this next phase of reopening is early May.    
B.  Visual and Audio Recording of Library Board Meetings:  Mr. Moore shared a directive from the

6.   NEW BUSINESS
       A.  Standing Calendar for Library Board Meetings:  Mr. Moore shared a directive from the City Clerk 
that all the City’s Boards and Commissions have a standing calendar of meetings for each calendar 
year.  In the past, the Library Board set their calendar of meeting dates based on each fiscal year.  
Meetings will be held on the 4th Monday of each month, and if the 4th Monday of the month falls on 
a holiday, the meeting will be held on the following Tuesday.  There will not be a meeting in 
December. 
       B.  Presentation on the Age Friendly City Initiative by Monika Lee of the San Mateo Senior Citizen 
Commission:  Monika Lee of the San Mateo Senior Commission and the Age-Friendly Community 
Task Force shared information about the City of San Mateo’s designation as an Age-Friendly City by 
the World Health Organization for its commitment to being a welcoming community for people of 
all ages.  San Mateo received this distinction with the help of the Age-Friendly Community Task 
Force comprised of primarily older adults in the community.  The task force, in collaboration with 
the Senior Commission, is committed to conducting a baseline assessment on the needs of older 
adults, establishing a 3-year action plan to address the identified needs, and developing tools to 
measure progress.  There are eight domains of an Age-Friendly City that focus on the physical and 
social environment of a community including:

Outdoor spaces and buildings

Transportation

Housing

Social participation

Respect and social inclusion

Civic participation and employment

Communication and information

Community support and health services
Ms. Lee, the Board of Trustees and Mr. Moore agreed to collaborate in the future.  Ms. Lee said 
some ways in which the Library can help the taskforce includes highlighting library services for 
seniors, helping to disseminate information to the community from the Age-Friendly Community 
Task Force, and helping the taskforce identify the best ways for seniors to get to the Hillsdale and 
Marina branches, something the Library is already working on. Discussion also ensued on possible 
future collaborative projects including intergenerational programs, and it was agreed to continue to 
discuss this topic at future meetings.

Mr. Moore was advised by Bob Gaines, the City of San Mateo’s lobbyist in Washington, D.C.,

Foundation helped the Library purchase.  It took approximately one month to process and 
set up the Chromebooks upon their arrival in late February.  The Library’s IT Specialist has 
completed that process and the Chromebooks are available for checkout by Library patrons.

The SMPL Homebound Delivery Service pilot program is off to a modest start.  Mr. Moore 
said that volunteers assist with the long-running home delivery service offered by the Palo 
Alto Library, and that may be a possibility in the future with SMPL.  So far, half of the 20 
slots have been reserved for the SMPL Homebound Delivery Service pilot.  Although these 
numbers are consistent with historical numbers from the previous service that operated in 
years past at SMPL, the librarians in charge of the pilot have expanded their marketing 
efforts to see if there are more people who can be reached.  A sustainable program can be a 
relatively inexpensive program if volunteers are used and numbers remain manageable with 
current staffing.  However, it may require that the Library’s part-time Community Service 
Coordinator position be expanded to a full-time position, or that additional hours are 
allotted for current part-time staff if the program grows substantially due to demand.

2.78.030 DIRECTOR—POWERS AND DUTIES.
There is created the office of director of emergency services. The City Manager shall 
be the director of emergency services.
The director is empowered:
(a) To request the City Council to proclaim the existence or threatened existence of a 
local emergency and the termination thereof, if the City Council is in session, or to 
issue such proclamation if the City Council is not in session, subject to the 
confirmation by the City Council at the earliest practicable time;
(b) To request through the mayor that the governor proclaim a state of emergency 
when in the opinion of the director the resources of the area or region are inadequate 
to cope with the emergency;
(c) To control and direct the effort of the emergency services organization of this city 
for the accomplishment of the purposes of this chapter;
(d) To direct coordination and cooperation between divisions, services and staff of the 
emergency services organization of this city, and to resolve questions of authority and 
responsibility that may arise between them;
(e) To represent the emergency services organization of this city in all dealings with 
public or private agencies pertaining to emergency services.
(Ord. No. 2007-14 § 2; Ord. No. 1980-10 § 1; prior code § 13.05.)
2.78.040 DIRECTOR—ADDITIONAL POWERS AND 
DUTIES.
In the event of the proclamation of a local emergency as provided in this chapter, as 
the proclamation of a state of emergency by the governor or the director of the State 
Office of Emergency Services, or the existence of a state of war emergency, the 
director is empowered:
(a) To make and issue rules and regulations on matters reasonably related to the 
protection of life and property as affected by such emergency; provided, however, 
such rules and regulations must be confirmed at the earliest practicable time by the 
City Council;

(b) To obtain vital supplies, equipment, and such other properties found lacking and 
needed for the protection of life and property, and bind the City for the fair value 
thereof, and if required immediately, to commandeer the same for public use;
(c) To require emergency service of any city officer or employee, and, in the event of 
a proclamation of a state of emergency by the governor in the county in which this 
city is located or the existence of a state of war emergency, to command the aid of as 
many citizens of the community as he or she determines are necessary in the 
execution of his or her duties; such persons shall be entitled to all privileges, benefits 
and immunities as are provided by State law for registered and disaster service 
workers;
(d) To requisition necessary personnel or material of any city department or agency;
(e) To execute all of his or her ordinary powers as city manager, all of the special 
powers conferred upon him or her by this chapter or by resolution adopted pursuant 
thereto, all powers conferred upon him or her by any statute, agreement approved by 
the City Council, or by any other lawful authority, and in conformity with Section 
38791 of the Government Code, to exercise complete authority over the City and to 
exercise all police power vested in the City by the constitution and general laws.
