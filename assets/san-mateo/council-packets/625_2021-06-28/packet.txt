A.  Update on Efforts to Return Library to Normalized Pre-Pandemic Operations:  City Librarian 
James Moore shared his monthly progress report with the Library Board of Trustees on the steps 
being taken to restore the Library to full pre-pandemic operations.  On July 6th, all three Library 
locations will open at approximately 75% capacity of the previous service hours.  The Main Library 
will be open from 10am to 6pm on Mondays and Wednesdays, 12-7pm on Tuesdays and Thursdays, 
10am to 5pm on Fridays and Saturdays, and 1-5pm on Sundays.  The Marina Library will be open 
from 1-6pm on Mondays, 11am to 4pm on Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Thursdays, 12-5pm on 
Fridays, 1-5pm on Saturdays, and closed on Sundays.  The Hillsdale Library will be open from 11am 
to 4pm on Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays, 1-6pm on Tuesdays, 12-5pm on Fridays, 1-5pm on 
Saturdays, and closed on Sundays.  A full reopening at 100% of pre-pandemic operations is 
tentatively scheduled by the first week of September.  The announcement of expanded hours will go 
out to the public on June 15th.  Mr. Moore is planning a conduct a survey to gather community input 
on whether the pre-pandemic Library hours are serving the public adequately, or if the schedule 
could be improved to better serve the community.  The Library will neither cut or expand current

       A.  Review and Adoption of Library Policies: Policy for Release of Information Concerning Patrons 
and  Policy for Maintaining Library Policies :  The Policy for Maintaining Library Policies was originally 
adopted by the Library Board of Trustees on March 27, 2010.  Pursuant to this policy, Library policies 
are to be reviewed at least every five years and updated if necessary.  The Trustees reviewed this 
policy to determine if there should be any revisions.  The Trustees also made a request to have this 
policy posted on the Library’s website.  A motion was made by Ms. De Winter to approve renewal of 
this policy without any revisions, seconded by Mr. Linhares, and the motion passed 5-0.

Moved:  Ms. De Winter, Seconded: Mr. Linhares

Ayes:  Ms. De Winter, Mr. Linhares, Ms. Diaz Nash, Mr. Hill and Dr. Chen

Noes:  None
The Policy for Release of Information Concerning Library Patrons was last revised by the Library 
Board of Trustees on March 27, 2010.  Mr. Moore presented some suggested revisions to the policy 
including in the first paragraph: 1) adding “patron use records” to the list of records that are 
confidential in accordance with the California Public Records Act (Government Code Section 6254), 
and 2) changing “Superior Court” to “court order”.  In the second paragraph, Mr. Moore suggested 
adding “the term patron use records includes written or electronic records that identify the patron, 
the patron’s borrowing information, or use of library resources, including database search records 
and any other personally identifiable information requests or inquiries”.  Trustees asked questions 
including 1) Can patrons opt out of statistical reports?; 2) How is privacy protected by third party 
vendors such as Hoopla or Kanopy?; and 3) Why is the confidentiality of library circulation records 
not extended to statistical reports of registration and circulation or to records of fines collected by 
the library?  Mr. Moore will consult with the IT Director of Peninsula Library Systems (PLS) to obtain 
this information.  This topic is continued for further discussion at the June 28th meeting.  [Update-
Mr. Moore obtained answers to these questions as follows:  1) There is not an option for patrons to 
not be counted as part of the anonymous aggregate data that San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) 
gathers to evaluate and improve service and create statistical reports and the Annual Report; 2) PLS 
does not track specific patron usage information and bars our vendors from doing so as well; and 3) 
Confidentiality of library circulation records are not extended to statistical reports because it allows 
SMPL to collect and share statistical information anonymously in order to improve services and 
provide accurate and informative statistical information in reports.  This topic is continued to the 
next meeting for the Trustees to vote on Mr. Moore’s suggested revisions to the policy.

Mr. Linhares visited the Main and Hillsdale Libraries and conducted his own informal survey.  
He asked approximately twenty patrons if they missed the Library, and if so, if they missed it

Policy for Release of Information 
Concerning Library Patrons 
                                       
 
In accordance with the California Public Records Act (Government Code Section 6254), 
library registration and circulation records are confidential.  It is the policy of the San 
Mateo Public Library to protect the privacy rights of all individuals by only disclosing 
such records to persons acting within the scope of their duties in the administration of 
the library, pursuant to written authorization by the individual to whom the records 
pertain or by order of the Superior Court. 
 
The San Mateo Public Library considers registration records to include verification of the 
fact that an individual is a registered borrower and any information required to become 
an eligible borrower such as name, contact information, etc.  Circulation records include 
any information which identifies the books and/or other materials borrowed by 
particular patrons.  
 
The confidentiality of library circulation records does not extend to statistical reports of 
registration and circulation or to records of fines collected by the library.  Information 
regarding delinquent accounts of library patrons may also be provided to collection 
agencies retained by the library to collect on delinquent accounts.
