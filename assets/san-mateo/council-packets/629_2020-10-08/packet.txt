RECOMMENDATION:
Accept and file the basic financial statements for the San Mateo - Foster City Public Financing Authority for the fiscal year 
ended June 30, 2020.
BACKGROUND:
The basic financial statements for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2020 marked the second fiscal year in which the Authority 
had financial activity to account for. The Authority's sole purpose is to issue debt on behalf of its member agencies, the 
City of San Mateo and the City of Foster City/Estero Municipal Improvement District. The Authority ended the fiscal year 
with $571.1 million in total assets and $630.7 million in total liabilities, which results in a net position of $(59.1) million. 
The changes in assets, liabilities, and net position reflect issuance of $45.8 million in lease revenue bonds on behalf of the 
City of San Mateo to finance capital improvement costs of North Shoreview flood control and city-wide street 
rehabilitation. As the Authority makes debt service payments, it will concurrently amortize the bond premiums from the 
2019 Wastewater Bonds and the 2020 Bonds, thus its net position will gradually approach $0. 
The Authority's operating income largely reflects the financing agreements between the member agencies and the 
Authority related to the 2019 Wastewater Bonds and the 2020 Bonds. In the fiscal year ended June 30, 2020, this 
amounted to $12.8 million.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Acceptance of the basic financial statements has no impact on the Authority's budget.

AUTHORITY PROFILE
The San Mateo – Foster City Public Financing Authority was formed through a joint exercise of powers 
agreement between the City of San Mateo (San Mateo) and the City of Foster City/Estero Municipal 
Improvement District (Foster City) to finance the capital improvements of each member agency. Both 
member agencies are located in San Mateo County and are situated south of San Francisco and north 
of San Jose. Both have a Council-Manager form of government and are full service cities that provide 
a broad range of services, including police, street construction and maintenance, sewer, parks, 
recreation, planning, and engineering. San Mateo is a charter city, while Foster City is a general law 
city. 
ECONOMIC CONTEXT
The information presented in the financial statements is perhaps best understood when it is considered 
from a broader perspective of the specific environment in which the Authority operates.  The 
Authority’s operating revenues are remitted from its member agencies, San Mateo and Foster City, 
pursuant to the respective financing agreements that ultimately reflect each member agency’s 
respective share of debt service. The debt service for the 2019 Bonds is backed by the net revenues of 
each member agency’s sewer enterprise funds. As such, the economic stability of the member agencies’ 
sewer enterprise has a direct impact on the Authority’s long-term financial sustainability. In February 
2020, the Authority issued $46 million in lease revenue bonds on behalf of the City of San Mateo to 
finance the cost of North Shoreview flood control improvements and city-wide street rehabilitation 
capital improvements. As such, the debt service for the 2020 Bonds is solely the responsibility of the 
City of San Mateo, and is backed by revenues held by the Trustee in any fund or account established 
under the indenture with the City. 
The member agencies’ service area includes the City of San Mateo, City of Foster City, and portions of 
the Town of Hillsborough, City of Belmont, Crystal Springs County Sanitation District, and some 
unincorporated areas of San Mateo County. The service area includes over 41,000 customer accounts 
and a population of approximately 170,000. The customer base is primarily residential: 93.6% for San 
Mateo and 96.3% for Foster City. Sewer rates are below median in comparison to neighboring agencies. 
The member agencies are part of the San Francisco-Redwood City-South San Francisco Metropolitan 
Area, which outperforms the state and nation with respect to per capita income and unemployment.

MAJOR INITIATIVES
CLEAN WATER PROGRAM 
The Clean Water Program (Program) is a comprehensive 10-year capital plan totaling approximately 
$1.0 billion to fund capital improvements to the jointly-owned Wastewater Treatment Plant and the 
San Mateo-owned wastewater collection facilities. The Program will provide the capital improvements 
necessary to comply with Cease and Desist Order No. R2-2009-0020 from the San Francisco Bay 
Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) to eliminate sanitary sewer overflows that primarily 
occur during heavy rain events.

MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION & ANALYSIS
Management of the San Mateo – Foster City Public Financing Authority (Authority) offers readers 
of the financial statements this narrative overview and analysis of the financial activities of the 
Authority for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2020. Readers are encouraged to consider the 
information presented here in conjunction with additional information that is furnished in our 
letter of transmittal, the financial statements, and notes. 
FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
At the end of the fiscal year, the Authority had $571.7 million in total assets; an increase 
of $29.0 million or 5 percent. The Authority’s assets consisted of $249.4 million in cash and 
investments held by the Authority’s trustee and $270 million in loans receivables which 
reflected the revenue bonds (2019 Bonds) that were issued in June 2019 to finance capital 
improvements in the Clean Water Program. The primary driver of the increase in total 
assets was the issuance of lease revenue bonds in February 2020. 
The Authority issued $45.8 million in lease revenue bonds (2020 Bonds) in February 2020 
to finance the cost of flood control improvements for North Shoreview flood control and 
city-wide street rehabilitation capital improvements for the City of San Mateo. The par 
value also includes $3.5 million to refund City lease revenue bonds issued in 2011 for 
South Bayfront flood control improvements. The 2020 Bonds are reflected in the 
Authority’s total assets via net investment in leases of $45.8 million. 
The Authority had $630.7 million in total liabilities at the end of the fiscal year; an increase 
of $34.9 million or 6 percent. The increase in total liability is attributable to the issuance of 
the 2020 Bonds. The Authority’s liabilities included $249.4 million that is due to the 
member agencies for their share of bond proceeds that are available to draw down upon 
for eligible capital improvement costs from the 2019 Bonds and the City of San Mateo’s 
sole responsibility for the 2020 Bonds. The other notable liability is the bonds payable 
liability of $374.2 million from both the 2019 Bonds and the 2020 lease revenue bonds, 
inclusive of $51.7 million in bond premiums for the 2019 Bonds and $8.0 million for the 
2020 Bonds.  
The Authority’s net position decreased by $5.9 million or 11 percent; from $(53.2) million 
to $(59.1) million, which is due to the issuance of the 2020 Bonds, and is representative of 
the amortization of bond premiums from both the 2019 Bonds and 2020 Bonds. As the 
Authority continues to make bond payments, bond premiums will concurrently be 
amortized, and the Authority’s net position will gradually approach $0. 
The Authority had $12.8 million in operating income. This was an increase of $13.5 million 
compared to the prior fiscal year, which reflected contributions from member agencies 
pursuant to the respective financing agreements between the Authority and member 
agencies. Operating income was largely shaped by $13.2 million in operating revenues, 
which accounted for loan and lease interest income, and $390 thousand in operating 
expenses, primarily reflecting the cost of issuance for the 2020 Bonds.

1. SUMMARY OF SIGNIFICANT ACCOUNTING POLICIES 
The basic financial statements of the San Mateo – Foster City Public Financing Authority (Authority) have 
been prepared in conformity with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) as applied to 
governmental agencies.  The Governmental Accounting Standards Boards (GASB) is the accepted standard 
setting body for establishing governmental accounting and financial reporting principles.  The more 
significant of the Authority’s accounting policies are described below. 
A. Financial Reporting Entity 
San Mateo – Foster City Public Financing Authority (Authority) is a jointly governed organization 
between the City of San Mateo (San Mateo) and the City of Foster City, Estero Municipal Improvement 
District (Foster City) under the laws of the State of California.  The Authority was organized to provide 
financial assistance to San Mateo and Foster City by financing the members’ capital improvements. 
B. Basis of Accounting and Measurement Focus 
The Authority accounts for its activities as a proprietary fund. The financial statements are accounted 
for on a flow of economic resources measurement focus, using the accrual basis of accounting. Under 
this method all assets, deferred outflows and inflows of resources, and liabilities associated with 
operations are included on the balance sheet, revenues are recorded as earned and expenses are 
recorded at the time liabilities are incurred. 
The Authority applies all applicable GASB pronouncements, including all NCGA Statements and 
Interpretations currently in effect. 
The Authority distinguishes operating revenues and expenses from non-operating items. Operating 
revenues include revenues from financing activities with San Mateo and Foster City; operating expenses 
include all expenses applicable to the furnishing of these services. Nonoperating revenue and expenses 
include revenue and expenses not associated with the Authority’s normal business. Non-operating 
revenues and expenses include interest income and expense, gain and loss on disposition of property 
and equipment, grants, and other peripheral activities. Although capital contributions, as well as special 
and extraordinary items, when there are any, are shown separately, technically they are subcategories of 
non-operating revenues and expenses. 
C. Long-Term Obligations 
In the basic financial statements, long-term debt and other long-term obligations are reported as liabilities in 
the business-type activities.  Bond premiums and discounts, if material, are deferred and amortized over the 
life of the bonds using the straight-line method.  Bonds payable are reported net of the applicable bond 
premium or discount.  Bond issuance costs are expensed as incurred.

D. Deferred Outflows of Resources 
In addition to assets, the statement of financial position will sometimes report a separate section for 
deferred outflows of resources. This separate financial statement element, deferred outflows of 
resources, represents a consumption of net position that applies to a future period(s) and so will not be 
recognized as an outflow of resources (expense) until then.  
E. Net Position 
In the basic financial statements, Net Position is classified in the following category: 
Restricted Net Position – This amount is restricted by external creditors, grantors, contributors, or laws or 
regulations of other governments. 
F. Use of Restricted / Unrestricted Net Position 
When an expense is incurred for purposes for which both Restricted and Unrestricted Net Position is 
available, the Authority’s policy is to apply Restricted Net Position first.
G. Use of Estimates 
The preparation of basic financial statements in conformity with generally accepted accounting principles 
requires Authority management to make estimates and assumptions that affect certain amounts and 
disclosures.  Accordingly, actual results could differ from those estimates.
