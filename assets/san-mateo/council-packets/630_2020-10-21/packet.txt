SUBJECT:
Community Needs Assessment for 2021-22 Community Development Block Grant 
Annual Plan
RECOMMENDATION:
That the Community Relations Commission provide recommendations to determine community priorities in preparation of 
the 2021-22 Annual Plan for the Community Development Block Grant Program.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City of San Mateo receives funds from the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) 
for the Community Development Block Grant Program (CDBG).  The program provides funding to support needed projects 
and services to San Mateo's most vulnerable community members.  CDBG project and program areas include, but are not 
limited to, public service grants to nonprofit agencies, home repairs to low income homeowners, and improvements to 
neighborhood parks and public infrastructure in the CDBG target areas (See Attachment 1-CDBG Target Area Map).
Part of the process to obtain the CDBG funds is to provide HUD with an Annual Plan that identifies community needs and 
prioritizes the types of programs and projects it will fund.
Community Needs Meeting
For the next fiscal year, 2021-22, the Annual Plan will include a summary of the City's programs and projects to address 
community development, housing and homelessness, and public services for lower income households.  The Community 
Relations Commission (CRC) is holding the Community Needs Assessment to gather input on the priority needs in order to 
develop the plan.
COVID-19 Response
The CRC also requests input specifically to address emerging needs and concerns due to the spread of the corona virus. 
HUD has provided the City an additional allocation of approximately $821,000 to respond to the needs of the community.  
The City allocated funds for rental assistance with a portion set aside specifically for childcare providers earlier this 
summer. To continue to mitigate the negative effects of the lasting pandemic, the CRC may recommend that the City 
Council continue to fund existing activities or suggest new programs to prepare, prevent, and respond to the corona virus.
BUDGET IMPACT:

for next year.  This can be used as a guide for further recommendations for the Annual Plan.  These figures should be used 
as estimates only, since future entitlements can be higher or lower, depending on US Congress approval to the final federal 
budget.
