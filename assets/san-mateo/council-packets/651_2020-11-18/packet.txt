MINUTES – April 29, 2020 
The April 29, 2020 Regular Meeting of the Community Relations Commission (CRC) was called to order at City 
Hall, Conference Room C, at 7:05 pm. 
 
ROLL CALL 
Commissioners Fernandez, Mahesri, Rivera, and Torrijos were present. Commissioner Hsiung was absent. 
 
CITY STAFF PRESENT 
Sandy Council, Housing Manager and Nicholas Vu, Housing Specialist were present.   
 
CONSENT CALENDAR / APPROVAL OF MINUTES 
The minutes of the February 19, 2019 meeting were approved. (Fernandez/Mahesri; 4-0) 
 
PUBLIC COMMENT 
None 
 
NEW BUSINESS 
1. Community Development Block Grant and HOME 2020-2021 Draft Annual Action Plan and Amendments to the 
Community Participation Plan and 2018-2022 Consolidated Plan  
 
Staff gave a presentation for the CRC to introduce new funding available to address concerns regarding COVID-19, 
reprogrammed funds that would be used to supplement response, and amend language to allow for expedited 
processing. The CRC would vote afterwards on whether to approve amendments to the Community Participation 
Plan and the 2018-2022 Consolidated Plan and recommend that the City Council adopt the 2020-2021 Annual Action 
Plan for the Community Development Block Grant and HOME programs. 
 
Staff also included new funding available from HOME project repayments that would be directed toward affordable 
housing projects. 
 
Community Presentations / Remarks

move to recommend approval of amendments to the 2020 Action Plan (Rivera/Torrijos; 4-0)  
 
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS 
Chair Rivera and Commissioner Torrijos said farewells for their last CRC meeting. 
 
ADJOURNMENT 
There being no further business before the Commission, the meeting adjourned at 8:00pm.

MINUTES – October 21, 2020 
The April 29, 2020 Regular Meeting of the Community Relations Commission (CRC) was called to order virtually 
over Zoom at 7:00 pm. Vice Chair Mahesri called the meeting to order. 
 
ROLL CALL 
Commissioners Mahesri, Hsiung, Overall and Nugent were present. Chair Fernandez was not present. 
 
CITY STAFF PRESENT 
Sandy Council, Housing Manager and Nicholas Vu, Housing Specialist were present.   
 
CONSENT CALENDAR / APPROVAL OF MINUTES 
The minutes of the August 19, 2020 meeting were approved. (Nugent/Overall; 4-0) 
 
PUBLIC COMMENT 
None 
 
NEW BUSINESS 
1. Community Needs Assessment for 2021-22 Community Development Block Grant Annual Plan 
Staff asks that the Community Relations Commission provide recommendations to determine community priorities 
in preparation of the 2021-22 Annual Plan for the Community Development Block Grant Program. 
 
The following comments were received: 
▪ Debra Smith, HIP Housing. There is a large need for more affordable housing. San Mateo residents who have tried 
to apply to HIP home sharing program still need to find homes. A new challenge has risen for how to interview 
clients while social distancing. Rent is getting too high. Clients need more food, financial assistance, and basic 
necessity grants.  Gig workers are experiencing significant losses in income. 
▪ Shirley Gibson, Legal Aid. Due to loss of income, there have been lots of eviction situations. AB 3088 has stopped 
some evictions temporarily due to rent conversion; rent is being recharacterized as consumer debt. New 
programs being developed should try to create efficiencies by having landlords come forward. There is a need to 
get more financial assistance into people's hands before January, since next eviction sessions start in February. If 
protection policies do not keep up with the rent need more evictions will continue. 42 cases have been seen 
since Oct 5. The debt cliff will start in March and people will need financial counseling for credit hits or 
bankruptcy. One possible program to look into starting is a debt relief program since people are fleeing debt 
accumulation by moving into overcrowded homes or going into cars. From this point, another program to look 
into would be a safe parking program. 
▪ David Fleishman, 4C’s. Childcare needs to be a priority for CDBG-CV, because childcare is especially important for 
low income families. Early care is the biggest factor in childhood development and being unhoused is critical. 
Generally, childcare providers are women of color. More than 2/3 of childcare providers have shutdown due to 
corona virus. Both an immediate and long term need has been established since the county program has 
exceeded funding. 
▪ Jill Morris, CORA. Since COVID, there have been a large increase in law enforcement referrals due to domestic 
violence victims being in lethal situations. There has been an increase of 236% of reports. Victims are being 
placed in hotels due to lack of shelter space. 43 families are in transitional housing. There is a low inventory of 
affordable housing, so some clients have become homeless or had to return to their abuser. Both rental 
assistance and child care assistance is necessary.  
▪ Bernie Mellott, Ombudsman. Many residents in assisted long-term care facilities have aged past their savings. 
Currently, there is a gap between their social security payments and rent of the facility. A program to look into 
would be rental assistance with an earmark for elderly. These elderly residents are at risk of homelessness once 
they use up all their funds.  
▪ Micheal Weinhauer, Central Neighborhood Association. Accidents have happened in community due to close 
proximity to freeway. Residents are asking for capital improvements: pedestrian improvements on overpass on 
3rd street and 4th street and better lighting in 3rd and 4th street. There is a litter problem and they suggest 
doing a neighborhood cleanup day for 3rd, 4th, 5th street. Fragmented volunteer groups have lots of human 
capital but there needs more management in terms of timing.

the 2018-22 Consolidated Plan.

housing prices. Job growth also continues, though it is not as prosperous for low-income residents. Many residents still face housing and food 
security issues. In particular the skyrocketing rental market continues to create undue hardships on many senior and low/moderate income 
families. 


Home Repair Programs. Accessibility modifications are provided by the Center for the Independence of Individuals with Disabilities 
(CIID). Minor home repairs services are performed by RTP.  Energy efficiency related repairs are provided by El Concilio of San Mateo 
County in conjunction with the Pacific Gas & Electric sponsored Energy Savings Assistance Program, as well as the Low-Income Home 
Energy Assistance (LIHEAP) program



Assess how the jurisdiction’s use of funds, particularly CDBG, addresses the priorities and specific objectives identified in the plan, 
giving special attention to the highest priority activities identified.
The City has significant housing, social service, and community development needs that were developed into allocation priorities through the 
community participation process.
In March 2020, the City complied with shelter-in-place orders along with other municipalities in the 9-county Bay Area region in response to the 
emergence of the corona virus pandemic. As a result, many individuals and businesses in the City struggled to find sufficient work opportunities 
to pay for rent or other basic necessities. The first round of $440,000 dollars of corona virus funding awarded from the federal government 
(CDBG-CV) was programmed by the City to be used for rental assistance with an earmark of  $120,000 dollars for assistance specifically to in-
home childcare providers.
The City’s goals this year had a heavy focus on housing and housing-related services, indicative of the strain the high cost of both rental and for 
sale housing is having on the community. The Community Funding program was originally budgeted at the maximum allowed by CDBG program, 
but was increased as allowed by a HUD waiver to assist with responding to the pandemic.  The community needs far exceed the resources.  The 
funding priorities for the Community Funding Program included:

Basic Human Needs; such as access to food, shelter, clothing, legal services, etc.

Senior Services

Youth Services

Affordable Housing Services 
Construction was completed for a pedestrian safety project in the CDBG Priority Neighborhood, which was identified as a priority from 
community members in the Community Needs Assessment process.  Community requests to improve pedestrian and bike safety have also been 
received as part of the Neighborhood Traffic Forums coordinated by the Public Works Department. The neighborhood Traffic Action Plans 
included priorities to reduce speeding and increase safety particularly for pedestrians and bicyclists, so several related improvements were 
incorporated into the street project design.

The City of San Mateo’s
2019-20 Draft Consolidated Annual Performance & Evaluation Report 
(CAPER)
NOTICE OF PUBLIC COMMENT PERIOD & PUBLIC MEETINGS
The Consolidated Annual Performance Evaluation Report (CAPER) is an annual report that 
summarizes the City’s accomplishments of its Community Development Block Grant Program 
(CDBG) from the U. S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) that is intended 
to provide services to meet the needs of low-income residents and areas of the community.  
The public is encouraged to review the Report and provide comments from October 30 to 
November 15, 2020.  Copies will be available by request at San Mateo City Hall, 330 W. 20th 
Ave. or online at https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/1091/Annual-Reports. Comments may be 
mailed to the attention of Nicholas Vu at the above address or emailed to 
nvu@cityofsanmateo.org. 
The San Mateo City Council will hold a public meeting on Monday, November 16, 2020 at 7:00 
pm online via Zoom at https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/3971/Agendas-Minutes-Public-Meeting-
Portal, to take public comments and review the draft report.
In compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act, those with disabilities requiring special 
accommodations to participate in this meeting may contact the City’s Housing Division at (650) 
522-7220 or housing@cityofsanmateo.org. Notification 48 hours prior to the meeting will enable 
the City to make reasonable arrangements to ensure accessibility to this meeting.
