1.
44 E. 3rd Ave. Draper University Pre-Application – Study Session/Design Charrette (PA-2018-054) 
Planning Manager Sailesh Mehra provided a brief review of the design charette that took place on December 8th, 2020 
and clarified that the purpose of this evening’s charette would be to provide an in-depth review of design options 
proposed by the applicant and receive public and Planning Commission input.  
Associate Planner Wendy Lao gave a brief presentation of the Draper University Pre-Application, a review of the 
project description, and the building’s historic status. Lao reviewed concerns raised by the Commission including the 
impact to the historic elements of the building and the location of the recycling and trash carts.  
Tim Draper, Applicant, expressed the need to make changes to the building to meet the University’s vision of providing 
space for venture capitalists and professionals to operate and mentor Draper University students.
Michael Castro, Brereton Architects for the Applicant, presented renovations of other historic properties completed by 
Brereton Architects. Castro provided a review of the Draper University Change of Use studies and the proposed design: 
the conversion of the upper floor dormitories into office space and constructing a gurney-sized, exposed elevator in 
the southwest corner of the building with direct service to the top floors, adding additional glass panes to the east 
façade. In addition, an enclosed fire/life safety stairwell and a trash room at the ground level would be constructed. 
Notable design revisions made from the previous charette included additional glass panes on the exposed elevator and 
the relocation of the trash enclosure.
Public Comment:
Commission Chair Mallory requested two letters be read into the record. Gill Gordon (via written comment) made 
suggestions to maintain the existing structure, suggested that the building allow more year-round access to residents, 
and suggested that dormitory space be retained as affordable housing. Diane Whittaker (via written comment) 
commented on the irreplaceability of historic buildings and was not in favor of the design. Tareq Aljaber (in session)

commented on Draper University’s positive impacts on local start-ups. Kieran White (in session) spoke in favor of the 
proposed design.
The Commission asked questions of the applicant regarding location of the trash room, design revisions, and 
operations between Draper University and Hero City offices. 
The Commission provided comments. 
Commissioner Etheridge was not supportive of the Change of Use of the upper floors and felt the conversion would 
not justify the proposed exterior changes. Using the existing interior elevator to bridge the ground floor and top floor 
would be less impactful to the exterior of the building. On the design, Etheridge acknowledged that the building is not 
officially recognized as a historical resource, but it is eligible for this designation and is of value to the downtown 
historic district. In addition, the proposed elevator does not match the existing architectural style of the building. 
Commissioner Ebneter thanked the applicant for clarifying the purpose of the proposed changes. The value of an 
exposed exterior elevator with views of the downtown does not equate to the historic nature of the building.  Ebneter 
noted that preserving the city’s history is valued by residents and city leadership. Etheridge was not in support of the 
changes presented. 
Commissioner Williams expressed appreciation for Draper University’s positive impacts and the mentorship and 
apprenticeship opportunities they provide. Williams supported the Change of Use on the top three floors. Williams 
commended Brereton Architects work on other historic buildings. Williams noted the modern juxtaposition against the 
existing historic elements and improvements to stairway exiting solutions. Williams noted that a separate entry to the 
offices has potential to provide an inviting element, however, the building’s value as a historic resource cannot be 
replaced once modified. The proposed design would eliminate distinctive features on the rear elevation including the 
molded stringcourse. Williams was not supportive of the changes as designed. 
Vice Chair Maldonado thanked the applicant for the presentation, their insight, and adjusting the design to place the 
elevator inside the building. Maldonado acknowledged the purpose of having an elevator with direct service to the 
upper floors to be used by certain individuals. While the glass and natural light of the exposed elevator is pleasing, 
Maldonado noted that using the existing interior elevator to bridge the ground floor and top floor would be less 
impactful the historic elements of the exterior. Maldonado was in favor of the addition of the trash storage locations. 
Maldonado was not supportive of the structure’s design as presented. 
Commission Chair Mallory concurred with the comments the Commission made regarding the importance of 
preserving the building’s historic elements. Mallory felt that the new changes did not reflect the Commission’s 
statements made at the December 8th study session. Mallory was not supportive of the design. 
Tim Draper expressed disappointment in the Commissions reception of the design and noted financial impacts the 
presence of Draper University has on the city. Without support for the proposed design, Draper University may 
reconsider operating in the City of San Mateo. 
REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Planning Manager Sailesh Mehra announced that the Planning Commissioners comments would be summarized and 
provided for consideration at the next meeting. 
ADJOURNMENT
The meeting adjourned at 7:00 pm.

1.
Zoning Code Amendments
Principle Planner Julia Klein presented recommended amendments to the San Mateo Municipal Code. Changes to 
Chapters 27.04, “Definitions” would better define paving; Chapter 27.18, “R1 Districts - One Family Dwelling” changes 
would clarify limits on paving, landscaping, uncovered decks, and add a map for San Mateo Park Planning Area; 
Chapter 27.20 “R2 Districts – Two Family Dwellings” changes would replace the black & white Floor Area Ratio maps 
with colored versions; and Chapter 27.32 “C2 Districts – Regional/Community Commercial” changes would update 
emergency shelter provisions, removing the 300 ft. “parks and schools” buffer. Changes would be encompassed in an 
Ordinance to be recommended to City Council.
The Commissioners asked questions of staff. 
Discussion ensued. Klein clarified the housing element and emergency shelter provisions as mandated by Senate Bill 
2, which states that one zoning district in the city must allow emergency shelters by right in the event of a local 
emergency. Planning Manager Sailesh Mehra outlined the intent to review the Municipal Code annually, followed by a 
more comprehensive review after the General Plan is complete. Klein clarified the intent to revise language in the R1 
code to better clarify parameters on paving and landscaping and that no changes to the regulations would be made. 
Motion to recommend to the City Council to adopt an Ordinance to amend the San Mateo Municipal Code Chapters 
27.04, “Definitions” to add a paving definition; Chapter 27.18, “R1 Districts - One Family Dwelling” to clarify limits on 
paving, landscaping, uncovered decks, and add a map for San Mateo Park Planning Area; Chapter 27.20 “R2 Districts – 
Two Family Dwellings” to replace the Floor Area Ratio maps; and Chapter 27.32 “C2 Districts – Regional/Community 
Commercial” to update emergency shelter provisions.

Ordinance Introduction – Municipal Code Amendment to Update Current Accessory Dwelling Unit and Junior 
Accessory Dwelling Unit Ordinance and Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit Definition 
Associate Planner Phillip Brennan gave a presentation on an ordinance amending San Mateo Municipal Code Chapter 
27.19  with regard to Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) and Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit (JADU) in Residential Zones 
noting that the changes are required due to changes to State law . Brennan clarified that staff is requesting the 
Planning Commission provide a recommendation of the proposed draft ordinance to City Council. Brennan presented 
background on the ADU/JADU regulation changes occurring at the State level. Staff’s update of the local ordinance 
would be comprised of two phases: Phase I, updating local ordinance to comply with State regulations; Phase II, 
community outreach and a comprehensive, stand-alone ADU/JADU ordinance update. 
The update of the ordinance would mirror the changes to the State regulations, notably the exempted floor area, 
maximum unit size, architectural standards for detached units, and an updated definition of “Junior Accessory
