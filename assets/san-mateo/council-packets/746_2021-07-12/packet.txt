SUBJECT:
Interactive Pandemic Healing Art ‘Wall’ Proposal - Recommendation to Council
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council the approval of the Interactive Pandemic Healing Art ‘Wall’ temporary art project and the 
expenditure of $1,300 from the Art in Public Places Fund.
BACKGROUND:
In November 2020, San Mateo resident Amie Tyler contacted the City Manager with an idea for a “pop up”, temporary 
community art project. This idea was similar to the popular chalkboard projects that are in publicly accessible locations 
where participants can complete the posed sentence “Before I die...” Her concept was changing the question to focus on 
the community’s response to the pandemic. The idea could be a cathartic, community activity that "Research shows that 
art brings communities together and heals after times of trauma."  
Staff discussed the concept and some details of the project with Ms. Tyler. She explained that she would be very engaged, 
sponsor the project, coordinate the construction, and monitor the status of the wall throughout its installation time. This 
type of project would be most viable in an outdoor, easily accessible public space with a good amount of pedestrian traffic 
and good weather. Since the original request was in late fall and heading into winter, the weather was not going to be 
ideal due to possible rain and cold. Concurring with those conditions, she agreed that waiting until the spring / summer 
would be a better environment.  
With B St. having been closed for pedestrian use and outdoor dining through September 2021, this seemed like the ideal 
location and time of year for this type of project, warmer and more pedestrians who would hopefully participate. Staff did 
some general research and requested input from Public Works about possible locations. Attachment 2 illustrates the 
possible B St. locations identified by Public Works, who also affirmed the requirement to keep the access lane open for 
emergency response. Staff met Amie downtown to review the recommended locations for her to consider. The northeast 
corner of 3rd Ave. and B St. on street level was the identified preferred location due to the greatest pedestrian visibility 
and interaction. 
Ms. Tyler has provided additional information in her proposal (Att 1), including defining the installation program period as 
four (4) weeks, and included a request for $1,300 for the internal, structural support materials. The City Manager had 
previously concurred that due to the small size of the project, the City may be willing to support the project with some of 
the ‘infrastructure’ expenses if requested.
The Art in Public Places Program requires Civic Arts Committee review and recommendation to the City Council to approve 
the project and authorize the expenditure of funds.

Interactive Pandemic Healing Art ‘Wall’ Proposal 
Amie Tyler 
Introduction: 
Hello! I am Amie Tyler, a San Mateo resident (born at Mills!) and I have a Master’s Degree in 
Transpersonal Psychology with a concentration in Expressive Arts & Innovation.  
I’m married with two boys, have worked full-time in Corporate America for over 20 years and 
have led the largest contract at Visa ($60M over 5 years), but nothing in the corporate world has 
been as meaningful as my side business in the healing arts teaching meditation, working with 
parents who have children with disabilities, and working with people who have been recently 
diagnosed with cancer. I am passionate about serving the community in using the skills and gifts 
I can to contribute our collective wellbeing. 
Situation: 
During the Pandemic I was struggling as a mom of two boys and a husband who is an essential 
worker, who was working outside of the home. The more I spoke to colleagues and friends, the 
more I realized everyone was struggling in their own boat, but not many were expressing their 
‘dark nights of the soul’ and how they were coping. Two people on my team at work lost their 
fathers to Covid, one lost his younger brother, and my step-mom, while she was in the hospital 
with Covid, was diagnosed with cancer. A dear friend is a Pediatric Physician at Stanford and 
worked with children who had Covid and other complications. It was a trying time for so many! 
During this time I was in the middle of studying Creative Expression for Communities and all of 
the research that shows how community arts provide a healing ground for communities on the 
whole.  
San Mateo, California, just 17 miles South of San Francisco, is a very fast-paced city where 
housing costs are high. There is increased job and financial pressure due to the Pandemic. 
Several families living near me have lost their jobs and others are under immense pressure to 
work and find childcare. There was a time the city felt like it was holding its breath and full of 
stress. I felt people need a healing mechanism. 
Proposal: 
I am proposing a pop-up (temporary) interactive community outdoor art project to bring people 
together and lift our collective spirit.

Taking inspiration from the “Before I Die...” project, with the potential space downtown on a 
closed street, wooden walls will feature the repeating phrase, “During the Pandemic I...”  Or 
“During the Pandemic I lost…” and “The Pandemic made me grateful for…” where San Mateo 
residents can express and honor those we have lost and what we are grateful for due to the 
Pandemic. 
Detailed Project Summary 
Taking inspiration from the "Before I Die" project, the wall will be constructed of metal, free 
standing and bolted for safety, rectangular structure, passable on all sides, where the 
community can write their words on the walls of the structure with chalk.  
Size:  
The structure will be 7 feet tall and 7 feet wide on two sides, and 7 feet tall and 4 feet wide on 
two sides (a rectangular structure).  
The company, Custom Metal Fabrication in Belmont has constructed a metal frame where 
plywood sheets would be attached, painted with chalkboard paint, and stenciled.  
Safety:  
There are tabs on base of metal structure (see drawing with holes) for bolts which we (my 
brother who was head of Public Works for City of Belmont) or City of San Mateo Public Works 
could fill with crack seal when done - a common practice. The reason for this is the intense wind 
that sometimes occurs in downtown San Mateo in the late afternoons. The plywood would be 
attached to the metal frame with self-drilling screws for a secure attachment.  
Other options for securing the structure which are said to be commonly used by traffic 
departments and public works: 
1.
Lag bolts with plastic sleeves 
2.
Asphalt nails - commonly used by traffic department. 
3.
Hilti nail gun (used in concrete more often but can also use in asphalt) 
Holes can be filled with tar or crack seal per the City’s preference. (Happy to do this or for San 
Mateo Public Works to do as well). 
Monitoring: During the project duration, I would visit the site in person twice a day to ensure 
there are no inappropriate vandalism signs and to take pictures to submit to the City for however 
you’d like to use them as it develops and more people interact with the structure.  
Mitigation Planning: 
Potential Vandalism: Certainly any structure, even the electrical box in front of Peet’s, is an 
invitation for vandalism or destruction for some. If something should occur, I would aim to re-
paint the wood with in 1-2 days to ensure it is not an eyesore. If it is irreparable, I would remove 
the structure and notify the city. (Have to think of Plan B’s!) 
What if it rains? 
In the event of rain, the chalk and previous writing would be impacted. In this case, I would 
clean the wooden walls so there would be a clean slate. Previous writings would be lost, but 
new room would be made for a “fresh” start. The stencils with the prompts would stay as those 
will be painted on.

For the Community 
Community Messaging (Proposed): 
The Pandemic Wall Art Project invites you to write an honest note from your heart to the 
Universe. Taking time to reflect on all we have been through can help us 1) find more 
compassion for ourselves and each other, 2) de-stigmatize talking about difficult subjects like 
mental health, grief, and depression and 3) anchor all we are grateful for in the middle of our 
community.  
Here is what some are saying. During the Pandemic I...  
1.
Got cancer and started chemotherapy. 
2.
Learned to appreciate teachers.   
3.
Lost my uncle to Covid-19. 
4.
Got a puppy to keep me company! 
5.
Really miss holding my 84 year old mom's hands. 
So, grab a piece of chalk and add to the wall. Schedule time to come alone to reflect, or gather 
those in your household over the holidays for a safe, social distanced family outing. What have 
you lost? What have you gained? What are you grateful for? Come and leave your mark as a 
reminder that we're all in this together. 
Community Memorial Opening Night: 
On opening night, I would like to invite nurses, doctors, and essential workers from San Mateo 
organizations and hospitals to a candlelight memorial to have a spokesperson from the City say 
a few words, and I’m happy to as well, but to invite all of our essential workers who carried our 
city through - to thank them, honor them, and allow them to be the ‘firsts’ to write on the wall.  
Vision & Mission Statement: 
My vision is to provide an outlet through healing arts projects to my community to foster healing 
and conscious awareness where I live. 
My mission for this project is to hold sacred space for others during the Pandemic by taking the 
healing arts online or outside. This pop-up art project is one way to foster connection, healing, 
and gratitude through times of struggle. As Thich Nhat Hanh says, we cannot take away 
suffering, but we can say to one another, “Darling, I care about your suffering.”
