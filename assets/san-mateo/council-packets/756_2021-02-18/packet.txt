SUBJECT:
Measure S Long-Term Plan Update – Capital Improvement Program Funding Allocations
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive the Measure S Long-Term Plan Update and recommend projects for inclusion in the 2021-22 proposed Capital 
Improvement Program (CIP) budget to be funded by available Measure S funding capacity.
BACKGROUND:
While the City's sales tax revenues have been materially impacted by COVID-19 due to the proportion of taxable 
transactions coming from general retail and food services, the City's transactions and use tax revenues have been less 
impacted due to the destination-based allocation nature of district taxes in the State of California. 
The increase in online transactions due to the varying degrees of local health orders is diluted due to the provisions of the 
Bradley-Burns Uniform Sales and Use Tax law, where use tax is allocated by each local municipality's proportional share of 
the 1% Bradley Burns sales tax. As of the most recent quarter of sales tax revenues remitted by the California Department 
of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA), the City's proportional share of the county pool that allocates use tax was 11.8%, 
or $1.3 million. Conversely, district tax, which is synonymous with transactions and use tax, is destination based, thus the 
City receives 100% of the Measure S 1/4% transactions and use tax on taxable online transactions that have a delivery 
address in the City. 
The accompanying slide deck provides an updated long-term financial plan for Measure S, which shows additional funding 
capacity. Given the ongoing uncertainty and volatility with the recession brought on by COVID-19, staff's recommendation 
is to appropriate projected available funding capacity to one-time Capital Improvement Program (CIP) projects rather than 
recurring operating needs. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Based on the updated Measure S Long-Term Financial Plan, there is approximately $0.8 million in available funding 
capacity each year through fiscal year 2024-25. The Measure S Oversight Committee's recommendation will be 
incorporated into the proposed 2021-22 CIP budget and presented to the City Council during the first study session on 
June 7, 2021.
