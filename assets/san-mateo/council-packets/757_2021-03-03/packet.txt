SUBJECT:
Proposed Parks and Recreation Fee Schedule for FY 2021-22
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend to the City Council adoption of the proposed FY 2021-22 fee schedule for Parks and Recreation facilities and 
miscellaneous fees as provided in Attachment 1 of the Administrative Report.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City reviews and adjusts fees. Generally, fees are adjusted by the Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) for the 
previous year. The Finance Department has identified the official COLA for this year to be 1.1%. The recommended fee 
changes for the Parks and Recreation Department are detailed in Attachment 1 and outlined below. As the COLA increase 
is at a historic low, the department is only changing fees to streamline or adjust to market. In addition there are some fees 
that have not been previously codified that are now being added as well as number of new fees. Typically, fee increase 
recommendations are based on information gathered from market surveys or other industry standards. In some cases, it 
has become practice to maintain fees at the same rate and then recommend a more reasonable increase as opposed to 
raising them incrementally every year. 
Recommended Fee Changes: 

Picnic Facilities: For 2022 most fees have remained the same. The fees that have increased higher than COLA (page 
8.2, Section Facility Fees) are impacting a small number of our facilities largely bringing them to range with our 
other similar facilities while still representing good value compared to our neighboring cities. 

Parks and Recreation Staff Costs: Where staff time is being charged, the department is recommending that the 
hourly rate reflect the existing salary schedule for the position performing the work. This also achieves a 
streamlining as there are no changes required annually for these charges. These changes can be found: Page 8.6, 
Facility Rental Fees, Field Attendant. Page 8.7, Facility Personnel Rates: Custodian, Facility Specialist, Lifeguards: 
1st Guard, Senior Guard and 2nd & each additional Guard. Page 8.7, Miscellaneous Fees: Custodian

Parks and Landscape Resources: We are introducing a number of fees that are largely pass through fees where the 
fee is equal to that of the costs borne by the city to provide the service. These charges can be found on page 8.8, 
Section VI and include Heritage Tree planting deposits, the installation of commemorative benches and trees and 
the cost of community garden plots.  A new fee is being added for Heritage Tree appeals to the Commission and 
City Council.  The fee will not apply to permit applicants.  

Poplar Creek Golf Course: Golf is piloting a membership loyalty program that will be subscription based and cost 
$29.99 per month with a goal to retain many of the new players who have been attracted to golf during the


Park in-Lieu/Park Impact Fees:  Annually Parks and Landscape Resources staff has revised the fee schedule (page 
8.8) to reflect changes in the Park in-Lieu/Park Impact Fees. These fees are adjusted based on a calculation that 
includes an annual determination of the median land value and the average number of people per dwelling type. 
The median land value increased this year by 3% as did the population factor and thus the fees dependent on it 
also increased.
Park in-lieu/Park Impact Fees are administered per the City’s Municipal Code, Chapter 26.64 Dedication of Land for 
Community Purposes (Attachment 5). This code outlines how City staff are to calculate the fees each year by 
discussing various elements of the fee calculation. Some important elements of the code include:

“In order to properly serve the subdivision, two acres are needed for each 1,000-estimated population as 
determined by applying factors per dwelling structure types as outlined in the City’s fee schedule in effect 
at the time the fees are to be paid.” 

“The fair market value of land in the City, used to calculate each subdivider’s in-lieu fee and determined 
by the Parks and Recreation Director or designee, will be included in the City’s fee schedule adopted by 
City Council resolution.” 
Using these pieces of the Municipal Code, City staff develops a fair market value of land in the City and multiplies it 
by the two acres per 1,000 estimated population and the most updated population factors per dwelling unit type. 
Fee = population factor per dwelling unit type X 2 acres/1000 population X median land value.  The calculation for 
the FY 2021-22 Park in-Lieu/Impact Fee is detailed in Attachment 2. Both the median home value and the 
population factor per dwelling unit have been updated and this is reflected in the new fees.
The City's Park in-lieu fee is subject to the state Quimby Act, which is codified at Government Code Section 66477. 
Section 66477 requires that the fee be based upon residential density. Residential density is calculated based on 
the subdivision map and the average number of persons per household. The fee cannot exceed the amount 
necessary to provide three acres of parkland per 1,000 residents -- unless the existing park area exceeds that limit, 
in which case a city may adopt a higher standard not to exceed five acres per 1,000 residents. In San Mateo, the 
standard is 2 acres per 1,000residents.
In the past Commissioners have requested staff to study more broad policy changes to the current In Lieu Fee 
ordinance. Currently, the City Council is undertaking a comprehensive review of all developer impact fees, 
including Park in-Lieu. This current effort is being led by the Community Development Department with input from 
the various departments. Considering this on-going effort, staff is not proposing any substantive changes other 
than those resulting from changes in land value or population factor.  The findings of the study as well as the 
consultant’s recommendations are anticipated to be presented to the City Council sometime this spring. 
BUDGET IMPACT:
Most recommended changes would not have a significant impact on the Department’s budgeted revenue outside of COLA. 
For fees related to Park in-Lieu or Park Impact, the impact is always unknown since revenue depends on economic factors 
enabling a project to proceed and is out of our control.

PARKS AND RECREATION DEPARTMENT
RECREATION COST RECOVERY AND PRICING POLICY*
The following shall serve as the policy guidance for establishing a Recreation Activities Cost Recovery Plan, developing annual activity budgets, and 
establishing fees to be charged for recreation activities. The specific elements of this policy are that:
The Park and Recreation Cost Recovery and Pricing Model shall be used.
In recognition of the wide variety of service values and revenue generating potentials of various activities, specific cost recovery thresholds shall be 
established for each recreation activity area at the most specific programming level that is administratively practical.
Facility Indirect Overhead (Fac IOH): Calculated charges for the Public Works Building Maintenance for on-going maintenance 
and repairs (e.g. plumbing, electrical, mechanical, carpentry, painting).
As part of the budgetary process, the Park and Recreation Commission shall review the Cost Recovery Plan and the performance of each activity 
compared to the plan and make any recommendations for changes to the plan.
Recreation Cost Tier Definitions
Direct: The specific “go-away” costs at the course or activity level. These include leaders, instructors and materials or services (e.g. 
transportation) specific to the course or activity.
Program Direct Overhead (DOH): Direct supervision of the program, and minor miscellaneous items such as supervisor mileage.
Facility Overhead (FOH): The combination of facility direct costs (the day-to-day costs to keep the building open- receptionist, 
custodial services, custodial products, utilities) and facility direct overhead (primarily facility supervision).
Operating Indirect Overhead (Op IOH): Division level and shared costs, such as departmental management, brochure, printing, 
photocopying, training, accounting support, technology, credit card discount costs, administrative office clerical support, computer, 
and recreation equipment sinking fund charges
