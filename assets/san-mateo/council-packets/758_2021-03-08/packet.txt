RECOMMENDATION:
Staff will provide an informational overview for determining budget allocations and criteria for selecting Senior Program 
offerings. Chair Lee will lead a discussion on ideas for enhancing the coordination of Senior Service delivery in San Mateo. 
BACKGROUND:
During the agenda review for the March 8 Senior Commission meeting, Chair Lee requested that staff come prepared to 
provide an overview for determining the budget allocation for Senior Program offerings as well as the criteria staff 
considers when selecting which programs to offer at the Senior Center. The Senior Services budget is one of nine program 
areas within the broader Recreation/Community Services Budget with the remaining areas being Beresford Center/Youth 
Camps; Cultural Arts and Contract Classes; Martin Luther King Center; Athletics and Outdoor Facilities; Early 
Childhood/Police Activities League; Aquatics; Dance and Fitness and Business Services. The Recreation/Community 
Services budget is one of four major program areas within the Parks and Recreation Department, the others being 
Landscape Resources/Arboriculture Services; Golf Services; and Administrative Services.  The appropriated Department 
budget for FY 2020-2021 is approximately $20 million, with Recreation/Community Services comprising the largest portion 
at approximately $9.6 million, followed by Landscape Resources/Arboriculture/Administrative Services at $8.1 million and 
Golf Services at $2.4 million.  Due to the programming limitations resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic, expenditures in 
the Recreation/Community Services Division are anticipated to be approximately one-half of the budgeted amounts.  
Conversely, revenue generated from recreation programs is anticipated to be less than one-quarter of projected ($4.2 
million), at approximately $800,000. 
The appropriated budget for Senior Services for FY 2020-2021 is approximately $960,000 which includes $150,000 for the 
Get Around Transportation Program.  
Budget direction is provided by the City Manager’s office and the Finance Department prior to the start of each budget 
cycle and considers both revenue estimates as well as anticipated growth in expenditures.  Departments are generally 
directed to prepare a base budget based on the current levels of service delivery.  As conditions allow, new budget 
requests from Departments are developed as Decision Packages.  Within the Parks and Recreation Department, each 
division is provided the opportunity to suggest additions to their existing budget which are then reviewed by the broader 
Department Management team, including the Director.  Those Decision Package requests deemed the highest priority are 
then sent forward to the City Manager and Finance Director for consideration and are either approved or rejected.  
Conversely, during periods of economic downturn, every Department is requested to prepare options for budget 
reductions.  There is a similar review process within the Department with a final list forwarded to the City Manager for 
their consideration.  Ultimately, the City Council has final approval authority for the City’s budget.
