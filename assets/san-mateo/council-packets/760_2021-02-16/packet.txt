SUBJECT:
Citywide Traffic Management Update 
RECOMMENDATION:
Receive an informational presentation on the Citywide traffic management plan.
BACKGROUND:
Staff continues to develop traffic management strategies to meet the City Council goal of addressing traffic issues around 
the City. Staff presented preliminary strategies at the November 16, 2020 City Council meeting, which included preliminary 
traffic data analysis and findings, efforts to improve public access to traffic collision data, and outreach and safety 
campaigns being proposed to address these findings. At the meeting, staff received feedback from City Council to continue 
to develop the collision map, analyze the collision data, build on the outreach efforts and return quarterly to provide 
progress updates. 
As recommended during the November 2020 City Council meeting, staff will use the City’s internal data for future traffic 
analysis to identify trends and hot spots because it provides greater accuracy and includes data for property damage 
collisions where the previous data set did not. As discussed during that meeting, utilization of this internal data required 
staff to first map that data before it could be analyzed. Staff recently completed the mapping of internal data from the 
past four years. Now that this step is complete, staff will use this expanded data set to move forward with analysis with 
the expectation to present findings in the second quarter of 2021. Staff will present a brief status update on this effort.
Staff also continued efforts to provide public access to traffic collision data and implement the communications plans 
identified in the November 2020 City Council meeting. These interrelated efforts resulted in a collaboration between the 
City’s Public Works, Police, Information Technology, and City Manager’s Office departments in the form of a new 
educational social media campaign, “Safe Streets San Mateo,” and an interactive online traffic data map. Both the social 
media campaign and interactive online map launched on Thursday, January 14, 2021. Staff will present an overview and 
demonstration of the new interactive map and will discuss the implementation strategy for the educational campaign.
