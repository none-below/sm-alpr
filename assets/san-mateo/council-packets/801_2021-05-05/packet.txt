SUBJECT:
Proposed Five Year (2021-2026) Parks and Recreation Capital Improvement Plan 
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on the Proposed Five Year Capital Improvement Plan for FY 2021-2026. 
BACKGROUND:
The Parks and Recreation Department develops an updated five year capital improvement plan every year.  Every other 
year, alternating with the development of the two-year operating budget, the update of the CIP plan is more extensive.  
This is one of those years.  Staff reviews the current list of identified park improvements as well as estimates of expected 
revenues over the same period and recommends changes based on priorities and anticipated income. 
The Proposed FY 2021-2026 CIP plan is included as Attachment 1.  Budget estimates for new and existing projects are 
provided for each of the next five years.  As the largest source of revenue for park and recreation capital improvements 
are park in-lieu and park impact fees which rely on residential development activity, the proposed expenditure plan is 
subject to change, especially for years 3-5.  Parks and Recreation staff perform due diligence by speaking with Planning 
Division staff as well as making direct contact with developers in order to ascertain the likelihood of a project becoming 
“shovel ready” within the time frame.  Based on those conversations, staff feels relatively comfortable with the revenue 
projections proposed for FY 2021-2023.  Beyond that period however, the revenue estimates are considered preliminary. 
Below are the highlights for the proposed five year plan:

The primary focus for improvement projects are upgrades to playgrounds and surrounding areas given their age 
and current condition.  Priorities continue to be East Hillsdale, Sunnybrae, Shoreview, Washington, Los Prados, 
West Hillsdale and King.  All of these projects have exceeded (or will shortly exceed) their 20 year life cycle.  

Additional funding is also being recommended for project #610027 Central Park Master Plan Implementation 
Phase I which includes the complete renovation of the children’s play area, a new restroom building, new picnic 
areas and the Fallen Heroes Memorial Grove. 

Two projects recommended for funding within the three year horizon are #610035, Citywide Special Facilities 
Upgrade ($250,000) and #TBD Skate Plaza Updates ($400,000).  Following community feedback on the condition of 
the two skate plazas at Shoreview and Beresford Parks, staff agrees that after 15+ years of use, the plazas are 
beginning to show their age.  There are some spots of exposed aggregate at Beresford and both parks have a 
number of surface cracks.  While correction of safety hazards will be a top priority, no further project scope has 
been developed at this time. 

Further out and as funding allows, staff has tentatively programmed funds for the conversion of a third sports field 
to synthetic turf as well as identified some funds to support the implementation of the Recreation Facilities 
Strategic Plan.
