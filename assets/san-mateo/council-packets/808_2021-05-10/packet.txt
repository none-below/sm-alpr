DRAFT  SUMMARY PENDING FINAL DATA VERIFICATION
Senior Concentrations by Property - City of San Mateo 
Properties identified and listed include: 
(1) Multi-residential condominium or rental apartments of 40 or more units limited to seniors; 
(2)  Smaller residential condominium or rental apartments limited to seniors that are within 
approximately 0.5 miles from other larger properties included and would share some of 
the same walking routes
(3) Multi-residential  condominium or rental apartments not limited to seniors but which are 
estimated to have senior populations significantly larger than their representation in the 
city overall; 
(4) Large multi-residential condominium or rental apartments not limited to seniors but which 
are estimated to have senior populations approximating their representation in the city 
overall.
The rationale for including these specific properties was to identify concentrated populations of 
senior housing and to estimate the total number of seniors living in those properties. That would 
enable a  calculation of the estimated number and percentage of San Mateo seniors that would 
benefit from a targeted sidewalk repair plan that prioritized correction of hazardous conditions on 
identified walking routes from these properties to typical destinations within approximately 0.5 
miles.  Such an effort would help fulfill a commitment made by the City in its Age-Friendly 
certification application
The properties are listed by category above in order of highest to lowest number of units in each.
 The number of units was obtained either on line or from the respective property manager. The
 estimated number of senior residents was obtained either from the property manager or, when
 not available, applying a factor derived from a sample property.  In the typical case a factor of 
1.5 residents per unit was used.  For “Studios” and “beds” a factor of 1.0 was used. Calculated
 numbers were rounded down to allow for vacancies. 
In the case of housing not limited to seniors, estimates were based on the best data available
from property managers,  realtors with knowledge of the properties, HOA current or
former Board Members and long-time residents. 
The accuracy of any individual property’s number of residents is not critical as the goal was
 simply to approximate the number of seniors in the aggregate across all included properties and
compare that to the senior population at large.  Many property managers reported they could not
provide the demographic data requested due to Fair Housing Law restrictions. 
It should be noted that what qualified as a “senior” in the senior-restricted properties varies as
well, complicating the estimation process.

Additional Notes justifying impact assessment:
We recognize that not all seniors in these properties walk the routes identified, but that is true of 
seniors in neighborhoods of strictly single-family housing as well.  Additionally, it is reasonable to 
assume that visits to seniors by friends, family members, caregivers, service people, contractors, 
etc. result in higher walking traffic on these sidewalks and extend the benefits of improving 
sidewalk conditions preferentially in these target areas.

Department Activity Guide.

BACKGROUND 
 
State Transit Assistance (STA) County Block Grant Program  
 
In February 2018, MTC Resolution 4321 established the new State Transit Assistance (STA) 
County Block Grant Program policy whereby the nine Bay Area County Transportation Agencies 
(CTA’s), also known as Congestion Management Agencies, would determine how to invest the 
Population-Based STA funds in public transit services and lifeline transportation services. Under 
the STA Block Grant Program, C/CAG would be solely responsible for determining the 
distribution policy among STA-eligible transit operators and funds that will be spent benefiting 
Communities of Concern each fiscal year.  
 
In the past, the MTC Resolution 3837 governed the State Transit Assistance (STA) Population- 
Based fund distribution policy. Under resolution 3837, funding was distributed by MTC to fund 
1) Bay Area Northern County small transit operators, 2) Regional Paratransit, 3) the Lifeline 
Transportation Program, and 4) MTC regional coordination programs. Paratransit and Lifeline 
Transportation Program funds were further distributed among the nine bay area counties. The 
guidelines for the regional Lifeline Transportation Program Cycles 1-5 were developed by MTC,

but each CTA was responsible for administering the program.  
 
As the County Transportation Agency, C/CAG coordinates with STA-eligible transit operators 
and develops the STA Population-Based distribution policy within San Mateo county to fund 
Paratransit service and the Lifeline Transportation Program. SamTrans is the only STA eligible 
transit operator in San Mateo County. Beginning in Fiscal Year 2018-2019, C/CAG and 
SamTrans staff have coordinated and developed an annual STA Population-Based distribution 
policy. In May 2020, the C/CAG Board approved Resolution 20-37 which adopted the 
distribution policy for Fiscal Year 2020-21 STA Population-Based funds.  
 
Along with the previous STA Block Grant Program distributions from Fiscal Years 2018-19 and 
2019-20, C/CAG has accumulated approximately $3,399,304 in STA funds available for the 
Lifeline Transportation Program Cycle 6 beginning in Fiscal Year 2021-22. Per STA Block 
Grant guidelines, any remaining balances from each annual STA fund allocation is rolled over to 
the next fiscal year for San Mateo County. Any unallocated funds will stay in San Mateo County. 
 
Community Based Transportation Plan (CBTP) 
 
Lifeline Transportation Program projects are related to the Community Based Transportation 
Plans (CBTP).  CBTPs are based in Communities of Concern; MTC’s designation of 
communities that have high concentration of both minority and low-income households or that 
have a concentration of other factors including people with disabilities, seniors, and cost-
burdened renters. There are currently 4 CBTPs in San Mateo County (San Bruno/South San 
Francisco; North Central San Mateo, Bayshore, and East Palo Alto). 
 
The CBTP are based on grass roots input, and the perspectives of residents and community 
organizers. Each CBTP will establish solutions to transportation gaps identified during diverse 
outreach campaigns and coordination with local community groups. The CBTP’s include a series 
of transportation solutions designed to benefit low-income residents, the disabled, senior citizens, 
those without vehicles and other disadvantaged communities. The goal of the Lifeline 
Transportation Program is to provide funds to complete projects that are included in, or derived 
from, CBTPs and other local planning efforts for low-income communities. 
 
C/CAG is currently updating the CBTPs for Daly City and Southeast San Mateo County, but the 
update has been delayed by the COVID-19 crisis. C/CAG had originally planned to complete the 
update of the CBTPs in 2020 and then issue the Cycle 6 Call for projects.  However, the COVID-
19 crisis has delayed the update of the CBTPs due to the challenges of creating meaningful 
public engagement and input during the pandemic.   
 
However, C/CAG decided to proceed with the Cycle 6 Call for projects in late 2020 to provide 
the opportunity for existing Lifeline projects, such as community shuttle operations and other 
transit services, to apply and to see if there were additional projects/programs that were 
responding to the needs created by the COVID-19 crisis.
