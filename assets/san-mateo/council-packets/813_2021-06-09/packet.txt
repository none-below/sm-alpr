SUBJECT:
Electric Leaf Blower Rebate Pilot Program
RECOMMENDATION:
Provide feedback and direction on the development of an electric leaf blower rebate pilot program.
BACKGROUND:
Off-road equipment accounts for 8% of communitywide greenhouse gas emissions and includes the use of portable 
equipment and vehicles that do travel on roads, such as construction equipment and lawn and garden equipment. At the 
November 2020 Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission, staff brought forward a report reviewing different policy 
options to address gas leaf blower use in the community. The Commission did not recommend that staff move forward 
with policy action but instead recommended that staff focus on outreach and engagement to encourage the use of electric 
leaf blowers. In response to that feedback, staff has been working on the development of an electric leaf blower rebate 
program. This report outlines different electric leaf blower rebate programs in other jurisdictions and identifies key factors 
for consideration.
Example Rebate Programs
Staff researched electric leaf blower programs in other jurisdictions to identify common strategies and best practices. 
There are a number of voucher programs administered by regional air district agencies, however, staff found that electric 
leaf blower rebate programs administered by cities were the most relevant. Currently, the Bay Area Air Quality 
Management District (BAAQMD), the air district applicable to San Mateo, does not offer vouchers or incentives for electric 
leaf blowers or any lawn and garden equipment. BAAQMD’s Lawn Mower Exchange Program is closed and there are no 
updates on future funding.

San Mateo Electric Leaf Blower Pilot Program
The cost of an electric leaf blower can vary greatly. On the residential scale there seems to be prices that are very 
competitive with the gas alternative; electric cord-powered leaf blowers range between $30-$110 and battery-powered 
electric leaf blowers between $150-$300. For the commercial application, for landscapers, the power of the equipment is a 
more critical consideration. While the cord-powered electric leaf blowers can provide more power, they are still not 
practical for large areas and sites without power outlets. Electric backpack leaf blowers range between $300- $1,100 (price 
includes equipment and one battery). With the electric backpack leaf blower lasting roughly two hours before needing a 
charge, a landscaper would need to purchase additional batteries and chargers. Batteries cost between $200-$650 and 
chargers cost between $40-$60. A commercial landscaper might potentially have to purchase multiple chargers for 
simultaneous charging. 
Based on the varied costs of electric leaf blower equipment and the example programs in other cities, staff recommends 
providing a different rebate amount for residents and commercial gardener and landscape professionals. Furthermore, 
commercial operators appear to be the primary users of leaf blowers in the community. Staff aims to shape the electric 
leaf blower rebate program to have an attractive incentive for commercial landscaping businesses with hopes of having a 
higher impact on noise and emissions. 
Staff developed two possible options for an electric leaf blower equipment rebate program for Commission review and 
consideration. Other permutations are also feasible but these options represent a starting point for discussion:
Option 1:

Residents: 50% of the purchase price, maximum $100 rebate

Commercial Operators: 50% of the purchase price, maximum $500 rebate
Option 2: 

Residents: 50% of the purchase price, maximum $100 rebate

Commercial Operators: 75% of the purchase price, maximum $500 rebate

Eligible expenses would include electric leaf blowers and associated battery and charging equipment. In order to be 
eligible for the rebate, residents would need to submit a local utility bill and professional gardeners and landscapers would 
need to provide a current San Mateo business license. 
Staff anticipate allocating $5,000 of the Sustainability budget for the pilot program. If the pilot is successful, additional 
funds can be budgeted to the program. The City can launch the program as early as Summer 2021. Staff will need to 
develop application and outreach materials and establish administrative rebate processes prior to launch. Staff also 
recommends that the pilot program run though the end of the fiscal year (June 30, 2022) or until the allocated budget is 
expended. Extending the pilot program would be based on the program’s success and budget availability. 
Staff seeks Sustainability and Infrastructure Commission feedback on the rebate amount and structure, program eligibility 
and any other feedback on components of the pilot program. Staff plan to incorporate Commission feedback and launch 
the program in Summer 2021.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Staff plan to allocate $5,000 of the Sustainability budget to the Electric Leaf Blower Rebate Pilot Program.
