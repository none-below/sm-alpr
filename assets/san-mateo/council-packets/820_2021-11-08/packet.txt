Mountain View, CA
Previous collaborations include: Department of 
General Services in Washington, DC, Target, US 
Soccer Federation, and Pow Wow San Jose
After moving into the US from japan in 2011, I started to live in 
the Bay Area in 2016 and recently started a family here after 
giving birth to my son in San Mateo county. I quite often visit San 
Mateo Library since it has a great selection of Japanese children
books. Now, as a new family of three, seeing many friends with 
kids moving out to different states due to the high priced rent 
and property, it is true that I have such fear sometimes if we 
could afford to live in the Bay in the future. The housing issue is 
real for all of us, and I’d really like to support and advocate this 
affordable housing movement as an artist, a resident, and a new 
mother. I have worked with SV@Home in San Jose multiple 
times for their visual needs as well.

Harumo Sato
There are three main themes for the designs: Diverse Culture, Community Bonding, and Harmonious Life. The artwork embrace 
diverse culture as a reflection of the diverse community in San Mateo, promote community bonding through visualization of working 
together, gathering together, and encourage harmonious life with local fauna and flora. I would like to treat kiku/Chrysanthemum as 
symbol of diversity since there are an abundance of different shapes and colors. Each panel has a different way/variety to visualize 
kiku and it’s a reflection of the unique diverse community in San Mateo.

PREVIOUS MURAL WORK
BIO
Having been born in Texas along the Mexican border, raised in 
Mexico, and then spending the majority of my adult life in the USA, 
my work is greatly informed by my binational Mexican/American 
heritage, my queer identity, and the intersection of the LGBTQI and 
Latino communities of San Francisco. 
I have explored these communities and concepts through varied and 
diverse artistic approaches, creating murals, posters, sculptures, and 
art installations that in some way satisfy my own personal curiosity, 
respond to the immediate context, honor the message, and impact 
the spectator. 
As I continue to explore the power of public art, I hope to use my 
work to inspire individuals with the pride and love that I feel for my 
two countries of heritage, my LGBTQI community, and to bring focus 
to the duality of millions of Mexican-Americans living and traveling 
between both sides of this border.
Pronouns: He/His/Him 
JUAN MANUEL CARMONA
