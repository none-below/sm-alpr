SUBJECT:
Diversity and Equity Taskforce – Guidance for Formation
RECOMMENDATION:
Provide guidance to staff on the structure, composition, and objectives for the formation of a Diversity and Equity 
taskforce.
BACKGROUND:
At the City Council goal setting session, held on January 30, 2021, the City Council discussed the formation of a Diversity 
and Equity taskforce. Staff brought back some considerations for the formation of this taskforce at the March 15, 2021 
Study Session. Based on the direction given by the Council at this meeting, staff has developed a revised recommendation 
for the proposed structure, composition, and objectives for the taskforce.
Taskforce Structure
Staff recommends modeling the Diversity and Equity taskforce after the Small Business Recovery taskforce. The taskforce 
would be a non-Brown Act body composed of residents and representatives of City partner organizations and convened by 
staff. The participating organizations would be identified by staff, as discussed later in this report, but there would be 
flexibility to adjust membership as needed over time. City staff would help coordinate and facilitate the meetings. Up to 
two members of City Council could serve as liaisons and attend task force meetings in order to listen and bring ideas back 
to the City Council.  Staff recommends that the taskforce meet monthly for a period of one year.
Taskforce Composition
City Council gave direction to staff to develop a 12 to 15 person taskforce to allow for a broad spectrum of residents and 
partner organizations to participate in the conversation. Given this direction, staff proposes to approach the following 
types of organizations and request that they designate a representative to attend the meetings:

Homeowner Associations (2-3 neighborhoods represented) 

Business Organizations (Chamber of Commerce) 

Social Service providers (i.e. Samaritan House, Peninsula Family Services, Police Activities League, Peninsula 
Conflict Resolution Center) 

School District representatives (San Mateo Foster City School District, San Mateo Unified High School District) 

Organizations serving underrepresented populations (i.e. NAACP, San Mateo Pride Center) 

Faith-based coalitions (i.e. Peninsula Solidarity Cohort, Peninsula Multifaith Coalition) 

Community Health organizations (i.e. Bay Area Community Health Advisory Council) 

County of San Mateo Office of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion 
 
Please note that this is a preliminary list and that staff has not yet reached out to any of these organizations to gauge their 
level of interest in participating. City Council guidance is needed on possible adjustments to this target list of organizations.

Taskforce Objectives
The City Council emphasized the importance of providing clear objectives to the Diversity and Equity taskforce to guide 
their work. Staff has identified the following draft objectives for the taskforce:

Diversifying City Boards and Commissions 

Coordination of resources around community engagement and public forums 

Identifications of gaps in community engagement 

Providing input on Racial Equity Community Art Project 
City Council guidance is needed to identify other potential objectives or to revise or clarify the ones suggested by staff.
BUDGET IMPACT:
There would be no direct impact on the General Fund from the creation of this taskforce. 
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
The formation of a taskforce is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is an organizational or administrative activity that 
will not result in direct or indirect physical changes in the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(5).)
