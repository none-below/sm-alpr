SUBJECT:
Fiscal Year 2020-21 Financial Update
RECOMMENDATION:
This is an informational item on the financial update for Measure S Fund fiscal year 2020-21 as of April 30, 2021. 
BACKGROUND:
This administrative report provides a brief narrative to four financial reports on the City of San Mateo's Measure S Fund as 
of April 30, 2021. 
Att 1 is a balance sheet report, which shows $5.5 million in assets, solely consisting of cash, $0 in liabilities, and $5.7 
million in equities (fund balance). The current year-to-date actuals of $1.1 million in revenue control offset by $1.3 million 
in expenditure control net to a loss of $0.2 million. If the fiscal year were to close as of April 30, 2021, the net loss would 
reduce the unrestricted surplus (equity account 273621) by $0.2 million. 
Att 2 provides a budget to actual comparison for Measure S Fund revenues and expenditures. The Fund has accounted for 
$3.7 million in Measure S transactions and use tax revenues in comparison to the $5.5 million budgeted. Based on the 
latest projection from the City's sales tax consultant, by year end, total Measure S transactions and use tax revenues will 
be $5.4 million, or $0.1 million less than budgeted. Of the $1.9 million budgeted for Measure S expenditures, through April 
2021, the Fund has accounted for $1.3 million, and is projected to end the fiscal year at $1.5 million based on the year-to-
date actuals. The primary driver of favorable budget variance is in other contractual service (11-6114-20-5281), which has 
accounted for only $23,000 in senior transportation costs, reflecting the impact of the pandemic. 
Att 3 lists the funding source status for every active Capital Improvement Program (CIP) project that has the Measure S 
Fund as a funding source. The only projects that have Measure S CIP appropriations for FY 2020-21 are Neighborhood 
Traffic Management (462226), which has received $81,000 of the $250,000 budgeted, and Citywide Street Reconstruction 
Package 2 (467772), which has not received any of the $800,000 budgeted, which represents the "pay go" portion of the 
project. Att 4 provides the project expenses for the same subset of Measure S-funded CIP projects. Any unexpended CIP 
appropriations will be carried forward to the subsequent fiscal year unless the project has been completed. These 
carryover CIP appropriations are reflected in Att 1 as an equity account - Designated CIP Interfund (273411) to ensure that 
these carryover commitments are accounted for and not available to be reappropriated.
BUDGET IMPACT:
The Measure S Fund has a healthy net position, revenues are projected to end FY 2020-21 at $5.4 million, or $0.1 million 
less than budgeted. Measure S expenditures are projected to end the year at $1.5 million, or $0.4 million less than 
budgeted, principally due to the pandemic's impact on the demand for senior transportation vouchers.

SUBJECT:
Measure S Proposed 2021-22 Budget – Approval Recommendation 
RECOMMENDATION:
Recommend that the City Council adopt the the Measure S proposed 2021-22 budget. 
BACKGROUND:
Measure S total revenues for fiscal year 2021-22 are projected to be $6.3 million, comprised of $6.1 million in Measure S 
transactions and use tax, and $160,000 per year over the next two years for a Lifeline Transportation Grant from the 
City/County Association of Governments of San Mateo County (C/CAG) to support the City's Senior Transportation 
Program. There is a City match of $40,000, and a small pass-through fee paid to SamTrans of approximately $6,000 per 
year. 
Measure S total expenditures for fiscal year 2021-22 are projected to be $1.9 million. Salaries and benefits comprise the 
majority, at $1.7 million, which reflects Measure S serving as the funding source for 6.51 full time equivalent (FTE) of staff:
3.0 FTE Police Officer 
0.16 FTE Community Services Supervisor 
2.0 FTE Police Sergeant 
0.75 FTE Library Assistant II 
0.60 FTE Library Assistant II 
Measure S transfers out for 2021-22 total $5.0 million, which is comprised of $1.9 million for Capital Improvement 
Program (CIP) projects, $2.7 million for debt service payments on the 2020 lease revenue bonds that were issued to 
finance the cost of flood control and street rehabilitation costs, and $0.3 million to repay the General Fund for costs 
incurred before Measure S funding was available. Based on staff recommendation and the Measure S Oversight Board's
