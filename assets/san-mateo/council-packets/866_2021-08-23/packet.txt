5.   OLD BUSINESS
A.  Potential Late Fine Elimination at the San Mateo Public Library:  City Librarian James Moore 
presented information for the Board to consider regarding the elimination of fines for items 
returned late: San Mateo Public Library is one of the last three libraries in the Peninsula Library 
System (PLS) still charging late fines and expects to soon be the last one; The revenues from late fine 
collection have been steadily declining over the years; PLS libraries have already eliminated fines on 
youth materials and for seniors, all steps that have already reduced the amount of fines being 
collected by SMPL; replacement fees for items not returned are still being charged at all PLS libraries 
and will continue to be charged; and if late fines are eliminated, the City would endeavor during the 
upcoming 2-year budget planning process to adjust the Library’s budget to cover Library operating 
costs, taking into account the relatively small amount of revenue relinquished by no longer charging 
late fines.  The Board asked questions and offered feedback about the possibility of changes to 
replacement fees on such items as Chromebooks and shortening the time before a replacement fee 
is charged.  Motion was made to recommend late fine elimination at SMPL and refer it to the City 
Council for a decision, and the motion passed 4-1.

B. City Librarian Update on Library Restoration of Full Pre-Pandemic Service:  Mr. Moore shared his 
monthly progress report with the Library Board of Trustees on the steps being taken to restore the 
Library to full pre-pandemic operations.  The Library is on track for a full reopening of 100% of pre-
pandemic operations in September.  San Mateo County recently reissued a mask mandate for all 
employees and visitors to San Mateo County offices.  City of San Mateo will also continue to require 
employees and visitors to City of San Mateo buildings to wear masks.  
C. Clarification of Policies Versus Operations:  At the last Board meeting, Ms. Diaz Nash and Dr. Chen 
volunteered to be on a subcommittee to work on preparing a clear list of questions and examples to 
present to the City Council for guidance to help determine what policy versus programming/ 
operational matters are.  Ms. Diaz Nash and Dr. Chen presented a draft of examples and questions 
on what is clear and what needs clarification in the categories of policy, operational procedures and 
operations.  Discussion ensued and an agreement was reached on the examples and questions that 
would be submitted to Council Member Diane Papan for initial guidance.  The Board also agreed 
that it would be helpful for City Attorney Bahareh Abdollahi to attend a future meeting to discuss 
this topic and provide further guidance.
6.   REPORTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS
Reports by Board Members:

Ms. De Winter met with Council Member Diane Papan at the Marina Library to tour the 
facility and spoke about the structural damage to the facility.

Ms. Diaz Nash attended the reception at the Main Library for artist Julien Schuster whose 
engravings are on exhibit at the Main Library from June 30 to August 25.  For future agenda 
topics, Ms. Diaz Nash recommended a discussion on staff recommendations for next steps 
once the Library no longer charges late fines, and a review of the San Mateo Public Library 
Artwork Donation Policy & Guidelines to make sure it is aligned with the City’s policy.

Mr. Hill attended the reception for artist Julien Schuster.  As a future agenda topic, Mr. Hill 
would like to learn the process on how art gets reviewed for possible exhibition at the Main 
Library.

Mr. Linhares attended the reception for artist Julien Schuster.  Mr. Linhares visited the 
Marina Library and was taken aback by the sloping floor caused by structural shifting and 
settling of the building, despite official assessments that the library is safe for activity.  He 
recommended better street signage for the Marina Library, and to alternate holding Board 
meetings at all three library locations when in-person meetings resume.
Report by Lynne Esselstein, Executive Director of the Foundation:

Ms. Esselstein was delighted to see the Trustees at the artist reception.  She was not advised 
by the organizers of the artist reception that 5% of the purchase proceeds could be 
designated by the buyer to benefit the Foundation.

SAN MATEO PUBLIC LIBRARY 
COMPUTER, INTERNET & WIRELESS ACCESS POLICY
[Revised and Adopted by Library Board, 7/23/12]
San Mateo Public Library provides PC compatible computers.  These are self-help computers 
that have Internet access, word processing, the Library Databases and Catalog.  The Library 
also provides wireless Internet access to users with their own laptops and wireless devices.  
This policy applies to both users of the Library PCs and users who access the Library’s wireless 
network.
It is the mission of San Mateo Public Library to provide high quality library materials and 
services in an efficient, fiscally responsible and timely manner, and to cooperate with other 
institutions dedicated to similar goals in order to meet the individual, informational and 
educational interests of a diverse community.  San Mateo Public Library has incorporated 
Internet access to expand the information resources available to Library users.
All Internet resources accessible through the Library are provided equally to all library users.  To 
ensure fair access to the Library’s PCs, the Library observes daily time limits.  The time is 
automatically tracked using software shared by the Peninsula Library System.
The San Mateo Public Library does not restrict access to online materials; and the Library does 
not monitor nor track the information accessed from the Internet by the public.  However, 
viewing obscene content on personal or Library computers is not permitted [Library Rules of 
Conduct].
The Library also does not have control over the information on the Internet and is therefore not 
responsible for its content.  Parents and guardians are responsible for the Internet information 
selected and/or accessed by their children.  
Misuse of the computer or the Internet may result in the loss of computer and Library privileges.  
Specific examples of misuse include, but are not limited to, the following:

Viewing obscene content on personal or Library computers is not permitted [Library Rules of Conduct]. 

Using the Library computer network, or any part of it, to make unauthorized entry into other computational, 
information or communication services or resources, or other action detrimental to the efficient operation of the 
library computer system or any part of it

Distributing unsolicited advertising

Invading the privacy of others

Making any attempt to damage or cause harmful effect to computer equipment or software

Engaging in any activity that is harassing or defamatory

Using the Internet for any illegal activity, including violation of copyright

Downloading or streaming audio or video files or other action detrimental to the efficient operation of the Library 
computer system or any part of it.
Staff Assistance
Library staff can help with basic computer use, but are unable to provide detailed individual 
instruction. The Library provides other resources for computer instruction.

Guidelines for Using Computers
Computer use is regulated by timing and print management software (EnvisionWare).  A library 
card barcode number is used to log on to computers and library users are required to bring their 
library card at all times. 
Due to high demand, time limits have been set so that everyone will have the same opportunity 
to use the computers.  The maximum time limit for using computers is three hours per day.
Individuals must use their personal library card, and may not use another individual’s card.  
Anyone who uses a card other than their own may have their computer privileges suspended for 
the day (or longer for repeated infractions).
Visitors are provided temporary access to the Library’s computers ( 60-minute time limit at the 
Main Library and Branches).  
No more than two individuals may be at a single PC station.  If conversations are determined to 
be disruptive to other users, the Library reserves the right to limit a PC station to a single user.
Failure to comply with the Library’s computer use policy may result in the suspension of 
computer use privileges.
Printing and Saving Documents
A shared public printer is available for printing. The first three pages per day are free.  After the 
first three pages there is a charge of 15¢ per page.  
The option to save files or documents requires an external storage device.  For security 
reasons, neither files nor programs may be saved or installed on the hard drive of a Library 
computer. USB flashdrives are available for purchase at the Accounts Desk.
Computer sessions end automatically when the time limit is reached.  It is the Library user’s 
responsibility to save or print documents before the end of a session.  The Library does not take 
responsibility for the loss of user documents.

Small Conference Room Use Policy
Adopted by the Library Board of Trustees (5/29/18)
The San Mateo Public Library (SMPL) provides free access to small conference 
rooms that are reserved for use by individuals and small groups. The ten rooms are 
located throughout the San Mateo Main Library and can be reserved online from the 
Library’s website, www.smplibrary.org or by calling the library at 650.522.7818 for 
assistance. Because the rooms are a limited resource, the Library regulates them in 
order to insure equitable access. To make a room reservation, patrons need a valid 
library card from a member library of the Peninsula Library System. 
Room descriptions and rules:
1.) Rooms 320, 321, 322, are located on the third floor of the Main Library. 

These rooms are available for use by individuals or groups of 1-2 persons
2.) Rooms 311, 312, 345 and 346 are located on the third floor Main Library. 

These rooms are available for use by individuals or groups of 1-4 persons
  3.) Room 213 is located on the second floor of Main Library and is reserved for group 
usage only. This is the only room that is not available for reservation for use by only 
one individual. 

This room is available for use by groups of 2-6 persons
4.) Rooms 152 and 153 are located on the first floor in the Children’s area of the Main 
Library. Although these rooms are open to all, they are furnished with smaller chairs 
and tables meant specifically to accommodate usage by children.

These rooms are available for use by individuals or groups of 1-4 persons

Guidelines:

Rooms may be reserved for up to 2-hours per day. Room reservations begin and 
end on the hour, e.g. from 1:00 p.m. to 3:00 p.m. 

Rooms may be reserved up to one week in advance.

Patrons have a 10-minute grace period to enter and occupy the room at the 
beginning of the reserved time. At that point, the room becomes available to 
walk-in patrons, who must request assistance from staff to reserve the room for 
the remainder of the original reservation.

Patrons are allowed one booking per day.

A daily schedule of bookings will be printed and displayed.

Since the study rooms are not soundproof, noise levels must be kept at a 
reasonable conversational volume. Noise should not be audible to those outside 
the room.

The Library’s Rules of Conduct Policy applies to all activities conducted in the 
rooms (e.g. no drugs, alcoholic beverages or tobacco products may be used in 
any room). 

Patrons must leave rooms in clean condition, with all trash in the receptacle and 
the whiteboard wiped clean.

Additional furniture may not be brought into the rooms.

Windows in rooms must remain uncovered at all times.

Lights must remain on at all times for safety purposes.

Do not block the entrance to the room at any time.

Never leave personal belongings unattended inside the rooms.
Failure to comply with these guidelines may result with the suspension of small 
conference room reservation and usage privileges.
