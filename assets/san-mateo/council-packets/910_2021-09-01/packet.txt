SUBJECT:
Central Park Play Area- Preferred Alternative 
RECOMMENDATION:
Review and comment on the preferred design alternative, Adventure Village for the new Central Park Play Area. 
BACKGROUND:
In March 2018 the City Council adopted an updated Master Plan and Mitigated Negative Declaration for Central Park 
which identified a full scope of long term improvements for the park.  Due to its age and condition, the play area was 
identified as the first priority for renovation.  Currently, the playground consists of a mix of equipment including a number 
of metal structures likely installed in the 1950’s as well as a wood structure installed in 1994.  There is also an existing 
restroom building, expansive sand areas, mature trees and a miniature train.  Although several options to expand the 
miniature train footprint were presented during the Master Plan, the consensus was to leave the train in its existing 
location. 
The community outreach process for the playground design started in October 2019 with an on-site workshop to collect 
initial feedback on preferred playground character, features, materials, etc.  A subsequent community-wide survey was 
also conducted to solicit feedback.  The top responses for how the community would like the playground to feel were safe 
and fun, with the highest-ranking priorities listed as imaginative, inclusive and natural.  The results for the favorite play 
elements were swings, slides and climbing features with a desire to see additional shade, seating areas and water 
elements.  Many participants commented on the need for an inclusive playground that could be used by children of all 
ages and abilities as well as one that was enclosed for increased safety.  
The feedback collected from these efforts resulted in the design of two distinctly different conceptual alternatives, 
Concept 1 Adventure Village and Concept 2 Playful History (Attachment 1).  These design alternatives were shared at 
several events to solicit feedback-at a LEAP pod with elementary school children held at the Martin Luther King Jr. 
Community Center (April 2021), a virtual workshop (June 2021), an on-site workshop (June 2021) and a second community 
survey that was online from May-June 2021.  The overwhelming majority of children and adults preferred Concept 1, 
Adventure Village.  They felt the natural character of Adventure Village fit well within the majestic tree canopy of the 
playground while the magical forest inspired creative and imaginative play.  There were several features in Concept 2 
Playful History which the public liked such as a zip line, bronze sculptures and a musical garden that have now been 
incorporated into Adventure Village. Utilizing the collective input from these public outreach events, a single Adventure 
Village Concept is now being presented in the design development stage. (Attachment 2)
The design team from RRM Design Group will review the preferred alternative in more detail at the Commission meeting.  
Worth noting in the Site Plan is that in addition to the renovated playground, the plan includes a new restroom building, 
located adjacent to the main pathway that will serve both the playground area as well as the future tennis courts that will

DESIGN CONCEPT, ALTERNATIVE 2 :  PLAYFUL HISTORY
Inspiration Sketch
Illustration of the Kohl Mansion
Central Park was formerly the site of a beautiful private estate – and William 
Kohl’s mansion was its centerpiece. This Victorian-era property was landscaped 
by the famous horticulturist John McLaren, and included gardens, specimen trees, 
sculptures and a wrought-iron fence that surrounded the property. Today all that 
remains of the estate is the wrought-iron fence, a sculpture and two out-buildings. 
This design is inspired by the historic estate and it’s lush gardens.  The Kohl 
Mansion playhouse offers numerous play experiences and immersive educational 
opportunities to tell some of the site’s stories.
The playhouse opens out onto the hub of the playground – the sculpture plaza that 
features a water table, engaging bronze sculptures and shaded tables for picnicking. 
Beyond this is a fenced garden-themed tot zone, group picnic areas, a formal garden 
area, a wood pergola and perhaps the best of all: a traditional carousel ride.
