1.
Fiscal Year 2020-21 Financial Update 
Finance Director Rich Lee provided a financial update presentation for the Measure S Fund fiscal year 2020-21 as 
of April 30, 2021. Lee reviewed a balance sheet report, showing $5.5 million in assets, solely consisting of cash, $0 
in liabilities, and $5.7 million in equities. 
Lee provided information on budget actuals for Measure S Fund revenues and expenditures. Measure S sales tax 
had budgeted $5.5 million through April. The City accounted for $3.7 million. By year end, the total Measure S 
transactions and use tax revenues are projected to be $5.4 million. In total expenditures, of the budgeted $1.9 
million dollars through April, $1.3 million were spent. The year-end estimate for total expenditures for Measure S 
was $1.5 million. Lee noted that the correlation between the pandemic and senior transportation vouchers 
accounted for much of the projected variance. 
The committee asked questions about the current fiscal year financial update.
Public comment. There were no speakers.
NEW BUSINESS
2.
Measure S Proposed 2021-22 Budget – Approval Recommendation 
Finance Director Rich Lee a presented a preview of the proposed 2021-22 budget. Lee reminded the committee 
that they are specifically directed by ordinance to make a recommendation to the City Council for the proposed 
budget for Measure S. 
Lee presented the proposed budget, noting ongoing post-pandemic recovery specifically for Measure S
to get back to the 2018-19 baseline. Lee provided an in-depth financial report. Measure S is projected to account 
for $6.3 million in total revenues, $1.9 million in total expenditures, and $5.0 million in transfers out for fiscal year 
2021-22, which would result in a drawdown on available fund balance of $0.6 million.

SUBJECT:
Measure S Independent Accountant's Report - Review and Accept
RECOMMENDATION:
Accept the Independent Accountant's Report on Agreed-Upon Procedures Applied to Schedule of Measure S Revenues and 
Expenditures for the year ended June 30, 2021.
BACKGROUND:
In November 2015, San Mateo voters approved Measure S, which extended the 1/4 cent transactions and use tax that had 
been established in 2009 via Measure L, and was set to expire on March 31, 2018. In compliance with San Mateo 
Municipal Code 3.50.160, the Measure S Oversight Committee reviews the annual auditor's report. As noted in the report 
for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2021, the auditor noted no exceptions to the agreed-upon procedures. The Measure S 
sub-fund accounted for $5.7 million in total revenues, $1.6 million in expenditures, and $3.2 million in transfers out.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Measure S revenues were $0.3 million more than the adopted 2020-21 budget, which illustrated the beginning of a 
gradual recovery from the financial impact of COVID-19 on local businesses. Expenditures were under budget by $0.3 
million, largely due to the impact of the pandemic on the use of senior transportation vouchers compared to what was 
assumed in the adopted 2020-21 budget. Transfers out were $0.9 million under budget, as transfers for capital projects 
posted as projects progress through completion. Unused transfers out for capital projects are carried over to the 
subsequent fiscal year. 
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This Independent Accountant's Report is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is a government fiscal activity which 
does not involve any commitment to any specific project which may result in a potentially significant physical impact on 
the environment. (CEQA Guidelines Section 15378(b)(4).)

RECOMMENDATION:

BACKGROUND:
The amended 2021-22 budget projected $6.3 million in total Measure S Fund revenues, $1.8 million in total expenditures, 
and $5.0 million in transfers out. 
Through October 31, 2021: 

Measure S has received $1.2 million in sales tax revenues and based on the latest estimate from the City's sales tax 
consultant, is projected to end the year with $5.9 million in sales tax revenues. 

Measure S has accounted for $0.5 million in expenditures and is projected end the year with $1.4 million in total 
expenditures. 

Measure S has accounted for $1.0 million in transfers out; $0.1 million in transfers out to the General Fund to 
repay the loan, and $0.9 million in transfers out for debt service.
BUDGET IMPACT:
Revenues are projected to be $0.2 million less than budgeted. Based on year-to-date trends, expenditures are projected to 
be $0.4 million less than budgeted, and transfers out are projected to be aligned with the budget. Based on the year-to-
date trends, the Measure S Fund will end the year with a $0.5 million net loss, which will reduce fund balance from $6.6 
million to $6.1 million.
ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINATION:
This Measure S Financial Update is not a project subject to CEQA, because it is a government fiscal activity which does not
