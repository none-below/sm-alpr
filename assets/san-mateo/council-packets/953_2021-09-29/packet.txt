SUBJECT:
Housing and Community Needs Workshop for 2022-23 Community Development Block Grant Annual Plan and Housing 
Element Update
RECOMMENDATION:
That the Community Relations Commission determine community priorities in preparation of the 2022-23 Annual Plan for 
the Community Development Block Grant Program and provide comments on priority housing needs for the Housing 
Element Update.
BACKGROUND:
Each year the City of San Mateo receives funds from the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) 
for the Community Development Block Grant Program (CDBG).  The program provides funding to support projects and 
services to San Mateo's most vulnerable community members.  CDBG project and program areas include, but are not 
limited to, public service grants to nonprofit agencies, home repairs to low income homeowners, and improvements to 
neighborhood parks and public infrastructure in the CDBG target areas (See Attachment 1-CDBG Target Area Map). Part of 
the process to obtain the CDBG funds is to submit to HUD with an Annual Plan that identifies community needs and 
prioritizes the types of programs and projects it will fund.
The Housing Element is a section of the City’s General Plan, which is the citywide document that will guide and plan for 
growth, change, and conservation over the long term to 2040. The Housing Element includes the programs and actions the 
City will undertake to ensure that the diverse and complex housing needs of the City are able addressed. The Housing 
Element is updated in 8-year cycles, with the upcoming cycle for the period from 2023 - 2031. The underlying vision of the 
General Plan’s Housing Element is to facilitate the production of housing that is abundant, affordable, and accessible.
San Mateo Housing Background Information
The City of San Mateo continues to face the challenge of providing housing that meets all of the various needs of the 
community. The population of San Mateo has steadily grown every year, with a current total of 104,430 people. This 
represents a 26% increase since 1980 compared to a 25% increase in housing units. The largest age demographic 
represented in the current population of the City is adults aged 20-34 years old, the primary age group that is in the 
market for purchasing or renting a new home. Seniors aged 65+ years and above represent 15% of the population and 
have different housing needs such as aging in place or downsizing.
The price of housing in the city has risen well past levels of affordability for many members of the community. The average 
monthly rent of an apartment in the City starts at $2,300 for a 1 bedroom unit while a 2 and 3 bedroom unit will rent at 
$3,200 and $4,400, respectively. The annual salary needed to be able to afford these units ranges from $83,000 to 
$115,000 to $158,000. Meanwhile the median home price is $1.62 million, a price that puts them essentially unattainable 
for 2/3 all of people living in San Mateo.

CITY OF SAN MATEO
The issue of housing affordability affects people of color disproportionately. In San Mateo, 59% of Black residents and 
54.8% of Latino residents pay over a third of their income towards housing, in comparison to 33.9% of White residents 
who are the same situation. Similarly, only 14% and 10% of Black and Latino adults own the homes they occupy, compared 
to 37% of White adults who own their homes. Addressing affordability and addressing equity will be evaluated in the 
Housing Element.   
Community Needs Workshop
For the next fiscal year, 2022-23, the Annual Plan will include a summary of the City's programs and projects to address 
community development, housing and homelessness, and public services for lower income households.  The Community 
Relations Commission (CRC) is holding the Community Needs Assessment to gather input on the priority needs in order to 
develop the plan.
The purpose of the workshop is to request input to determine the needs and concerns of the community as they relate to 
both CDBG and housing in the City of San Mateo. The feedback from the community will also be compiled into the 
outreach efforts of the update of the Housing Element, which is a part of the Strive San Mateo 2040 General Plan Update.
