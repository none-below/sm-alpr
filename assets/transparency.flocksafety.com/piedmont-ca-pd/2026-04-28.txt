Overview

The Piedmont Police Department uses Flock Safety's Operating System to capture objective evidence without compromising on individual privacy. Piedmont PD utilizes retroactive search to solve crimes after they've occurred. Additionally, PPD utilizes real-time alerting of hotlist vehicles to capture wanted criminals. In an effort to be open with the public to demonstrate our proper usage and communicate what guardrails are in place, we have made the below policies and usage statistics available to the public.

Policies

What's Detected

Vehicles, License Plates

What's Not Detected

Facial recognition, People, Gender, Race

Acceptable Use Policy

Data is used for law enforcement purposes only. Data is owned by Piedmont PD and is never sold to 3rd parties.

Prohibited Uses

Immigration enforcement, traffic enforcement, harrassment or intimidation, usage based solely on a protected class (i.e. race, sex, religion), Personal use.

Access Policy

All system access requires a valid case number and is stored indefinitely. This data is regularly audited every 90 days.

Hotlist Policy

Hotlist hits are required to be human verified prior to action.

Success Story

On 01/07/2026 at approximately 1647 hours, Piedmont Police Officers were dispatched to a report of a tool theft in progress. Upon our quick response, it was determined that unknown suspect(s) had just stolen a 2026 GMC Sierra 3500HD along with an attached dump trailer hauling a large quantity of concrete. The suspect(s) fled the scene in the stolen vehicle and trailer but lost control several blocks away due to the weight, ultimately colliding with a residence.

Using nearby surveillance cameras, we observed a suspect vehicle, a dark colored Honda Accord, associated with the theft of the GMC. Through Flock ALPR technology, I initially captured only a side profile image of the suspect vehicle as it fled out of town. I submitted a request for additional footage from one of our Flock cameras and was able to review the footage frame by frame. This allowed me to obtain a partial rear license plate; while four characters were obstructed, the last three characters were visible.

Using the vehicle description, visible stickers, and the last three characters of the plate, I searched 30 days of history for similar vehicles ending in #310. This led to the identification of the suspect vehicles full license plate and a possible suspect believed to be related to or associated with the registered owner.

The case is still ongoing, but thanks to Flock ALPR technology, we now have strong, reliable leads that are significantly advancing the investigation.
