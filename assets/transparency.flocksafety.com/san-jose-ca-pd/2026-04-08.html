Overview

San Jose CA PD uses Flock Safety technology to capture objective evidence without compromising on individual privacy. San Jose CA PD utilizes retroactive search to solve crimes after they've occurred. Additionally, San Jose CA PD utilizes real time alerting of hotlist vehicles to capture wanted criminals. In an effort to ensure proper usage and guardrails are in place, they have made the below policies and usage statistics available to the public.

Policies

What's Detected

License Plates, Vehicles

What's Not Detected

Facial recognition, People, Gender, Race

Acceptable Use Policy

Data is used for law enforcement purposes only. Data is owned by San Jose CA PD and is never sold to 3rd parties.

Prohibited Uses

Immigration enforcement, traffic enforcement, harrassment or intimidation, usage based solely on a protected class (i.e. race, sex, religion), Personal use.

Access Policy

All system access requires a valid reason and is stored indefinitely.

Hotlist Policy

Hotlist hits are required to be human verified prior to action.

On June 3, 2024, AIR3 was notified of a Flock stolen vehicle hit. AIR3 located and followed the suspect vehicle until it parked. AIR3 put out updates as patrol responded and the driver abandoned the vehicle. AIR3 guided in patrol units ten minutes later to the suspects location and took him into custody. On June 4, 2024, AIR3 was notified of a Flock stolen vehicle hit. AIR3 located the suspect vehicle parked behind a power relay station. AIR3 saw two male suspects approach the vehicle and one of the suspects got into the drivers seat. AIR3 put out the information and confirmed the vehicle was stolen. AIR3 saw the suspect move the truck roughly forty feet where they tried to jump start a red Ford sedan. Patrol officers arrived on scene and arrested the driver. On June 6, 2024, AIR3 responded to a Flock hit for a Ford pickup truck. Patrol Officers located the vehicle near the highway and AIR3 immediately responded to the location. Patrol Officers initiated an enforcement stop on the stolen vehicle and it immediately fled at a high rate of speed. AIR3 took over tracking the vehicle and advised patrol of the vehicles location and direction of travel. The driver ghost rode the truck as he fled on foot from the vehicle. AIR3 guided patrol units to the suspect who ran approximately one block after abandoning the vehicle. The suspect was taken into custody and booked into Santa Clara County Main Jail.
