Overview

Shasta County SO uses Flock Safety technology to capture objective evidence without compromising on individual privacy. Shasta County SO utilizes retroactive search to solve crimes after they've occurred. Additionally, Shasta County SO utilizes real time alerting of hotlist vehicles to capture wanted criminals. In an effort to ensure proper usage and guardrails are in place, they have made the below policies and usage statistics available to the public.

Policies

What's Detected

License Plates, Vehicles

What's Not Detected

Facial recognition, People, Gender, Race

Acceptable Use Policy

Data is used for law enforcement purposes only. Data is owned by Shasta County SO and is never sold to 3rd parties.

Prohibited Uses

Reproductive care enforcement and searches for out-of-state or federal agencies per California Senate Bill 34. Immigration enforcement, traffic enforcement, harassment or intimidation, usage based solely on a protected class (i.e. race, sex, religion), Personal use.

Access Policy

All system access requires a valid reason and is stored indefinitely. For further information on Shasta County SO's ALPR policy, refer to Lexipol Policy 433 on the Sheriff's Office Homepage.

Hotlist Policy

Hotlist hits (i.e., stolen vehicles, wanted vehicles, missing persons, etc.) that are entered into the CCIC/NCIC, NCMEC/Amber Alert systems will provide an alert notification. Based on policy all scanned license plates are required to be verified by law enforcement personnel and checked in the CCIC/NCIC information system(s) prior to action.

Recent Success Story

The Shasta County Sheriffs Office has utilized the Flock Safety ALPRs for one full-month since project completion in January. Since the full roll-out of the Flock Safety ALPRs system, the Shasta County Sheriffs Office has seen the successful resolution of 10 cases via Flock Safety ALPR Hotlist hits. The Shasta County Sheriffs Office recovered 4 stolen vehicles and successfully resolved 6 felony cases via arrest using the system in that timeframe. Without Flock Safety ALPRs, the victims of the aforementioned crimes may still be looking for closure and justice for their stolen property or crimes perpetrated against them.
