Overview

Woodland CA PD uses Flock Safety technology to capture objective evidence without compromising on individual privacy. Woodland CA PD utilizes retroactive search to solve crimes after they've occurred. Additionally, Woodland CA PD utilizes real time alerting of hotlist vehicles to capture wanted criminals. In an effort to ensure proper usage and guardrails are in place, they have made the below policies and usage statistics available to the public.

Policies

What's Detected

License Plates, Vehicles

What's Not Detected

Facial recognition, People, Gender, Race

Acceptable Use Policy

Data is used for law enforcement purposes only. Data is owned by Woodland CA PD and is never sold to 3rd parties.

Prohibited Uses

Immigration enforcement, traffic enforcement, harassment or intimidation, usage based solely on a protected class (i.e. race, sex, religion), Personal use.

Access Policy

All system access requires a valid reason and is stored indefinitely.

Hotlist Policy

Hotlist hits are required to be human verified prior to action.

Recent Success Story's

January 2025
CHP Kidnapping Suspects Arrested- Incident 25007035
On January 16, 2025, at approximately 1638 hours, the California Highway Patrol let law enforcement agencies in Woodland know they were tracking a kidnapping suspect via a phone ping and they were headed into Woodland. The suspect vehicle, a white Acura, was captured on a Flock License plate Reader (LPR) camera at County Road 98 and W. Kentucky Ave. as it entered Woodland, and Woodland PD officers checked the area for the suspect. The suspect vehicle was soon located by CHP on County Road 102 at Maxwell Ave., and it was involved in a traffic collision with an uninvolved vehicle after a brief vehicle pursuit as law enforcement
attempted to stop the vehicle. The two suspects fled on foot from the Acura, but were quickly apprehended.

February 2025
Stolen Vehicle Suspect Fled to Southern California- Case WDP25-000445
On February 3, 2025, at approximately 0817 hours, a Woodland PD officer was dispatched to the 300 block of W. Main St. for a stolen vehicle report. The victim advised she parked her Ford in her apartment parking lot a few days prior and just noticed it missing. The officer checked the Flock system and noticed the Ford was captured on a camera in Moreno Valley a couple hours before taking this report. The Ford was entered into the Stolen Vehicle System.

Suicidal Person Located/Saved  Incident #25022031
On February 20. 2025, officers received information through dispatch of a suicidal person reported to the Yolo County Sheriffs Department. The license plate was provided in the informational be on the lookout incident shared to Woodland PD. A Woodland officer used the Flock camera system to check for the vehicle and saw that it had entered Woodland at 0210 hours in the area of County Road 98 and Main Street. He later located the vehicle a few blocks away. Several officers responded to the area, located the subject, and applied life saving measures and administered Narcan. Their proactive use of Flock and quick actions resulted in the individuals life being saved.

July 2025
Stolen GMC from Lincoln, CA- Case WDP25-002725
On July 9, 2025, at approximately 2343 hours, Woodland PD officers received an alert on their department phones that a stolen GMC was just captured on a Flock LPR camera on East St. at Sports Park Dr. It was quickly determined the GMC was stolen out of Lincoln, CA, and the identity of the suspect was known. The suspect, a 36-year-old Woodland resident, also had a confirmed felony warrant out of Placer County. Officers located the GMC at Walmart (1250 E. Gibson Rd.), and the known suspect was contacted nearby. The suspect initially provided a false name, but his real identity was confirmed and he was arrested on multiple charges including the
vehicle theft, the warrant, impersonating another person while committing a felony, and possession of drug paraphernalia.

August 2025
College St. and Main St. Shooting- Case WDP25-003327
On August 16, 2025, at approximately 0011 hours, officers responded to the area of Main St. and College St. regarding multiple gunshots heard outside the Stag Bar. Bullet casings were located, shattered glass from a nearby business was determined to be caused by bullets, and witnesses were interviewed. After video surveillance was obtained from a business, the suspect vehicle was found to be a Chevrolet Equinox with some unique characteristics. Officers checked the Flock LPR cameras nearby, and located a Chevrolet that matched the suspect vehicle description around the time of the shooting. Officers searched the city for the Chevrolet with this license plate, located it, and found it matched the unique identifiers as seen in the initial video surveillance. A search warrant was served at the Chevrolet owner&#39;s residence. The suspect, a 25-year-old Woodland resident was arrested and multiple firearms were located. Once this first suspect was arrested, officers wrote search warrants into his social media. With this information, a second shooting suspect was eventually identified, who was a 24-year-old Woodland resident. A search warrant for his residence was served, he was arrested, and multiple firearms were located.

February 2026
On February 12, 2026, officers responded to a residence in Woodland regarding a disturbance involving a mother and her estranged teenage daughter. The mother arrived at the home and attempted to take property from the daughter. A physical altercation followed, during which the mother assaulted the daughter. The daughter sustained injuries and was later treated at a local emergency room. During the incident, the mothers boyfriend attempted to flee the scene in a vehicle while the
daughter was partially hanging out of it. Based on the circumstances, a felony child abuse want was established for the driver. Officers conducted follow-up efforts over the next two weeks to locate the suspects and the vehicle but were initially unsuccessful. The investigating officer entered the suspect vehicle into he FLOCK mobile application. On February 26, the officer received an alert indicating the vehicle was entering Woodland. Officers responded to the area, located the vehicle and both
suspects, and took them into custody without incident.
